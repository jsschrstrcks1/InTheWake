First Look

Carnival Breeze entered service on June 3, 2012 as the third and final Dream-class ship—and quickly became the flagship for Texas cruisers. Her homeport in Galveston makes Caribbean cruising accessible to millions across Texas, Oklahoma, Louisiana, and the central United States.

At 130,000 GT with capacity for 3,690 guests, Breeze incorporates all Dream-class innovations plus refinements from lessons learned on Dream and Magic. She features WaterWorks aqua park, SkyCourse ropes course, the Lanai outdoor promenade, and the full complement of Guy Fieri and Carnival dining concepts.

Built by Fincantieri at Monfalcone, Breeze represents the culmination of the Dream-class design philosophy—open-air spaces, generous public areas, and the signature Fun Ship experience that defines Carnival cruising.

Dream-Class Sister Ships

All three Dream-class ships were built by Fincantieri at their Monfalcone shipyard in Italy. Breeze completed the class, incorporating refinements from both earlier sisters.

Frequently Asked Questions

Where does Carnival Breeze sail from?

Carnival Breeze operates from Galveston, Texas, offering Western Caribbean, Eastern Caribbean, and Mexico itineraries. Galveston's location near Houston makes cruising accessible to Texas, Oklahoma, Louisiana, Arkansas, and the central United States without requiring flights to Florida ports.

What class is Carnival Breeze?

Carnival Breeze is a Dream-class ship, the third and final vessel in the class. Her sisters are Carnival Dream (2009) and Carnival Magic (2011). All three were built by Fincantieri at their Monfalcone shipyard in Italy.

How big is Carnival Breeze?

Carnival Breeze measures 130,000 gross tons and is 1,004 feet (306 meters) long. She accommodates 3,690 guests at double occupancy across 1,845 staterooms on 15 guest decks, with a crew of 1,386.

Does Carnival Breeze have a water park?

Yes, WaterWorks is Carnival Breeze's onboard water park featuring the Twister waterslide, DrainPipe tube slide, splash zones for kids, and the PowerDrencher tipping bucket. Located on the Sports Deck, it's included with your cruise fare.

What dining is on Carnival Breeze?

Carnival Breeze features complimentary venues including Sapphire/Blush dining rooms, Lido Marketplace, Guy's Burger Joint, BlueIguana Cantina, and Pizzeria del Capitano. Specialty options (additional cost) include Fahrenheit 555 Steakhouse, Cucina del Capitano, Bonsai Sushi, and JiJi Asian Kitchen.
