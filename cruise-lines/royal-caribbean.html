Royal Caribbean

Neighborhood-style ships, headline entertainment, and a specialty-dining bench deep enough to turn a sea day into a progressive tasting. Start with your class—Icon, Oasis, Freedom, Voyager, Radiance, Vision—then pick the ship whose vibe (and venues) match your crew.

Packing for slides, show nights, and breezy pool decks? See Packing Lists (reef-safe sunscreen, magnet hooks, lanyards, small dry bag = MVPs on RCI).

Ship Classes

Icon Class debut 2024

Royal’s “world’s best family vacation” era: waterpark neighborhood, seven pools, and a show slate that spans air, ice, water, and stage. Expect maximal choice with smarter crowd flow and a grown-up palette of lounges for date-night escapes.

Premium venues by class (varies by ship): Empire Supper Club, Wonderland, 150 Central Park, Chops Grille, Izumi / Teppanyaki, Hooked Seafood, Giovanni’s, Chef’s Table. See venues & menus.

Icon of the Seas

The headline-maker. Category 6 slides, Surfside for families, adults-only Cloud 17, and an AquaDome that turns acrobatics into goosebumps. Big-ship energy with pockets of calm when you want them.

Star of the Seas

Second in class and dialed for sun-chasing weeks: seven pools, party-forward weekends, and the same “something for everyone” engine—plus fresh tweaks to nightlife flow.

Star of the Seas (Aug 2025 debut)

Refinements on the formula you already know—Icon comfort with new show kits and venue placements. Watch this space for venue-by-venue coverage.

Oasis Class neighborhood megaships

The original “neighborhood” revolution: Central Park, Boardwalk, AquaTheater, and the Ultimate Abyss on many ships. Broadway-scale shows meet casual-to-premium dining and resort-level pool decks.

Premium venues (varies by ship): 150 Central Park, Wonderland, Chops, Giovanni’s, Izumi / Teppanyaki, Hooked, The Mason Jar, Portside BBQ, Chef’s Table.

Oasis of the Seas

The blueprint: Central Park strolls, Boardwalk fun, and big-evening theater. Perfect for first-timers who want “it all” without guesswork.

Allure of the Seas

Entertainment-forward with classic Oasis charm—AquaTheater spectacles, family spaces that work all day, and easy dinner-to-show flow.

Harmony of the Seas

“Go big on bold” energy: Ultimate Abyss dry slide, Perfect Storm waterslides, and a nightlife circuit that hums after dark.

Symphony of the Seas

Variety king. Superb show package, Central Park date nights, and family magnets like laser tag and ice shows.

Wonder of the Seas

Weekender icon: resort-style pools by day, party-forward neighborhoods by night, with CocoCay pairings that deliver max fun in short bursts.

Utopia of the Seas

The “ultimate weekend” playbook—short sailings, big thrills, dialed dining. Think rapid-fire fun with zero compromise.

Freedom Class

Sweet spot scale—big-ship staples without mega-ship sprawl. FlowRider surf, sports decks, comedy/live-music nights, and versatile dining that works for mixed-age groups.

Premium venues commonly found: Chops, Giovanni’s, Izumi, Chef’s Table, Johnny Rockets.

Freedom of the Seas

Weekend warrior favorite: Perfect Day pairings, refreshed pool deck, and just-right variety for families and friend groups.

Liberty of the Seas

FlowRider + Splashaway Bay + glow-in-the-dark laser tag = effortless sea days. Evenings swing from pub singalongs to steakhouse classics.

Independence of the Seas

Short-sailing pro: dueling slides, sports-deck energy, and late-night venues that keep the vibe rolling.

Voyager Class

The class that taught cruising to skate—literally. Ice rinks, Royal Promenade parades, and compact layouts that make “do it all” days surprisingly easy.

Premium venues often present: Chops, Giovanni’s, Izumi, Chef’s Table, Johnny Rockets.

Voyager of the Seas

Trailblazer that still punches above its weight: ice shows, rock wall views, and family-friendly flow on port-heavy routes.

Explorer of the Seas

Easygoing crowd pleaser—great for “first RCI” trips. Expect promenade buzz, pool-deck contests, and classic specialty hits.

Adventure of the Seas

Island-hopper spirit: Perfect Day pairs beautifully with laser tag and slide time; nights lean lively in the pub and Boleros.

Navigator of the Seas

Short-cruise specialist out of LA: Blaster aqua coaster, double-size pool deck, and the Lime & Coconut rooftop scene.

Mariner of the Seas

Boredom-buster for 3–5 nights: Perfect Storm slides, Playmakers hangouts, and reliable specialty lineup.

Radiance Class

Glass-and-views ships built for scenery: Alaska, fjords, and coastals. Floor-to-ceiling windows, calmer vibe, and classic specialty pairings.

Premium venues typically seen: Chops, Izumi, Chef’s Table.

Radiance of the Seas

Window-to-water cruising: quiet Solarium, theater nights, and scenery that’s part of the itinerary.

Brilliance of the Seas

Refined and well-paced—wine at Vintages, steaks at Chops, and sunrise-at-sea views all over.

Serenade of the Seas

Great Alaska fit: glass elevators to glaciers, easy deck flow, evenings with jazz or production shows.

Jewel of the Seas

Smaller-ship warmth meets big-views DNA—ideal for port-intense Med and Caribbean runs.

Vision Class

Cozy classics with atrium life and big-itinerary charm. Think Bermuda, New England, or Caribbean hops with simpler logistics and personable crews.

Premium venues (select ships): Chops, Chef’s Table. Packing: smart storage & formal-lite.

Vision of the Seas

Glass-canopy Solarium, promenade life in the Centrum, and itineraries that put ports first.

Rhapsody of the Seas

Intimate, musical, and friendly—perfect for easygoing island chains and classic cocktails at sunset.

Enchantment of the Seas

Stretch-added decks, great pool-day flow, and a nostalgic Royal feel that still feels fresh.

Grandeur of the Seas

Beloved “Grande Dame” vibe—crew-forward hospitality and atrium evenings that feel like a neighborhood.

Majesty of the Seas (archive)

Historical reference: Royal’s compact classic—kept for context and nostalgia.
