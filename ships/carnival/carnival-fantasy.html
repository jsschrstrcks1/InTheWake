Carnival Fantasy

Retired 2020 Lead Ship

The Glass Cathedral That Changed Everything

Carnival Fantasy is a Fantasy Class cruise ship operated by Carnival Cruise Line. She entered service in 1990, measures 70,367 gross tons, and carries approximately 2,056 guests at double occupancy.

Quick Answer: Carnival Fantasy (70,367 GT | 2,056 guests | 1990) was the original Fantasy Class ship — the vessel that launched an entire class of Fun Ships and helped make cruising accessible to mainstream travelers. This page covers historical deck plans, dining venues, and ship details.

Best For: Cruise history enthusiasts — Fantasy was the ship that proved Carnival's fun-first formula could scale, launching eight sister ships over the decade.

Dining Venues

Carnival Fantasy featured classic Fantasy Class dining venues including the Celebration Main Dining Room, the Sensation Buffet, and specialty additions from Fun Ship 2.0 upgrades including Guy's Burger Joint and BlueIguana Cantina. The Revolutionary Pioneer

Carnival Fantasy wasn't just a cruise ship — she was a revolution floating on water. Launched in 1990 as the first of the Fantasy Class, she introduced the glass-topped atrium that would define modern cruise ship design for decades.

Designed by the legendary Joe Farcus, Fantasy's interior flooded with natural light through her revolutionary glass ceiling. Every cruise ship with a soaring atrium today owes something to this pioneer. She proved that cruise ships could be cathedrals of joy rather than floating hotels.

Godmother: Tellervo Koivisto, wife of Finnish President Mauno Koivisto — honoring the Helsinki shipyard that built this revolutionary vessel.

Frequently Asked Questions About Carnival Fantasy

When was Carnival Fantasy retired?

Carnival Fantasy was retired in July 2020 during the COVID-19 pandemic after 30 years of service. She was scrapped at Aliaga, Turkey.

What made Carnival Fantasy revolutionary?

Fantasy was the first cruise ship with a glass-topped atrium, flooding the interior with natural light and establishing design standards every modern cruise ship follows.

Who was Carnival Fantasy's godmother?

Tellervo Koivisto, wife of Finnish President Mauno Koivisto, christened Carnival Fantasy — honoring the Helsinki shipyard that built this revolutionary vessel.

How many ships were in the Fantasy Class?

Eight ships comprised the Fantasy Class: Fantasy (lead ship), Ecstasy, Sensation, Fascination, Imagination, Inspiration, Elation, and Paradise. Only Elation and Paradise remain in service today.

What was Carnival Fantasy's final voyage?

Carnival Fantasy's final sailing was on March 14, 2020, when the COVID-19 pandemic halted cruise operations. She never returned to service and was sold for scrap.
