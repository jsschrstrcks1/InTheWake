Carnival Cruise Line Fleet

Welcome aboard the Fun Ships! Carnival Cruise Line operates 27 vessels ranging from intimate classics to the largest ships ever built for a North American cruise line. Whether you're chasing thrills on BOLT (the first roller coaster at sea), relaxing in a Havana cabana, or sampling Guy Fieri's famous burgers poolside, there's a Carnival ship designed for your kind of fun.

Below you'll find every ship organized by class, from the groundbreaking Excel-class giants to the beloved Fantasy-class favorites. Each class has its own personality, signature venues, and sweet spots for different travelers.

Excel Class (XL)

The biggest party at sea. These 180,000+ GT behemoths feature BOLT roller coaster, themed Lido zones (like 820 Biscayne and La Piazza), Guy's Pig & Anchor Smokehouse, and LNG-powered propulsion. Perfect for families and thrill-seekers who want maximum variety.

Signature venues: BOLT Roller Coaster, Emeril's Bistro, Rudi's Seagrill, The Gateway, Zone-themed Lidos

  • Mardi Gras
  • Celebration
  • Jubilee

Vista Class

SkyRide and IMAX pioneers. These 133,500 GT ships introduced aerial cycling (SkyRide), IMAX theaters, and the Havana section with Cuban-inspired cabins and private pool. The perfect balance of innovation and classic Carnival fun.

Signature venues: SkyRide, IMAX Theatre, Havana Bar & Pool, Fahrenheit 555 Steakhouse, RedFrog Pub

  • Vista
  • Horizon
  • Panorama

Dream Class

The sweet spot. At 130,000 GT, Dream-class ships offer WaterWorks aqua park, the open-air Lanai promenade, and Ocean Plaza—a hub for live music and dancing. These ships hit the perfect balance between mega-ship amenities and manageable size.

Signature venues: WaterWorks, Ocean Plaza, Lanai, The Punchliner Comedy Club, Cucina del Capitano

  • Dream
  • Magic
  • Breeze

Conquest Class

The Fun Fleet workhorses. These reliable 110,000 GT ships deliver classic Carnival experiences with Serenity adults-only retreat, Guy's Burger Joint, and the iconic Lido Deck. Great value and consistently solid sailings from ports across the U.S.

Signature venues: Serenity Adult Retreat, Guy's Burger Joint, BlueIguana Cantina, Alchemy Bar, Piano Bar 88

  • Conquest
  • Glory
  • Valor
  • Liberty
  • Freedom

Spirit Class

Classic Carnival, refined. These 88,500 GT ships offer a more intimate experience while still delivering Carnival's signature fun. Popular for longer itineraries and exotic destinations like Alaska and Hawaii.

Signature venues: Spa Carnival, Serenity, The Steakhouse, Nouveau Art Gallery, Victoria Lounge

  • Spirit
  • Pride
  • Legend
  • Miracle

Sunshine Class

Mid-size favorites, modernized. Originally Destiny-class ships, these 101-103,000 GT vessels have been extensively refurbished with modern Carnival amenities. They offer a great balance of space, value, and the latest Fun Ship features.

Signature venues: Guy's Burger Joint, BlueIguana Cantina, RedFrog Rum Bar, Alchemy Bar, Cloud 9 Spa

  • Sunshine
  • Sunrise
  • Radiance

Splendor Class

One of a kind. At 113,300 GT, Carnival Splendor stands alone in her class. Originally built as a bridge between Spirit and Dream classes, she offers a unique layout with Cloud 9 Spa thermal suites and Cucina del Capitano Italian dining.

Signature venues: Cloud 9 Spa, Cucina del Capitano, The Taste Bar, Pinnacle Steakhouse

  • Splendor

Venice Class

Italian heritage meets Carnival fun. These ships transferred from Costa Cruises bring European elegance to the Fun Fleet. Featuring grand piazzas, authentic Italian dining, and a distinct Mediterranean flair—all with Carnival's signature energy.

Signature venues: Piazza, Cucina del Capitano, Pizzeria del Capitano, Venetian-inspired decor

  • Venezia
  • Firenze
  • Luminosa

Grand Class

New arrivals from Princess. These former Princess Cruises ships bring premium features to Carnival's fleet, including spacious staterooms and refined public spaces. Currently being transformed with Carnival's signature venues and Fun Ship atmosphere.

Signature venues: Being updated with Carnival venues—Guy's Burger Joint, BlueIguana, Alchemy Bar

  • Adventure
  • Encounter

Fantasy Class

Budget-friendly classics. The remaining Fantasy-class ships offer great value for shorter cruises. At 70,000 GT, they deliver the essential Carnival experience—fun atmosphere, solid dining, and friendly service—at accessible price points.

Signature venues: Serenity, Lido Deck, Piano Bar, Comedy Club

  • Paradise
  • Elation
