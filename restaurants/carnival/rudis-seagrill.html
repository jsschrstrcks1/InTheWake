Rudi's Seagrill Review: Carnival's European Seafood Concept

Introduction. Rudi's Seagrill is Carnival's European-inspired seafood restaurant, named after Rudi Sodamin — Holland America's master chef and a Carnival Corporation culinary figure. Available on select Excel-class ships, it's a surcharge venue (~$42 per person) that focuses on fresh seafood with European technique — think Provençal flavors, Mediterranean preparations, and plating that actually cares about presentation.

Food & Drinks

The Dover Sole meunière is the signature dish and earns it — whole fish, tableside deboning, brown butter, capers, lemon. It's the most elegant dish on any Carnival ship. The seafood tower appetizer (oysters, shrimp, crab, lobster) is a showpiece that actually delivers on flavor — the oysters are fresh and the shrimp is properly chilled. The Bouillabaisse is rich and well-seasoned with saffron, fennel, and a good variety of shellfish. The Lobster Thermidor is indulgent and classic. Where it dips: the non-seafood options (chicken, steak) feel like afterthoughts for companions who don't eat fish — they're fine but not why you're here. The crème brûlée is standard. The wine list emphasizes French and Italian whites, which suits the menu — the Sancerre pairing with the Dover Sole is spot-on.

Service

This is Carnival's most polished service experience outside the Chef's Table. The tableside Dover Sole deboning is theatrical in the best way — the server clearly practices this. Wine service is knowledgeable. The pace is appropriately unhurried for a $42 cover, and courses are timed well. Reservations are required and should be made early — this venue is small and fills quickly.

Atmosphere

The design is Rudi Sodamin's artistic vision — bright, slightly whimsical, with his signature food-as-art paintings on the walls. It's a more visually interesting space than most cruise ship restaurants. The room is intimate (smaller than Fahrenheit 555 or Il Viaggio), which helps create a refined, special-occasion feeling. Lighting is warm, noise level is low, and the overall energy says "this is a nice dinner, not just another cruise meal."

Conclusion

Rating: 4.3/5. Rudi's Seagrill is Carnival's best seafood restaurant and one of the strongest specialty dining options on the Excel-class ships. The Dover Sole and seafood tower are worth the cover alone, and the Bouillabaisse is legitimately good. It loses a fraction for the uninspired non-seafood options, the standard desserts, and the limited availability (not on all ships). If you eat seafood and you're on a ship that has Rudi's, this should be your top specialty reservation. Tip: order the Dover Sole — the tableside presentation alone is worth the experience.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Rudi's Seagrill on Carnival?

Premium seafood restaurant by Chef Rudi Sodamin on Excel-class ships (Mardi Gras, Celebration, Jubilee). Features oysters, lobster tail, Dover sole, crab cakes, halibut, and prime NY strip. Cover charge applies.

How much does Rudi's Seagrill cost?

Rudi's Seagrill has a cover charge per person. Once you pay the cover, you can order from the full menu.

What is the dress code for Rudi's Seagrill?

Smart Casual is recommended. Carnival designates some evenings as Cruise Elegant — collared shirts for men, dresses or dressy separates for women.

Do I need reservations for Rudi's Seagrill?

Reservations are recommended, especially for dinner. Book through the Carnival HUB app or Guest Services for the best availability. Popular specialty restaurants fill quickly.

What are the menu highlights at Rudi's Seagrill?

Popular items include Wild Caught Tuna, Blue Mussels, Coquilles Saint-Jacques, Seared Shrimp, and more. The menu may vary by ship and sailing.
