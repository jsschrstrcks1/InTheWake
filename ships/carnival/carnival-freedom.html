Ship Overview

Carnival Freedom is the final Conquest-class ship built for Carnival Cruise Line, delivered in 2007. As the last of her class, she represents the culmination of Fincantieri's Conquest design — and she pioneered features that would become fleet standards, including the beloved Serenity adults-only retreat.

Named by supermodel and entrepreneur Kathy Ireland in Venice, Italy, Carnival Freedom now calls Galveston, Texas home, serving as a popular gateway to Western Caribbean adventures for travelers throughout the Gulf Coast region.

Key Highlights

  • Pioneer Status: First Carnival ship with Serenity adults-only area
  • Texas Gateway: Year-round Caribbean cruises from Galveston
  • Classic Fun Ship: Full Carnival entertainment, Guy Fieri dining, WaterWorks
  • Conquest Excellence: Final and refined ship of the popular Conquest class

Frequently Asked Questions

Where does Carnival Freedom sail from?

Carnival Freedom sails from Galveston, Texas, offering Western Caribbean cruises that are convenient for travelers from Texas and the Gulf Coast region.

What class is Carnival Freedom?

Carnival Freedom is a Conquest-class ship — the fifth and final of her class. Her sisters are Carnival Conquest, Glory, Valor, and Liberty, all built by Fincantieri.

What is Serenity on Carnival Freedom?

Serenity is an adults-only (21+) retreat featuring padded loungers, whirlpools, and a tranquil atmosphere away from the pools. Carnival Freedom was the first ship in the fleet to feature this now-beloved amenity.

When was Carnival Freedom built?

Carnival Freedom was built by Fincantieri at Marghera, Italy and delivered February 28, 2007. She was formally named by godmother Kathy Ireland on March 4, 2007 in Venice.

What amenities does Carnival Freedom have?

Key amenities include: Serenity adults-only retreat, WaterWorks aqua park, 1,500-seat theater, 5 pools (one with retractable roof), Fahrenheit 555 Steakhouse, Guy's Burger Joint, Cloud 9 Spa, casino, and Sports Square.
