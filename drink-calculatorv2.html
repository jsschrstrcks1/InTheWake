👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Important for Families with Minors

When adults purchase the Deluxe Beverage Package, Royal Caribbean requires all minors (under 21) in the stateroom to purchase the Refreshment Package.

Example: If 2 adults + 1 teen (age 16):

  • 2 adults × Deluxe Package = 2 Deluxe packages required
  • 1 minor × Refreshment Package = 1 Refreshment package required

This applies even if the minor doesn't plan to use the package. Factor this into your total cost calculation.

🇺🇸 USA Departures: Minors under 21 cannot purchase or consume alcoholic beverages on cruises departing from US ports, regardless of package type.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate if the Royal Caribbean drink package is worth it?

Enter the number of drinks you expect per day by type (cocktails, beer, wine, soda, coffee). The calculator multiplies each by its average price, then compares your daily spending to the package cost. If your total exceeds the package price plus 18% gratuity, the package saves money. The break-even chart shows exactly where that crossover happens.

What is the break-even point for Royal Caribbean drink packages?

For the Deluxe Beverage Package at approximately $89/day (pre-cruise pricing), you break even at 6-7 drinks per day when factoring in the 18% automatic gratuity. The exact break-even point depends on your drink preferences — cocktails break even faster than soda. Crown & Anchor Diamond+ members with free vouchers need even more drinks to justify the package.

What's included in the Deluxe Beverage Package?

Cocktails, wine by the glass, beer, spirits, soda, specialty coffee, tea, fresh juice, mocktails, energy drinks, milkshakes, and bottled water. Drinks up to $14.00 per serving (before 18% gratuity) are included. Excludes bottles of wine, souvenir cups, and specialty shots over the cap.

Do I have to buy a drink package for everyone in my stateroom?

Yes. Royal Caribbean requires ALL adults (21+) in the same stateroom to purchase the same adult beverage package. There are absolutely no exceptions — not for pregnancy, medical conditions, religious reasons, or designated drivers. If adults purchase Deluxe, all minors must purchase Refreshment.

Should I buy the drink package pre-cruise or onboard?

Always buy pre-cruise. Pre-cruise pricing is typically 20-30% cheaper than onboard. You can usually cancel or modify up until a few days before sailing. Watch for Royal Caribbean sales — Black Friday and Wave Season (January-March) often have the best deals.

What are Crown & Anchor drink vouchers?

Crown & Anchor loyalty members at Diamond level (80-174 nights) get 4 free drink vouchers per day. Diamond Plus (175-699 nights) gets 5, and Pinnacle (700+ nights) gets 6. Each voucher covers one drink up to $14. If you have vouchers, the calculator adjusts your break-even point — you may not need the package at all.

What is the Refreshment Package vs. the Deluxe?

The Refreshment Package (~$38/day) covers non-alcoholic drinks: soda, specialty coffee, fresh juice, mocktails, water, and energy drinks. The Deluxe Beverage Package (~$89/day) includes everything in Refreshment plus all alcoholic beverages up to $14/drink. If you don't drink alcohol, the Refreshment Package is the better value.

How does sea day vs. port day weighting work?

Sea days typically mean more onboard drinks (lounging by the pool), while port days mean fewer (you're off exploring). The calculator adjusts estimates: sea days increase by your chosen percentage (default 20%), port days decrease by the same. This gives a more realistic total than assuming the same consumption every day.
