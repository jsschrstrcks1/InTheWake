The Deli Review: Old-School Sandwich Counter at Sea

Introduction. The Deli is Carnival's complimentary deli counter — a grab-and-go sandwich spot modeled after a New York-style deli. It's typically located near the Lido area and serves sandwiches, wraps, salads, and soups during lunch hours. No cover charge, no reservations — just point at what you want and eat. Simple concept, decent execution.

Food & Drinks

The Reuben is the star — corned beef, sauerkraut, Swiss cheese, Russian dressing on grilled rye. It's one of the better complimentary lunch items on the ship. The Italian sub with salami, ham, provolone, and vinaigrette is another strong pick. The chicken salad wrap is reliable for something lighter. Soup of the day varies — the New England clam chowder (when available) is thick and well-seasoned. Where it misses: the bread can be inconsistent — sometimes fresh and soft, sometimes stale by late afternoon. The turkey sandwich is bland without extra mustard. The salads are basic (garden variety, nothing creative). No hot food beyond soup, so if you want something warm, this isn't your stop.

Service

Counter service — walk up, order, receive. The deli crew make sandwiches to order and will customize (extra meat, hold the onions, etc.) if you ask. Speed is the strength — you can have a sandwich in hand within 2 minutes. Lines are short because most guests default to the Lido, which makes the Deli a stealth efficient option when the buffet is mobbed.

Atmosphere

It's a counter with a few nearby tables — no dedicated dining room. Grab your sandwich and sit wherever you find a spot, whether that's a Lido table, a deck chair, or your stateroom. The deli-counter aesthetic (glass display case, sliced meats, pickles) is charming in a simple way. Zero ambiance, maximum convenience.

Conclusion

Rating: 3.4/5. The Deli is an underrated complimentary option — the Reuben and Italian sub are genuinely good sandwiches, and the speed beats any other food venue on the ship. It loses ground for inconsistent bread, bland turkey, basic salads, and the lack of hot options. But as a fast, free, no-wait lunch alternative to the Lido? The Deli earns a regular spot in your rotation. Tip: get the Reuben before 12:30 p.m. when the bread is freshest, and ask for extra Russian dressing.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is The Deli on Carnival?

Complimentary deli counter serving 8 hot sandwiches (buffalo chicken, meatball, Reuben, Cuban, steak & cheese, hot dog, veggie burger) and 6 cold sandwiches (BLT, turkey & Swiss, tuna, chicken salad, and more) with fries and cole slaw.

How much does The Deli cost?

The Deli is complimentary — included in your Carnival cruise fare at no extra charge.

What is the dress code for The Deli?

Come as you are. The Deli is a casual venue — pool attire and casual clothes are fine. Carnival has Cruise Casual and Cruise Elegant nights.

Do I need reservations for The Deli?

No reservations needed. The Deli is walk-up service — just show up and enjoy.

What are the menu highlights at The Deli?

Popular items include 1. Buffalo Chicken, 2. Meatball, 3. Grilled Ham & Cheese, 4. Reuben, and more. The menu may vary by ship and sailing.
