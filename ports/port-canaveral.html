Port Canaveral: Where Rockets and Cruise Ships Share the Sky

Port Canaveral is one of the world's busiest cruise ports, serving as Florida's gateway for Disney, Royal Caribbean, Carnival, Norwegian, and MSC cruises. Located on the Space Coast, it offers a unique pre-cruise opportunity: watch a rocket launch before you sail.

About an hour east of Orlando, the port sits near Kennedy Space Center, Cocoa Beach, and all the Orlando theme parks. Multiple terminals handle the constant flow of ships heading to the Bahamas, Caribbean, and beyond.

Space Coast Magic: Check the SpaceX launch schedule before your cruise. Nothing beats watching a Falcon 9 light up the sky from Jetty Park the night before you sail.

Getting to the Port

  • From Orlando (MCO): 45-60 minutes via SR-528 (Beachline Expressway)
  • From Orlando theme parks: 60-75 minutes
  • From Melbourne (MLB): 30 minutes
  • Uber/Lyft: $50-70 from Orlando airport
  • Shuttles: Multiple companies offer MCO-to-port transfers

Parking Options

  • On-site parking: Available at each terminal
  • Cost: $17-20/day depending on terminal
  • Off-site lots: Several third-party options nearby with shuttles
  • Reserve ahead: Essential for peak sailing dates

Nearby Hotels

  • Radisson Resort at the Port: Popular pre-cruise hotel with shuttle
  • Homewood Suites Cape Canaveral: Suites near the port
  • Hampton Inn Cocoa Beach: Beachside option
  • Hilton Cocoa Beach: Oceanfront with restaurants

Pre-Cruise Activities

  • Kennedy Space Center: Iconic space exploration experience — plan a full day
  • Cocoa Beach: Classic Florida beach, surfing, Ron Jon Surf Shop
  • Jetty Park: Watch ships sail in and out, great for launch viewing
  • Orlando theme parks: Disney, Universal, SeaWorld (60-75 min drive)
  • Manatee viewing: Winter months bring manatees to local waters

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Which terminal is my ship at?
A: Check your cruise documents — Port Canaveral has multiple terminals. Disney uses their own, Royal Caribbean and others use various numbered terminals.

Q: Is Kennedy Space Center worth it?
A: Absolutely — it's one of the best attractions in Florida. Plan a full day or at least half a day.

Q: How's the traffic from Orlando?
A: Usually smooth on the Beachline, but allow extra time during holidays and peak season.
