MSC World Asia — Deck Plans, Live Tracker, Dining & Videos

MSC World Asia is a World Class cruise ship from MSC Cruises, scheduled for delivery in November 2026 by Chantiers de l'Atlantique at Saint-Nazaire, with a maiden voyage departing Barcelona on 4 December 2026. At 215,863 gross tons she is the third ship of the class, carrying 5,079 guests at double occupancy (6,762 maximum) and a crew of 2,138.

The Y-shaped aft with its floor-to-ceiling glass walls creates distinct neighbourhoods — each with its own pool, dining, and entertainment — a class signature carried over from World Europa. Asian influences show up in the ship's design language — a suspended stainless-steel Dragon sculpture above the World Galleria, koi-carp artworks, the Manila Bay pool name, and the new Pan-Asian Street Food specialty restaurant — rather than in the deployment pattern.

A First Look at MSC World Asia

MSC World Asia is the third ship of MSC Cruises' World Class, scheduled for delivery in November 2026 at Chantiers de l'Atlantique in Saint-Nazaire (yard Y34). Steel was cut on 9 April 2024 at a ceremony held in Miami Beach, Florida, to spotlight MSC's North American strategy; the keel-laying/coin ceremony took place at the shipyard on 27 March 2025 with MSC executives Marialuisa Iaccarino and Flavie Biondo acting as coin-ceremony godmothers. She floated out on 7 November 2025. She is planned to debut with a 7-night maiden voyage from Barcelona on 4 December 2026, following Western Mediterranean itineraries calling at Marseille, Genoa, Civitavecchia (Rome), Naples, Messina, and Valletta.

Visually she follows the class template — the Y-shaped aft with the seven-deck outdoor World Promenade and the Panorama Lounge glass walls — but with distinct Asian-themed design choices. The World Galleria atrium carries a suspended stainless-steel Dragon sculpture about 12 m long and 9 m wide, hanging roughly 32 ft above the floor and finished with about 700 mirrors and 3,000 LED lights. Her signature slide is The Spiral @ Tree of Life, a 266.7 ft (81 m) dry slide spanning 12 decks — MSC Cruises confirms it as MSC's longest dry slide at sea as of 2026, one deck taller than the 11-deck Venom Drop on sisters World Europa and World America. The Harbour, the outdoor family park on the upper decks, has been redesigned for World Asia with the 164-ft Cliffhanger over-water swing, a Sky Rails high-ropes course, an aquapark, the Harbour Bar & Bites, and an open Play Deck, while new indoor venues include The Clubhouse retro family zone with bumper cars, roller skating, basketball, table games, and a LEGO Family Zone. Two new pools join the class inventory: Manila Bay, the primary outdoor pool, fronted by the Pearl Lagoon Bar; and Coral Cove, an indoor pool with a retractable roof and a large water-droplet art installation by Italian sculptor Elisabetta Milan.

Who MSC World Asia Is For

You'll love MSC World Asia if: you want the newest MSC World Class ship with its Y-shaped aft architecture, the suspended Dragon sculpture above the World Galleria, and the longest dry slide at sea (as of 2026 — 12 decks at The Spiral @ Tree of Life); you prefer a Mediterranean base closer to Rome, Naples, Barcelona, and Marseille rather than flying to Miami; you like distinct onboard neighbourhoods — MSC Yacht Club, family-focused Harbour with the Cliffhanger swing, adult-focused aft lounges, and the 13 dining venues including the new Pan-Asian Street Food; and you value MSC's Italian-European service style with multilingual staff and strong family programming.

You may prefer a different ship if: you are booking specifically for an Asia-Pacific itinerary (despite the name, she sails in the Mediterranean); you dislike mega-ship scale or crowds of 5,000+ guests; you want Cirque du Soleil at Sea (the partnership has been phased out from MSC); or you prefer a more intimate, port-focused small or mid-sized ship where the ship itself is not the destination.

The Logbook: MSC World Asia (World Class)

MSC World Class — Sister Ships

MSC World Asia is the third of four scheduled MSC Cruises World Class ships, all built by Chantiers de l'Atlantique at Saint-Nazaire. Two additional World Class ships were ordered in November 2025 for 2030s delivery.

  • MSC World Europa — Lead ship, delivered 24 October 2022, Mediterranean-based (Western Med year-round).
  • MSC World America — Second ship, delivered 27 March 2025, christened 9 April 2025 at PortMiami by Drew Barrymore. Year-round 7-night alternating Eastern/Western Caribbean from PortMiami.
  • MSC World Asia — Third ship, scheduled November 2026 delivery, maiden voyage 4 December 2026 from Barcelona.
  • MSC World Atlantic — Fourth ship, scheduled 2027 delivery.

All four share the Y-shaped aft with floor-to-ceiling glass walls, LNG dual-fuel propulsion, the seven-deck outdoor World Promenade, and full MSC Yacht Club enclaves. Deck names and specialty venues differ ship-to-ship.

Entertainment & Activities on MSC World Asia

  • The Spiral @ Tree of Life — 266.7 ft (81 m) dry slide spanning 12 decks. MSC's longest dry slide at sea as of 2026, one deck taller than the 11-deck Venom Drop on sisters MSC World Europa and MSC World America.
  • World Galleria Dragon sculpture — Suspended stainless-steel Dragon, ~12 m long and ~9 m wide, hanging about 32 ft above the World Galleria floor, built from roughly 700 mirrors and 3,000 LED lights.
  • World Theatre — MSC's in-house production shows. (Cirque du Soleil at Sea is NOT on MSC World Asia — the partnership has been phased out from the MSC fleet.)
  • Luna Park Arena — Acrobatic, family, and variety shows in MSC's class-defining arena venue.
  • The Harbour Family Park — Outdoor family zone on the upper decks: the 164-ft Cliffhanger over-water swing, Sky Rails high-ropes course, aquapark, Harbour Bar & Bites, and Play Deck.
  • The Clubhouse — New retro-inspired family venue with bumper cars, roller skating, basketball, table games, and a LEGO Family Zone.
  • Manila Bay Pool (outdoor, with Pearl Lagoon Bar) and Coral Cove (indoor with retractable roof and an Elisabetta Milan water-droplet art installation).
  • World Promenade — Seven-deck open-air promenade along the Y-shaped aft with floor-to-ceiling glass walls.

Frequently Asked Questions About MSC World Asia

What dining options are on MSC World Asia?

MSC World Asia has four complimentary main dining rooms — La Foglia, Esagono, Hexagon, and Bubbles — plus the Aurea-exclusive Les Dunes. Buffets include Il Mercato and La Brasserie. Specialty dining includes Butcher's Cut steakhouse, Kaito Teppanyaki & Sushi Bar, HOLA! Tacos & Cantina, and Jean-Philippe Chocolat, Café & Crêperie. A new-to-class specialty restaurant debuts on World Asia: Pan-Asian Street Food, serving Southeast Asian flavours on an outdoor terrace. Yacht Club suite guests have the private MSC Yacht Club Restaurant.

When does MSC World Asia enter service?

MSC World Asia is scheduled for delivery in November 2026 by Chantiers de l'Atlantique at Saint-Nazaire (yard Y34). Her maiden voyage departs Barcelona on 4 December 2026 on a 7-night Western Mediterranean round-trip. Steel was cut on 9 April 2024; the coin ceremony and keel-laying took place on 27 March 2025 with godmothers Marialuisa Iaccarino and Flavie Biondo; and she floated out on 7 November 2025. Sea trials are expected in mid-to-late 2026, and as of April 2026 she is in final fit-out at the yard.

Which ships are in the MSC World Class?

MSC World Asia (2026) is the third ship of MSC Cruises' World Class. Her sisters are MSC World Europa (delivered October 2022, Mediterranean-based) and MSC World America (delivered March 2025, PortMiami-based). MSC World Atlantic is the fourth ship, scheduled for 2027 delivery. Two additional World Class ships were ordered in November 2025 for 2030s delivery. All World Class ships are built by Chantiers de l'Atlantique at Saint-Nazaire and share the class-defining Y-shaped aft with floor-to-ceiling glass walls, LNG dual-fuel propulsion, the seven-deck outdoor World Promenade, and full MSC Yacht Club enclaves.

Does MSC World Asia have Cirque du Soleil?

No. The MSC × Cirque du Soleil at Sea partnership was limited to earlier Meraviglia-generation ships and has since been phased out entirely from the MSC fleet. MSC World Asia was never part of the Cirque partnership. Her main production shows take place in the World Theatre, with additional entertainment in the Luna Park Arena, at The Loft (a comedy and karaoke venue carried over from World America), and at the All-Stars Sports Bar — MSC's first all-American-style sports bar sailing in Europe.

Where does MSC World Asia sail?

Despite her name, MSC World Asia deploys in the Western Mediterranean — not Asia-Pacific. Her maiden season is year-round 7-night round-trips from Barcelona, with additional homeports at Marseille, Genoa, Civitavecchia (Rome), Naples, Messina, and Valletta. Typical itineraries combine Italian, French, Spanish, and Maltese ports. This deployment mirrors sister ship MSC World Europa's Western Mediterranean schedule.

How many Yacht Club suites does MSC World Asia have?

MSC World Asia has 144 Yacht Club suites across 7 categories — the largest choice of Yacht Club categories in the MSC fleet as of 2026. Categories include the new Royal Duplex Suite (53 sqm, 2-deck, sleeps 6), multi-room Owner's Suites, Royal Suites with whirlpool, Duplex Suites with and without whirlpool, Deluxe Suites, and Interior Suites. The Yacht Club enclave totals more than 9,900 sqm with a private MSC Yacht Club Restaurant, the panoramic Top Sail Lounge, Grill & Bar, a bi-level private sundeck and pool, butler service, and 24-hour concierge.

What is The Spiral @ Tree of Life on MSC World Asia?

The Spiral @ Tree of Life is MSC World Asia's 266.7-ft (81 m) dry slide spanning 12 decks — MSC Cruises confirms it as MSC's longest dry slide at sea as of 2026, one deck taller than the 11-deck Venom Drop @ The Spiral on sisters MSC World Europa and MSC World America. The slide wraps around a sculptural Tree of Life art installation in the World Galleria atrium, where a separately suspended stainless-steel Dragon (roughly 12 m long, 9 m wide, hanging 32 ft above the floor and built from about 700 mirrors and 3,000 LEDs) is the signature Asian-inspired feature of the ship.
