## Top-line summary

In the Wake is a one-author cruise-planning site that has built unusual leverage in two specific places:

1. **AI search visibility.** Microsoft Copilot and Bing Chat cite In the Wake roughly 21,000 times per 90-day window. That's a mid-five-figure rate of being named as a source by AI assistants when people ask cruise-planning questions.
2. **Editorial trust.** No display advertising, no cruise-line affiliate commissions, no shore-excursion booking fees, no sponsored placements that read as editorial. Readers find us because the answers are useful and the site doesn't try to sell them anything they didn't come for.

The traffic-from-traditional-search numbers are smaller than the AI-citation numbers — Bing organic clicks run around 1,100 per 90-day window at a 2.1% CTR — and we've been honest with ourselves that the audience is in research mode, not buyer mode. That research-mode profile is exactly the audience profile most travel-industry partners have the hardest time reaching: people 8–12 weeks out from a trip, comparing options, gathering information, not yet booked.

## What the site is

In the Wake is a planning companion, not a news site or a deals site. The library:

- **388 port pages** with practical accessibility, transit, and on-the-ground detail
- **~300 ship pages** across 14+ cruise lines (Royal Caribbean, NCL, Carnival, MSC, Princess, Celebrity, Holland America, Disney, Virgin, Cunard, Oceania, Regent, Seabourn, Silversea, Viking)
- **~400 cruise-restaurant pages** with menus, costs, and honest review-aggregated assessments
- **9 interactive planning tools** including the Drink Calculator (the highest-converting research tool on the site), the Stateroom Sanity Check, and the Countdown
- A logbook of essays — single-author narrative writing on grief at sea, solo cruising, accessibility, and the working-class realities of cruise travel
- A pastoral surface that treats grief-adjacent and faith-adjacent readers as adults (and that does not carry advertising of any kind)

The site is a Progressive Web App: it caches its core surfaces for offline use. Cruisers regularly load it before sailing and consult it from a ship's spotty Wi-Fi.

## Who reads us

We use six audience personas for content design. Two are most relevant for partners:

| Persona | Description | What partners care about |
|---|---|---|
| **The First-Timer** | Never cruised before. Overwhelmed by options. Found us through Google or Copilot. Average research window: 8–12 weeks before booking. | High consideration for travel insurance, multi-currency cards, packing essentials. Low brand loyalty. Reads carefully. |
| **The Experienced Cruiser** | 5–20 cruises. Knows their preferences. Looking for new ports or new ships. Often the household decision-maker. | Mid-funnel for travel insurance renewal, premium card products, accessibility and mobility equipment. Prefers data over marketing. |
| **The Grieving Traveler** | Bereaved, often widowed. Cruising because it was their shared thing, or because someone told them to try. | **Off-limits for promotion.** Pastoral surface; no advertising of any kind ever. |
| **The Disabled Traveler** | Mobility limitations, chronic illness, invisible disability. Wants honest accessibility information. | High intent for accessibility-equipment rentals (Special Needs at Sea, Scootaround equivalents). |
| **The Solo Cruiser** | Traveling alone by choice or circumstance. Independent, not lonely. | Above-average household income; high consideration for premium cabin categories and travel insurance. |
| **The Caregiver** | Cruising with elderly parents, special-needs children, or medically fragile family. | High consideration for medical-evacuation insurance, cruise-line trip-protection products, accessibility. |

Geographic skew: ~75% U.S. (heavy concentration in Florida, Texas, the upper Midwest, and California), ~12% Canada, ~8% UK, balance international. Age skew: 45–70 with a long tail in both directions.

- **~1,100 organic Bing clicks** at ~2.1% CTR

The AI-citation numbers significantly outpace the traditional-search numbers. This is unusual — most niche publishers have the opposite ratio — and reflects the site's content depth, structured data discipline, and answer-first writing.

For a partner, the practical consequence: a working relationship with In the Wake can show up not just in our pages but in how AI assistants describe our domain when users ask about cruise planning. That's an authority surface that can't be bought through display advertising.

## What we won't do

These are absolute. They're not negotiable, and we've turned down opportunities that crossed them:

- **No cruise-line affiliate commissions.** We don't take a percentage of a cruise booking. This is the editorial firewall — without it, we can't be honest about which line is right for a given reader.
- **No shore-excursion booking commissions.** Same logic. Viator, GetYourGuide, and similar are not partners we'll work with.
- **No display advertising.** Anywhere on the site. Our trust badge says *"No ads. Independent of cruise lines."* That promise is permanent.
- **No medication-product affiliate links.** FTC Health Products Compliance Guidance (December 2022) raises the bar on substantiation when content is consumed by older or vulnerable readers; our audience meets that profile, so we don't promote medication products even where commission models exist.
- **No advertising on grief, accessibility, or faith content.** The pastoral surface is permanent. Logbook entries about loss, solo-cruising-after-bereavement essays, and disability-related content do not carry promotional language of any kind.
- **No sponsored content that reads as editorial.** If a partner placement appears, it is labeled clearly and lives outside the editorial voice. The reader always knows what they're reading.

The model we'd discuss most readily is **a one-page editorially-firewalled review or comparison sponsored by a single brand in a relevant category**, with the brand visible at the top, clearly labeled as a sponsored review, written by us with the same voice and substantiation discipline as the rest of the site. The brand pays a flat annual or semi-annual fee for the placement; we maintain editorial control over the content; the content does not appear in any of the no-advertising surfaces (grief, faith, accessibility content).

We are also open to **multi-year content licensing or data licensing** if the right fit emerges.

## Editorial standards

Anything we publish that has any partnership relationship attached to it follows these standards:

- **FTC compliance.** Clear disclosure label visible above the fold; "this is a sponsored review" or equivalent. No buried disclosure footers.
- **Substantiation.** Every claim about a product is backed by something we can show — first-person use, primary-source documentation, or verified third-party data. We don't promote products we haven't tested or can't substantiate.
- **Vulnerable-consumer awareness.** Our audience skews older. Health, mobility, and financial claims hold a higher bar. We don't run promotions that prey on age-related uncertainty.
- **Brand voice.** All content reads in our voice — calm, factual, no urgency framing, no banned-vocabulary "must-haves." Brand-supplied copy is rewritten before publication.
- **AI-citation honesty.** When AI assistants cite the site, they cite our editorial conclusions, not your marketing claims. We won't structure content to game grounding signals on behalf of a partner.
- **Pastoral firewall.** No partner placement appears on any pastoral surface (grief, faith, accessibility-as-disability content), period.

## What a conversation with us looks like

If you've read this far and a partnership feels worth exploring:

1. Email us. Tell us who you are, what brand you represent, and what kind of relationship you're picturing. One paragraph is enough.
2. We'll respond within a week with whether the fit makes sense.
3. If it does, we'll draft a one-page outline of what the placement could look like, what it would cost, and what the editorial firewall looks like in practice.
4. If you want to move forward, we sign a simple agreement and ship the content within 30–60 days.

We don't run pitches through ad networks, ad agencies, or programmatic platforms. The relationship is direct, with the founder.
