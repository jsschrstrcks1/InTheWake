Grandeur of the Seas

Grandeur (1996) carries a loyal following for good reason. A five‑week, $48M revitalization added concierge and Diamond lounges, refreshed staterooms, and a transformed Viking Crown Lounge — the glass‑crowned perch guests remember long after disembarkation.

She’s the definition of cozy‑elegant: warm woods, spacious ocean views, and service that feels personal. Evenings bring classic production shows and live bands that fit the ship’s scale — more ballroom glow than stadium roar.

On itinerary, Grandeur shines where days are port‑rich and distances are sensible: Bermuda warm‑ups, Caribbean samplers, and shoulder‑season escapes where sea days feel like spa days.

What people love most is her rhythm. There’s time to read on deck without a soundtrack, time to linger over MDR classics, time to watch sail‑away from the Viking Crown like a chapter break between ports.

Pick Grandeur when you want a welcoming ship that still sails like a ship — you’ll spend more time near the rail, and less time in a line. For couples and friends who travel for conversation, sunsets, and the gentle hum of a ship at speed, this one’s a keeper.
