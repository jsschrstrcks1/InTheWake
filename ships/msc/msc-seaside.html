MSC Seaside — Deck Plans, Live Tracker, Dining & Videos

MSC Seaside is the lead ship of MSC Cruises' original Seaside Class, delivered on 29 November 2017 at Monfalcone, Italy by Fincantieri and christened on 21 December 2017 at PortMiami Terminal B by Sophia Loren — MSC Cruises' first ship christening ceremony ever held in North America, with host Mario Lopez and performances by Andrea Bocelli (with the Voices of Haiti children's choir) and Ricky Martin. She measures 153,516 gross tons and 323 metres in length, carries approximately 4,140 guests at double occupancy (5,336 maximum) across 2,066 cabins with a crew of 1,413, and flies the Maltese flag from Valletta. Her 20-deck layout introduced MSC's 'Miami Beach condo' aesthetic with an outdoor wrap-around Waterfront Boardwalk on Deck 8, the glass-floored Bridge of Sighs on Deck 16, twin 120-metre ziplines on Deck 19, the Forest Aquaventure waterpark, the Jungle Pool with retractable sky dome, and a full MSC Yacht Club enclave with 86 suites. Her 2026 deployment is year-round 3- and 4-night Bahamas round-trips to Nassau and Ocean Cay from PortMiami.

Who She's For: South Florida cruisers looking for short Bahamas weekend getaways from PortMiami, families who will use the Forest Aquaventure slides, the twin ziplines, and the bowling alley, and suite travellers who want a full MSC Yacht Club on a 2017-build hull for a reasonable price. Dining enthusiasts drawn to the Asian Market Kitchen by Roy Yamaguchi — a chef collaboration that is distinctive to the original Seaside class and not found on the larger EVO sisters. Not for cruisers expecting Cirque du Soleil at Sea (Meraviglia-class only).

MSC Seaside is the lead ship of the original Seaside Class, together with her only direct sister MSC Seaview (2018). The enlarged Seaside EVO sub-class — MSC Seashore (2021) and MSC Seascape (2022) — are separate derivatives, not direct sisters. MSC Seaside was built at Fincantieri Monfalcone, delivered on 29 November 2017, and christened three weeks later on 21 December 2017 at PortMiami Terminal B — MSC Cruises' first ship christening ceremony ever held in North America. Her inaugural cruise departed Miami on 23 December 2017 for a 14-night Caribbean itinerary. Her design introduced a distinctive 'Miami Beach condo' aesthetic centred on the outdoor Waterfront Boardwalk that wraps Deck 8, a cluster of plazas (The Piazza and Piazza Grande) with open-air dining and shops, the glass-floored Bridge of Sighs walkway on Deck 16, twin 120-metre (~394 ft) ziplines across Deck 19, and the Forest Aquaventure waterpark with multiple slides. Her deck names are themed around beaches and resort towns: Cape Cod (5), Copacabana (6), South Beach (7), Ocean Cay (8), Miami Beach (16), Playa Paraiso (18), and Biarritz (19). Her unique Asian Market Kitchen by Roy Yamaguchi — a trio of Pan-Asian à la carte, sushi counter, and teppanyaki — is a signature collaboration not found on the Seaside EVO sisters. A full MSC Yacht Club enclave with 86 suites has been aboard from her 2017 delivery, with the two-story Top Sail Lounge on Deck 16, private Yacht Club Restaurant on Deck 18, and the 1,600 m² Yacht Club Pool on Deck 19.

Entertainment & Activities on MSC Seaside

Entertainment on MSC Seaside is built around the Metropolitan Theater (Decks 6-7, 944 seats) for main production shows, with the Miami Casino on Deck 7 South Beach (226 seats across 1,150 m²) and the late-night Garage Club disco alongside. Deck 7 also holds the F1 Simulator, the two-lane Bowling Alley, the Interactive XD Cinema, the Champagne Bar, the Haven Lounge, and the outdoor South Beach Pool and South Beach Bar. Deck 8 Ocean Cay is the signature outdoor Waterfront Boardwalk level with the main Marketplace Buffet, the Bistrot La Bohème, Seaview Lounge, Sports Bar, the open-air Piazza, and the 2,410 m² MSC Aurea Spa with its distinctive Snow Room. The dining and entertainment zone on Deck 16 Miami Beach holds the specialty restaurants (Asian Market Kitchen by Roy Yamaguchi trio — Pan-Asian, sushi, teppanyaki; Butcher's Cut; Ocean Cay Restaurant), the Biscayne Bay Restaurant & Buffet, the Venchi 1878 Gelato & Creperie, L'Enoteca Wine & Cocktails, the glass-floored Bridge of Sighs, and the main Miami Beach Pool (458 seats). Deck 18 Playa Paraiso carries the Jungle Pool Lounge with its sliding sky dome. Deck 19 Biarritz tops the ship with the twin 120-metre Ziplines, the Top 19 Exclusive Solarium, the Aurea Bar, and the Miramar Bar. Deck 20 Sun Deck adds the MSC Sports Arena and the Adventure Trail hanging bridge. Families also have the Forest Aquaventure Park waterpark (Decks 18-19) and MSC Yacht Club suite guests have a full private enclave spanning Decks 16, 18, and 19. Note: MSC Seaside does NOT carry Cirque du Soleil at Sea (that concept is Meraviglia-class only).

Key Facts About MSC Seaside

MSC Seaside Deck Plans

MSC Seaside's 20-deck layout uses beach-and-resort-themed deck names: Cape Cod (5), Copacabana (6), South Beach (7), Ocean Cay (8), Miami Beach (16), Playa Paraiso (18), Biarritz (19), and the Sun Deck (20). Deck 5 holds the Seashore Restaurant main dining room, the Seaside Bar atrium, and the Emotions Immersive Gallery. Deck 6 adds the Ipanema Restaurant, the Venchi 1878 Chocolate Bar, the Shine Bar, the Piazza Grande plaza with live music, and the lower Metropolitan Theater. Deck 7 is the indoor entertainment axis with the Miami Casino, Miami Casino Bar, Champagne Bar, Haven Lounge, Garage Club disco, the Interactive XD Cinema, F1 Simulator, Bowling Alley, and outdoor South Beach Pool and Bar. Deck 8 Ocean Cay is the signature outdoor Waterfront Boardwalk level with the Marketplace Buffet, Bistrot La Bohème, Seaview Lounge, Sports Bar, The Piazza, the 2,410 m² MSC Aurea Spa (with Snow Room), and the Infinity Bridge glass walkway. Deck 16 Miami Beach is the specialty-dining cluster with the Asian Market Kitchen by Roy Yamaguchi trio, Butcher's Cut, Ocean Cay Restaurant, Biscayne Bay Restaurant & Buffet, Venchi 1878 Gelato & Creperie, L'Enoteca Wine & Cocktails, the glass-floored Bridge of Sighs, the Miami Beach Pool, and the forward MSC Yacht Club Concierge Area and two-story Top Sail Lounge. Deck 18 Playa Paraiso carries the Jungle Pool Lounge (with retractable sky dome), Jungle Bar, and the MSC Yacht Club Restaurant and Grill. Deck 19 Biarritz has the Forest Aquaventure waterpark, the twin 120-metre ziplines, the Top 19 Exclusive Solarium, the Aurea and Miramar bars, and the 1,600 m² MSC Yacht Club Pool. Deck 20 is the topmost Sun Deck with the MSC Sports Arena and the Adventure Trail hanging bridge.

Frequently Asked Questions About MSC Seaside

What dining options are on MSC Seaside?

MSC Seaside has two main dining rooms — Ipanema Restaurant (Deck 6 Copacabana) and Seashore Restaurant (Deck 5 Cape Cod) — plus two buffets: Marketplace Buffet on Deck 8 Ocean Cay and Biscayne Bay Restaurant & Buffet on Deck 16 Miami Beach (formerly branded the Colonial Buffet & Pizzeria at 2017 launch). Specialty dining on Deck 16 includes the signature Asian Market Kitchen by Roy Yamaguchi — a chef-collaboration trio of Pan-Asian, sushi, and teppanyaki venues — plus Butcher's Cut steakhouse and the Ocean Cay Restaurant seafood specialty. Sweet venues include Venchi 1878 Chocolate Bar (Deck 6) and Venchi 1878 Gelato & Creperie (Deck 16). Yacht Club suite guests have the private MSC Yacht Club Restaurant (Deck 18) and MSC Yacht Club Grill.

Which ships are in the same class as MSC Seaside?

MSC Seaside is the lead ship of the original Seaside Class, together with her only direct sister MSC Seaview (2018). The enlarged Seaside EVO sub-class — MSC Seashore (2021) and MSC Seascape (2022) — are derivatives at 170,412 GT on 339 m hulls (vs 153,516 GT and 323 m for the original Seaside pair). The EVO ships also carry four main dining rooms and a different interior design language; Seaside and Seaview retain the original 2-MDR layout plus the distinctive Asian Market Kitchen by Roy Yamaguchi chef collaboration that is unique to the original Seaside pair.

Does MSC Seaside have the MSC Yacht Club?

Yes. MSC Seaside has carried a full MSC Yacht Club ship-within-a-ship enclave from her 2017 delivery — the MSC Yacht Club concept had already debuted on the earlier Fantasia-class ships and was integrated into Seaside from build. The enclave spans Decks 16, 18, and 19 with 86 suites and butler service, a 24-hour Concierge Area (Deck 16), the two-story Top Sail Lounge on Deck 16, the MSC Yacht Club Restaurant and Yacht Club Grill on Deck 18, the 1,600 m² MSC Yacht Club Pool on Deck 19 with two whirlpools, and the MSC Yacht Club Sundeck & Bar on Deck 19.

Where does MSC Seaside sail in 2026?

MSC Seaside runs short Bahamas itineraries year-round from PortMiami, Florida — 3-night and 4-night round-trips calling at Nassau and MSC's private island Ocean Cay Marine Reserve, with turnarounds on Fridays and Mondays. Her Caribbean season at Miami runs 13 April through 14 December 2026. Earlier in 2026 she sailed a Mediterranean season from Barcelona (La Goulette, Palermo, Naples, Livorno, Marseille) before a 14-night transatlantic repositioning via Funchal and San Juan back to Miami in October 2026. On 5 February 2026 she experienced a brief mooring incident at Ocean Cay when sudden wind gusts (29-37 mph) snapped mooring lines and her bow drifted from the pier — passengers were ashore, no injuries, no structural damage, and she departed on schedule the same day.

Who christened MSC Seaside?

MSC Seaside was christened on 21 December 2017 at PortMiami Terminal B — MSC Cruises' first ship christening ever held in North America. Her godmother was Sophia Loren, MSC Cruises' long-standing godmother. The ceremony was hosted by Mario Lopez and featured a live performance by Andrea Bocelli with the Voices of Haiti children's choir and Ricky Martin. She had been delivered three weeks earlier, on 29 November 2017, by Fincantieri at Monfalcone, Italy, and her 14-night inaugural cruise departed Miami on 23 December 2017. She is registered in Malta (Valletta) and carries IMO number 9745366.
