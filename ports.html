Destination Ports

These are the places your ship visits — the ports of call where you'll spend a few hours exploring, shopping, or just soaking in a new place. Looking for where to start your cruise? Check out the departure ports on our Planning page.

Jump to: Caribbean · Alaska · Mexico · Mediterranean · Northern Europe · Other Regions

Caribbean

The most popular cruise destination — warm water, easy beaches, and familiar US dollar in many ports.

Private Islands

  • CocoCay (Royal Caribbean) — Thrill Waterpark, Coco Beach Club, no tenders
  • Labadee (Royal Caribbean) — Haiti's north coast, Dragon's Breath zip line
  • Perfect Day at CocoCay — Full-day destination with Oasis Lagoon
  • Half Moon Cay (Holland America/Carnival) — Low-key beach day
  • Princess Cays (Princess) — Eleuthera, Bahamas
  • Castaway Cay (Disney) — Family-focused, character meet-and-greets

Eastern Caribbean

  • Nassau, Bahamas — Walking distance shops, Atlantis day passes available
  • St. Thomas, USVI — Duty-free shopping, Magens Bay beach
  • St. Maarten — Dutch/French sides, Maho Beach plane spotting
  • San Juan, Puerto Rico — Old San Juan forts, no passport needed
  • Tortola, BVI — The Baths at Virgin Gorda (excursion)

Western Caribbean

  • Cozumel, Mexico — Snorkeling, Mayan ruins (Tulum excursion)
  • Costa Maya, Mexico — Beach clubs, less crowded than Cozumel
  • Roatán, Honduras — Affordable excursions, sloth sanctuaries
  • Belize City — Tender port, Mayan ruins at Altun Ha
  • George Town, Grand Cayman — Stingray City, Seven Mile Beach
  • Falmouth, Jamaica — Dunn's River Falls, newer cruise pier

Southern Caribbean

  • Aruba — Eagle Beach, consistent weather, easy walking
  • Curaçao — Colorful Willemstad, great snorkeling
  • Bonaire — World-class diving, flamingo sanctuary
  • Barbados — Harrison's Cave, rum tours
  • St. Lucia — The Pitons, drive-in volcano
  • Grenada — Spice island, Grand Anse Beach

Alaska

Glacier viewing, wildlife, and small-town charm. Most sailings run May through September.

Popular Ports

  • Juneau — State capital, Mendenhall Glacier, whale watching. No roads in/out — arrive by air or sea only.
  • Ketchikan — "Salmon Capital," totem poles, Creek Street boardwalk. Rainiest city in Alaska.
  • Skagway — Gold Rush history, White Pass Railway. Small town with big character.
  • Sitka — Russian heritage, Raptor Center, less touristy feel.
  • Icy Strait Point — Hoonah village, world's largest zip line, whale watching.
  • Haines — Eagles, bears, less crowded alternative to Skagway.

Scenic Cruising (No Dock)

  • Glacier Bay — National Park, rangers board ship, calving glaciers
  • Hubbard Glacier — Largest tidewater glacier, ship gets close
  • Tracy Arm Fjord — Twin Sawyer glaciers, steep fjord walls
  • College Fjord — Multiple glaciers named after colleges

Tip: Book excursions early for Alaska — popular tours like helicopter glacier landings sell out months ahead.

Mexican Riviera

Pacific coast cruises from California ports — different vibe from Caribbean Mexico.

  • Cabo San Lucas — El Arco rock formation, tender port, beach clubs
  • Puerto Vallarta — Malecón boardwalk, old town charm, zip lines
  • Mazatlán — Historic centro, cliff divers, Golden Zone beaches
  • Ensenada — Wine country, La Bufadora blowhole, short cruises from LA

Mediterranean

History, culture, and cuisine. Longer port days mean more time to explore — but crowds at major sites can be intense.

Western Mediterranean

  • Barcelona, Spain — Sagrada Familia, La Rambla, Gaudí architecture
  • Civitavecchia (Rome) — 90-min transfer to Rome, book early
  • Naples, Italy — Pompeii, Amalfi Coast, pizza's birthplace
  • Livorno (Florence/Pisa) — Long drive to Florence, Pisa closer
  • Marseille, France — Old port, Provence day trips
  • Palma de Mallorca — Cathedral, old town, beach day option

Eastern Mediterranean

  • Santorini, Greece — Tender port, cable car/donkeys up caldera
  • Mykonos, Greece — Windmills, white buildings, party scene
  • Athens (Piraeus) — Acropolis, Parthenon, walkable from port
  • Dubrovnik, Croatia — Game of Thrones filming, walled city
  • Kotor, Montenegro — Stunning fjord approach, old town
  • Venice, Italy — Embark/debark port, St. Mark's Square

Tip: In the Med, ship-sponsored excursions guarantee you won't be left behind — independent touring requires careful timing.

Northern Europe & Baltic

Summer sailings to Scandinavia, Russia (when accessible), and the British Isles.

Scandinavia & Baltic

  • Copenhagen, Denmark — Tivoli Gardens, Little Mermaid, walkable
  • Stockholm, Sweden — Vasa Museum, archipelago approach
  • Oslo, Norway — Viking ships, Vigeland sculpture park
  • Bergen, Norway — Fjord gateway, Bryggen wharf
  • Helsinki, Finland — Design district, Orthodox cathedral
  • Tallinn, Estonia — Medieval old town, affordable
  • St. Petersburg, Russia — Hermitage, visa requirements (check current status)

Norwegian Fjords

  • Geiranger — UNESCO fjord, Seven Sisters waterfall
  • Flåm — Famous railway, Aurlandsfjord
  • Ålesund — Art Nouveau architecture
  • Stavanger — Old Stavanger, Pulpit Rock hike

British Isles

  • Southampton, England — Often embark port, easy London access
  • Edinburgh (Leith/Queensferry) — Castle, Royal Mile
  • Dublin, Ireland — Guinness, Temple Bar, friendly locals
  • Belfast, Northern Ireland — Titanic Museum
  • Reykjavik, Iceland — Golden Circle, Blue Lagoon

Other Regions

Hawaii

NCL's Pride of America is the only ship that sails inter-island without repositioning. Others visit Hawaii on longer Pacific crossings.

  • Honolulu, Oahu — Waikiki, Pearl Harbor
  • Maui (Kahului/Lahaina) — Road to Hana, Haleakala
  • Kona, Big Island — Coffee farms, volcanoes
  • Kauai (Nawiliwili) — Na Pali Coast, garden isle

Canada & New England

Fall foliage cruises are popular September–October.

  • Halifax, Nova Scotia — Peggy's Cove, maritime history
  • Quebec City — French Canada, Château Frontenac
  • Bar Harbor, Maine — Acadia National Park
  • Boston — Freedom Trail, often embark port

Australia & New Zealand

  • Sydney — Opera House, Harbour Bridge
  • Auckland — City of Sails, Maori culture
  • Milford Sound — Fiordland scenic cruising

Asia

  • Singapore — Gardens by the Bay, hawker centers
  • Hong Kong — Victoria Peak, dim sum
  • Tokyo/Yokohama — Temples, technology, food
