Vancouver

Vancouver: Where Mountains Meet the Sea

Vancouver's Canada Place terminal is one of the most spectacular cruise embarkation points in the world — five white sails reaching skyward, surrounded by mountains, water, and one of North America's most beautiful cities. This is the northern gateway for Alaska cruises, offering one-way itineraries to/from Seattle.

The city itself deserves serious exploration: Stanley Park's seawall, Granville Island's markets, Gastown's steam clock, and some of the best Asian cuisine outside of Asia. Plan extra days — you'll be glad you did.

Vancouver Magic: Rent bikes and ride the Stanley Park seawall at sunset. When the mountains turn pink and the cruise ships light up in the harbor, you'll understand why people fall in love with this city.

Getting to the Port

  • Terminal: Canada Place, 999 Canada Place, Vancouver
  • From YVR Airport: 25-35 minutes via Canada Line SkyTrain ($9.35 CAD adult fare, includes all-zone travel)
  • SkyTrain: Direct to Waterfront Station — short walk to terminal. TransLink
  • Taxi/Uber: $35-45 CAD from airport; Uber and Lyft both operate in Vancouver
  • From Seattle: 3-4 hours via I-5 North (bring passport — required for border crossing)

Getting Around Vancouver

  • SkyTrain: Three lines serve metro Vancouver — Canada Line (airport/Richmond), Expo Line (east), Millennium Line (east/north). Fare zones based on distance. TransLink SkyTrain
  • SeaBus: 12-minute ferry across Burrard Inlet to North Vancouver — leaves from Waterfront Station; includes access to Lonsdale Quay market
  • Aquabus & False Creek Ferries: Small ferries connecting Granville Island, Yaletown, Science World, and more — $4-7 CAD per hop
  • On Foot: Downtown core is compact and walkable; Stanley Park seawall is 10 km of waterfront walking
  • Bike Rentals: Mobi bike-share stations throughout downtown; excellent for seawall and False Creek exploration

Parking Options

  • Cruise parking: Limited and expensive in downtown Vancouver
  • Recommendation: Park at airport and take SkyTrain, or fly in
  • If driving from US: Several off-site lots offer cruise parking
  • Note: Most Alaska cruisers fly into Vancouver rather than drive

Nearby Hotels

  • Hilton Vancouver Downtown: Modern tower in the heart of downtown, walking distance to Canada Place and Robson Street shopping
  • DoubleTree by Hilton Vancouver Downtown: Waterfront location near the port with harbor views
  • Fairmont Waterfront: Steps from Canada Place, harbor views, rooftop herb garden
  • Pan Pacific Vancouver: Inside Canada Place itself — can't get closer to the cruise terminal
  • Fairmont Hotel Vancouver: Historic "Castle in the City" with green copper roof, opened 1939
  • Victorian Hotel: Budget-friendly boutique option in Gastown

Pre-Cruise Activities

  • Stanley Park: 1,000-acre urban park — rent bikes and ride the 10 km seawall, visit the totem poles, and explore lost lagoon. One of North America's finest urban parks
  • Granville Island: Public market with local produce, fresh seafood, artisan shops, and buskers. Take the Aquabus for scenic access
  • Gastown: Vancouver's oldest neighborhood — cobblestone streets, steam clock (whistles every 15 min), restaurants and boutiques
  • Capilano Suspension Bridge Park: Swaying bridge 230 feet above the canyon, cliff walk, and treetops adventure. Book through Viator
  • Grouse Mountain: "Peak of Vancouver" — Skyride gondola, grizzly bear sanctuary, stunning city and mountain views
  • Chinatown: One of North America's largest and oldest — Dr. Sun Yat-Sen Classical Chinese Garden is a peaceful oasis
  • FlyOver Canada: Thrilling flight simulation ride across Canada — located at Canada Place, perfect for embarkation day

History & Heritage

Vancouver's Burrard Inlet has been home to Coast Salish peoples for over 10,000 years. European settlement began in the 1860s, and the city was incorporated in 1886 — only to burn to the ground in the Great Fire that same year. The Canadian Pacific Railway reached Vancouver in 1887, transforming it into Canada's Pacific gateway.

Canada Place opened in 1986 for Expo 86, its five white sails becoming an instant Vancouver icon. The cruise terminal handles over 1 million passengers annually, serving as the premier gateway for Alaska Inside Passage voyages. The building also houses a convention center, hotel, and the FlyOver Canada attraction.

Practical Tips

  • Currency: Canadian dollars (CAD). US dollars often accepted but at poor exchange rates — use credit cards or withdraw CAD from ATMs
  • Weather: Mild but changeable; May-September are best months with average highs 65-75°F. Always pack a light rain layer
  • Compass Card: Reloadable transit card for SkyTrain, buses, and SeaBus — day pass available ($11 CAD)
  • Dim Sum: Don't miss Vancouver's legendary Chinese cuisine — try Dynasty Seafood or Sun Sui Wah for authentic dim sum
  • Sushi: Some of the best sushi outside Japan — local favorites include Miku (high-end) and Tojo's (legendary chef)

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Do I need a passport for Vancouver?
A: Yes — US citizens need a valid passport to enter Canada and for the Alaska cruise.

Q: Vancouver or Seattle for Alaska cruises?
A: Both are great. One-way cruises between the two see more of the Inside Passage. Vancouver is worth extra time to explore.

Q: Best way from airport?
A: The Canada Line SkyTrain is efficient, affordable, and drops you right at the waterfront.

Q: Currency?
A: Canadian dollars. US dollars often accepted but at poor exchange rates. Use credit cards or withdraw CAD.

Quick Answer: Spectacular Alaska cruise gateway where mountains meet the sea — Stanley Park seawall, Granville Island, Gastown, and world-class Asian cuisine make extra days essential.
