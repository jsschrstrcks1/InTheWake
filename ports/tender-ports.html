Tender Ports Guide

At tender ports, your cruise ship anchors offshore and passengers are transported to land via small boats called tenders. This affects boarding time, accessibility, and sometimes whether you can go ashore at all.

This page lists all 26 ports in our database that typically require tender service. Use this information when selecting itineraries, especially if you have mobility concerns.

Read: Tender Port Accessibility Guide

What You Should Know

  • Weather dependent — High winds or rough seas can cancel tender operations entirely, meaning you may not get ashore.
  • Mobility limitations — Wheelchairs and mobility scooters often cannot be accommodated on tenders. Contact your cruise line's accessibility desk before booking.
  • Time consuming — Tendering adds 20-40 minutes each way. Plan excursions accordingly.
  • Priority tendering — Passengers with ship excursions, suite guests, and loyalty elites often board first.
  • Tender tickets — Some ships use a ticket system to manage crowds. Check your daily program.

Caribbean & Atlantic (5 ports)

Grand Cayman Tender

Cayman Islands

Stingray City, Seven Mile Beach, George Town shopping. Very popular — tenders run frequently.

Belize City Tender

Belize

Cave tubing, Mayan ruins at Altun Ha, barrier reef snorkeling. Long tender ride to shore.

St. Barts Tender

French West Indies

Upscale boutiques, beautiful beaches, French cuisine. Small port, limited ship visits.

Virgin Gorda Tender

British Virgin Islands

The Baths (massive granite boulders), pristine beaches, quiet atmosphere.

Falkland Islands Tender

South Atlantic

Stanley, penguin colonies, British heritage. Remote expedition destination.

Mediterranean (10 ports)

Santorini Tender

Greece

Iconic white buildings, caldera views, Oia sunsets. Cable car or donkey to town.

Patmos Tender

Greece

Cave of the Apocalypse, Monastery of St. John, biblical significance.

Kotor Tender

Montenegro

Stunning fjord, medieval old town (UNESCO), fortress climb for views.

Hvar Tender

Croatia

Lavender fields, Venetian architecture, trendy nightlife, beautiful beaches.

Cannes Tender

France

Film festival glamour, La Croisette, day trips to Nice and Monaco.

Villefranche Tender

France

Gateway to Nice and Monaco, charming old town, deep natural harbor.

Portofino Tender

Italy

Picturesque fishing village, celebrity hangout, expensive but beautiful.

Amalfi Tender

Italy

Amalfi Coast beauty, Duomo, limoncello, narrow winding streets.

Sorrento Tender

Italy

Gateway to Pompeii and Capri, cliffside views, lemon groves.

Capri Tender

Italy

Blue Grotto, designer shopping, funicular to town, stunning beauty.

Alaska (2 ports)

Glacier Bay Tender

Alaska, USA

National park scenic cruising, no town — ship typically stays aboard. Rangers come to you.

Hubbard Glacier Tender

Alaska, USA

Scenic cruising only — no shore landing. Ship navigates close to calving glacier.

Northern Europe (1 port)

Geiranger Tender

Norway

UNESCO fjord, Seven Sisters waterfall, dramatic scenery. Small village.

New England (1 port)

Bar Harbor Tender

Maine, USA

Acadia National Park, lobster, fall foliage. Weather can disrupt tendering.

Asia (3 ports)

Koh Samui Tender

Thailand

Beach resorts, Big Buddha, Thai massage, island paradise.

Nha Trang Tender

Vietnam

Beach resort city, Po Nagar Cham towers, Vietnamese cuisine.

Komodo Tender

Indonesia

Komodo dragons, Pink Beach, remote expedition-style visit.

South Pacific & French Polynesia (4 ports)

Bora Bora Tender

French Polynesia

Overwater bungalows, turquoise lagoon, Mount Otemanu, bucket-list destination.

Moorea Tender

French Polynesia

Sister island to Tahiti, pineapple plantations, lagoon tours.

Mystery Island Tender

Vanuatu

Tiny uninhabited island, beautiful beach, snorkeling, local vendors.

Lifou Tender

New Caledonia

Coral island, limestone cliffs, Jinek Bay beach, Melanesian culture.

Quick Answer: 26 cruise ports require tender boats instead of dock-side boarding. Weather, mobility limitations, and time should factor into your itinerary planning.
