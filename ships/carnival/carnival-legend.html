Overview

Carnival Legend is a Spirit-class cruise ship that made history when she debuted in August 2002 as the first Carnival "Fun Ship" to feature an onboard wedding chapel. Built by Kværner Masa-Yards in Helsinki, Finland, she was christened by acclaimed British actress Dame Judi Dench in Harwich, England.

At 85,942 gross tons with capacity for over 2,100 guests, Legend offers the intimate atmosphere of a mid-sized ship while delivering Carnival's signature fun. With 80% of staterooms featuring ocean views and 80% of those having private balconies, she's ideal for guests who appreciate scenic cruising.

Legend operates seasonally from Baltimore and Tampa, offering Caribbean, Bahamas, and Bermuda itineraries that appeal to both first-time cruisers and Carnival loyalists.

Frequently Asked Questions

When was Carnival Legend built?

Carnival Legend was built by Kværner Masa-Yards in Helsinki, Finland. She was floated in December 2001 and christened by acclaimed British actress Dame Judi Dench on August 21, 2002, in Harwich, England.

What class is Carnival Legend?

Carnival Legend is a Spirit-class cruise ship, sharing her design with sisters Carnival Spirit (2001), Carnival Pride (2002), and Carnival Miracle (2004). Costa Cruises also operates two Spirit-class ships: Costa Atlantica and Costa Mediterranea.

How big is Carnival Legend?

Carnival Legend measures 85,942 gross tons and is 961 feet (293 meters) long. She has capacity for 2,124 guests at double occupancy across 1,062 staterooms on 12 decks, with a crew of 961.

Where does Carnival Legend sail from?

Carnival Legend operates seasonally from Baltimore, Maryland, and Tampa, Florida, offering Caribbean, Bahamas, and Bermuda itineraries. She's one of Carnival's East Coast favorites.

Does Carnival Legend have a wedding chapel?

Yes! Carnival Legend was the first-ever Carnival "Fun Ship" to feature an onboard wedding chapel when she debuted in 2002. This makes her a popular choice for couples planning destination weddings at sea.
