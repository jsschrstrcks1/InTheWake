Overview

Carnival Inspiration was the sixth of eight Fantasy-class cruise ships, serving Carnival Cruise Line for 24 memorable years. Built at Kværner Masa-Yards in Helsinki, Finland, she was floated on April 1, 1996, and christened by Mary Anne Shula, wife of legendary Miami Dolphins coach Don Shula.

Originally sailing Caribbean itineraries from Miami and Tampa, Inspiration later became a fixture of the West Coast cruise market. From her final homeport of Long Beach, California, she offered popular short getaways to Catalina Island and Ensenada, Mexico—perfect weekend escapes that introduced countless families to cruising.

The COVID-19 pandemic led to her sale in July 2020, ending a quarter-century of service that helped define the "Fun Ship" experience.

Signature Features

As a Fantasy-class ship, Carnival Inspiration featured the iconic design elements that defined 1990s cruising:

  • Mardi Gras Main Dining Room — Elegant two-deck atrium dining with New Orleans-inspired décor
  • Paris Show Lounge — Multi-level entertainment venue for Broadway-style production shows
  • Lido Deck Pool Complex — Resort-style pool area with Carnival's signature red waterslide
  • Spa Carnival — Full-service spa and fitness center
  • Camp Carnival — Youth program spaces for kids ages 2-17
  • Seaview Bistro — Casual poolside dining venue
  • Monte Carlo Casino — Vegas-style gaming with slots and table games
  • BlueIguana Cantina — Mexican cuisine added during Fun Ship 2.0 renovation

Frequently Asked Questions

What happened to Carnival Inspiration?

Carnival Inspiration was sold in July 2020 as part of Carnival's COVID-19 pandemic fleet reduction. She was de-inventoried in Curaçao before her final voyage to the Aliağa shipbreaking yard in Turkey, arriving August 5, 2020. Scrapping began in April 2021 and concluded in January 2022.

When was Carnival Inspiration built?

Carnival Inspiration was built in 1996 by Kværner Masa-Yards at the Helsinki New Shipyard in Finland. She was floated on April 1, 1996, and christened by Mary Anne Shula, wife of legendary NFL coach Don Shula. Her original construction cost was approximately $270 million.

What class was Carnival Inspiration?

Carnival Inspiration was the sixth of eight Fantasy-class ships, joining sisters Fantasy, Ecstasy, Sensation, Fascination, Imagination, Elation, and Paradise. The Fantasy-class revolutionized affordable cruising in the 1990s.

How big was Carnival Inspiration?

Carnival Inspiration measured 70,367 gross tons with capacity for 2,040 guests at double occupancy. She was 855 feet (262 meters) long with 10 passenger decks and carried approximately 920 crew members.

Where did Carnival Inspiration sail from?

Carnival Inspiration's final homeport was Long Beach, California, where she operated popular short 3-4 day cruises to Catalina Island and Ensenada, Mexico. Earlier in her career, she sailed from Miami and Tampa.
