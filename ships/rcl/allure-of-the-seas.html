A First Look

The Oasis Masterpiece

When Allure of the Seas launched in 2010, she didn't just set a new standard for cruising—she redefined what was possible at sea. As the second Oasis-class ship, Allure matched her sister Oasis of the Seas in size and innovation, but carved her own identity through refined execution and guest-focused design. This 225,282-gross-ton marvel carries 5,492 guests across 16 decks, featuring seven distinct neighborhoods that transform the traditional cruise ship layout into a floating destination.

The ship's name, "Allure," perfectly captures her magnetic appeal. From the nine-story AquaTheater where high-diving acrobats perform death-defying routines, to the 1,380-foot-long Central Park neighborhood with 12,000 live plants, Allure creates environments that feel remarkably like land-based destinations.

Dining Experiences

Main Dining Rooms

Allure features three main dining rooms with distinct personalities: Great Gatsby (Art Deco elegance), American Icon (patriotic theme), and Opus (modern sophistication). Each offers Royal Caribbean's multi-course My Time Dining experience.

Guest Stories from Allure

"The Zip Line Changed Everything" - Sarah M., 2023

"I'd never been on a cruise before, let alone one with a zip line nine decks above the ocean. The moment I launched from that platform, with the Royal Promenade shrinking beneath me and the Caribbean stretching to the horizon, I understood why they call it Allure. It's not just a ship—it's a launchpad for your best self."

"Central Park at Midnight" - Marcus T., 2022

"Everyone talks about the zip line and AquaTheater, but the real magic happens in Central Park after midnight. The restaurants close, the string lights come on, and suddenly you're not on a ship—you're in a European garden with 12,000 living plants swaying in the sea breeze. I sat at Park Café with a glass of wine, listening to a jazz trio, and forgot I was 20 miles from land."

"The Ultimate Family Suite" - Rodriguez Family, 2024

"With four kids under 12, we needed space. The Ultimate Family Suite gave us 1,772 square feet across two levels, including a private cinema and—get this—an in-suite slide from the kids' loft to the living room. The kids spent the entire week sliding, while my wife and I finally got some peace on the wraparound balcony. Worth every penny."
