The Logbook — Tales From the Wake

Carnival Adventure has had quite a journey. Built in 2001 at Fincantieri's Monfalcone shipyard in Italy at a cost of approximately $450 million, she first sailed as Golden Princess for Princess Cruises — one of the early Grand-class ships that defined the premium cruise experience of the early 2000s.

When the pandemic reshaped the cruise industry, Golden Princess found new life as Pacific Adventure for P&O Cruises Australia in 2021, serving the Australian market with South Pacific and Great Barrier Reef itineraries. Her transformation to Carnival Adventure in March 2025 marks another chapter — bringing refined Grand-class elegance to Carnival's Fun Ship fleet.

What makes this ship special is her heritage: the signature "Movies Under the Stars" poolside screen, multiple swimming pools, and a layout designed for elegant cruising. As she integrates Carnival's beloved venues like Guy's Burger Joint and BlueIguana Cantina, she becomes a unique blend of Princess sophistication and Carnival fun.

Frequently Asked Questions

What makes Carnival Adventure unique compared to other Carnival ships?

Carnival Adventure is a former Princess Cruises ship (Golden Princess) that brings Grand-class elegance to Carnival's fleet. At 108,865 GT with 2,600 guests, it features Movies Under the Stars, multiple swimming pools, and a refined mid-sized ship experience uncommon in Carnival's lineup.

What is the history of Carnival Adventure?

Carnival Adventure was built in 2001 by Fincantieri as Golden Princess for Princess Cruises. It sailed as Pacific Adventure for P&O Cruises Australia from 2021 to 2025, then joined Carnival Cruise Line in March 2025.

What dining options are available on Carnival Adventure?

Carnival Adventure offers multiple dining venues including the main dining room, Lido Marketplace buffet, Guy's Burger Joint, BlueIguana Cantina, pizzeria, and various specialty restaurants. Exact venues may vary as the ship integrates Carnival branding.

Where does Carnival Adventure typically sail?

Carnival Adventure operates from Mobile, Alabama for Gulf Coast cruises and from Sydney, Australia for South Pacific, Great Barrier Reef, and regional itineraries.
