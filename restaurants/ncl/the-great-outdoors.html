The Great Outdoors Review: Buffet Food, Better Setting

Introduction. The Great Outdoors is the outdoor extension of Garden Cafe — the alfresco buffet dining area found on most Norwegian ships. It serves the same food as the indoor buffet but in an open-air setting with ocean views. Complimentary and included in the cruise fare. It operates during breakfast, lunch, and sometimes dinner depending on weather.

Food & Drinks

The food is identical to Garden Cafe — same omelet station at breakfast (still the best free breakfast item), same carving station at lunch, same rotating international stations. The quality is the same because it is the same food, just carried outside. The difference is the experience of eating it. Where it falls short: the outdoor serving stations sometimes don't carry the full Garden Cafe selection — popular items run out faster because restocking is slower. Hot food cools faster in the open air, especially on windy days. The salad and cold stations hold up better outdoors. On port days in hot climates, the sun can make outdoor dining uncomfortable by midday.

Service

It's self-serve from the outdoor stations, with staff handling drinks and clearing plates. The table areas are served by waitstaff who are attentive with beverages. The outdoor setting means slightly less frenzied energy than the indoor buffet — people who come outside generally want a more relaxed pace. Plate clearing is prompt.

Atmosphere

This is where The Great Outdoors earns its name — ocean air, open sky, and sea views while you eat breakfast or lunch. On calm Caribbean mornings, there's nothing better than an omelet with a view of the horizon. The seating is a mix of tables and lounger-style chairs. Wind can be a factor — napkins and light items need anchoring. On Alaska and Northern European itineraries, the outdoor area may be too cold for comfortable dining. When the weather cooperates, it's the best dining setting on the ship.

Conclusion

Rating: 3.5/5. The Great Outdoors offers the same buffet food as Garden Cafe with the significant upgrade of fresh air and ocean views. It loses points for reduced selection, food cooling quickly, and weather dependence. Tip: Eat breakfast here on Caribbean sea days — an omelet, bacon, coffee, and an ocean view at 7:30 a.m. is one of the best free experiences on the cruise. Check the wind before committing to a table.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is The Great Outdoors on Norwegian Cruise Line?

Top-deck outdoor buffet and grill serving burgers, hot dogs, and grilled favorites with ocean views. Come as you are, even in swimwear.

How much does The Great Outdoors cost?

Pricing varies — check the Norwegian Cruise Line app or NCL app for current The Great Outdoors pricing.

What is the dress code for The Great Outdoors?

Come as you are. The Great Outdoors is a casual venue — pool attire and casual clothes are fine.

Do I need reservations for The Great Outdoors?

No reservations needed. The Great Outdoors is walk-up counter service — just join the queue.

What are the menu highlights at The Great Outdoors?

The Great Outdoors offers a curated selection that changes by ship and sailing. Check the Norwegian Cruise Line app onboard for the latest menu.
