The Neon Cathedral

Carnival Sensation was Joe Farcus at his most theatrical. The legendary cruise ship designer created a seven-deck atrium that transformed light into spectacle — neon tubes climbing the walls, colors shifting through evening transitions, a space that felt more like art installation than cruise ship interior.

The third Fantasy Class ship launched in 1993, bringing Farcus' enhanced design philosophy to Miami. Where Fantasy proved the concept and Ecstasy refined it, Sensation elevated it into theater.

For 29 years, she served as Miami's reliable short-cruise option, delivering affordable Caribbean escapes while her neon atrium dazzled first-time cruisers and veterans alike.

Accessibility Notes

Sensation's Miami homeport made her one of the most accessible Fantasy Class ships geographically. Her neon atrium created natural navigation landmarks for guests with cognitive disabilities, while her intimate size kept distances manageable for wheelchair users.
