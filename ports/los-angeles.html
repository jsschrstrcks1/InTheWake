Los Angeles: West Coast Cruising Capital

Greater Los Angeles serves cruise passengers through two distinct terminals — the World Cruise Center in San Pedro and the Long Beach Cruise Terminal. These facilities handle departures for Mexican Riviera voyages, Hawaiian adventures, and Pacific coastal sailings, all within reach of LAX and Southern California's legendary attractions.

San Pedro's facility handles the majority of cruise traffic, welcoming ships from Carnival, Princess, and Norwegian. Long Beach operates independently near the historic Queen Mary. Each terminal sits roughly 25 miles from downtown Los Angeles.

SoCal Secret: Plan an early arrival to discover San Pedro's waterfront revival — exceptional dining, the USS Iowa battleship, and spectacular Pacific sunsets far from Hollywood's tourist masses.

Docking Information

Cruise vessels berth at the World Cruise Center's modern terminals in San Pedro, specifically at Berths 91 through 93. The complex features two primary passenger facilities equipped with check-in areas, baggage handling, and security screening. Situated approximately 25 miles from downtown Los Angeles, the port location offers a quieter alternative to the city's hustle.

While San Pedro handles most traffic, Long Beach Cruise Terminal accommodates certain cruise lines and itineraries. Both locations provide retail shops, casual dining options, and passenger amenities. The terminals are purpose-built for efficient embarkation and disembarkation.

  • San Pedro (World Cruise Center): 100 Swinford Street, San Pedro, CA 90731
  • Berth Assignment: Berths 91-93 (check your cruise documents)
  • Long Beach Cruise Terminal: 231 Windsor Way, Long Beach, CA 90802
  • Port Facilities: Gift shops, snack bars, and waiting areas at both terminals

Transportation & Getting Around

Los Angeles is infamous for traffic congestion, and visitors should plan transportation carefully. The sprawling metropolitan area lacks the comprehensive public transit found in older East Coast cities, though options exist for reaching major destinations.

From the Airport:

  • From LAX: 30-50 minutes to either terminal, heavily traffic-dependent
  • From John Wayne Airport (SNA): 30-40 minutes to Long Beach terminal
  • Uber/Lyft: $40-70 from LAX depending on demand and traffic
  • Taxis: Available but expensive for longer distances ($60-90 to downtown LA)
  • Shared Shuttles: More economical but slower with multiple stops

Around Los Angeles:

  • Metro Rail: Limited service from San Pedro; Silver Line bus connects to downtown rail network
  • Rental Cars: Most flexible option for exploring multiple areas independently
  • Rideshare Services: Uber and Lyft operate citywide; expect surge pricing during peak hours
  • Shore Excursion Buses: Recommended for distant attractions like theme parks
  • Traffic Reality: Rush hour (7-9 AM, 4-7 PM) can double travel times; plan accordingly
Insider Tip: Don't underestimate LA distances. What looks like a quick trip on a map can take an hour or more. Build in generous buffer time, especially for ship departure.

Top Los Angeles Attractions

The Los Angeles region offers world-famous destinations, though reaching them from the port requires planning due to distances and traffic. Focus your time wisely rather than attempting too much in one day.

Hollywood & Entertainment:

  • Hollywood Boulevard: Walk of Fame stars, TCL Chinese Theatre forecourt, Dolby Theatre
  • Universal Studios Hollywood: Working film studio with theme park rides and attractions
  • Warner Bros. Studio Tour: Behind-the-scenes look at active production facilities
  • Griffith Observatory: Planetarium, exhibits, and breathtaking city panoramas (free admission)

Beach Communities:

  • Santa Monica: Historic pier, beach, Third Street Promenade shopping district
  • Venice Beach: Colorful boardwalk, street performers, Muscle Beach, eclectic shops
  • Malibu: Scenic Pacific Coast Highway, Getty Villa, surfing beaches

Culture & Shopping:

  • Getty Center: World-class art collection, stunning architecture, hilltop gardens
  • Beverly Hills: Luxury shopping on Rodeo Drive, celebrity sightings, elegant neighborhoods
  • The Grove & Farmers Market: Popular outdoor shopping and dining complex
  • Downtown LA: Grand Central Market, The Broad museum, Walt Disney Concert Hall

Theme Parks:

  • Disneyland Resort: Original Disney park in Anaheim, approximately 30 miles southeast
  • Disney California Adventure: Adjacent park with California-themed attractions
  • Knott's Berry Farm: Historic theme park in Buena Park

San Pedro Port Area Attractions

For visitors with limited time or seeking to avoid LA's notorious traffic, San Pedro itself offers worthwhile destinations within minutes of the cruise terminals.

  • Battleship USS Iowa Museum: Explore the historic warship that served from WWII through the Cold War
  • Cabrillo Marine Aquarium: Free admission aquarium focusing on Southern California marine life
  • Korean Bell of Friendship: Massive bell in hilltop pavilion with harbor views
  • Ports O'Call Village: Waterfront shopping, restaurants, and harbor-view dining
  • Point Fermin Lighthouse: Victorian-era lighthouse and coastal park
  • San Pedro Fish Market: Casual seafood dining with harbor atmosphere
  • Angels Gate Park: Scenic overlooks of the Pacific and Los Angeles Harbor

Popular Shore Excursions

Organized excursions handle transportation logistics and ensure timely return to your ship, though independent exploration offers more flexibility for experienced travelers.

  • Hollywood & Beverly Hills Tour: Walk of Fame, celebrity homes, Rodeo Drive shopping
  • Universal Studios Adventure: Full-day theme park experience with studio tour
  • Santa Monica & Venice Beach: Coastal tour with beach time and boardwalk exploring
  • Disneyland Day Trip: Transportation and admission to the theme park (long day)
  • Getty Center & Museum: Art appreciation with architectural marvels and city views
  • Wine Country Excursion: Temecula Valley wineries east of LA (2-hour drive)
  • Celebrity Homes Tour: Narrated bus tour through Beverly Hills, Bel Air, and Hollywood Hills
  • LA Highlights Overview: Downtown, Hollywood, Beverly Hills, and Santa Monica in one tour

Parking Options

Both cruise terminals offer on-site parking, though reservations are strongly recommended during peak season. Early booking ensures availability and sometimes offers discounted rates.

  • San Pedro World Cruise Center: Multi-level parking garage adjacent to terminals
  • Long Beach Terminal: Surface lot parking directly at the facility
  • Daily Rate: $20-25 per day at official port parking areas
  • Reservation: Book through your cruise line or port authority website
  • Off-Site Alternatives: Third-party lots offer shuttle service at reduced rates ($15-18/day)
  • Valet Services: Some providers offer valet parking with vehicle detailing during your cruise

Nearby Hotels

Pre-cruise hotel stays eliminate airport stress and allow time to explore the port area. Several properties cater specifically to cruise passengers with parking packages and shuttle service.

  • DoubleTree by Hilton San Pedro: Short walk to World Cruise Center, cruise parking packages
  • Crowne Plaza Los Angeles Harbor Hotel: Harbor-view rooms, shuttle to terminal
  • Best Western Plus Dragon Gate Inn: Budget-friendly option near San Pedro waterfront
  • Queen Mary Hotel (Long Beach): Stay aboard the legendary ocean liner itself
  • Hyatt Regency Long Beach: Modern rooms near convention center and waterfront
  • Renaissance Long Beach Hotel: Full-service property on the Pine Avenue entertainment district

Pre-Cruise Activities

Arriving a day early provides insurance against flight delays and chances to experience Southern California before departure. The port areas offer museums, waterfront dining, and coastal scenery.

  • USS Iowa Battleship Museum: Self-guided and docent-led tours of the legendary warship
  • Aquarium of the Pacific: Long Beach's premier aquarium with 12,000+ animals
  • Queen Mary: Historic ocean liner tours, restaurants, and Art Deco splendor
  • Cabrillo Marine Aquarium: Free admission, touch tanks, Southern California sea life
  • Ports O'Call Village: Waterfront shopping and restaurants with harbor views
  • Point Fermin Park: Coastal bluff walking trails and lighthouse grounds
  • Seafood Dining: Fresh catches at San Pedro Fish Market and waterfront restaurants

Practical Tips for LA Port Visits

Los Angeles presents unique challenges for cruise visitors. These practical considerations help maximize your time and minimize stress.

  • Traffic Planning: Congestion can double expected travel times; always add 30-60 minutes buffer for ship return
  • Distance Reality: LA spans over 500 square miles; attractions are farther apart than they appear on maps
  • Focus Your Itinerary: Select one or two areas to explore thoroughly rather than rushing between distant locations
  • Theme Park Reservations: Purchase tickets and make park reservations in advance; same-day admission may be limited
  • Weather Layers: Coastal fog and marine layer common in mornings; temperature varies between beach and inland areas
  • Return Time Buffer: Plan to be back at the terminal 90-120 minutes before ship departure due to unpredictable traffic
  • Rush Hour Avoidance: Morning (7-9 AM) and evening (4-7 PM) traffic significantly impacts travel times
  • Comfortable Footwear: Expect significant walking at theme parks and attractions
  • Hydration: Inland areas can be hot and dry; carry water especially for outdoor activities
  • Organized vs. Independent: Ship excursions guarantee timely return; independent touring offers flexibility but requires time management
Critical Warning: Missing your ship due to traffic is a real risk in Los Angeles. If exploring independently, set a firm return deadline with substantial cushion time. The port won't hold the ship for late passengers.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Which terminal will my cruise use — San Pedro or Long Beach?
A: Your cruise line determines the terminal. Check your booking confirmation and cruise documents. Most major lines use San Pedro's World Cruise Center, while some Carnival sailings depart from Long Beach.

Q: How serious is Los Angeles traffic?
A: Very serious. Traffic congestion can easily double your expected travel time. Rush hours (7-9 AM and 4-7 PM) are particularly challenging. Always build in substantial buffer time when returning to the ship.

Q: Can I visit Disneyland on a port day?
A: It's possible but tight. Disneyland is about 30 miles from the port, and with traffic, park time, and return travel, you'll have limited time in the park. Book a ship excursion or arrive very early if attempting independently. Theme park tickets should be purchased in advance.

Q: Is it worth going to Hollywood from the cruise port?
A: It depends on your priorities and time. Hollywood is 25+ miles away, requiring 60-90 minutes each way depending on traffic. If you've never seen it, organized tours handle logistics efficiently. For limited time, San Pedro's waterfront attractions are more practical.

Q: What's the best way to get around Los Angeles?
A: Rental cars offer the most flexibility but mean dealing with traffic and parking. Rideshare services (Uber/Lyft) work well for point-to-point trips. Organized shore excursions eliminate transportation stress and guarantee timely return.

Q: Should I rent a car or use rideshare?
A: For a single port day, rideshare is usually more practical — no rental paperwork, no parking hassles. For pre- or post-cruise stays with multiple destinations, rental cars provide better value and flexibility.

Q: What can I do near the port if I don't want to go far?
A: San Pedro offers the USS Iowa battleship, Cabrillo Marine Aquarium, Korean Bell of Friendship, Point Fermin Lighthouse, and excellent waterfront dining. All are within 10-15 minutes of the terminal.

Q: How early should I return to the ship?
A: Plan to be back at least 90-120 minutes before departure. Traffic delays are unpredictable, and the ship won't wait. If you miss departure, you're responsible for meeting the ship at the next port at your own expense.

Q: What's the weather like, and how should I dress?
A: Coastal areas often have morning fog with temperatures in the 60s-70s. Inland areas can be 10-20 degrees warmer. Dress in layers you can remove as the day warms up. Comfortable walking shoes are essential.

Q: Are there luggage storage options if I want to explore before or after my cruise?
A: Some hotels offer luggage storage for guests. The cruise terminals generally don't provide long-term storage for independent exploration. Check with your hotel or look for luggage storage services in downtown LA or at Union Station.

Quick Answer: Southern California's cruise gateway with two terminals serving Mexican Riviera and Hawaii itineraries. World-famous attractions like Hollywood, Disneyland, and beach communities accessible but require traffic planning. San Pedro waterfront offers USS Iowa and local dining within minutes of the port.

Port Type: Homeport

Terminals: San Pedro (Berths 91-93) & Long Beach

Nearest Airport: LAX (30-50 min, traffic-dependent)

Key Warning: Traffic can be severe; plan extra time
