Quick answer: Wonder of the Seas (236,857 GT · 5,734 guests at double occupancy · 2022) is an Oasis-class Royal Caribbean ship built by Chantiers de l’Atlantique in Saint-Nazaire, France. She carries the class’s seven-neighborhood layout — Central Park, the Boardwalk, the Royal Promenade — and is one of the largest cruise ships in the world. This page covers who she suits, dining, onboard activities, deck plans, and live tracking.

Who she’s for

You’ll love her if you want grand-scale cruising with room to find something new each day: the neighborhood concept (Central Park, Boardwalk, Royal Promenade), the AquaTheater diving shows, the Ultimate Abyss slide, and a full spread of dining and activities.

You may prefer another ship if 5,000-plus-guest vessels feel overwhelming, or you want a smaller, quieter ship — in which case look at Royal’s Radiance, Voyager, or Vision class instead.

Common questions

What makes Wonder of the Seas unique?

She’s the highest-capacity ship in the Oasis Class (5,734 guests at double occupancy), with Royal Caribbean’s seven-neighborhood layout: Central Park’s 12,000-plus live plants, the Boardwalk and its hand-carved carousel, and the AquaTheater’s high-diving shows.

Which ships are in the same class?

The Oasis Class: Oasis, Allure, Harmony, Symphony, and Utopia of the Seas.

Where does she sail?

Typically Caribbean itineraries from Port Canaveral and other ports. Deployments shift by season — the live tracker above shows where she is today.

How current is this page?
