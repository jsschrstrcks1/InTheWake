## Rule
Content prose must not use AI-overrepresented vocabulary. The ban list is maintained in `Like-a-human.md` and scanned by `voice-audit.md`. Writers and reviewers should recognize these as reliable signals that a paragraph was machine-drafted and not pressure-tested for voice.

## Why (rationale)
These words and phrases appear with statistically abnormal frequency in LLM output. A human writer rarely reaches for "holistic framework" or "tapestry of experiences"; an LLM reaches for them constantly. Presence is a tell. This rule is not enforced by any regex-based validator today because a small number of legitimate uses exist (e.g., a restaurant literally named "Tapestry"). That's why severity is `warn` and implementation is `llm-review`, not blocking.

## Pass example
> Cable Beach is quieter than the downtown strip. Jitney 10 from Bay Street drops you at the west end of the sand — $1.25, twenty-five minutes, correct change. The water is warmer than Atlantis but the beach thins after 4 PM when the day crowd leaves.

## Fail example
> Nassau offers a rich tapestry of experiences. Travelers can navigate a comprehensive landscape of beaches, historic sites, and dining options — a truly holistic Bahamian experience that elevates every cruiser's day.

Machine tells stacked: *tapestry*, *navigate*, *comprehensive*, *landscape*, *holistic*, *elevates*. Five bans in two sentences.

## Fix guidance
When a reviewer flags VOI-001 on a paragraph, rewrite for concrete observation, not promotion. Delete the word, then ask what concrete sensory detail would have gone in its place. Usually a deck number, a price, or a specific time of day.
