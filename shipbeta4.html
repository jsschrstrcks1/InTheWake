Wonder of the Seas

One of the largest ships at sea — a park open to the sky, and a dive theater built over the wake.

Wonder of the Seas is the highest-capacity ship in Royal Caribbean’s Oasis Class — built by Chantiers de l’Atlantique in Saint-Nazaire and delivered in 2022. She carries the class’s seven-neighborhood layout and the scale that comes with it: room to find something new each day, and enough people aboard to fill a small town.

Is she your ship?

Yes, if…

you want grand-scale cruising — the neighborhood concept, the AquaTheater dive shows, the Ultimate Abyss, and a different corner of the ship to explore every day.

Maybe not, if…

a 5,000-plus-guest ship feels overwhelming, or you want something quieter. Look instead at Royal’s Radiance, Voyager, or Vision class.

What you’ll remember

Central Park

An open-to-the-sky garden amidships with more than 12,000 live plants and trees.

The AquaTheater

An open-air theater at the stern where divers perform over the water.

The Ultimate Abyss

A tall, twisting dry slide that drops off the back of the ship.

The Boardwalk

A seaside-style promenade with a hand-carved carousel.

Common questions

What makes Wonder of the Seas unique?

She’s the highest-capacity ship in the Oasis Class (5,734 guests at double occupancy), with Royal Caribbean’s seven-neighborhood layout: Central Park’s 12,000-plus live plants, the Boardwalk and its hand-carved carousel, and the AquaTheater’s high-diving shows.

Which ships are in the same class?

The Oasis Class: Oasis, Allure, Harmony, Symphony, and Utopia of the Seas.

Where does she sail?

Typically Caribbean itineraries from Port Canaveral and other ports. Deployments shift by season — the live tracker above shows where she is today.
