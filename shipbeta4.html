When she entered service in 2022, Wonder of the Seas became the highest-capacity ship in Royal Caribbean’s Oasis Class — 236,857 gross tons, room for 5,734 guests at double occupancy, and built by Chantiers de l’Atlantique in Saint-Nazaire, France. She is one of the largest cruise ships in the world.

This page is a beta4 layout demo for ship guides. What makes it different from the generic template is simple: the photographs are hers. The hero above and the gallery below show Wonder of the Seas specifically — never a stand-in or a sister ship — each image identity-verified by the lettering on the hull, and credited to its photographer.

What she’s like to sail

The Oasis Class is organized into seven “neighborhoods,” and Wonder carries the full set. Central Park sits open to the sky amidships with more than 12,000 live plants; the Boardwalk runs aft to the AquaTheater, an open-air dive and acrobatics stage built over the wake. The ship’s own additions over earlier Oasis sisters include The Mason Jar — a Southern restaurant and bar — and Wonder Playscape, a kids’ play area. Dining runs to more than twenty venues across the ship.
