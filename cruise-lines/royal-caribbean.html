Royal Caribbean

Neighborhood-style ships, headline entertainment, and a specialty-dining bench deep enough to turn a sea day into a progressive tasting. Start with your class—Icon, Oasis, Freedom, Voyager, Radiance, Vision—then pick the ship whose vibe (and venues) match your crew.

Packing for slides, show nights, and breezy pool decks? See Packing Lists (reef-safe sunscreen, magnet hooks, lanyards, small dry bag = MVPs on RCI).

Ship Classes

Icon Class debut 2024

Royal’s “world’s best family vacation” era: waterpark neighborhood, seven pools, and a show slate that spans air, ice, water, and stage. Expect maximal choice with smarter crowd flow and a grown-up palette of lounges for date-night escapes.

Premium venues by class (varies by ship): Empire Supper Club, Wonderland, 150 Central Park, Chops Grille, Izumi / Teppanyaki, Hooked Seafood, Giovanni’s, Chef’s Table.

Icon of the Seas

The headline-maker. Category 6 slides, Surfside for families, adults-only Cloud 17, and an AquaDome that turns acrobatics into goosebumps.

Star of the Seas

Second in class and dialed for sun-chasing weeks: seven pools, party-forward weekends, and fresh tweaks to nightlife flow.

Oasis Class neighborhood megaships

The original “neighborhood” revolution: Central Park, Boardwalk, AquaTheater, and the Ultimate Abyss on many ships. Broadway-scale shows meet casual-to-premium dining.

Oasis of the Seas

The blueprint: Central Park strolls, Boardwalk fun, and big-evening theater.

Allure of the Seas

Entertainment-forward with classic Oasis charm—AquaTheater spectacles, family spaces that work all day.

Harmony of the Seas

“Go big on bold” energy: Ultimate Abyss, Perfect Storm slides, and nightlife that hums after dark.

Symphony of the Seas

Variety king. Superb show package, Central Park date nights, and family magnets.

Wonder of the Seas

Resort-style pools by day, party-forward neighborhoods by night.

Utopia of the Seas

The “ultimate weekend” playbook—short sailings, big thrills, dialed dining.

Freedom Class

Sweet spot scale—big-ship staples without mega-ship sprawl. FlowRider surf, sports decks, comedy/live-music nights, and versatile dining.

Freedom of the Seas

Weekend warrior favorite: Perfect Day pairings and refreshed pool deck.

Liberty of the Seas

FlowRider + Splashaway Bay + glow-in-the-dark laser tag.

Independence of the Seas

Short-sailing pro: dueling slides, sports-deck energy, late-night venues.

Voyager Class

Ice rinks, Royal Promenade parades, and compact layouts that make “do it all” days surprisingly easy.

Voyager of the Seas

Trailblazer that still punches above its weight: ice shows and rock wall views.

Explorer of the Seas

Easygoing crowd pleaser—great for “first RCI” trips.

Adventure of the Seas

Island-hopper spirit: Perfect Day + slides by day, lively nights in the pub.

Navigator of the Seas

LA short-cruise specialist: Blaster aqua coaster and rooftop scene.

Mariner of the Seas

Boredom-buster for 3–5 nights: Perfect Storm slides and Playmakers.

Radiance Class

Glass-and-views ships built for scenery: Alaska, fjords, and coastals. Floor-to-ceiling windows, calmer vibe, and classic specialty pairings.

Radiance of the Seas

Window-to-water cruising: quiet Solarium, theater nights, scenery included.

Brilliance of the Seas

Refined and well-paced—Vintages, Chops, and sunrise-at-sea views.

Serenade of the Seas

Great Alaska fit: glass elevators to glaciers and easy deck flow.

Jewel of the Seas

Smaller-ship warmth meets big-views DNA—port-intense runs shine here.

Vision Class

Cozy classics with atrium life and big-itinerary charm. Think Bermuda, New England, or Caribbean hops with simpler logistics and personable crews.

Vision of the Seas

Glass-canopy Solarium, Centrum life, and itineraries that put ports first.

Rhapsody of the Seas

Intimate, musical, and friendly—classic cocktails at sunset.

Enchantment of the Seas

Stretch-added decks, easy pool-day flow, nostalgic Royal feel.

Grandeur of the Seas

Beloved “Grande Dame”: crew-forward hospitality and atrium evenings.

Majesty of the Seas (archive)

Historical reference: compact classic—kept for context and nostalgia.
