Carnival Freedom in Galveston

Ship Overview

Carnival Freedom is the fifth and final Conquest-class ship, entering service in 2007. Named by supermodel and entrepreneur Kathy Ireland in Venice, Italy, she pioneered the Serenity adults-only retreat that is now beloved across the fleet.

As the last of her class, Freedom represents the culmination of Fincantieri's Conquest design. She now sails year-round from Galveston, Texas, serving as a popular gateway to Western Caribbean adventures for Gulf Coast travelers.

FAQ

What is Serenity on Carnival Freedom?

Serenity is an adults-only (21+) retreat with padded loungers, whirlpools, and a tranquil atmosphere. Carnival Freedom was the first ship in the fleet to feature this now-beloved amenity.

Where does Carnival Freedom sail from?

Galveston, Texas — Western Caribbean itineraries including Cozumel, Mahogany Bay, and Belize.

What class is Carnival Freedom?

Fifth and final Conquest-class ship (2007), with sisters Conquest, Glory, Valor, and Liberty.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the dining options on Carnival Freedom?

Carnival Freedom offers excellent dining variety. Complimentary options include the elegant Posh and Chic main dining rooms with Your Time Dining, Guy's Burger Joint for hand-crafted burgers, BlueIguana Cantina for fresh Mexican, and the Lido Marketplace buffet. Specialty restaurants requiring reservations include Fahrenheit 555 steakhouse, Cucina del Capitano Italian family-style dining, Guy's Pig & Anchor for slow-smoked BBQ, and the exclusive Chef's Table multi-course experience.

Where does Carnival Freedom sail from?

Carnival Freedom sails year-round from Galveston, Texas, offering Western Caribbean itineraries to popular ports including Cozumel, Mexico, Mahogany Bay in Honduras, and Belize City. Galveston serves as a convenient homeport for travelers from Texas and the Gulf Coast region.

What makes Carnival Freedom unique?

Carnival Freedom is historically significant as the first Carnival ship to feature Serenity, the adults-only retreat with padded loungers and whirlpools that is now a beloved amenity across the fleet. As the fifth and final Conquest-class ship, she represents the culmination of that popular design series.
