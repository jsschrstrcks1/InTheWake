Radiance of the Seas

Answer: Radiance of the Seas is the lead Radiance-class ship for Royal Caribbean, known for its extensive glass construction, global itineraries, and mid-sized, traditional cruise experience.

A Note on Radiance

Radiance of the Seas holds a special place for many cruisers. It's the ship that proved 'mid-sized' doesn't mean 'missing features.' The walls of glass are its defining feature, making it one of the best ships in the world for an Alaskan or Norwegian Fjords itinerary, where the scenery is the main attraction.

Questions & Answers

What is Radiance of the Seas?

Radiance of the Seas is a cruise ship operated by Royal Caribbean. It is the lead vessel of the Radiance class, known for its extensive use of glass, giving it a very open and airy feel with great ocean views.

When was Radiance of the Seas built?

The ship was built in 2001. It was last refurbished in 2011 to add several new dining venues and other features.

What is the passenger capacity of Radiance of the Seas?

The ship can carry 2,466 passengers at double occupancy and has a crew of 894.

What itineraries does Radiance of the Seas typically sail?

It varies by season. The ship often sails one-way Alaska itineraries in the summer from Vancouver and cruises the Caribbean from Florida (like Tampa) or Puerto Rico in the winter.

Is there an adults-only pool area?

Yes, the 'Solarium' is an adults-only pool area. It is fully covered by a glass roof, making it usable in all weather, including on cooler Alaska sailings.
