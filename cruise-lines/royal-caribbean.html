Royal Caribbean

This is vacation revolution, amplified. Royal Caribbean doesn't just take you places—it transforms ordinary getaways into bucket-list-worthy adventures where adrenaline-soaked thrills meet world-class dining, and every sea day feels like a destination in itself.

From the neighborhood-style variety of Oasis-class megaships to the glass-wrapped panoramas of Radiance-class vessels, Royal Caribbean delivers one-of-a-kind experiences that turn first-time cruisers into lifelong fans. Picture yourself conquering the FlowRider surf simulator, soaring on the North Star observation pod, or catching Broadway-caliber shows while the Caribbean sunset paints the sky.

Perfect Day at CocoCay awaits—Royal Caribbean's private island paradise featuring the tallest waterslide in North America, the first Overwater Cabanas in the Bahamas, and beaches so pristine you'll forget you're still at sea. It's island hopping elevated to an art form.

Is Royal Caribbean Right for You?

Royal Caribbean invites cruisers who want big-ship energy with cutting-edge innovation. If you crave:

  • Active adventures — Rock walls, zip lines, ice skating, surf simulators
  • Neighborhood variety — Central Park with real trees, Boardwalk nostalgia, Royal Promenade parades
  • Family-friendly scale — Kids clubs, teen zones, multi-generational bonding
  • Bold bites — From casual El Loco Fresh to specialty Wonderland

…then Royal Caribbean delivers. The "wow factor" is the point—these ships are designed to make you say "I can't believe this is on a cruise ship."

Not your style? Celebrity Cruises offers refined elegance. Carnival brings pure fun at lower price points.

Frequently Asked Questions

What ship classes does Royal Caribbean offer?

Royal Caribbean operates 7 ship classes: Icon Class (largest, newest), Oasis Class (mega-ships with neighborhoods), Quantum Ultra and Quantum Classes (technology-forward), Freedom Class (active family fun), Voyager Class (Royal Promenade pioneers), Radiance Class (glass and views), and Vision Class (classic intimate ships).

What are Royal Caribbean's signature features?

The Royal Promenade indoor boulevard, ice skating shows, rock climbing walls, FlowRider surf simulators, water parks (including Category 6 on Icon Class), and neighborhood concepts on larger ships. Plus Perfect Day at CocoCay—their private island in the Bahamas.

Which Royal Caribbean ship is best for first-timers?

Oasis-class ships (Wonder, Symphony, Harmony) offer the most variety without being quite as massive as Icon Class. You get neighborhoods, shows, and activities without feeling lost. Freedom and Voyager classes also work well if you prefer smaller crowds.

Dress Code

Casual (Daytime & Most Venues)

Jeans, polos, sundresses, and blouses. Keep swimwear to the pool deck; shorts welcome for breakfast and lunch.

Smart Casual (Evenings & Specialty)

A step up—collared shirts, dresses, skirts, blouses, or pantsuits. Jackets and blazers welcome.

Formal (Dress Your Best)

The night the ship gleams—suits and ties, tuxedos, cocktail dresses, or evening gowns.

5-night voyages: 1 formal night · 6–10: 2 nights · 11–14: 3 nights · 15+: up to 4 (captain's discretion). Guests in smart casual are not turned away on formal night.

Specialty Dining

Most specialty restaurants are smart casual. Chef's Table encourages formal attire.

Ashore

Modesty at churches and sacred sites is good seamanship. See local guidance on our Ports pages.
