## Rule
Logbook prose must not contain brochure/sales patterns. The port validator maintains a `FORBIDDEN_PATTERNS` list at lines 284-300 including: `you'll love`, `perfect for`, `ideal choice`, `value-packed`, `must-do`, `once-in-a-lifetime`, `life-changing`. These overlap with the Like-a-human promotional-drift ban list and LOGBOOK_ENTRY_STANDARDS "no sales pitch" rule. BLOCKING severity — this is not a suggestion.

## Why (rationale)
A logbook is a pastoral document, not a sales tool. "You'll love the buffet" is marketing; "The buffet clears out by 7:15 AM — if you're an early riser, you eat in near-silence" is observation. The former sells; the latter serves. The distinction is the entire difference between this site and a cruise-line marketing page.

## Fix guidance
Delete the flagged phrase and ask what the writer was actually trying to say. Usually a specific observation was hiding behind the brochure language. See VOI-001 for the broader AI-vocabulary ban; LOG-005 catches the cruise-specific subset.
