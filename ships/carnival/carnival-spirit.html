Overview

Carnival Spirit is an 88,500 gross ton cruise ship and the lead vessel of Carnival's Spirit-class. Built in 2001 at Kværner Masa-Yards in Helsinki, Finland, Spirit pioneered many design elements that became hallmarks of subsequent Carnival ships, including the distinctive funnel design that integrates the atrium's skylight dome.

The ship was christened by Elizabeth Dole, former U.S. Senator from North Carolina and former Secretary of Transportation, on April 27, 2001, in Miami, Florida. Spirit made history as the first Carnival ship permanently homeported outside North America when she relocated to Sydney, Australia in October 2012 — a deployment that lasted nearly a decade.

Following her October 2023 refurbishment, Carnival Spirit now sails from Mobile, Alabama, offering Caribbean and Mexico cruises.

Frequently Asked Questions

Where does Carnival Spirit sail from?

Carnival Spirit is currently homeported in Mobile, Alabama, offering 6-8 day cruises to Mexico, Belize, Jamaica, and the Bahamas. She previously spent nearly a decade based in Sydney, Australia (2012-2020), followed by Brisbane (2021).

Who is the godmother of Carnival Spirit?

Elizabeth Dole, former U.S. Senator from North Carolina (2003-2009) and former Secretary of Transportation, christened Carnival Spirit on April 27, 2001, in Miami, Florida.

What are Carnival Spirit's sister ships?

Carnival Spirit's sisters are Carnival Pride (2001), Carnival Legend (2002), and Carnival Miracle (2004). Costa Atlantica and Costa Mediterranea are also Spirit-class ships operated by Costa Cruises.

When was Carnival Spirit last refurbished?

Carnival Spirit was refurbished in October 2023. The dry dock added new carpeting, refreshed decor, technical maintenance, and the new Heroes Tribute Bar & Lounge honoring military and first responders.

Was Carnival Spirit based in Australia?

Yes, Carnival Spirit made history as the first Carnival ship permanently homeported outside North America when she arrived in Sydney in October 2012. She offered cruises to New Zealand, South Pacific islands, Fiji, Vanuatu, and Tasmania until 2020, then moved to Brisbane in 2021 before returning to the US.
