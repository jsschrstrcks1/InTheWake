## Rule
Logbook entries must contain at least one emotional pivot — the turn where "what happened" becomes "what it meant." CLAUDE.md lists flattening the emotional pivot as a NEVER — one of the site's inviolable integrity rules. The pivot must not be immediately resolved with comfort ("but it all worked out!"). Hard things need time to land before redemption arrives.

## Why (rationale)
The pivot is where the site earns its name — "In the Wake" is about what follows, not what happened. A logbook without a pivot is a trip report. A logbook with a flattened pivot (grief immediately redeemed, tension immediately resolved) is dishonest to readers who came because their own hard things haven't resolved yet.

## Fix guidance
Look for the moment in the story where the tone naturally shifts. If there isn't one, the story hasn't been told yet — it's been described. The voice-audit skill scans for "comfort before hard truths have time to land" as a diagnostic. Read `LOGBOOK_ENTRY_STANDARDS_v2.300.md` "emotional pivot" section for examples.
