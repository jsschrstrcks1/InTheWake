Costa Venezia — Deck Plans, Live Tracker, Dining & Videos

Costa Venezia is a Venice Class cruise ship operated by Costa Cruises. She entered service in 2019, measures 135,500 gross tons, and carries approximately 4,232 guests at double occupancy with a crew of 1,600.

Key Facts

  • Class: Venice Class
  • Year: 2019
  • Tonnage: 135,500 GT
  • Guests: 4,232
  • Crew: 1,600
  • IMO: 9801689

Quick Answer: Costa Venezia is the lead ship of the Venice Class — 135,500 GT, 4,232 guests, launched in 2019. Themed after Venice with Venetian-inspired interiors, she was originally designed for the Asian cruise market. This page covers deck plans, live tracking, dining venues, and videos.

Who She's For: Travelers who appreciate Italian-themed design and want a large modern ship. Costa Venezia suits cruisers interested in Mediterranean or Asian itineraries with a distinctly Venetian ambience and Costa's European-style service.

Costa Venezia was originally built for Costa's Asian operations, featuring Venetian-themed interiors that set her apart from her Florence-themed sister Costa Firenze. Both are Venice Class ships at roughly 135,500 GT. The onboard atmosphere blends Italian heritage with multilingual service. If you want Costa's newest mega-ships, the Excellence Class (Smeralda, Toscana) are significantly larger at 185,010 GT. For a smaller Costa experience, the Concordia Class offers a more traditional scale.

Frequently Asked Questions About Costa Venezia

What dining options are on Costa Venezia?

Two main dining rooms (Ristorante Marco Polo and Ristorante Canal Grande) and the Lido Marketplace buffet are complimentary. Specialty venues with cover charges include Casanova (fine dining curated by chef Umberto Bombana), Archipelago, Liu Hot Pot, Teppanyaki, Frutti di Mare, and Pizzeria del Capitano. See the Dining section above.

How big is Costa Venezia?

135,500 GT, 4,232 guests at double occupancy, 1,600 crew. Venice Class, entered service 2019. Sister to Costa Firenze.

Where does Costa Venezia sail?

Originally built for the Asian market, she has also sailed Mediterranean and Caribbean itineraries. Check the live tracker above for current position. Note: Costa Venezia has been transferred to Carnival Cruise Line as Carnival Venezia.
