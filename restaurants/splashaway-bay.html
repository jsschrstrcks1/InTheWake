Splashaway Bay

Royal Caribbean — Kids Water Park

Quick Answer: Splashaway Bay is Royal Caribbean's complimentary kids water park featuring water slides, fountains, splash buckets, and interactive water play areas. Designed for children (typically under 48 inches). Parent supervision required. Available on Oasis, Freedom, and Voyager Class ships.

Best For: Families with toddlers and young children, water-loving kids, parents seeking active play options, and anyone wanting to cool off the little ones.

Key Facts

  • Price: Complimentary
  • For: Children (height restrictions vary)
  • Supervision: Parent/guardian required
  • Features: Slides, fountains, splash buckets
  • Location: Pool deck area (varies by ship)

Watch your little ones splash, slide, and play in Royal Caribbean's colorful kids water playground.

Features & Details

Cost: Complimentary · Swim Diapers: Required for non-toilet-trained children · Lifeguards: None (parent supervision required)

Water Features

  • Kid-sized water slides
  • Tipping splash buckets
  • Pop-up fountains
  • Spray cannons
  • Shallow wading areas

What to Bring

  • Swimsuits
  • Swim diapers (if needed)
  • Sunscreen
  • Towels (or use ship towels)
  • Water shoes (optional)

Parent Tips

  • Arrive early for best lounge spots
  • Reapply sunscreen frequently
  • Bring snacks/drinks
  • Take breaks to avoid overtired kids
  • Use nearby restrooms before play

Some ships have separate areas for toddlers and older kids. Height restrictions apply to certain slides for safety.

Ship-Specific Variations
  • Oasis Class: Splashaway Bay on Boardwalk level
  • Freedom Class: H2O Zone kids water park
  • Icon Class: Surfside neighborhood with kids splash area
  • Features and size vary by ship class and age

Special Accommodations

Safety Notes: No lifeguards on duty—parent/guardian supervision required at all times. Children who are not toilet-trained must wear swim diapers. Waterproof life jackets available at pool deck for children. Surfaces can be slippery. Not accessible for guests in wheelchairs, though viewing areas typically are accessible.

Where You'll Find It

Splashaway Bay is located on the pool deck area, typically near the family pool or in a dedicated kids zone. Location varies by ship class.

Hours

  • Typically opens 9:00-10:00 AM
  • Closes at sunset or around 8:00 PM
  • May close during inclement weather
  • Usually open sea days and port days

4.5 ★ out of 5 Oasis Class • Family with Toddler

Splashaway Bay Review: Perfect for Little Ones

Introduction. Splashaway Bay was a lifesaver for our cruise with a 3-year-old. While he wasn't tall enough for the main slides and FlowRider, this kids water park gave him his own adventure to enjoy multiple times daily.

The Experience

The water features are perfectly sized for toddlers and young children. My son loved the tipping buckets (though the surprise splashes took some getting used to), the pop-up fountains, and especially the small slides. The water is shallow throughout, which made supervision easier. He could explore relatively independently while I stayed within arm's reach.

Practical Matters

Bring swim diapers if your child needs them—they're not provided onboard. We claimed a lounger nearby each morning and basically used Splashaway Bay as home base between other activities. The area can get crowded on sea days, so morning visits offered more space.

Conclusion

Splashaway Bay is essential for families with young children. It's age-appropriate, safe (with supervision), and genuinely entertaining for kids. Our son would have been disappointed if we only had the main pools available. Rating: 4.5/5. Highly recommended for families with toddlers and young children.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Splashaway Bay free?

Yes, Splashaway Bay is complimentary for children who meet the height/age requirements.

Are there lifeguards at Splashaway Bay?

No, there are no lifeguards. Parent or guardian supervision is required at all times.

Do I need swim diapers for my baby/toddler?

Yes, children who are not toilet-trained must wear swim diapers. These should be brought from home as they're not reliably available onboard.

Can adults use Splashaway Bay?

The water features are designed for children. Adults supervise from adjacent areas but typically cannot play on the equipment themselves.

What ages is Splashaway Bay for?

Designed for toddlers and young children, typically those under 48 inches tall. Some slides have specific height requirements. Check with crew for exact restrictions.
