MSC Musica — Deck Plans, Live Tracker, Dining & Videos

MSC Musica is the first-in-class Musica Class ship from MSC Cruises, delivered on 19 June 2006 at Saint-Nazaire by Aker Yards France (the shipyard now operating under its historic Chantiers de l'Atlantique name) and christened on 29 June 2006 in Venice by Sophia Loren. She measures 92,409 gross tons and 293.8 metres in length, carries 2,550 guests at double occupancy (3,060 maximum) across 1,275 cabins with a crew of 1,014, and flies the Panamanian flag from Colón. In 2026 she sails Mediterranean itineraries from Genoa and Civitavecchia; for winter 2026-27 she repositions to Brazil to homeport from Santos, Paranaguá, Itajaí, and Rio de Janeiro, replacing MSC Lirica in the Brazilian market after that ship's 2026-27 season was cancelled.

Quick Answer: MSC Musica (92,409 GT, 2,550 double / 3,060 max, 1,014 crew, delivered 2006) is the first-in-class Musica Class ship — sisters MSC Orchestra, MSC Poesia, and MSC Magnifica. She carries two main dining rooms (L'Oleandro and Belle Époque), the single Gli Archi & Il Giardino Buffet, Kaito Sushi Bar as the only specialty restaurant, the two-deck Teatro La Scala, the Bar della Cascata atrium waterfall bar, and the MSC Aurea Spa. Mediterranean summer 2026; Brazil Nov 2026 – Apr 2027 replacing MSC Lirica's cancelled season.

Who She's For: Mediterranean cruisers drawn to a manageable mid-size ship with classical Italian interiors, travellers wanting Venetian and Adriatic itineraries from Genoa or Civitavecchia in 2026, and Brazilian cruisers looking at the 2026-27 Santos/Paranaguá/Rio season she's replacing MSC Lirica to run. Good pick for guests who prefer a traditional atrium-centric layout over the LED-dome promenade feel of newer MSC ships. Not yet for guests seeking the MSC Yacht Club enclave — Musica's Yacht Club is scheduled to be added in a late-2026 refit ahead of the Brazil season but is not installed as of early 2026. Not for guests expecting Cirque du Soleil at Sea, waterslides, or multiple specialty dining venues (Kaito Sushi Bar is the only specialty currently on the ship map).

MSC Musica is the first of four Musica Class ships — MSC Orchestra (2007), MSC Poesia (2008), and MSC Magnifica (2010) followed. All four were built at Saint-Nazaire in the Chantiers de l'Atlantique yard under different ownerships (Alstom → Aker Yards → STX France → Chantiers de l'Atlantique). MSC Musica's entertainment centre is the two-deck Teatro La Scala, named after Milan's opera house. Her signature interior feature is the multi-deck atrium waterfall on Deck 5 with the Bar della Cascata (Waterfall Bar). Dining on the current deck plan is simpler than on newer MSC ships — two MDRs (L'Oleandro on Deck 5 and Belle Époque on Deck 6), the Gli Archi & Il Giardino buffet on Deck 13, and Kaito Sushi Bar as the single specialty. Bars include the Havana Club Cigar Room, L'Enoteca wine bar, Crystal Lounge, Q32 Disco, and the Sanremo Casino. Wellness is handled by the MSC Aurea Spa on Deck 13 and the Sport Arena on Deck 16. MSC announced in February 2026 that Musica will receive an MSC Yacht Club enclave in a late-2026 refit ahead of her Brazil deployment, joining sister ships Magnifica and Poesia which already received their Yacht Clubs in refits earlier in the 2025-26 fleet Yacht Club extension programme.

Entertainment & Activities on MSC Musica

Entertainment on MSC Musica centres on the Teatro La Scala, a two-deck production theatre named after Milan's famous opera house. The Deck 7 entertainment axis includes the Sanremo Casino, Crystal Lounge, Havana Club Cigar Room, L'Enoteca Wine Bar, and the Timeless Studio photo gallery. The Deck 6 public-room circuit adds four themed bars — Blue Velvet Bar, The Golden Bar, The Diamond Bar, and Il Tucano Lounge — plus the Sala Viola. Deck 5 has the signature Bar della Cascata, a bar built into the ship's multi-deck atrium waterfall feature. Late-night life runs at the Q32 Disco on Deck 14. Outdoor decks carry the Laguna Bar and Blue Marlin Bar (Deck 13) and the MSC Aurea Spa's Spa Bar. Upper decks house the Sport Arena on Deck 16 and the Top 16 Exclusive Solarium. Wellness is handled by the MSC Aurea Spa on Deck 13 with its thermal area. Families have MSC's multilingual youth programme. Note: MSC Musica does NOT carry Cirque du Soleil at Sea or the MSC Yacht Club — her Yacht Club is scheduled to be added in a late-2026 refit.

MSC Musica Deck Plans

MSC Musica's layout runs across 16 passenger decks. Deck 5 holds the Bar della Cascata atrium waterfall bar and the lower L'Oleandro MDR. Deck 6 carries the upper Teatro La Scala lower level, Belle Époque Restaurant, and the four themed bars (Blue Velvet, The Golden Bar, The Diamond Bar, Il Tucano Lounge) plus Sala Viola. Deck 7 is the entertainment and specialty axis with the Kaito Sushi Bar, Crystal Lounge, L'Enoteca Wine Bar, Havana Club Cigar Room, Sanremo Casino, Timeless Studio, and Cyber Café. Deck 13 has the Gli Archi & Il Giardino Buffet, the Laguna and Blue Marlin outdoor bars, and the MSC Aurea Spa. Deck 14 holds the Q32 Disco; Deck 16 has the Sport Arena and the Top 16 Exclusive Solarium.

Frequently Asked Questions About MSC Musica

What dining options are on MSC Musica?

MSC Musica has two main dining rooms — L'Oleandro Restaurant (Deck 5) and Belle Époque Restaurant (Deck 6) — plus the single Gli Archi & Il Giardino Buffet on Deck 13. The only open-to-all specialty restaurant on the current deck plan is Kaito Sushi Bar (Deck 7). Casual venues include L'Enoteca Wine Bar, the Cyber Café, and the Bar della Cascata on Deck 5 — a bar built into the ship's multi-deck atrium waterfall feature. MSC Musica does NOT carry Butcher's Cut, L'Atelier Bistrot, Hola!, or any of the newer MSC specialty concepts — the dining template here is the original 2006 Musica-class configuration. Older third-party guides sometimes mention "Il Galeone" as a specialty, but that venue does not appear on MSC's current official ship map. See the Dining section above for the full list.

Which ships are in the same class as MSC Musica?

MSC Musica is the first-in-class Musica Class ship, together with MSC Orchestra (2007), MSC Poesia (2008), and MSC Magnifica (2010) — four ships total. All were built at Saint-Nazaire in the Chantiers de l'Atlantique yard under different ownerships (Alstom → Aker Yards → STX France). MSC Musica was delivered under Aker Yards France branding in June 2006; the yard reverted to its historic Chantiers de l'Atlantique name in 2018.

Does MSC Musica have the MSC Yacht Club?

Not yet. The MSC Yacht Club ship-within-a-ship concept debuted on MSC Fantasia in 2008 and was never original equipment on the Musica-class ships. MSC Cruises announced in February 2026 that MSC Musica and MSC Orchestra would both receive Yacht Club suites as part of a fleet-wide Yacht Club extension program. Musica's Yacht Club installation is scheduled for a late-2026 refit ahead of her South American winter 2026-27 season. Sister ships MSC Magnifica and MSC Poesia already received their Yacht Club enclaves in refits earlier in the same programme.

Where does MSC Musica sail in 2026-2027?

In spring and summer 2026 MSC Musica sails 7-night Mediterranean round-trips from Genoa and Civitavecchia, calling at Marseille, Valencia or Barcelona, Ibiza, and Cagliari. For winter 2026-27 (November 2026 through April 2027) she repositions to South America for a Brazil season — her primary homeport will be Santos, with additional seven-night sailings from Paranaguá, Itajaí, and Rio de Janeiro, plus longer Argentina and Uruguay itineraries. She is replacing MSC Lirica in the Brazil market after MSC cancelled Lirica's planned 2026-27 South American season in March 2026 for operational reasons.

Who christened MSC Musica?

MSC Musica was christened on 29 June 2006 in Venice by Sophia Loren — MSC Cruises' long-standing godmother. Loren started christening MSC ships with MSC Lirica in 2003 and has continued with every major MSC ship since. Musica was delivered at Saint-Nazaire on 19 June 2006 by Aker Yards France and completed her maiden voyage from Venice on 1 July 2006. She is registered in Panama (Colón) and carries IMO number 9320087.
