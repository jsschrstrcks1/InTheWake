Voyager of the Seas

The Voyager-class marked a turning point for large-scale family cruising, introducing the now-iconic Royal Promenade that runs like a bustling main street through the heart of the ship. Lined with cafés, pubs, and shops, this indoor boulevard gives the vessel a small-city feel and spreads crowds throughout the day.

A hallmark of the class is its mix of active features—think an ice-skating rink, a rock-climbing wall, and generous sports decks—paired with classic sea views from ample exterior promenades. Stateroom choices span interior budget options to ocean-view and balcony cabins that make sea days feel effortless.

Dining follows Royal Caribbean’s familiar pattern of a main dining room and Windjammer buffet, complemented by casual venues. On refitted ships you’ll often find upgraded specialty options and refreshed public spaces, which keep the experience feeling contemporary without losing the class’s original charm.

For travelers deciding whether to book, Voyager-class ships strike a balance: big-ship amenities at approachable prices, strong entertainment, and itineraries that favor popular warm‑weather routes. If you want plenty to do without the scale of the very newest megaships, this class is a sweet spot.

Public perception at launch centered on how well the ship delivered the class promise—mixing amenities, entertainment, and service—while early reviews often highlighted crew friendliness and the ship’s signature venues. As the vessel matured, refits and programming kept it aligned with guest expectations.

Good Fit For

  • Travelers who match the Voyager-class profile described above
  • Guests who value the specific mix of amenities, entertainment, and itineraries typical of this class
  • Families, couples, or groups seeking Royal Caribbean’s style at the ship’s scale
