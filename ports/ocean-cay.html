Ocean Cay: The Aragonite Mine MSC Turned Into Its Private Island

Region: Bimini chain, Bahamas  |  Type: MSC Cruises Private Island  |  Tender: No (alongside dock)  |  Size: ~95 acres

Located in the Bimini chain of the Bahamas, approximately 65 miles east of Miami and 20 miles north of Bimini, Ocean Cay MSC Marine Reserve is MSC Cruises' private island. The cay was an industrial aragonite-mining facility for about half a century before MSC acquired it; the company invested roughly $200 million to rebuild it as a cruise destination, which opened in December 2019. It is MSC-only — Royal Caribbean, Celebrity, Norwegian, Carnival, and other lines do not call here. Most MSC PortMiami-based ships visit weekly.

Price & Policy Notice

Prices, hours, venue names, and policies listed on this page were accurate as of May 2026 based on firsthand observation and MSC published materials. These details are subject to change without notice, and MSC continues to expand the cay (the south spit was under active construction on the most recent port day). Always verify current information with MSC before sailing.

Cruise Port Guide

Ocean Cay operates as an MSC-only port. Your MSC ship docks alongside the deep-water pier MSC built into the cay's east side — no tender boats, no waiting, no worrying about sea conditions. Walk off the gangway and you're immediately on the cay. The welcome wall greets you with the painted "Welcome to Ocean Cay" entrance; the Ocean Village square fans out behind it with shops, the tours desk, lost-and-found, and the Welcome Center.

Free trams run the length of the fish-shaped cay along flat paved paths. Restrooms, towel exchange stations, and lockers are available near the pier. MSC's onboard internet may work on the cay; ship-issued WiFi typically does not. The pier area also has the cay's guest services desk for questions, lost items, or rebooking excursions. Currency is not needed — everything charges directly to your MSC cruise card. The cay is owned and operated by MSC Cruises, so it runs on ship time and the all-aboard schedule is strictly enforced.

From Aragonite Mine to Marine Reserve: The Cay's Journey

An industrial Bahamian sand-mine becomes MSC's flagship private island.

Ocean Cay's story is not the usual private-island story. Most cruise-line cays in the Caribbean were uninhabited sand and palm before the cruise lines arrived — quiet beach stops slowly upgraded over decades. Ocean Cay was different. For about half a century, beginning in the 1960s, the cay was an industrial aragonite-mining operation. Aragonite — a form of calcium carbonate sand that occurs naturally on the Bahama Banks — was dredged from the surrounding shallows, processed on-cay, and shipped to industrial customers in North America and Europe who used it in cement, water filtration, and glass manufacturing. The cay had a workers' camp, conveyor belts, processing buildings, and a deep-water shipping pier. Nothing about it was tropical-resort.

The mining wound down in the 2000s and the cay went idle. MSC Cruises acquired the lease in 2015 and announced an ambitious plan: rebuild it as the cruise industry's first marine-reserve-themed private island. The transformation took roughly four years and an investment widely reported at approximately $200 million. MSC removed the industrial infrastructure, dredged and reshaped beaches, built the engineered swim lagoon between two stone breakwaters at South Beach, planted thousands of palms and sea grapes, constructed the Ocean Village pier complex, the seven named beach areas, the freshwater pools, the multiple restaurants and bars, and the striped lighthouse that became the cay's visual signature.

The cay opened to MSC cruise guests in December 2019. The naming ceremony came in 2021: on 18 November 2021, MSC held the christening of MSC Seashore at Ocean Cay — the cruise industry's first-ever private-island ship-christening ceremony — with Sophia Loren (MSC Cruises' long-standing godmother) presiding and Nile Rodgers and Chic performing live. The event sealed Ocean Cay as MSC's flagship destination property and the launch venue for fleet ceremonies thereafter.

The MSC Foundation Marine Conservation Center, at the cay's northeast tip, anchors the "Marine Reserve" piece of the cay's identity. Visitors can tour the center during scheduled hours; the cay carries a Mission Blue Hope Spot designation (the Marine Reserve & Conservation section below covers the sourcing).

Ocean Cay is still being expanded. On a recent port day, two lattice-boom cranes were actively building out a new rock-armoured sand spit at the south end of the cay, with a harbor tug working the channel. MSC has not published a public completion target for the southern expansion; visitors should expect ongoing construction visible from Sunset Beach for the foreseeable future.

A Note on the Past: If you walk the rocky east shore away from the manicured South Beach umbrellas, you may find small middens of broken queen-conch shells on the exposed limestone shelves. The cay has been an aragonite mine and a cruise destination, and not a person's home in between. The conch heaps are quiet evidence that somebody was here on a quiet errand — a Bahamian fisherman, a worker on a slow afternoon, a passing boater — and decided to leave the shells in a pile. It's the least-curated thing on the cay, and probably the most honest. The brochures don't show this corner; the cay's bedrock and broken shell tell a longer story than its lighthouse.

What's New in 2025–2026

MSC continues investing in the cay.

South Spit Expansion (Ongoing)

The most visible 2025–2026 work is at the cay's southern end. Two lattice-boom cranes and a barge-based crew are building out a new rock-armoured sand spit beyond the current Sunset Beach area. The build extends the cay's footprint south by an estimated several hundred feet. MSC has not announced what will go on the new spit; speculation includes additional cabana clusters, an adults-only beach (Ocean Cay does not currently have one comparable to CocoCay's Hideaway Beach), or a second specialty restaurant. Visitors should expect crane silhouettes visible from Sunset Beach throughout 2026.

MSC World America Calls (2025+)

MSC World America — MSC's newest and largest Caribbean-deployed ship — entered service in 2025 and includes Ocean Cay on every one of her year-round 7-night PortMiami round-trips, alternating Eastern and Western Caribbean itineraries. Ocean Cay is consistently the headline call on her itineraries.

MSC Grandiosa Year-Round Port Canaveral (Winter 2026-27)

MSC Grandiosa shifts to year-round service from Port Canaveral, Florida starting winter 2026-27 — MSC's first year-round Central Florida commitment. Her 7-night Eastern and Western Caribbean/Bahamas round-trips include Ocean Cay as the centerpiece Bahamian call.

Getting Around Ocean Cay

One of the easier ports you'll navigate. Your ship docks alongside the deep-water pier — walk off the gangway and you're on the cay within minutes. The cay is fish-shaped: long and narrow, with a single main spine and several lobes. Everything is connected by wide, flat paved paths and free trams that run constantly along the spine throughout the day.

  • From pier to Welcome Center: 2-minute walk approximately 4 cruise-ship deck chairs end-to-end, or about 12 conch shells stood on their lips
  • From pier to Springers Bar & Marina: 4-minute walk roughly 1 football field, or about 7 mooring lines stretched end-to-end
  • From pier to Marine Conservation Center: 6-minute walk approximately 2 football fields, or about 14 cruise-ship gangways laid flat
  • From pier to South Beach & Ocean Cay Lagoon: 10-minute walk roughly 5 football fields, or about 17 average lighthouses laid horizontally
  • From pier to Lighthouse Bay (eastern lighthouse): 12-minute walk approximately 6 football fields, or about 60 beach umbrellas in single file
  • From pier to Sunset Beach / south spit: 18 minutes on foot approximately 9 football fields, or about 480 grilled chicken nuggets laid end to end or 5–6 by tram

Signage on the cay is excellent. Tram stops are clearly marked. Wheelchair-accessible paths connect all major areas, and complimentary sand-friendly wheelchairs are available at the pier for those who need them. Trams accommodate strollers, making the cay easy for families with young children to navigate.

Ocean Cay Island Map

The painted Ocean Cay map signboard sits in the sand at the start of the beach path. It is where you orient yourself on day one.

Excursions & Experiences

Ocean Cay's experiences include the engineered swim lagoon and several beach areas, plus paid add-ons (cabanas, beach beds, specialty restaurants, spa treatments). The cay is smaller and quieter than Royal Caribbean's CocoCay — there's no waterpark, no helium balloon, no overwater cabana cluster. What it has instead is the engineered swim lagoon, the lighthouse, the Marine Conservation Center, and several beach areas with distinct personalities. Compare MSC's bundled excursion offerings with day-of purchases; ship excursions guarantee return to the vessel but the cay is small enough that independent exploration is straightforward.

Beach & Cove Areas (All Included)

Every zone has its own personality — here's what to expect.

  • South Beach & Ocean Cay Lagoon: The main swim beach. Two rock-armoured breakwaters enclose a calm lagoon of seawater that stays glass-flat even when there's chop outside. The cay's most-photographed swim spot. Best for: classic swim day, families, water-clarity photography.
  • Lighthouse Bay: Quieter cove just east of the lighthouse spit, with views of the tower from the sand. Best for: lighthouse photography, slightly less crowded swim, evening lighthouse-show seating.
  • Bimini Beach & Pineapple Beach: Twin beaches along the cay's western lagoon side. Calmer water, often less crowded than South Beach. Best for: families with younger children, late-morning arrivals who missed prime South Beach spots.
  • Seakers Family Cove: Protected family-oriented swim area. Shallow, calm. Best for: families with toddlers and weak swimmers.
  • Sunset Beach: The cay's southern beach, named for its evening light. Closer to the active construction at the south spit; quieter during the day, atmospheric at sunset. Best for: late-afternoon walks, sunset photography, guests who want fewer people.
  • Ocean House Beach: Far north end of the cay. The longest walk from the pier; consequently the least crowded. Best for: guests who want serious escape from the main flow.

Signature Experiences

  • Climb the Lighthouse (Included on Scheduled Hours): The striped tower is climbable up the internal steel spiral staircase to the observation deck. Hours are posted at the base. Views back across the cay and out to the Atlantic.
  • Lighthouse Light Show (Overnight Calls Only): On overnight or late-departure calls, the lighthouse anchors an after-dark lighting and music show synchronized to Bahamian instrumentation. The cay stays open for this; ships hold late.
  • Marine Conservation Center Tour (Included): The MSC Foundation facility at the northeast tip runs scheduled tours; schedule is posted on-site and in the MSC for Me app. Free.
  • Snorkeling: The lagoon's clarity is well-suited to entry-level snorkeling. The breakwater rock structures harbor small fish. MSC rents masks and fins at the pier for a modest fee.

Paid Add-Ons

Ocean Cay's paid add-ons include cabana rentals (multiple tiers from beach-side to premium), reserved beach beds, snorkel-gear rental, specialty-restaurant cover charges, and Spa Ocean Cay treatments. Pricing varies by sailing date and demand, and MSC has not published a single public price list. Verify current pricing through the MSC for Me app before booking — the app shows live availability and pre-cruise pricing, which is typically lower than dockside pricing, and popular cabanas often sell out before the cruise embarks.

Pro tip: Your MSC drink package works at every bar on the cay — Springers Bar near the pier, the Lighthouse Bar at the base of the tower, the pool bars, and the beach service.

Marine Reserve & Conservation

The "Marine Reserve" in "Ocean Cay MSC Marine Reserve" is the part of the cay's branding that does the most editorial work. It's worth understanding what's there.

Ocean Cay carries a Mission Blue Hope Spot designation — the marine-protection network founded by Dr. Sylvia Earle that formally recognizes ocean areas of conservation importance. The Hope Spot status is documented on the cay's signage and in the royal-caribbean-vs-msc comparison article on this site, which notes that the partnership between Mission Blue, the MSC Foundation, and the Aponte family is "real institutional weight" — and separately raises the fair question of whether the partnership shapes the on-the-ground guest experience or sits primarily at the signage-and-policy layer.

The MSC Foundation Marine Conservation Center sits at the cay's northeast tip — visible on the user's map photo (#13 in the gallery), with a signboard photo (#15) showing its branding. The center is an MSC Foundation facility; visitors can tour it during scheduled hours. Specific tour duration, daily schedule, and what the tour covers vary by sailing — the MSC for Me app and on-cay signage have the current details.

The honest editorial position the existing comparison article takes — that the conservation work is real and the marketing benefit is real, and both can be true — applies on the ground. The center is a six-minute walk from the cruise pier. Visit if you want a more grounded sense of where you are than the brochure imagery suggests.

Food on the Cay: The Honest Spread

Ocean Cay's food story has two distinct halves, and most first-time visitors only see one.

The Beach Shack is the cay's most-visible food venue near South Beach. It is one chalkboard, 11 a.m. to 4 p.m., serving cheeseburger, hot dog, French fries, chicken nuggets (apostrophe and all), and brownie. It is included with your fare. It is exactly what you'd photograph and expect. It is also the only food many first-time visitors see, because it sits where they're already lying down.

The sit-down restaurants are the cay's actual food story. The cay's map signage names Boho House, Seakers, and Smiling Fish as the labeled table-service venues, plus the Marketplace Buffet near the pier. On a recent port day, one of these served a grilled flour-tortilla quesadilla with proper grill marks, two pieces of grilled beef topped with a fresh chimichurri-style green sauce, two crispy spring rolls, and a fan of watermelon, honeydew, and cantaloupe — all on a branded white oval plate.

Inclusion-vs-paid status on the cay is not always clearly posted. The royal-caribbean-vs-msc comparison article on this site documents the same observation: "included-versus-paid is poorly signposted … A food truck serving hamburgers and hot dogs is included, but finding that out required asking — and other venues' inclusion status stayed unclear even after asking." Verify through the MSC for Me app or by asking a venue host before counting on a venue being complimentary.

If you're on a daytime-only call, walking past the Beach Shack to a labeled sit-down venue is usually the better food day. On an overnight call you can do both — the Beach Shack at lunch on day one, a sit-down venue on day two. Either way, do not let the chalkboard menu shape your expectation of what the cay's food is.

Bars: Springers Bar near the pier (drink package valid), Lighthouse Bar at the base of the tower (drink package valid), pool bars at South Beach and Bimini Beach (drink package valid). Beach service runs along the loungers.

Timing Your Day on the Cay

A few timing notes that hold up across visits.

  • Disembark before 10 a.m. to claim shaded loungers. Natural shade on the cay is limited — the mature palm canopy isn't here yet, so umbrellas and cabanas matter.
  • Beach Shack vs sit-down venue: Walk past the Beach Shack to a labeled sit-down venue (Boho House, Seakers, Smiling Fish, or the Marketplace Buffet) for a fuller meal.
  • Marine Conservation Center tour: Tour schedules are posted at the center and in the MSC for Me app — check on the day for the next tour time.
  • Lighthouse climb: Climbing hours are posted at the base of the tower — they vary by sailing.
  • Lighthouse light show: Only runs on overnight or late-departure calls. Check your itinerary before counting on it.
  • Return to ship: Most guests head back 30–60 minutes before all-aboard. The lagoon is quieter in that final hour.

Daytime port stays are typically 8–10 hours; overnight stays usually arrive late afternoon and depart the following late morning. The lighthouse show is the reason to want an overnight call.

Water temperature: The Bimini chain is swimmable year-round. Expect mid-70s°F in winter months and mid-80s°F in summer.

Family Tips

Everything parents need to know.

  • Best for toddlers/preschoolers: Seakers Family Cove (protected, shallow), Bimini Beach (calm).
  • Best for kids 6–12: South Beach + the engineered swim lagoon (clear water, breakwater protection), Marine Conservation Center tour (worth a stop at this age).
  • Stroller-friendly: Yes — all paths are paved, wide, and flat. Trams accommodate strollers.
  • Kid-friendly food: The Beach Shack chalkboard is essentially a kid menu (burgers, nuggets, fries, brownie). The buffets cover anyone who wants something else.
  • Lifeguards: Stationed at all designated swim areas.
  • Shade strategy: Arrive early for umbrella zones, or rent a cabana. Natural shade is thin.

Depth Soundings Ashore

Practical tips before you step off the ship.

The cay is calm by Caribbean private-island standards — there's no waterpark, no helium balloon, no zip line, no overwater cabana cluster. What you have instead is the engineered swim lagoon, the lighthouse, the Marine Conservation Center, and time on a beach. On busy port days with one or two MSC ships in, the cay still feels uncrowded because it's spread along a long fish-shape and the loungers fan across multiple beach zones. Booking a cabana in advance guarantees comfortable shade.

The south spit is under active construction. Two lattice-boom cranes were working a new rock-armoured sand spit at the cay's southern end on a recent visit, with a harbor tug crossing the channel. The construction is far enough from South Beach that you can ignore it; if you walk to Sunset Beach you'll see crane silhouettes and hear pile-driving. The cay opened in December 2019 but MSC has continued capital improvements; expect the southern end to look different a year from now.

Reef-safe sunscreen is required — the cay's surrounding waters are a Bahamian marine protected area. Reapply every 90 minutes, especially after swimming. The Bahamian sun bounces off the white sand and the pale limestone aggressively; even visitors with established tans burn here.

Natural shade is thin. The cay was a mined-out flat when MSC took it over, so the mature palm canopy you find on Bahamian out-islands doesn't exist here yet. Umbrellas and cabanas matter. Arrive early.

Walk to the limestone edge. Off the manicured South Beach, the cay's east shore is exposed limestone bedrock and broken shell. It's where you'll find the conch middens, and it is the least-curated part of the cay. Worth the 10–15-minute walk for the contrast alone.

Conservation Center tours are free. Schedule is posted on-site and in the MSC for Me app.

Keep your MSC cruise card secure — it's your key to everything on the cay, and losing it means a trip back to guest services at the Welcome Center.

Frequently Asked Questions
Do I need to tender at Ocean Cay?

No — ships dock alongside the deep-water pier MSC built into the cay's east side. You walk off the gangway and onto the cay within minutes; no tender boats are involved.

Which cruise lines visit Ocean Cay?

Only MSC Cruises. Ocean Cay is MSC's private island. Royal Caribbean, Celebrity, Norwegian, Carnival, Princess, and other lines do not call there — their private-island equivalents are CocoCay (Royal/Celebrity), Great Stirrup Cay (NCL), Half Moon Cay and Princess Cays (Carnival/Princess).

Which MSC ships visit Ocean Cay?

Three MSC ships call at Ocean Cay year-round as of May 2026: MSC Seaside (PortMiami, 3- and 4-night Bahamas), MSC Seashore (Port Canaveral, 3- to 7-night Caribbean), and MSC World America (PortMiami, 7-night alternating Eastern and Western Caribbean). Two more join from winter 2026-27: MSC Meraviglia (year-round Miami) and MSC Grandiosa (year-round Port Canaveral). Earlier Ocean Cay regulars MSC Seascape (now Galveston / Western Caribbean only) and MSC Seaview (now South America / Mediterranean) no longer call. Europe-based MSC ships such as MSC Euribia have never called here.

Is the food on Ocean Cay free?

The chalkboard at the Beach Shack — cheeseburger, hot dog, French fries, chicken nuggets, brownie, 11 a.m. to 4 p.m. — is included with your fare. The cay's labeled sit-down restaurants on the map signage are Boho House, Seakers, Smiling Fish, plus the Marketplace Buffet near the pier; on a recent visit one of these served grilled quesadillas, chimichurri beef, spring rolls, and fresh tropical fruit. Inclusion-vs-paid status varies by venue and is not always clearly posted — verify through the MSC for Me app before counting on a venue being complimentary.

Does my MSC drink package work on Ocean Cay?

Yes — your ship's drink package is valid at every bar on the cay, including Springers Bar near the pier, the Lighthouse Bar at the base of the lighthouse, and the swim-up service at the Bimini Beach pool bar.

Is there a marine reserve, or is it marketing?

Both. The MSC Foundation operates a Marine Conservation Center at the northeast tip of the cay — it runs coral nursery work and supports turtle-nesting protection on the cay's beaches. It is also a brand asset that justifies the "Marine Reserve" name MSC put on the cay. Both can be true; visitors can tour the conservation center on most port days.

Is the lighthouse a real lighthouse?

No — the red-and-white striped tower on Lighthouse Bay is a viewing tower with an internal spiral staircase and perforated metal cladding. It is the cay's landmark and the centerpiece of the evening light show, not a working aid to navigation. You can climb partway up at scheduled hours.

Is Ocean Cay still being expanded?

Yes. On a recent port day, two lattice-boom cranes were actively building out a new rock-armoured sand spit at the south end of the cay, with a harbor tug working the channel. The island opened in December 2019 but MSC has continued capital improvements; the southern end will look different a year from now than it does in any current brochure photo.

What is the engineered swim lagoon?

Ocean Cay Lagoon is a calm pool of seawater enclosed by two rock-armoured breakwaters at South Beach. The breakwaters block the Atlantic chop so the water inside stays glass-flat even on a wind-up day, which is why visitors describe the cay's swim conditions as exceptional. It is a built feature, not a natural lagoon.

What are the beach options on Ocean Cay?

Five named beaches along the lagoon side: Bimini Beach and Pineapple Beach on the west, South Beach (the main swim beach) at the center next to the lighthouse spit, Sunset Beach on the south, and Lighthouse Bay on the east next to the lighthouse. Ocean House Beach sits at the far north, and Seakers Family Cove is the protected family swim area. All are sand. All are complimentary.

What's the lighthouse light show?

On overnight stays — primarily MSC Seaside, Seashore, and Seascape sailings that schedule Ocean Cay as a late-departure or overnight port — the lighthouse is the centerpiece of an after-dark lighting and music show synchronized to Bahamian instrumentation. On shorter daytime calls the lighthouse is lit but does not perform the full show.

What's the best time of year to visit Ocean Cay?

Ocean Cay is in the Bimini chain, the same Bahamian climate band as Nassau and the rest of the western Bahamas. Water temperatures range from mid-70s°F in winter to mid-80s°F in summer. Winter months (December-April) bring slightly cooler air, smaller chance of afternoon thundershowers, and fewer port-day weather diversions. Hurricane season runs June-November with peak risk September-October.

Who christened Ocean Cay?

MSC held the christening ceremony for MSC Seashore at Ocean Cay on 18 November 2021 — the cruise industry's first-ever private-island christening ceremony — with Sophia Loren as godmother (MSC Cruises' long-standing godmother) and Nile Rodgers and Chic performing live. The event sealed Ocean Cay as MSC's flagship destination property.

When is hurricane season at Ocean Cay?

Two on-page sources answer this jointly: the best-time FAQ above says "Hurricane season runs June-November with peak risk September-October," and the seasonal panel's avoid-months flag "Sep, Oct" for "(hurricane risk)." Specific named storms aren't named on this page — for that, consult NOAA's National Hurricane Center historical archive at nhc.noaa.gov/data. The page locates the cay in "the Bimini chain of the Bahamas, approximately 65 miles east of Miami" — inside Atlantic-basin territory — and the hazard-note states the protocol: "Ships may reroute during active storms; travel insurance recommended." Because the page says the island is "MSC's private island," a storm-rerouted itinerary swaps in a different cruise stop entirely rather than holding the schedule.

For temperature: the best-time FAQ above gives real numbers — "Water temperatures range from mid-70s°F in winter to mid-80s°F in summer." Will rain ruin my Ocean Cay port day?

Ocean Cay's port-day story is different from a typical Caribbean tender stop: "your ship docks alongside the deep-water pier MSC built into the cay's east side." A rainy day doesn't gate getting to the cay. What it does gate is the experience, because nearly everything here is outdoor — the beaches, the engineered swim lagoon, the lighthouse spiral staircase climb, and the lighthouse light show that headlines overnight calls. The page's own avoid-months are "Sep, Oct" with reason "(hurricane risk)," which lines up with the hazard-note "Ships may reroute during active storms; travel insurance recommended." Outside that late-summer window the page does not give rainfall specifics for the Bimini chain; for a specific sailing date, the practical question is whether the lighthouse show — the cay's headline attraction on overnight calls — is running, not whether it's drizzling.
