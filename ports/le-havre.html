Le Havre: My Gateway to Paris

We caught the 7:45 train and were sipping café crème under the Eiffel Tower by 10:30. The Seine sparkled, the chestnut trees were in bloom, and Paris smelled exactly like fresh baguette and romance. We walked from the Louvre (Mona Lisa smaller than expected, Venus de Milo perfect) across the Tuileries to the Orangerie where Monet's water lilies wrapped around us like a hug.

We had lunch at a tiny Montmartre bistro – steak-frites and a carafe of red while an accordion player outside made us cry into our fries. Quick metro to Sacré-Cœur for the view, then back to Gare St-Lazare with croissants for the train. The pros: you actually get Paris – real Paris – in a day. The cons: it's a long day with trains, but the French rail system is flawless and the memories are priceless. Le Havre itself – King Francis I's 1517 'Harbour of Grace' – was bombed nearly flat in 1944, losing 150 hectares of historic center in a single brutal stroke. What rose from those ashes under architect Auguste Perret is now a UNESCO World Heritage site: a modernist concrete city crowned by the 107-meter Church of Saint Joseph, stark and beautiful as a lighthouse made prayer.

The Moment That Stays With Me: Standing under the Eiffel Tower at exactly noon when it started sparkling in the sun and a thousand people gasped at once – pure movie magic made real.

Getting Around Le Havre/Paris

Direct trains from Le Havre station (5-minute taxi from ship) to Paris St-Lazare every hour.

Frequently Asked Questions
Q: When is the best time of year to visit Le Havre for Paris?

May through September offers the warmest weather and longest daylight hours for your Paris day trip. Summer months (June-August) provide mild temperatures (60-70°F) and minimal rain, perfect for walking the Seine and visiting outdoor attractions like the Eiffel Tower. May and September offer pleasant weather with fewer crowds at both Le Havre and Paris museums.

Q: What should I pack for Le Havre and Paris weather?

Pack for English Channel weather — bring light rain jacket or umbrella, comfortable walking shoes for Paris cobblestones and museums, light layers for fluctuating temperatures, and a camera for the Eiffel Tower and Louvre. Even in summer, mornings can be cool on the early train. Paris is walkable, so prioritize comfort over style for shoes.

Q: Is there a hurricane or storm season in Le Havre?

No — Le Havre is not in a hurricane zone. The English Channel experiences Atlantic storms year-round, but severe weather is rare during cruise season (May-September). Winter brings more frequent storms and wind, but summer visits typically have calm conditions. The bigger concern is rain rather than storms — bring rain gear but don't worry about hurricanes.

Q: Will rain ruin my visit to Le Havre and Paris?

No — rain in Le Havre and Paris is usually light drizzle rather than heavy downpours. Pack a light rain jacket or umbrella and don't let a little moisture ruin your Paris adventure. The Louvre, Musée d'Orsay, Sainte-Chapelle, and covered shopping arcades provide excellent rainy-day options. Trains run in all weather, and Paris is magical even in rain. May-September are the driest months with the most sunshine.

Q: Is Le Havre/Paris worth it?

The only realistic way to see Paris by cruise ship.

Q: Best thing?

Eiffel Tower + Louvre express + Montmartre.

Q: How long in Paris?

7–8 hours on ground is ideal.

Q: Walk from port?

No – train is essential.
