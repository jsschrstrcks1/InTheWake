Anthem of the Seas — Deck by Deck

Anthem of the Seas Deck Plans: Deck-by-Deck Layout & Cabin Strategy

Where the staterooms are, where the headline spaces sit, and which cabins are worth a second look before you book. Written as my own pre-cruise homework — I board her in about two weeks.

by Ken Baker | June 17, 2026

The short version: Anthem of the Seas is a 168,666-gross-ton Quantum-class ship with 16 decks, 14 of them open to passengers. Staterooms are on nine decks — Deck 3 and Decks 6 through 13. The headline spaces stack at the top and bottom: North Star, RipCord, the FlowRider and SeaPlex up on Decks 15 and 16; the pools, Windjammer and Solarium on Deck 14; and the theaters, casino and main dining rooms down on Deck 3. The quiet middle — Decks 8 to 10 — is where most of the cabins you actually want to book are.

Who This Is For — and One Honest Note

This is for the cruiser who already knows how a big ship works and just wants the map: which deck the thing they care about is on, and which cabins to steer toward or around. If you've sailed Royal Caribbean before, you can skim the deck table and skip to the cabin strategy.

The honest note: I have not sailed Anthem yet. I board her in about two weeks, and this page is the deck research I did for my own trip — official plans read, cabin-noise notes cross-checked. So where I tell you a fact about the layout, it's sourced and linked. Where I pass along which cabins are quietest, I'll say plainly that it's the common guidance I'm planning around and will confirm once I'm aboard. I'd rather tell you where the line is than pretend I've already walked every deck.

How Anthem's Decks Stack Up

Anthem reads from the outside like most big modern ships: amenities pushed to the top and bottom, cabins filling the middle. Of the 16 decks, 14 are open to passengers. Deck 2 is crew, tender and medical space you never see. The nine cabin decks are Deck 3 and Decks 6 through 13.

Three things are worth holding in your head before the table. First, the loud, fun, top-of-the-ship spaces — North Star, the FlowRider, RipCord by iFly, SeaPlex — are stacked on Decks 15 and 16. Second, the pool deck, the Windjammer buffet and the adults-only Solarium are all on Deck 14, directly above the highest cabins on Deck 13. Third, the theaters, the casino and the main dining rooms are all the way down on Deck 3, alongside that deck's cabins. Those three facts explain most of the cabin-noise advice further down.

Cabin Strategy — What's Confirmed, What's Worth Checking

I'll split this honestly. First the things the deck plans state outright. Then the things experienced cruisers widely advise, which I'm planning around but haven't lived yet.

What the deck plans confirm

Obstructed balconies are concentrated on Deck 6. The deck's staterooms run the length of the ship in even numbers, ending aft at 6310 on the port side and 6710 on starboard, where the Library and The Workshop sit. The obstruction is mid-deck: balcony categories 1E, 2E and 2F have views blocked by the top of a lifeboat — Royal Caribbean's own plan marks each obstructed cabin with an icon — and cabins 6254, 6258, 6654 and 6658 are specifically flagged (the plan even skips 6256 and 6656 right at that spot). The forward balconies on both sides are the opposite: larger verandas, unobstructed views. Obstructed balcony categories also turn up on Decks 7 and 13. If a balcony fare looks unusually low, this is usually why — check the category code against the plan before you book.

Aft inside cabins on Deck 7 sit above the Two70 lounge. The deck plan itself notes they "may hear noise from that area during show times." Two70 runs its evening sets there, so if you're a light sleeper or an early-to-bed traveler, that's a specific block to avoid rather than a vague warning.

What experienced cruisers advise (my pre-trip plan, to be confirmed aboard)

This is the part I'm taking on the common wisdom rather than my own feet, so I'm marking it as such:

  • Midship, Decks 8 to 10, is the usual sweet spot. Far from the pool-deck noise above and the entertainment decks below, with the shortest walk to the central elevators. It's the first place I'm looking for my own booking.
  • Forward and high means more motion. If you're prone to seasickness, the lower midship cabins ride more steadily than anything high and forward — a rule of physics more than opinion, but the degree is personal.
  • Deck 13 sits directly under the pool deck and Windjammer on Deck 14. Cruisers report early-morning deck-chair scraping and prep noise overhead. Lovely location, light sleeper's gamble.
  • Deck 3 cabins share the deck with the Royal Theatre, Music Hall and Casino Royale. Handy for getting to a show, but cabin guides consistently group Decks 3, 6 and 13 as the ones a light sleeper should look at twice — Deck 3 for the entertainment venues, Deck 6 for the obstructions, Deck 13 for the pool deck above.
  • Forward Decks 11 and 12 are near the Adventure Ocean kids' areas. Convenient if you're sailing with children, worth avoiding if a quiet hallway matters to you.

One more honest flag on cabin advice you'll find elsewhere: I saw "avoid the cabins near the Windjammer on Deck 8" and "avoid cabins in the 14600s" repeated on a couple of advice sites. Both are wrong. The Windjammer is on Deck 14, not near Deck 8, and Deck 14 has no cabins at all. I left them out rather than pass along a number that doesn't exist.

On accessible staterooms: Royal Caribbean's own deck plan marks the accessible categories directly. Deck 3, for instance, carries a Spacious Ocean View (Accessible) stateroom — extra square footage, an in-stateroom window, room for up to four, with one Royal King bed that converts to two twins. Accessible cabins sit across several decks rather than in one block, so the reliable move is to open the official plan and filter for them on your specific sailing. Using the Official Deck Plans

Everything above is meant to orient you. Before you book a specific cabin, open Royal Caribbean's own interactive deck plans and confirm your exact number — royalcaribbean.com/cruise-ships/anthem-of-the-seas/deck-plans. Its deck selector runs from Deck 3 up to Deck 16. Two reasons to confirm there:

  • Categories and what's for sale shift by sailing. Which cabins are released, and at what category, changes between cruises. The plan shows what's actually bookable for your dates.
  • The official plan is the source for your specific cabin. The deck-level layout here is cross-checked from the published plans on CruiseMapper and CruiseDeckPlans, with the top-line specs from Wikipedia. For the one cabin you're about to pay for, confirm it against Royal Caribbean's own plan.

A Note on the Other Quantum-Class Ships

Anthem is one of five Quantum-class ships, alongside Quantum of the Seas, Ovation of the Seas, Spectrum of the Seas, and Odyssey of the Seas. They share most of this layout — the North Star, the SeaPlex, the Two70 lounge, the Solarium, the same general stacking of decks. If you've sailed one, Anthem will feel familiar within minutes.

"Most" is doing real work in that sentence, though. Spectrum and Odyssey are the later "Quantum Ultra" variant with their own changes, and even the closer sisters differ in spots — a renamed restaurant here, a reconfigured suite block there. Use Anthem's own deck plan rather than memory from a different ship in the class.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many decks does Anthem of the Seas have?

Sixteen in total, with 14 open to passengers. Staterooms are on nine of them — Deck 3 and Decks 6 through 13.

Which decks have staterooms?

Decks 3, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10, 11, 12 and 13. Decks 4, 5, 14, 15 and 16 are public venues with no cabins, and Deck 2 is crew and operational space.

Which deck is North Star on, and where are RipCord and SeaPlex?

North Star sits at the very top, boarding from Deck 15 and rising from Deck 16. RipCord by iFly and the FlowRider are on Deck 16. SeaPlex spans Decks 15 and 16.

Which deck is the Windjammer buffet on?

Deck 14, together with the main pools, the H2O Zone, Coastal Kitchen, Johnny Rockets and the upper level of the adults-only Solarium.

Which Anthem cabins have obstructed views?

On Deck 6, balcony categories 1E, 2E and 2F are obstructed by the top of a lifeboat, and cabins 6254, 6258, 6654 and 6658 are specifically noted. Obstructed balcony categories also appear on Decks 7 and 13. Confirm any specific cabin on Royal Caribbean's official deck plans before booking.

Where are the quietest cabins on Anthem of the Seas?

The common guidance points to midship cabins on Decks 8 to 10, and to avoiding the aft inside cabins on Deck 7 that sit above Two70. The Two70 note comes straight from the deck plan; the midship guidance is general advice worth confirming for your own cabin.

Is Anthem's layout the same as other Quantum-class ships?

Largely. Anthem shares most of its layout and signature spaces with Quantum, Ovation, Spectrum and Odyssey of the Seas, but there are per-ship differences — so read each ship's own plan rather than assuming they match.
