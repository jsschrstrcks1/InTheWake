## Crew compensation context (for article)

The v0 draft attributed "gratuities make up to 95% of crew take-home pay" and "$600 to $1,500 monthly base" to **Forbes**. Verification:

- **Forbes 95% claim — DISQUALIFIED.** I could not locate a Forbes article supporting either the 95% figure or the $600–$1,500 base-salary range. The 95% number traces to **Cruise Critic's** "Gratuities — What's the Point?" article (https://www.cruisecritic.com/articles/cruise-line-gratuities-whats-the-point-cruise-tipping-and-salaries-dissected), where the language is: "The base wage is usually low — sometimes as little as a few dollars a day, leaving tips to represent as much as 95 percent of their total take-home pay." The article cites no primary research and uses qualifying hedges ("as much as," "sometimes"). Cite Cruise Critic, not Forbes — and use their hedged framing, not a flat assertion.
- **Base salary $600–$1,500 monthly — PARTIALLY VERIFIED.** Cruise Critic and Cruise Hive (https://www.cruisehive.com/how-much-do-cruise-ship-workers-make/64121) both publish role-specific ranges. Confirmed examples: kitchen cleaners ~$600/mo, laundry attendants ~$700/mo, cabin stewards $650-$1,150/mo (gratuity-inclusive), assistant waiters $900-$2,200/mo (gratuity-inclusive). The $600–$1,500 range matches the lower bound but the upper bound is conservative. Article should attribute to Cruise Critic + Cruise Hive, not Forbes, and note that ranges vary widely by role and contract.

**Recommendation:** Article may cite "industry trade reporting (Cruise Critic, Cruise Hive)" but must drop the Forbes attribution. Use the hedged 95% language verbatim or substitute "the majority of housekeeping and F&B crew take-home pay."

## Methodology notes

**Source hierarchy.** Official cruise-line policy pages were privileged over trade press. Where the official page returned a generic landing or a 403/404 (Carnival's `help.carnival.com`, Cunard's hotel-and-dining-policy page, Cruise Critic deep links, Oceania's Zendesk pages all returned authentication or redirect issues at one point), I followed redirects to the live URL (`carnival.com/help?topicid=1123`) or fell back to the line's "what's included" page plus a corroborating trade source (Cruise Critic news, Cruise Hive, Cruzely, Cruise.Blog, Eat Sleep Cruise). Every dollar figure in this document was either pulled from an official policy page or cross-confirmed across two independent trade sources reading from the same official rate card.

**Disagreements.** Trade press occasionally lagged official pages — some sources still cited Carnival $16/$18 (pre-April-2026) or Princess $17/$18/$19 (pre-March-2026). Where trade and official pages disagreed, I deferred to the official page and the cruise.blog 2026-dated change announcements, which were closest to the rate-change events.

**2026 rate-change announcements captured.**
- Carnival: 2026-04-02 ($16/$18 → $17/$19) and December 2025 service-charge bump from 18% to 20%.
- Princess: 2026-03-08 ($17/$18/$19 → $18/$19/$20) and 18% → 20% on F&B.
- MSC: 2026-04-10 (Med/N.Europe) and 2026-05-11 (Caribbean/Alaska); standard cabins $16 → $17, Yacht Club $20 → $23.
- Holland America: 2026-06-01 ($17/$19 → $18/$20) and 18% → 20% on bar/spa/specialty/room service.
- Margaritaville at Sea: 2026-02-01 (out of scope; not in the 15 lines).
- Virgin Voyages: 2025-10-07 (moved gratuities OUT of the fare; new bookings $20 prepaid / $22 onboard).
- Royal Caribbean, Celebrity, Norwegian, Cunard: no 2026 hike announced as of verification date.

**Reclassifications from the original plan list.**
- **Oceania** is bundled for 2026 (was listed as non-bundled in the plan). The "Your World Included" overhaul effective 2024-10-01 booked / 2025-01-01 sailing made all 2026 sailings bundled for new bookings. Treated here in the bundled column.
- **Virgin Voyages** is **not** bundled for 2026 (was tentatively listed as bundled in the plan). After 2025-10-07 it operates a per-night gratuity ($20/$22). Treated here as fee-based, with a legacy-booking carve-out.
- These two reclassifications shift the bundled count from 5 to 5 (Explora, Oceania, Regent, Seabourn, Silversea) and the per-night count from 10 to 10 (Carnival, Celebrity, Costa, Cunard, Holland America, MSC, Norwegian, Princess, Royal Caribbean, Virgin Voyages). Net same — just different members.

**Ambiguity flagged.**
- Celebrity has three rate tiers (Inside/Oceanview/Veranda $18; Concierge/AquaClass $19; Retreat $23). The two-column standard/suite schema in this document fits $18 standard / $23 suite, but the JSON tipping data file (Task 2) should preserve all three.
- Princess has three tiers ($18/$19/$20). Same caveat.
- Costa publishes a flat per-person rate with no suite differential and varies the rate by region/currency. The JSON file should expose region as a parameter.
- MSC's daily rate varies by sailing region and by currency; the JSON file should expose that.
