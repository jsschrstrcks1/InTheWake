Overview

Carnival Pride is the 2nd Spirit-class cruise ship, delivered in 2001. As Carnival's year-round presence in Baltimore, she pioneered consistent Caribbean cruising from the Mid-Atlantic region, making tropical getaways accessible to millions of East Coast cruisers.

Christened by NASA astronaut Tamara Jernigan at Port Canaveral on January 7, 2002, Carnival Pride features the stunning "Icons of Beauty" interior theme by renowned cruise ship designer Joe Farcus. The ship underwent a significant refurbishment in 2023.

Frequently Asked Questions

What class is Carnival Pride?

Carnival Pride is the 2nd Spirit-class ship, built in 2001 by Kvaerner Masa-Yards in Finland. Sister ships include Carnival Spirit, Carnival Legend, and Carnival Miracle.

Where does Carnival Pride sail from?

Carnival Pride is homeported year-round in Baltimore, Maryland. Since 2009, she's been the first Carnival ship to offer year-round Bahamas and Caribbean cruises from Baltimore.

Who was Carnival Pride's godmother?

Carnival Pride was christened by NASA astronaut and scientist Tamara Jernigan on January 7, 2002 at Port Canaveral, Florida.

What is the interior theme of Carnival Pride?

The interior design theme is "Icons of Beauty" created by renowned cruise ship designer Joe Farcus. It celebrates beauty in art, the human form, global architecture, and life in general.

When was Carnival Pride last refurbished?

Carnival Pride underwent its most recent refurbishment in 2023, receiving updated amenities and refreshed public spaces.
