Perfect Storm

Royal Caribbean — Waterslide Complex

Quick Answer: Perfect Storm is Royal Caribbean's trio of thrilling waterslides found on Oasis and Freedom Class ships. Features Typhoon (champagne bowl), Cyclone (enclosed tube), and Supercell (racer). All slides are complimentary for guests meeting height requirements (typically 48 inches). Wait times vary by time of day.

Best For: Thrill seekers, older children and teens, families looking for water fun, and anyone who enjoys waterslides with ocean views.

Key Facts

  • Price: Complimentary
  • Height: 48 inches (122 cm) minimum typically
  • Slides: 3 different experiences
  • Wait Time: 5-20 minutes typical
  • Location: Pool deck area

Experience heart-pounding waterslide action high above the ocean on Royal Caribbean's Perfect Storm.

Perfect Storm delivers three distinct waterslide experiences: the swirling champagne bowl of Typhoon, the twisting darkness of Cyclone, and the competitive racing of Supercell. Located on the pool deck, these slides offer thrills with spectacular ocean views. Return to the Restaurants hub →

The Slides

Cost: All slides complimentary · Height: 48" minimum (varies) · Best Time: Early morning or port days

Typhoon

  • Champagne bowl design
  • Swirl around the bowl
  • Drop through center
  • Most thrilling of the three
  • 48" height requirement

Cyclone

  • Enclosed tube slide
  • Twisting descent
  • Special lighting effects
  • Longer ride duration
  • 48" height requirement

Supercell

  • Racing slide (side-by-side)
  • Mat-based sliding
  • Competition element
  • Great for groups
  • May have different height req.

Slide configurations and names may vary by ship. Some ships have two slides instead of three. Always check signage for current height requirements.

Ship-Specific Variations
  • Oasis Class: Full Perfect Storm trio on Harmony, Symphony, Wonder
  • Freedom Class: Two-slide configuration typical
  • Icon Class: Different waterslide complex (Category 6)

Special Accommodations

Safety Notes: Height requirements strictly enforced for safety. Not recommended for guests with heart conditions, back problems, or who are pregnant. Guests must be able to climb stairs to reach slide entrances. Lifeguards on duty during operating hours. Weather may affect availability.

Where You'll Find It

Perfect Storm is located on the pool deck, typically near the sports court area. The distinctive slide structures are visible from many parts of the ship.

Hours & Tips

  • Opens around 9:00-10:00 AM
  • Closes at sunset or around 6:00 PM
  • Shortest lines: early morning, port days
  • Longest lines: sea day afternoons

Perfect Storm Review: Solid Waterslide Fun

Perfect Storm delivered good waterslide entertainment for our cruise. Typhoon was the highlight—the champagne bowl effect is genuinely thrilling as you swirl around before dropping through. Cyclone offered a fun enclosed experience with some lighting effects. Supercell was great for racing my kids.

Wait times were reasonable—about 10-15 minutes on sea days. We went early morning and had practically no wait. The slides aren't as extreme as land-based water parks, but they're impressive for a cruise ship and completely free.

Rating: 4.0/5. Recommended for families with kids who meet height requirements. Don't expect world-class water park thrills, but do expect solid fun.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are Perfect Storm slides free?

Yes, all Perfect Storm slides are complimentary for guests who meet height requirements.

What's the height requirement?

Typically 48 inches (122 cm) for most slides. Requirements may vary by ship and slide. Check signage at the attraction.

Which slide is most thrilling?

Typhoon (the champagne bowl) is generally considered the most thrilling due to the swirling bowl effect and drop through the center.

Are there lifeguards at Perfect Storm?

Yes, lifeguards are on duty during operating hours and monitor the slides from top and bottom.

How long is the wait for Perfect Storm?

Typically 5-20 minutes depending on time of day. Early morning and port days have shortest waits.
