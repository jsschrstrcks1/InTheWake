Overview

Casino Royale brings Las Vegas-style gaming to sea, featuring everything from penny slots to high-limit table games. The casino size and offerings vary by ship class, with Oasis and Icon Class ships featuring the largest gaming floors. Every Royal Caribbean ship has a Casino Royale, making it one of the most consistent entertainment options across the fleet.

Unlike land-based casinos, shipboard gaming operates on a unique schedule dictated by maritime law. Casinos can only operate in international waters, which means they typically open about an hour after departure and close as the ship approaches port. Sea days offer the longest gaming hours, often from morning until late night.

Gaming Floor Layout

Most Casino Royale venues are located on lower decks (typically Deck 4 or 5) near the Promenade or Royal Promenade. The layout typically includes:

  • Slot machine sections (including penny, nickel, quarter, and dollar denominations)
  • Table games pit with blackjack, roulette, and craps
  • Poker room for tournaments and cash games
  • VIP/high-limit area on larger ships

Frequently Asked Questions

Is smoking allowed in Royal Caribbean casinos?

Policies have evolved and vary by ship. Many newer ships have non-smoking casinos or designated non-smoking sections. Check your specific ship's current policy. Non-smoking slot areas are typically available on all ships.

What is the minimum gambling age on Royal Caribbean?

The minimum gambling age is 18 on all Royal Caribbean ships, regardless of which port you depart from. Valid photo ID is required to enter the casino.

Does Royal Caribbean have a casino loyalty program?

Yes, Club Royale is the free casino loyalty program with tiers from Classic to Seven Stars. Benefits include free play, complimentary drinks while gaming, priority boarding, and even free cruise offers for higher-tier members.

When is the casino open on Royal Caribbean?

Casinos open when the ship reaches international waters (typically about an hour after departure) and close when returning to US territorial waters. Sea days offer the longest hours, often 10 AM to 3 AM.

Can I use my credit card in the casino?

Yes, credit and debit cards are accepted for cash advances, but fees apply (typically 3% + ATM fees). Using cash or your SeaPass account is more economical. ATMs are available near the casino.

Are drinks free in the casino?

Complimentary drinks are provided to active players. Cocktail servers circulate the floor while you play. The casino bar also serves players. Tipping servers ensures better service throughout your cruise.

What are the typical table minimums?

Minimums vary by ship, time of day, and game. Expect $10-15 for blackjack during off-peak times, rising to $25+ during busy periods. Roulette often has $5 minimums. Ask at the tables for current limits.
