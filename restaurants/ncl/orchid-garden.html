Orchid Garden

Norwegian Cruise Line — Dining

Quick Answer: Orchid Garden is the Asian Fusion restaurant on Norwegian's Jewel-class ships — and one of the line's rare complimentary specialty-style venues. You eat the noodles, wok-fried dishes, and soups for no cover; the neighboring Sushi Bar and Sake Bar are the parts that cost extra.

Best For: Guests who want an Asian meal included in the fare, sushi lovers who want a few pieces à la carte without a full specialty cover, and anyone who likes a casual, conversational room

Key Facts

  • Price: Asian Fusion menu complimentary; Sushi Bar à la carte (about $5–$8.50 per item); Sake Bar extra
  • Location: Deck 7 on Norwegian Gem, beside the Sushi Bar and Teppanyaki; Jewel-class ships
  • Cuisine: Asian Fusion — noodles, wok-fried dishes, soups, fried rice
  • Dress Code: Smart Casual
  • Reservations: Not usually required; reserve in the Norwegian Cruise Line app if you prefer

Orchid Garden pairs a complimentary Asian Fusion menu with an adjacent à la carte Sushi Bar and Sake Bar — an unusually flexible, budget-friendly way to eat Asian at sea. Return to the Restaurants hub →

Additional Notes
  • Orchid Garden's Asian Fusion dining is complimentary per the most recent published information; on some earlier sailings the venue carried a per-person cover charge, so policies can and do change — confirm in the app for your specific cruise.
  • The complimentary dishes and the paid sushi come from adjacent kitchens, so it is easy to combine free appetizers with a few à la carte sushi pieces.
  • Desserts are included with the Asian Fusion menu.

Special Accommodations

Allergen & Dietary Notes: Norwegian Cruise Line follows allergen policies. Please disclose allergens to your server before ordering — Asian cooking commonly uses soy, sesame, peanut, shellfish, and gluten. Vegetarian wok dishes and many adjustments are available on request; ask about gluten-free soy sauce.

Where You'll Find It

Orchid Garden appears on Norwegian's Jewel-class ships — Norwegian Gem, Jewel, Jade, and Pearl. On Norwegian Gem it sits on Deck 7, overlooking the two-deck atrium and set between the Sushi Bar and the Teppanyaki room, with the Sake Bar alongside. Check your ship's deck plan or the Norwegian Cruise Line app for exact location and current hours.

4.1 ★ out of 5 Norwegian Jewel-class • 2024-2025

Orchid Garden Review: The Rare Free Asian Room

Introduction. Orchid Garden is a genuinely unusual thing on a cruise ship: an Asian restaurant you can eat in for no cover charge. On the Jewel-class ships the Asian Fusion menu is included, and it shares a corner of Deck 7 with the à la carte Sushi Bar and a small Sake Bar. That layout is the key to the whole experience — you can make a complimentary meal of it, or use the free appetizers as a runway into a few pieces of paid sushi.

Food & Drinks

The kitchen punches above its "included" billing. Guests single out the Asian crab wontons — crisp outside, creamy inside, with a spicy plum sauce — and the salt-and-pepper calamari, which comes out crisp rather than rubbery and, notably, not over-breaded. The hot-and-sour soup has real punch, and when the Chengdu spiced lamb is on, it arrives sizzling on a cast-iron skillet with some flair. From the Sushi Bar next door, the crispy rice at about six dollars is the runaway favorite: lightly fried rice patties with a sauce that people order twice. The complimentary noodles and wok-fried dishes are solid rather than dazzling, but for zero cover that is a very fair trade.

Service & Atmosphere

The room is casual — few guests dress up — and the décor leans modern with traditional Asian touches: lattice work, silk scrolls, geometric smoked-glass panels. Because it overlooks the atrium, it feels open and conversational, though that is also the one honest knock on it: music drifting up from the bar below can be audible, so it is livelier than a hushed specialty room. Service is friendly and prompt. The single most useful thing to know is the pricing quirk — the Asian menu is free, the sushi is not — which trips up first-timers who assume it is all one or the other.

Pro Tips

  • Start free, finish à la carte. Order complimentary appetizers in Orchid Garden, then add a few sushi pieces from the bar next door — you control the spend.
  • Get the crispy rice. At roughly $6 it is the most-praised item on the sushi menu.
  • Confirm the price in the app. The Asian menu is complimentary now, but the venue has carried a cover charge on past sailings — check before you go.
  • Want quiet? This isn't it. The atrium view is pleasant but the bar music carries; choose Le Bistro or a main dining room for a hushed dinner.

Conclusion

Rating: 4.1/5. Orchid Garden is one of the best-value meals on a Jewel-class ship — real Asian cooking with no cover, plus optional sushi when you want it. Tip: go in understanding the free-menu / paid-sushi split, order the crab wontons and the crispy rice, and don't expect a library-quiet room.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Orchid Garden complimentary on Norwegian Cruise Line?

Yes. As of the most recent published information, Orchid Garden's Asian Fusion menu is complimentary — included in your cruise fare. The adjacent Sushi Bar is à la carte (roughly $5 to $8.50 per item), and the neighboring Sake Bar charges for sake and Japanese beer. Pricing and policies change, so confirm in the Norwegian Cruise Line app for your sailing.

What food does Orchid Garden serve?

Orchid Garden is an Asian Fusion venue: freshly prepared noodles, wok-fried dishes, authentic soups, fried rice, appetizers, and desserts. Guest favorites include the Asian crab wontons, salt-and-pepper calamari, hot-and-sour soup, and — from the à la carte sushi menu — the crispy rice. The exact menu varies by ship and sailing.

How does the Sushi Bar pricing work at Orchid Garden?

The complimentary Asian Fusion dishes are included, but sushi from the adjacent Sushi Bar is à la carte, generally around $5 to $8.50 per item. A common approach is to start with the free appetizers in Orchid Garden and then order a few sushi pieces — the crispy rice, at about $6, is the most-praised.

Where is Orchid Garden located?

On Norwegian Gem, Orchid Garden is on Deck 7, overlooking the two-deck atrium and sitting between the Sushi Bar and the Teppanyaki room, with the Sake Bar alongside. It appears on Norwegian's Jewel-class ships (Gem, Jewel, Jade, Pearl).

Do I need reservations for Orchid Garden?

Reservations are not usually required for the complimentary Asian Fusion dining, though you can reserve a time in the Norwegian Cruise Line app. Smart Casual dress is recommended; the room is relaxed and conversational.
