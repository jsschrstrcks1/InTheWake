# Phase 3.1 revert audit — 4 cross-ship-comparison rewrites

**Date:** 2026-05-07
**Branch:** `claude/review-client-proposal-vILOr`
**Trigger:** `audit-reports/internal-consistency/_comparison-check.md` flagged 26 swaps; 4 confirmed factually wrong on disk.

## What was reverted

Each entry shows: the file, the offending sentence as it stood post-Phase-3.1, the revert applied, and the rationale.

### `ships/cunard/queen-anne.html` (line 426)

**Post-3.1:** "Queen Elizabeth or Queen Victoria may be a better fit at roughly 90,000 GT and **2,996 guests**."
**Reverted to:** "...at roughly 90,000 GT and **2,081 guests**."
**Rationale:** Sentence subject is Queen Elizabeth / Queen Victoria, not Queen Anne. The 2,081 figure is the published lower-berth capacity for the Vista-class sisters. Rewriting it to Queen Anne's 2,996 made the page tell readers Queen Elizabeth carries Queen Anne's guest count.

### `ships/princess/coral-princess.html` (line 399)

**Post-3.1:** "If you want a larger Princess ship, the Grand Class carries **~1,970 guests**; for the newest design, the Sphere Class is the current flagship."
**Reverted to:** "...the Grand Class carries **~3,000 guests**; ..."
**Rationale:** Sentence subject is the Grand Class as a category. The "~3,000" figure approximates Grand-class lower-berth capacity (Caribbean Princess, Crown Princess, Diamond, Sapphire, etc., all ~3,000 LB). Rewriting it to 1,970 (Coral's specific number) produced an absurd recommendation: "want a larger ship? — the Grand Class carries the same number Coral does."

### `ships/princess/island-princess.html` (line 399)

**Post-3.1:** "If you want a larger Princess ship with more amenities, the Grand Class has **~1,970 guests**; for the newest design, the Sphere Class is the current generation."
**Reverted to:** "...the Grand Class has **~3,000 guests**; ..."
**Rationale:** Identical to coral-princess. Same boilerplate template; same wrong rewrite.

### `ships/norwegian/norwegian-sun.html` (line 405)

**Post-3.1:** "Norwegian Sun is one of NCL's most intimate ocean ships at under **1,878 guests**."
**Reverted to:** "...at under **2,000 guests**."
**Rationale:** "Ocean ships at under N" describes a class bucket — the bucket is the population of ships, of which Sun is one example. The 2,000 figure is the bucket descriptor; replacing it with Sun's specific 1,878 makes the bucket too tight to describe its members ("under 1,878" excludes Sun herself, who is at ~1,936 LB / 2,002 max).

## What was NOT reverted (reviewed and kept)

The audit flagged 26 contexts; 22 stand. Categories:

- **Vision-class "Viking Crown" sentences** (Brilliance, Vision, Rhapsody, Splendour, Enchantment, Grandeur, Jewel, Radiance, Legend) — phrasing like "mid-size atmosphere with about 2,100, the signature Viking Crown" describes the page-ship herself; sharpening "about 2,100" to a precise per-ship LB is defensible.
- **"larger than Voyager class"** (Liberty, Freedom, Independence) — the integer was the page-ship's max; the rewrite to LB satisfies Policy 0.2 and the comparison "larger than Voyager class" remains true on either count.
- **norwegian-spirit** — context "she now carries 2,018" → 1,972 was self-reference (Spirit's own capacity); the regex falsely matched "Star Cruises" in the surrounding text.
- **norwegian-star** — fact-block "Star (Star Class, 91,740 GT, 2,224, 2001)" is a self-reference; both 2,224 and 2,298 are self-claims about Star.

## Skill hardening

- `if you want/prefer/need/...`
- `may/might/would be a better fit/choice/option`
- `the [Class] class carries/has/holds/features/sails`
- `intimate/small/large ... ocean/cruise/cunard/princess/... ships at/under/over/around/near`
- `by contrast`, `in contrast`, `unlike`
