Wonder of the Seas tends to suit travelers who appreciate grand-scale cruising with seven distinct neighborhoods including Central Park, the Boardwalk, and the dramatic AquaTheater. It's ideal if you want one of the world's largest cruise ships with extensive dining and entertainment options, and the space to explore something new each day. Frequently Asked Questions

What makes Wonder of the Seas unique compared to other Royal Caribbean ships?

Wonder of the Seas is the newest and largest ship in the Oasis Class, featuring Royal Caribbean's signature seven-neighborhood layout including Central Park (with over 12,000 live plants and trees), Boardwalk with a hand-carved carousel, and the AquaTheater with high-diving shows. At 236,857 GT with space for up to 6,988 guests, it's one of the largest cruise ships in the world, offering extensive dining, entertainment, and activities across its multiple decks.

Which ships are in the same class as Wonder of the Seas?

Wonder of the Seas is part of the Oasis Class, which includes Oasis of the Seas, Allure of the Seas, Harmony of the Seas, Symphony of the Seas, and Utopia of the Seas. All share the iconic seven-neighborhood design and massive scale.
