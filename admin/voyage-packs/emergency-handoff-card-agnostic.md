# Emergency Handoff Card
## A sailing-agnostic template for any cruise

*Cruiser: fill this in, print one copy for the family contact, keep a photo on your phone. Contact: keep this somewhere accessible — fridge, glove box, phone notes — for the week.*

</div>

## How to use this card

This is a one-page emergency reference designed to work for any cruise on any major line. **Fill in the blanks for your specific sailing** (ship, dates, ports, your cabin) and hand it to one trusted person ashore before you sail. The cruise line and consulate numbers below are pre-filled for the major US-departing lines and the most common Caribbean/Alaska/European itineraries.

**The cruise-line number that matters most is the 24/7 emergency or Care Team line** — not the booking line. The numbers in the table below are the right ones; the toll-free sales line your travel agent gave you is not.

## What the at-home contact does if 36+ hours pass without word

1. **Don't panic** — cruise Wi-Fi is unreliable; silence usually means signal, not emergency.
2. **Call the cruise line's 24/7 number** above; identify yourself as the at-home contact. They will confirm the cruiser boarded and can relay messages to the ship.
3. **Reach the group host** (if shared) — host is the in-person on the ship, fastest eyes.
4. **If the cruise line confirms an incident:** US Coast Guard for US/Alaska waters; nearest US Consulate for foreign ports; ship's medical for health emergencies.
5. **Track the ship's position** at `cruisinginthewake.com/tools/ship-tracker.html` or `marinetraffic.com` for context.

## What the cruiser should pack for emergencies

- Printed list of medications, allergies, and emergency contacts (in your cabin nightstand)
- Photocopy of passport, separate from the passport itself
- Travel insurance policy number, printed
- This handoff card, in your phone's notes app and shared with one trusted person ashore
