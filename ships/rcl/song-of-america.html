Historical Overview

Song of America was a Royal Caribbean ship that served from 1982 to 1999. At over 37,700 gross tons, she was one of the largest cruise ships in the world when built. The ship featured modern amenities for its era and helped establish Royal Caribbean as a major cruise line during the industry's growth period in the 1980s and 1990s.

After leaving the Royal Caribbean fleet in 1999, the ship was sold and renamed multiple times before being scrapped in 2025, ending a 43-year career at sea.

Historical Context

Song of America was the lead ship of her class and represented a significant step forward in cruise ship design for Royal Caribbean. At the time of her debut, she was among the largest cruise ships afloat.
