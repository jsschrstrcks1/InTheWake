MSC Seascape — Deck Plans, Live Tracker, Dining & Videos

MSC Seascape is the second and final ship of MSC Cruises' Seaside EVO Class, delivered on 16 November 2022 at Monfalcone, Italy by Fincantieri and christened on 7 December 2022 at the Manhattan Cruise Terminal in New York by Sophia Loren. She measures 170,412 gross tons and 339 metres in length, carries 4,540 guests at double occupancy (5,877 maximum) across 2,270 cabins with a crew of 1,648, and flies the Maltese flag from Valletta. Her 19-deck layout includes four main dining rooms, the 1,200-seat Chora Theatre, the glass-floored Bridge of Sighs on Deck 16, the signature aft Infinity Pool, the Pirates Cove Aquapark with AquaTube spiral slides, the Kraken-themed Adventure Trail ropes course, the Robotron robotic-arm ride, and a full ~32,000 sq ft MSC Yacht Club enclave. She runs 7-night Western Caribbean round-trips year-round from Galveston, Texas.

Quick Answer: MSC Seascape (170,412 GT, 4,540 double / 5,877 max, 1,648 crew, delivered 2022) is the second and final Seaside EVO Class ship — only sister is MSC Seashore. She carries an unusual four main dining rooms (The Green Wave, Cala Jondal, Aegean, Skyline), the 1,200-seat Chora Theatre, the two-deck Moulin Rouge-style Le Cabaret Rouge, the Marketplace Buffet, Butcher's Cut steakhouse, Kaito Sushi Bar and Teppanyaki, Hola! Tacos & Cantina, Ocean Cay Restaurant, Venchi 1878 gelato and chocolate bars, and a full MSC Yacht Club with the Top Sail Lounge and private sundeck. Signature features: Bridge of Sighs glass walkway, aft Infinity Pool, Pirates Cove Aquapark, Robotron robotic-arm ride, Kraken-themed Adventure Trail ropes course. Galveston / Western Caribbean year-round 2026.

Who She's For: Texas-based cruisers who want a big modern MSC ship out of the brand-new Galveston Cruise Terminal 16, families who will use the Pirates Cove Aquapark and Adventure Trail ropes course, and suite travellers looking for a full MSC Yacht Club on a relatively new hull. Western Caribbean regulars who already know Costa Maya, Cozumel, and Roatán. Not for cruisers expecting Cirque du Soleil at Sea — that concept is limited to the Meraviglia-class ships, not the Seaside EVO class.

MSC Seascape is the second and final ship of the Seaside EVO Class, together with her only sister MSC Seashore (2021). The Seaside EVO class is an enlarged evolution of the original Seaside Class — MSC Seaside (2017) and MSC Seaview (2018) form the parent class but are not sisters of the EVO pair. Both Seaside EVOs were built at Fincantieri Monfalcone in Italy. Seascape's delivery occurred on 16 November 2022, and she was christened three weeks later in a ceremony at the Manhattan Cruise Terminal in New York — Sophia Loren's 18th MSC ship ceremony, though she appeared via a special broadcast rather than in person; Alexa Aponte-Vago (daughter of MSC Group chairman Gianluigi Aponte) cut the ribbon. Her defining Seaside-family features are outdoor-focused: the aft Infinity Pool with its wrap-around promenade, the glass-floored Bridge of Sighs walkway that extends out over the ship's side on Deck 16, the Pirates Cove Aquapark on Decks 18-19 (two AquaTube spiral slides, the AquaPlay 150 themed play structure, Pool Sider kids slide, and tipping bucket — supplied by WhiteWater West), and the Kraken-themed Adventure Trail ropes course on Deck 19. Unique to Seascape at sea is the Robotron robotic-arm amusement ride on Deck 20. On 7 November 2025 she repositioned from Miami to Galveston, Texas — MSC Cruises' first-ever Texas homeport — where she now runs 7-night Western Caribbean round-trips every Sunday through at least October 2027.

Entertainment & Activities on MSC Seascape

Entertainment on MSC Seascape is built around the two-level Chora Theatre (Decks 6-7, ~1,200 seats) for the main production shows, with the two-deck Moulin Rouge-inspired Le Cabaret Rouge beside it on Decks 6-7 for late-night cabaret. Deck 7 holds the MSC Signature Casino, the Champagne & Wine Bar, the Interactive XD Cinema, and the MSC Formula Racer F1 simulator. Deck 8 is the specialty-dining and lounge hub: Butcher's Cut, Kaito Sushi Bar, Kaito Teppanyaki, Hola! Tacos & Cantina, and Ocean Cay Restaurant all cluster around the Chef's Court Cocktail Bar, with The Wine Cellar, Uptown Lounge, Sports Bar, Venchi 1878 Gelato, the indoor glass Infinity Bridge walkway, and the aft Infinity Pool with its Infinity Bar. Deck 16 has the Jungle Pool Lounge with retractable roof, the glass-floored Bridge of Sighs observation walkway, and the Top Sail Lounge (the private Yacht Club lounge). Deck 18 carries the Marina Pool, The Studio nightclub, Beat Hero VR, VR Rafting, the Hall of Games, and the MSC Yacht Club Restaurant. The topmost Deck 19 and Deck 20 house the Pirates Cove Aquapark (two AquaTube spiral slides, AquaPlay 150 play structure, Pool Sider kids slide, tipping bucket), the Kraken-themed Adventure Trail ropes course, the MSC Sports Arena, the Top 19 Exclusive Solarium, and Seascape's signature Robotron robotic-arm amusement ride (with no equivalent at sea). Wellness lives at the MSC Aurea Spa on Deck 8 with thermal area, MediSpa, and full beauty, nail, hair, and barber services. Note: MSC Seascape does NOT carry Cirque du Soleil at Sea (that concept is Meraviglia-class only).

MSC Seascape Deck Plans

MSC Seascape's 19-deck layout runs from Deck 4 through Deck 20. Deck 5 holds The Green Wave Restaurant and the Seascape Bar atrium; Deck 6 adds the Cala Jondal Restaurant, the Venchi 1878 Chocolate Bar, the Shine Bar, and the lower Chora Theatre and Le Cabaret Rouge. Deck 7 is the entertainment axis with the Aegean and Skyline restaurants, the MSC Signature Casino, and the XD Cinema. Deck 8 is the specialty-dining cluster with Butcher's Cut, Kaito Sushi Bar, Kaito Teppanyaki, Hola! Tacos & Cantina, and Ocean Cay Restaurant grouped around the Cocktail Bar, plus the indoor Infinity Bridge glass walkway, Venchi 1878 Gelato, Loft Café, The Wine Cellar, Uptown Lounge, Sports Bar, the MSC Aurea Spa, and the aft Infinity Pool with Infinity Bar. Deck 16 is the main pool deck with the Marketplace Buffet, Jungle Pool Lounge (retractable roof), Sky Bar, the glass-floored Bridge of Sighs, and the forward MSC Yacht Club Concierge Area and Top Sail Lounge. Deck 18 carries the Marina Pool and Bar, The Studio nightclub, Beat Hero VR, VR Rafting, Hall of Games, and the MSC Yacht Club Restaurant. Decks 18-19 host the Pirates Cove Aquapark; Deck 19 adds the Horizon Bar, Top 19 Exclusive Solarium and Top 19 Bar, and the Adventure Trail ropes course. Deck 20 tops the ship with the MSC Sports Arena, Robotron, and the private MSC Yacht Club sundeck (The One Pool, The One Pool Grill & Bar, MSC Yacht Club Grill & Bar).

Frequently Asked Questions About MSC Seascape

What dining options are on MSC Seascape?

MSC Seascape has four complimentary main dining rooms — The Green Wave Restaurant (Deck 5, the largest MDR), Cala Jondal Restaurant (Deck 6), Aegean Restaurant (Deck 7), and Skyline Restaurant (Deck 7, for Aurea Experience guests) — which is one more than the usual two MSC MDRs. She also carries the Marketplace Buffet (Deck 16). Specialty dining clusters on Deck 8 around a shared Cocktail Bar in the Chef's Court area: Butcher's Cut, Kaito Sushi Bar, Kaito Teppanyaki, Hola! Tacos & Cantina, and Ocean Cay Restaurant. Sweet venues include Venchi 1878 Gelato (Deck 8) and Venchi 1878 Chocolate Bar (Deck 6). Yacht Club suite guests have the private MSC Yacht Club Restaurant (Deck 18) and The One Pool Grill & Bar (Deck 20).

Which ships are in the same class as MSC Seascape?

MSC Seascape is the second and final ship of the Seaside EVO Class, together with her only sister MSC Seashore (2021). The Seaside EVO class is an enlarged evolution of the original Seaside Class — MSC Seaside (2017) and MSC Seaview (2018) form the parent class but are not EVO sisters. The EVO pair sail at 170,412 GT on 339 m hulls (vs ~153,516 GT and 323 m for the original Seasides) and carry 2,270 cabins with four main dining rooms instead of two.

Does MSC Seascape have the MSC Yacht Club?

Yes. MSC Seascape has a full MSC Yacht Club ship-within-a-ship enclave from her 2022 delivery. Per MSC Cruises the enclave is approximately 32,000 square feet and contains the Concierge Area and the panoramic Top Sail Lounge on Deck 16, the MSC Yacht Club Restaurant on Deck 18, and a private sundeck on Deck 20 with The One Pool, The One Pool Grill & Bar, and the MSC Yacht Club Grill & Bar. Yacht Club suite guests receive 24-hour butler service and private access to a section of the MSC Aurea Spa.

Where does MSC Seascape sail in 2026?

MSC Seascape is homeported year-round at Galveston, Texas, operating out of the Port of Galveston's new Cruise Terminal 16. She repositioned from Miami on 7 November 2025 — MSC Cruises' first-ever Texas homeport — and runs 7-night Western Caribbean round-trips every Sunday, calling at Costa Maya and Cozumel (Mexico) and Roatán (Honduras). The Galveston homeport is scheduled to continue through at least October 2027. Current AIS (11 April 2026) shows her returning from Cozumel to Galveston for a Sunday 12 April turnaround — consistent with the weekly rotation. Note: she does NOT carry Cirque du Soleil at Sea.

Who christened MSC Seascape?

MSC Seascape was christened on 7 December 2022 at the Manhattan Cruise Terminal in New York City by Sophia Loren, MSC Cruises' long-standing godmother — her 18th MSC ship ceremony, though Sophia Loren appeared via a special broadcast rather than in person. The in-person ribbon cutter was Alexa Aponte-Vago, daughter of MSC Group chairman Gianluigi Aponte. She had been delivered by Fincantieri at Monfalcone, Italy on 16 November 2022. She is registered in Malta (Valletta) and carries IMO number 9843807.
