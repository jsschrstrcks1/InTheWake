First Look: Carnival Panorama

Carnival Panorama at the Long Beach cruise terminal — West Coast's flagship Fun Ship

The main pool deck featuring WaterWorks water park and poolside Guy's Burger Joint

Ship Overview

Carnival Panorama is the third and largest Vista-class ship in Carnival's fleet, bringing groundbreaking Fun Ship innovation to the West Coast. Launched in 2019 as Carnival's first new ship deployed to California in 20 years, she features the signature Vista-class attractions including SkyRide (suspended cycling above the ship), the first IMAX theater at sea, and the exclusive Havana section with Cuban-inspired staterooms.

Sailing year-round from Long Beach, Carnival Panorama offers week-long Mexican Riviera itineraries with stops at popular ports including Cabo San Lucas, Mazatlán, and Puerto Vallarta. At 133,868 GT, she's the largest of the three Vista-class sisters.

Key Highlights

  • SkyRide: Pedal-powered aerial cycling 150 feet above the ocean on an 800-foot track
  • IMAX Theater: First IMAX at sea with 187-seat venue showing Hollywood films
  • Havana Section: Cuban-themed cabins with exclusive pool and bar access
  • WaterWorks: Water park featuring Kaleid-O-Slide and splash zones
  • West Coast Exclusive: Only Vista-class ship based on the Pacific coast

Signature Features

SkyRide

Suspended pedal-powered cycling attraction with 800-foot track circling the ship's top deck, 150 feet above sea level. Vista-class exclusive.

IMAX Theater

187-seat venue showing first-run Hollywood films and documentaries — the first IMAX experience at sea. 3D capabilities included.

Havana Section

Cuban-themed cabins and suites with exclusive daytime access to the Havana Bar and Havana Pool — adults-only tropical retreat.

WaterWorks

Water park featuring the Kaleid-O-Slide, Twister waterslide, and Fun Ship splash zone for families.

SkyZone

Sports court with basketball, volleyball, mini-golf, and the SkyCourse ropes course for active guests.

Serenity Adult Retreat

Adults-only (21+) sanctuary with padded loungers, infinity whirlpools, and dedicated bar service. Peace at sea.

Frequently Asked Questions

Where does Carnival Panorama sail from?

Carnival Panorama sails year-round from Long Beach, California, offering week-long Mexican Riviera itineraries to Cabo San Lucas, Mazatlán, and Puerto Vallarta.

What class is Carnival Panorama?

She is the third and largest Vista-class ship at 133,868 GT — sister to Carnival Vista (2016) and Carnival Horizon (2018).

What is SkyRide?

SkyRide is a pedal-powered aerial cycling attraction suspended 150 feet above the ocean on an 800-foot track circling the ship's top deck. Vista-class exclusive.

Does Carnival Panorama have an IMAX theater?

Yes! The 187-seat IMAX theater shows first-run Hollywood movies and documentaries — the first IMAX experience at sea.

What is the Havana section?

The Havana section offers Cuban-themed staterooms with exclusive daytime access to the Havana Bar and Havana Pool — a tropical retreat for Havana cabin guests.

What ports does Carnival Panorama visit?

The typical Mexican Riviera itinerary visits Cabo San Lucas, Mazatlán, and Puerto Vallarta with multiple sea days for enjoying ship amenities.
