Main Dining Room

Royal Caribbean — Complimentary Dining

Rotating menus, classic cruise favorites, and white-tablecloth service — included in your cruise fare for breakfast, lunch (on select days), and dinner nightly.

The Main Dining Room (MDR) anchors your onboard dining with a mix of staples and nightly features. It’s the most reliable way to sample a little of everything — with vegetarian/vegan options, kids’ choices, and allergen support always available. Return to the Restaurants hub →

Menu & Price

Price: Complimentary (included). Service: Breakfast daily, Lunch on sea days (and select port days), Dinner nightly. Menus rotate by theme; exact line-up varies by ship/itinerary (check the app on board).

Vegan & Indian Options

Vegan: Since 2023, each dinner theme includes 1–2 vegan choices per course (starter, entrée, dessert). Expect Ratatouille/Vegetable Wellington (French), Eggplant Parmesan (Italian), Falafel Plate (Mediterranean), Stir-Fried Tofu or Pad Thai (Asia), Sorbet Trio, etc.

Indian & Vegetarian: Most sailings can provide Indian vegetarian mains (e.g., chana masala, dal, aloo gobi) and breads on request; some ships rotate an Indian entrée on the printed menu.

How to request: Note preferences in the Royal App or via special_needs@rccl.com pre-cruise, then confirm with the MDR host on Day 1.

Special Accommodations

Allergen & Dietary Notes: Royal Caribbean follows SAFE Food Policy. Please disclose allergens to your server before ordering. Gluten-free, dairy-free, vegetarian, and many vegan adjustments are available on request. For severe allergies, contact Royal Caribbean’s accessibility team at least 30 days prior to sailing and confirm with the venue on embarkation day.

4 ★ out of 5 Icon of the Seas • Aug 2025 • Dinner (French, Italian, Caribbean Nights) Price: Complimentary • Optional add-ons $16.99–$25

Sailed aboard Icon of the Seas.

Royal Caribbean Main Dining Room Review 2025: Elegant Complimentary Dining with Themed Twists

Introduction. My overall impression of the Main Dining Room (MDR) on Royal Caribbean is that it's a delightful, value-packed cornerstone of cruise life, offering multi-course meals with global flair that feel surprisingly upscale for being completely complimentary. It's the perfect blend of convenience, variety, and warmth, making every evening a mini-celebration without the hassle or extra cost. I dined at the Main Dining Room on the Icon of the Seas during a 7-night Eastern Caribbean cruise in late August 2025, hitting it for both lunch and dinner across three themed nights—French, Italian, and Caribbean. Reservations were flexible via the Royal App at around 7 PM most evenings. The price level is unbeatable: entirely free for core menus (starters, entrées, desserts), with optional premium add-ons like Maine lobster tails ranging from $16.99 to $25, delivering exceptional bang for zero bucks on the basics.

Food

On French Night, escargot in garlic butter arrived sizzling; the beef tenderloin (béarnaise) was a juicy, medium-rare showstopper with crisp asparagus and fingerlings. Italian Night featured fork-tender osso buco, bright caprese, and a feather-light tiramisu. Caribbean Night brought island energy with jerk pork chops and plantain-crusted mahi-mahi; a quick remake on a busy evening was handled gracefully (with a bonus grilled-pineapple side). Presentations were clean, ingredients fresh, and themes kept the lineup fun for both comfort-seekers and adventurers.

Service

Our team—led by Sofia—was warm and proactive, remembered preferences by Night 2, paced courses at a relaxed 45–60 minutes, and handled dietary tweaks (gluten-free swap for a side) without fuss. Lively peak-evening energy felt communal rather than chaotic, and a surprise sorbet intermezzo was a thoughtful touch.

Atmosphere / Ambiance

On Icon of the Seas, MDR feels like a modern floating ballroom—three levels, ocean-blue upholstery, and chandelier lighting that settles to a cozy glow by dinner. Window tables at sunset are magic; book early in the app. Noise rises on sea days, but the mood stays upbeat and inclusive.

Conclusion

Rating: 4/5. A cruise essential that punches far above its free price tag. Perfect for families, first-timers, and anyone chasing global flavors without add-on costs. Pro tip: Use My Time Dining in the app to target window seats on theme nights.
