Norwegian Aqua — Deck Plans, Live Tracker, Dining & Videos

Norwegian Aqua is a Prima Plus Class cruise ship operated by Norwegian Cruise Line. She entered service in 2025, measures 156,300 gross tons, and carries approximately 3,571 guests at double occupancy with a crew of 1,388.

Key Facts

  • Class: Prima Plus
  • Year: 2025
  • Tonnage: 156,300 GT
  • Guests: 3,571
  • IMO: 9824007

Quick Answer: Norwegian Aqua (156,300 GT, 3,571 guests, 2025) is the newest (as of 2026) and first Prima Plus Class ship — NCL's most advanced vessel with the Aqua Slidecoaster, redesigned Haven suite complex, and expanded outdoor spaces. This page covers deck plans, live tracking, dining venues, and videos.

Best For: Families and active cruisers who want NCL's newest hardware with the most water features, thrill rides, and the largest (as of 2026) Haven suite-ship-within-a-ship in the fleet.

Norwegian Aqua is NCL's flagship and the largest (as of 2026) Prima Class ship, bigger than Norwegian Prima and Viva at 156,300 GT. She debuts the Aqua Slidecoaster water ride and an expanded Haven complex. Freestyle Cruising means no fixed dining times and no dress codes. If you want a similar experience on a proven ship, Norwegian Encore offers a comparable size with Breakaway Plus amenities.

Frequently Asked Questions About Norwegian Aqua

What dining options are available on Norwegian Aqua?

Norwegian Aqua offers complimentary dining including the main dining room and buffet. Specialty restaurants vary by ship and may require reservations or carry an additional charge. Check the dining section above for specific venues available on this ship.

How do I find the deck plans for Norwegian Aqua?

Deck plans are available through the links on this page. You can also find official deck plans on the Norwegian Cruise Line website or in the cruise planner app.

Where does Norwegian Aqua sail?

Norwegian Aqua debuts in 2025 with Caribbean cruises from Port Canaveral, offering convenient access to Orlando theme parks. As NCL's first Prima Plus-class vessel, she introduces new features and expanded capacity. Check the Norwegian Cruise Line website for inaugural sailings.

What makes Norwegian Aqua unique?

Norwegian Aqua is NCL's largest ship yet, introducing the first-ever water park (as of her 2025 debut) at sea called Aqua Park. Features include enhanced Prima Speedway, The Drop slide, new Aqua Lounge, expanded Indulge Food Hall with 16 venues, and larger Haven suite complex with exclusive sundeck.

How can I track Norwegian Aqua's current location?

Use the live ship tracker section on this page to see Norwegian Aqua's current position, speed, heading, and next port in real-time via MarineTraffic AIS integration. The tracker updates automatically once the ship enters service.
