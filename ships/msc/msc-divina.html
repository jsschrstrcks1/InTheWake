MSC Divina — Deck Plans, Live Tracker, Dining & Videos

MSC Divina is the third ship of MSC Cruises' Fantasia Class, delivered on 19 May 2012 at Saint-Nazaire by STX France (the yard now operates under its historic name, Chantiers de l'Atlantique). She was christened on 26 May 2012 in Marseille by Sophia Loren. She measures 139,072 gross tons and 333.33 metres in length, carries 3,502 guests at double occupancy (4,202 maximum) across 1,751 cabins with a crew of 1,388, and flies the Panamanian flag from Colón. From 24 April 2026 she sails 7-night Eastern Mediterranean itineraries from Civitavecchia (Rome), then repositions to Miami in November 2026 for Caribbean and Bahamas cruises through spring 2027. She completed her most recent drydock at Palumbo Malta Shipyard (Valletta) on 9 May 2025.

Quick Answer: MSC Divina (139,072 GT, 3,502 double / 4,202 max, 1,388 crew, delivered 2012) is a Fantasia Class ship — christened by Sophia Loren in Marseille — carrying the MSC Yacht Club enclave on Decks 15-18, the Pantheon Theatre, the MSC Aurea Spa, and an Italian-themed dining programme across two MDRs (The Black Crab and Villa Rossa). She splits 2026 between Civitavecchia (Rome) for summer Eastern Mediterranean cruises and Miami for winter Caribbean and Bahamas round-trips.

Who She's For: Travellers who want classical Italian cruise styling — atriums, crystal, marble, and traditional multi-deck show lounges — rather than the high-tech LED-dome promenade feel of MSC's newer ships. Strong pick for Miami-based Caribbean cruisers who prefer a mid-sized ship over megaships, for European cruisers wanting a Civitavecchia departure in summer 2026, and for guests drawn to the MSC Yacht Club ship-within-a-ship enclave with butler service. Less of a fit for guests expecting the newest hardware (see the Meraviglia, Seaside EVO, or World class), elaborate waterpark rigs, or Broadway-scale production shows.

MSC Divina is the third of four Fantasia Class ships, built at Saint-Nazaire by STX France (yard now operating under the historic Chantiers de l'Atlantique name). Sister ships are MSC Fantasia, MSC Splendida, and MSC Preziosa. Divina's design leans into classical ocean-liner styling: a multi-deck atrium at Piazza del Doge, the Pantheon Theatre, a Casinò Veneziano, Galleria d'Arte, and the Sky & Stars Library on the upper sports deck. Dining is organised around two main dining rooms (The Black Crab and the panoramic Villa Rossa on Deck 6), with specialty venues Butcher's Cut, Galaxy Restaurant, and La Cantina di Bacco on Deck 7. The MSC Yacht Club occupies the forward top decks with Le Muse Restaurant, the Top Sail Lounge, and the Deck 18 One Bar, One Pool, and private solarium.

Entertainment & Activities on MSC Divina

Entertainment on MSC Divina centres on the Pantheon Theatre, a two-deck show lounge on Decks 6-7 hosting nightly production shows and MSC's multilingual guest acts. Late-night life runs through the Golden Jazz Bar, La Luna Piano Bar, and the Black & White Lounge on Deck 7, the Casinò Veneziano on Deck 6, and the Galaxy Disco on Deck 16 (which doubles as Galaxy Restaurant during dinner service). Upper sports decks add the 4D Cinema, MSC Formula Racer F1 simulator, the MSC Sports Arena, a Virtual World arcade, and a Sky & Stars Library. Deck 7 has a bowling alley, the Galleria d'Arte, and the Timeless Studio photo venue. Wellness lives on Deck 14 at the MSC Aurea Spa (thermal area, treatment rooms, and a hammam) and MSC Gym. Families have the Doremiland kids' area, the I Puffi and Junior Club programmes, and the I Graffiti teen club on Deck 15, all run by MSC's multilingual youth staff.

Key Facts About MSC Divina

MSC Divina Deck Plans

MSC Divina's deck plan covers 18 decks (13 passenger). Deck 5 holds the Divina Bar and the lower level of The Black Crab MDR. Deck 6 adds the Villa Rossa panoramic MDR, Piazza del Doge atrium, Casinò Veneziano, Silver Lounge, and the Pantheon Theatre lower level. Deck 7 is the main dining/entertainment axis with Butcher's Cut, La Cantina di Bacco, Galleria d'Arte, bowling, the Caffè Italia, Black & White Lounge, Golden Jazz Bar, La Luna Piano Bar, and Sports Bar. Decks 14-15 hold the Calumet and Manitou buffets, Aqua Park, Garden Pool, and MSC Aurea Spa. The MSC Yacht Club occupies Decks 15-18 with Le Muse Restaurant, Top Sail Lounge, the One Bar, One Pool, and private solarium. Galaxy Restaurant/Disco sits aft on Deck 16.

Frequently Asked Questions About MSC Divina

What dining options are on MSC Divina?

MSC Divina has two complimentary main dining rooms — The Black Crab (Decks 5-6) and Villa Rossa (Deck 6, panoramic) — plus two separate buffets on Deck 14: Calumet Buffet and Manitou Buffet. Specialty (extra charge) venues include Butcher's Cut steakhouse, Galaxy Restaurant (which doubles as Galaxy Disco at night), and La Cantina di Bacco pizzeria and wine bar. Caffè Italia handles espresso and pastries. MSC Yacht Club guests have their own private Le Muse Restaurant on Deck 15 plus the Top Sail Lounge and the One Bar on Deck 18. See the Dining section above for the full list.

Which ships are in the same class as MSC Divina?

MSC Divina is one of four Fantasia Class ships together with MSC Fantasia (2008), MSC Splendida (2009), and MSC Preziosa (2013). The class was built by STX France at Saint-Nazaire (the yard now operates under its historic name, Chantiers de l'Atlantique). Divina and Preziosa are sometimes described as a modified or stretched sub-group, but MSC and Cruise Industry News treat all four ships as a single Fantasia class.

Where does MSC Divina sail in 2026-2027?

MSC Divina operates a split season in 2026. From 24 April 2026 she is based in Civitavecchia (Rome) for 7-night Eastern Mediterranean itineraries calling at Naples, Mykonos, Kuşadası (Ephesus), Marmaris, and Syros, continuing through October. In November 2026 she repositions back to Miami for Caribbean and Bahamas round-trips through spring 2027. Check the live tracker above for her current position and next port.

How big is MSC Divina compared to the rest of the MSC fleet?

At 139,072 gross tons and 333.33 metres long, MSC Divina is a mid-to-large MSC ship — larger than the Lirica or Musica class but smaller than the Meraviglia, World, or Seaside EVO ships. She carries 3,502 guests at double occupancy (4,202 maximum) across 1,751 cabins with a crew of 1,388. Fantasia Class ships predate MSC's later indoor-promenade designs and use a more traditional multi-deck atrium layout centred on Piazza del Doge.

Who christened MSC Divina?

MSC Divina was christened on 26 May 2012 in Marseille by Sophia Loren. Loren is MSC Cruises' long-standing godmother and has christened multiple MSC ships across the fleet. Divina was laid down as "MSC Fantastica" and renamed during construction. She is registered in Panama (Colón) and carries IMO number 9585285.
