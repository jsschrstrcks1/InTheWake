A Cruise Traveler’s Logbook

Important

Photograph your travel documents. Keep images of passports, IDs, visas, and insurance cards on your phone (and a cloud backup). If wallets go missing or you need embassy help, this speeds everything up.

Tips

1) Plan around flights and liquid rules

Know your airline’s bag sizes/weights and pre‑weigh at home. When flying, liquids/gels are limited to 3.4oz/100ml in a single quart bag. Put full‑size toiletries in checked luggage or buy them at embarkation.

2) Pack a smart carry‑on

Checked bags can arrive late on embarkation day. Keep meds, documents, valuables, swimwear, a change of clothes, and chargers with you.

3) Edit your checked luggage

Lay everything out, then remove 20%. Share across suitcases—if one is lost, everyone still has clothes. Roll items to save space and reduce creases.

4) Know dress codes

Most lines lean resort‑casual; premium/luxury can be stricter. Check your line’s guidance and pack one versatile dressy outfit just in case.

5) Use packing aids

Packing cubes keep categories together; compression bags shrink bulk (great for hoodies and kids’ items).

6) Laundry options

Some ships have self‑service laundries (usually paid); all offer paid cleaning. A few sink‑wash items dry quickly with clips or magnetic hooks.

7) Toiletries reality check

Mainstream lines often stock basic soap/shampoo only. Bring lotion, conditioner, face wash, and any preferred hair products.

8) Pack for destination & activities

Review your ports for weather and cultural norms. Modest clothes are helpful for religious sites; supportive shoes for long touring days.

9) Leave room for souvenirs

Tuck in a foldable duffel so return‑trip purchases have a ride home.

10) Re‑wear smartly

Repeat bottoms with different tops; use accessories to refresh looks without extra bulk.

11) Don’t forget the basics

Small items cost more on board: OTC meds, batteries, memory cards, sunscreen, ear plugs, and adapters.

12) Keep IDs with you

Never put passports or cruise docs in checked bags. Carry them and any required visas/immunization records.

13) Prohibited items

Skip irons, candles/incense, weapons, drones (varies), and surge‑protected power strips—lines can confiscate banned items.

14) Photo your documents

Store photos of passports, IDs, cards, and insurance on your phone and a cloud drive for emergencies.

15) Emergency contacts

Print a one‑page list including your cruise line’s emergency number and ship name—keep a copy with each traveler.

16) PPE varies by port

Some destinations reinstate mask rules seasonally. Check port guidance a week before you sail.

17) Pack lighter than you think

You won’t see most shipmates again—prioritize comfort and re‑wearability. Most forgotten items are available on board or ashore.

18) Temperatures on board

Venues often hover around low‑70s °F (21–22°C). If you run cool, bring a light layer—especially for evenings in theaters and dining rooms.

For destination‑specific weather and dress considerations, visit Ports.

Notes

  • **Accessibility & medical**: Bring any mobility aids, sensory supports, CPAP supplies, spare parts, and doctor letters for medications/devices.
  • **Airline vs. cruise limits**: Airlines usually limit luggage more than cruise lines—pack to the strictest leg of your trip.
