First Look

Carnival Mardi Gras revolutionized cruising when she debuted in July 2021 as Carnival's first Excel-class vessel. At 180,000 GT, she introduced BOLT — the first roller coaster at sea — and established the template for the entire Excel-class fleet.

Named after the original Mardi Gras (1972) that launched Carnival Cruise Line, this ship brings New Orleans spirit to sea with the French Quarter zone, jazz music, and Creole cuisine. LNG propulsion makes her one of the most environmentally advanced cruise ships afloat.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is BOLT roller coaster?

BOLT: Ultimate Sea Coaster is the first roller coaster at sea, debuting on Carnival Mardi Gras in 2021. The electric-powered coaster reaches speeds up to 40 mph on a track above the ship's top deck. Riders control their speed through twists, dips, and drops.

Where does Carnival Mardi Gras sail from?

Carnival Mardi Gras is homeported at Port Canaveral, Florida year-round, offering 6-8 day cruises to Eastern Caribbean (San Juan, St. Thomas, St. Maarten) and Western Caribbean (Cozumel, Costa Maya) destinations.

What is the French Quarter zone?

French Quarter is a New Orleans-themed zone exclusive to Carnival Mardi Gras. It features Emeril's Bistro 1396 (Creole cuisine), the Brass Magnolia jazz bar, Fortune Teller Bar, and NOLA-inspired entertainment.

Who is the godmother of Carnival Mardi Gras?

TV personality Kathie Lee Gifford christened Carnival Mardi Gras in 2021. Her daughter, Cassidy Gifford, later became godmother of Carnival Celebration.

Why is the ship named Mardi Gras?

The name honors the original Mardi Gras (1972) — a converted transatlantic liner that became Carnival Cruise Line's first ship and launched the "Fun Ship" concept that transformed the cruise industry.

Is Carnival Mardi Gras the largest Carnival ship?

At 180,000 GT, Mardi Gras was Carnival's largest ship when launched. She's now slightly smaller than Celebration and Jubilee (183,521 GT) but remains the Excel-class pioneer with the original BOLT coaster.
