Ship History

Carnival Sensation was a 70,367 gross ton cruise ship that served the Carnival Cruise Line fleet for 29 years. Built in 1993 at Kværner Masa-Yards in Helsinki, Finland, she was the third of eight Fantasy-class ships and became known for her creative, over-the-top public spaces designed by legendary cruise ship architect Joe Farcus.

The ship featured a dramatic seven-deck-high neon-splashed atrium that exemplified Farcus' "entertainment architecture" philosophy — designs so theatrical they would make Las Vegas blush. Sensation's imaginative interiors helped define Carnival's "Fun Ships" identity throughout the 1990s and 2000s.

In an unusual move, Carnival enlisted four distinguished female executives — Vicki Freed, Cherie Weinstein, Roberta Jacoby, and Gerry Donnelly — to serve as co-godmothers for the ship's christening ceremony.

Frequently Asked Questions

What happened to Carnival Sensation?

Carnival Sensation was retired in February 2022 after 29 years of service. The ship was sold for $11 million and sailed to Aliağa, Turkey in March 2022, where she was scrapped at the ship-breaking yard.

When was Carnival Sensation built?

Carnival Sensation was built in 1993 at Kværner Masa-Yards in Helsinki, Finland. She was the third Fantasy-class ship to join the Carnival fleet, following Carnival Fantasy (1990) and Carnival Ecstasy (1991).

Who were Carnival Sensation's godmothers?

Carnival Sensation had four co-godmothers — Vicki Freed, Cherie Weinstein, Roberta Jacoby, and Gerry Donnelly — all distinguished female Carnival executives at the time of her christening.

What was special about Carnival Sensation's design?

Designed by legendary cruise architect Joe Farcus, Carnival Sensation featured a dramatic seven-deck-high neon-splashed atrium that exemplified his "entertainment architecture" philosophy. The ship was known for its creative, imaginative public spaces that helped define Carnival's "Fun Ships" identity.

Which Fantasy-class ships are still sailing?

As of 2025, only Carnival Elation and Carnival Paradise remain active from the original eight Fantasy-class ships. The other six ships (Fantasy, Ecstasy, Sensation, Imagination, Inspiration, and Fascination) have all been retired and scrapped.
