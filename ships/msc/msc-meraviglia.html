MSC Meraviglia — Deck Plans, Live Tracker, Dining & Videos

MSC Meraviglia is the first ship of MSC Cruises' Meraviglia Class, delivered on 31 May 2017 at Saint-Nazaire by STX France (the yard now operating as Chantiers de l'Atlantique) and christened in Le Havre on 3 June 2017 by Sophia Loren. She measures 171,598 gross tons and 315.83 metres in length, carries 4,488 guests at double occupancy (5,700 maximum per MSC) across 2,244 cabins with a crew of 1,536, and flies the Maltese flag from Valletta. She was the first MSC ship to host Cirque du Soleil at Sea, with performances in the purpose-built 410-seat Carousel Lounge on Deck 7. From April 2023 until April 2026 she was exclusively homeported in New York, becoming the largest cruise ship ever to call at Manhattan. From winter 2026-27 onwards she becomes MSC's first year-round Miami ship.

Quick Answer: MSC Meraviglia (171,598 GT, 4,488 double / 5,700 max, 1,536 crew, delivered 2017) is the first-in-class Meraviglia Class ship — sister to MSC Bellissima, and the original home of Cirque du Soleil at Sea. She carries four main dining rooms (Waves, L'Olive Doree, L'Olivo d'Oro, Panorama), the Galleria Meraviglia LED-sky promenade, the 410-seat Carousel Lounge for Cirque shows, the Broadway Theatre, and the MSC Yacht Club enclave. Western Mediterranean summer 2026, then Miami year-round from winter 2026-27.

Who She's For: Families and entertainment lovers drawn to Cirque du Soleil at Sea — Meraviglia is where the MSC-Cirque partnership launched and the Carousel Lounge on Deck 7 is the original venue. Strong pick for travellers wanting a Galleria-era Meraviglia experience at a slightly smaller scale than the Meraviglia-Plus ships (Grandiosa, Virtuosa, Euribia). Good for North American cruisers eyeing her year-round Miami deployment from winter 2026-27 onwards. The MSC Yacht Club enclave makes her compelling for guests wanting a premium cocoon inside a large ship. Not for guests looking for the newest MSC hardware (see the World class), a smaller quieter ship, or the Meraviglia-Plus signature venues like L'Atelier Bistrot (which are not on Meraviglia).

MSC Meraviglia is the first of two base Meraviglia Class ships together with MSC Bellissima (2019). She launched MSC's indoor-promenade concept — the Galleria Meraviglia on Decks 6-7 runs under a two-deck LED sky ceiling that cycles through atmospheric scenes. She also introduced Cirque du Soleil at Sea to the cruise industry in 2017; the exclusive Cirque shows perform in the purpose-built Carousel Lounge on Deck 7. MSC later built three larger Meraviglia-Plus ships — MSC Grandiosa (2019), MSC Virtuosa (2021), and MSC Euribia (2023) — based on the same platform but with different dining and venue configurations. Dining on Meraviglia runs across four MDRs (Waves, L'Olive Doree, L'Olivo d'Oro, Panorama), the Marketplace Buffet, and specialty venues Butcher's Cut, Kaito Sushi Bar, Kaito Teppanyaki, Hola! Tacos & Cantina, and Ocean Cay seafood restaurant. Note that Ocean Cay on Meraviglia is a Deck 6 specialty seafood restaurant named after — but distinct from — the MSC Ocean Cay Marine Reserve private island in the Bahamas.

Entertainment & Activities on MSC Meraviglia

Entertainment on MSC Meraviglia is centred on the Carousel Lounge — the purpose-built 410-seat Cirque du Soleil at Sea venue on Deck 7, and the first Cirque at Sea stage in the cruise industry. Exclusive Cirque productions have been performed here since her 2017 delivery. The Broadway Theatre runs MSC's nightly production shows and guest acts. The signature Galleria Meraviglia (Decks 6-7) is a two-deck indoor promenade crowned by an 80-metre LED sky ceiling, flanked by the Meraviglia Bar & Lounge, Edge Cocktail Bar, Champagne Bar, Brass Anchor Pub, TV Studio & Bar, and the Jean-Philippe cafés. The Casino Imperiale on Deck 7 handles gaming. Deck 18 adds the Attic Club, Sky Lounge, and Horizon Bar. Deck 19 houses the aquapark with the Polar Bar, and the MSC Yacht Club private pool and sundeck. Wellness lives at the MSC Aurea Spa. Families have MSC's multilingual youth programme for toddlers through teens.

Key Facts About MSC Meraviglia

MSC Meraviglia Deck Plans

MSC Meraviglia occupies 18 decks (15 passenger-accessible, 11 with cabins). Deck 5 holds the Waves Restaurant MDR and Infinity Bar at the atrium. Deck 6 is the main dining and Galleria axis with L'Olive Doree, L'Olivo d'Oro, and Panorama Restaurant MDRs, Ocean Cay seafood specialty, Jean-Philippe Chocolat & Café, Jean-Philippe Crêpes & Gelato Bar, the Meraviglia Bar & Lounge, and the Edge Cocktail Bar. Deck 7 is the entertainment and specialty axis with Butcher's Cut, the Kaito pair (Sushi Bar and Teppanyaki), the Brass Anchor Pub, Champagne Bar, TV Studio & Bar, Casino Imperiale, and the purpose-built Carousel Lounge (Cirque du Soleil at Sea). Deck 15 carries the Marketplace Buffet and pool deck with the Atmosphere Pool Bars and Bamboo Bar. Deck 16 has the Sports Bar and the MSC Yacht Club Top Sail Lounge. Deck 18 adds the Sky Lounge, Attic Club, Horizon Bar, and the MSC Yacht Club Restaurant. Deck 19 has the aquapark Polar Bar and the Yacht Club private pool, sundeck, and Yacht Club Sundeck Bar & Grill.

Frequently Asked Questions About MSC Meraviglia

What dining options are on MSC Meraviglia?

MSC Meraviglia has four main dining rooms — Waves Restaurant (Deck 5), L'Olive Doree, L'Olivo d'Oro, and the panoramic Panorama Restaurant (all Deck 6) — plus the Marketplace Buffet on Deck 15. Specialty (extra-charge) venues include Butcher's Cut steakhouse, Kaito Sushi Bar, Kaito Teppanyaki, Hola! Tacos & Cantina (Meraviglia uses the "Tacos & Cantina" branding like Bellissima, NOT the "Tapas Bar" branding on Grandiosa), and Ocean Cay seafood restaurant on Deck 6 (named after but distinct from MSC's Ocean Cay MSC Marine Reserve private island in the Bahamas). Sweets come from Jean-Philippe Chocolat & Café and Jean-Philippe Crêpes & Gelato. MSC Yacht Club guests have their own Yacht Club Restaurant on Deck 18. MSC Meraviglia does NOT carry L'Atelier Bistrot — that French bistro is only on the Meraviglia-Plus ships.

Which ships are in the same class as MSC Meraviglia?

MSC Meraviglia is the first-in-class Meraviglia Class ship, with MSC Bellissima (2019) as her only direct sister. The separate Meraviglia-Plus sub-class comprises MSC Grandiosa (2019), MSC Virtuosa (2021), and MSC Euribia (2023) — slightly larger hulls at 181,541 GT and 184,011 GT. All five ships share the signature two-deck Galleria indoor promenade with the LED sky ceiling. All were built by Chantiers de l'Atlantique at Saint-Nazaire (Meraviglia was delivered under the STX France name in 2017 before the yard reverted to its historic name in 2018).

Does MSC Meraviglia have Cirque du Soleil at Sea?

Yes — she was the original home of Cirque du Soleil at Sea when she entered service in June 2017. Exclusive Cirque productions perform in the purpose-built Carousel Lounge on Deck 7, a 410-seat venue with a custom 270° stage. Cirque du Soleil at Sea is carried on MSC Meraviglia, MSC Bellissima, MSC Grandiosa, and MSC Virtuosa — but NOT on MSC Euribia, which has a Carousel Lounge but is not Cirque-equipped.

Where does MSC Meraviglia sail in 2026-2027?

From April 2023 until April 2026 MSC Meraviglia was exclusively homeported in New York, becoming the largest cruise ship ever to port in Manhattan. In April 2026 she repositions across the Atlantic for a Western Mediterranean summer 2026 season visiting Livorno, Naples, Palermo, Tunis, Barcelona, and Marseille. From winter 2026-27 onwards she transitions to year-round service from Miami — MSC's first year-round Miami ship, announced August 2025 — running Bermuda (6-night King's Wharf overnights), Bahamas/Caribbean (7-8 night to Nassau and Ocean Cay MSC Marine Reserve), and Canada/New England (10-11 night) itineraries. Check the live tracker above for her current position.

Who christened MSC Meraviglia?

MSC Meraviglia was christened on 3 June 2017 in Le Havre, France by Sophia Loren — MSC Cruises' long-standing godmother who has christened every MSC ship since MSC Lirica in 2003. She was delivered at Saint-Nazaire on 31 May 2017 by STX France and completed her maiden voyage on 4 June 2017. She is registered in Valletta, Malta and carries IMO number 9760512.
