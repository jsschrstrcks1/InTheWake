Jamaica: My Reggae & Waterfall Day

Sailing into Falmouth's historic Georgian port feels like stepping back in time, but I'm here for the waterfalls and jerk. My perfect day: off the ship and straight to Dunn's River Falls with a private driver (surprisingly affordable for up to 6 people the whole day) – climbing the 600-foot waterfall hand-in-hand with strangers is pure joy.

Then the Blue Hole (way less crowded, higher jumps, Tarzan swing – my favorite!). Lunch at Scotchies for the best jerk chicken and festival I've ever had – spicy, smoky perfection. Afternoon chilling at a beach club or river tubing on the White River. One love, mon.

The Moment That Stays With Me: Jumping off the 20-foot rock at Blue Hole into electric-blue water while everyone cheered – felt like a superhero.

Getting Around Jamaica

Falmouth dock – walkable historic town. Taxis to Dunn's River are reasonably priced. Private driver for the day is the way to go.

  • Dunn's River Falls: 45-min drive from Falmouth
  • Blue Hole: Near Ocho Rios, combine with Dunn's River
  • Scotchies: Locations in Ocho Rios and Montego Bay

Positively Worded Word of Warning

The legendary Jamaican hospitality and "soon come" timing are the island's way of teaching you to slow down and enjoy the moment – irie vibes only!

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Dunn's or Blue Hole?
A: Both – Blue Hole is more fun, Dunn's is iconic.

Q: Safe?
A: Stick with licensed drivers/tours and yes.

Q: Best jerk?
A: Scotchies in Montego Bay or Ocho Rios – no debate.
