La Scala Theatre

Royal Caribbean — Entertainment Venue

Quick Answer: La Scala Theatre is the main entertainment venue on Voyager Class ships, named after the legendary Milan opera house. Features Broadway-style productions, variety shows, comedians, and live performances. All shows are complimentary with open seating. The elegant multi-level theater seats approximately 900 guests.

Best For: Show lovers, families, couples seeking elegant evening entertainment, and anyone appreciating classic theater ambiance.

Key Facts

  • Ship Class: Voyager Class
  • Capacity: ~900 seats
  • Cost: Complimentary
  • Reservations: Not required
  • Location: Forward, Decks 3-4

Classic elegance meets cruise ship entertainment.

Special Accommodations

Accessibility Notes: La Scala Theatre offers wheelchair-accessible seating locations. Assistive listening devices available at Guest Services. The theater has elevator access to multiple levels. Contact Royal Caribbean's Access Department for specific accommodation requests.

Where You'll Find It

La Scala Theatre is located at the forward end of Voyager Class ships, spanning Decks 3 and 4. The elegant entrance opens onto the Royal Promenade area.

Tips for Best Experience

  • Early Arrival: 20 minutes before for best seat selection
  • Center Orchestra: Premium sightlines
  • Balcony Level: Great views, often less crowded
  • Second Show: Usually easier to get good seats

4 ★ out of 5 Navigator of the Seas • Caribbean Cruise

La Scala Theatre Review: Classic Entertainment Venue

Introduction. La Scala Theatre on Navigator has a classic elegance that sets the mood for evening entertainment. We attended shows every night and appreciated both the venue and the performances.

The Theater

Beautiful design with rich colors and classic theater styling. The name is appropriate—it feels like a scaled-down opera house. Sightlines were good from our orchestra seats about halfway back. Sound was clear throughout.

The Shows

We saw the main production show twice (early and late dining), a comedian, and a variety act. The production was well-done with talented performers. The comedian had the audience in tears laughing. Solid entertainment all around.

The Experience

Making La Scala part of our evening routine added structure to our nights. Dinner, then a show, then drinks at a nearby bar. The theater's location near the Royal Promenade made it easy to grab a cocktail before curtain.

Conclusion

La Scala provides quality entertainment in an elegant setting. The shows may not be quite as elaborate as on newer mega-ships, but the intimate atmosphere has its own appeal. Rating: 4/5.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is it called La Scala?

The theater is named after Teatro alla Scala in Milan, Italy, one of the world's most famous opera houses, reflecting the venue's elegant, classical design.

Are reservations required?

No, seating is first-come, first-served for all shows. Arrive 15-20 minutes early for the best seat selection.

What's the dress code?

No strict dress code. Most guests wear casual to smart casual attire. On formal nights, you'll see more dressed-up guests.

How many shows are there per night?

Typically two showtimes per evening—an early show and a late show—to accommodate both dining seatings.

Can I bring drinks from the bar?

Yes, you're welcome to bring drinks into the theater. Bars are located nearby for pre-show cocktails.
