MSC Virtuosa — Deck Plans, Live Tracker, Dining & Videos

MSC Virtuosa is the second and final ship of MSC Cruises' Meraviglia-Plus Class. Her keel was laid on 27 February 2019 at Chantiers de l'Atlantique in Saint-Nazaire (hull H34), she was floated out on 1 December 2019, and — after a COVID-19 delivery delay from 8 November 2020 — she was handed over on 1 February 2021. She measures 181,541 gross tons and 331.43 metres in length, carries 4,810 guests at double occupancy (6,334 maximum) across 2,421 cabins with a crew of 1,704, and flies the Maltese flag from Valletta. She made a phased debut: UK-only domestic 'Seacations' from Southampton in May 2021, followed by a formal naming ceremony on 27 November 2021 at Port Rashid, Dubai by Sophia Loren — her 17th MSC ship christening. Her direct sister is MSC Grandiosa (2019). She entered her first statutory dry dock at Damen Verolme Rotterdam on 27 October 2025, departed 19 November 2025, and repositioned transatlantically for her 2025-26 Caribbean winter season.

Quick Answer: MSC Virtuosa (181,541 GT, 4,810 double / 6,334 max, 1,704 crew, delivered 2021) is the second and final Meraviglia-Plus Class ship — direct sister to MSC Grandiosa. She carries four complimentary main dining rooms (Blue Danube, The Opera, The Simphony, and the pan-Asian Indochine Restaurant — unique to Virtuosa among the class), Le Grand Théâtre, the Carousel Lounge (now hosting MSC's in-house Carousel Productions at Sea), the signature MSC Starship Club on Deck 6 with Rob the humanoid robot bartender, the LED-sky Galleria Virtuosa promenade, the glass-floored Himalayan Bridge, the Savannah Aquapark, and a full MSC Yacht Club enclave with 95 suites. Caribbean winter 2025-26; Northern Europe / Mediterranean summer 2026.

Who She's For: UK and Northern European cruisers in summer (Southampton-based fly-and-cruise crowd), Caribbean cruisers in winter, families who will use the Savannah Aquapark and the Himalayan Bridge, tech and design enthusiasts drawn to the MSC Starship Club with Rob the robot bartender, and suite travellers looking for a full 95-suite MSC Yacht Club on a 2021-build hull. Guests who enjoy the Meraviglia-class 'Galleria' LED-sky promenade format. Not for cruisers expecting Cirque du Soleil at Sea — the MSC × Cirque du Soleil at Sea partnership has been phased out; the Carousel Lounge now hosts MSC's in-house Carousel Productions at Sea instead.

MSC Virtuosa is the second and final ship of the Meraviglia-Plus Class, together with her direct sister MSC Grandiosa (2019). The Meraviglia-Plus class is an enlarged evolution of the original Meraviglia Class (MSC Meraviglia 2017 and MSC Bellissima 2019), at 181,541 GT vs 171,598 GT. Virtuosa was built at Chantiers de l'Atlantique in Saint-Nazaire as hull H34, with her keel laid on 27 February 2019 and a float-out on 1 December 2019. Her delivery was originally scheduled for 8 November 2020 but was delayed by COVID-19 to 1 February 2021. She made an unusual phased debut: she entered service with UK-only domestic 'Seacations' from Southampton in May 2021 (because international cruising was still restricted), and her formal naming ceremony didn't happen until 27 November 2021 at Port Rashid, Dubai, during the UAE Golden Jubilee celebrations — with Sophia Loren as godmother (her 17th MSC ship christening) and a traditional champagne-bottle smash and fireworks. Her defining interior features are the MSC Starship Club on Deck 6 with Rob the humanoid robot bartender (the first at sea), the signature class-wide LED-ceiling Galleria Virtuosa indoor promenade on Decks 6-7, the glass-floored Himalayan Bridge skywalk on Deck 19, and the Savannah Aquapark waterpark. Her four complimentary main dining rooms — Blue Danube, The Opera, The Simphony (MSC's spelling), and Indochine Restaurant — make her the only Meraviglia-generation ship with a dedicated pan-Asian MDR. She entered her first statutory dry dock at Damen Verolme Rotterdam on 27 October 2025, departed 19 November 2025, and began her 2025-26 Caribbean winter season from Pointe-à-Pitre (Guadeloupe). As of 11 April 2026 she is mid-Atlantic on a 14-night transatlantic repositioning voyage from Pointe-à-Pitre to Europe via the Azores, for her summer 2026 Northern Europe and Mediterranean season.

Entertainment & Activities on MSC Virtuosa

Entertainment on MSC Virtuosa is built around Le Grand Théâtre (Decks 5-6) for main production shows, and the two-level Carousel Lounge on Deck 7 — originally built for the MSC × Cirque du Soleil at Sea partnership and now hosting MSC's in-house Carousel Productions at Sea shows after the Cirque partnership was phased out. The class-signature Galleria Virtuosa (Decks 6-7) is the LED-ceiling indoor promenade that runs through the heart of the ship. The most distinctive bar is the MSC Starship Club on Deck 6, featuring Rob, the cruise industry's first humanoid robot bartender, alongside 3D holographic projections and a digital art wall. Other bars and lounges include the glass-domed Infinity Bar (Deck 5 atrium), the Virtuosa Bar & Lounge, Minuetto, L'Atelier Bar & Art (Deck 6), the Champagne Bar, Masters of the Sea pub, TV Studio & Bar, and Red Gem Casino Bar (Deck 7), plus the Sky Lounge and Attic Club on Deck 18. Deck 16 is the active/arcade hub with the Hologate Arena, Interactive XD Cinema, MSC Formula Racer, Virtual Games Arcade, Bowling, Sportplex, and the MSC Gym powered by Technogym. Deck 19 tops the ship with the Himalayan Bridge glass walkway, the Savannah Aquapark, and the private MSC Yacht Club Pool, Grill & Bar, and Top 19 Exclusive Solarium. The Red Gem Casino is on Deck 7. Families have the class-wide Doremiland kids' club cluster on Deck 18 (Baby Club Chicco, Mini Club LEGO, Junior Club LEGO, Young Club, Teen Club, MSC Teen Lab, Web Creator Room). Note: MSC Virtuosa does NOT carry Cirque du Soleil at Sea — the partnership has been phased out.

MSC Virtuosa Deck Plans

MSC Virtuosa's 19-deck layout concentrates dining and entertainment on the lower decks and recreation on the upper decks. Deck 5 holds the Infinity Atrium with Infinity Bar, the Blue Danube Restaurant main dining room, and the lower Le Grand Théâtre. Deck 6 adds The Opera, The Simphony, and Indochine restaurants, the MSC Starship Club with Rob the robot bartender, Virtuosa Bar & Lounge, Minuetto, L'Atelier Bar & Art, HOLA! Tacos & Cantina, Jean-Philippe Chocolat & Café, Jean-Philippe Crêpes & Gelato, the Emotions Immersive Gallery, and the lower Galleria Virtuosa. Deck 7 is the specialty-dining and entertainment spine with Butcher's Cut, Kaito Sushi Bar, Kaito Teppanyaki, Il Campo Restaurant, MSC Aurea Spa, Carousel Lounge, Champagne Bar, Masters of the Sea pub, TV Studio & Bar, Red Gem Casino, and the upper Galleria Virtuosa. Deck 15 is the main pool deck with the Marketplace Buffet, Marketplace Sea Pavilion by Jereme Leung, Atmosphere Pool, Tropical Pool, and outdoor Atmosphere and Tropical Bars. Deck 16 is the active zone with the MSC Gym powered by Technogym, Hologate Arena, Interactive XD Cinema, MSC Formula Racer, Virtual Games Arcade, Bowling, Sportplex, Sports Bar, Horizon Pool, and the aft Horizon Amphitheatre. Deck 18 adds the Sky Lounge, Horizon Bar, Attic Club, and the MSC Yacht Club Restaurant plus Doremiland kids' clubs. Deck 19 tops the ship with the Savannah Aquapark, Savannah Bar, Himalayan Bridge glass walkway, Top 19 Exclusive Solarium, and the private MSC Yacht Club Pool, Sundeck & Bar, and Grill & Bar.

Frequently Asked Questions About MSC Virtuosa

What dining options are on MSC Virtuosa?

MSC Virtuosa has four complimentary main dining rooms — Blue Danube Restaurant (Deck 5), The Opera Restaurant (Deck 6), The Simphony Restaurant (Deck 6 — MSC's spelling), and the pan-Asian Indochine Restaurant (Deck 6, unique to Virtuosa among the Meraviglia generation) — plus the Marketplace Buffet on Deck 15 and the Marketplace Sea Pavilion by Jereme Leung family buffet. Specialty dining on Deck 7 includes Butcher's Cut, Kaito Sushi Bar, Kaito Teppanyaki, and Il Campo (Italian). Deck 6 adds HOLA! Tacos & Cantina (the 'Tacos & Cantina' variant, matching Meraviglia and Bellissima; sister Grandiosa uses the 'Tapas Bar' variant) and the Jean-Philippe Chocolat & Café and Crêpes & Gelato venues by chef Jean-Philippe Maury. Yacht Club suite guests have the private MSC Yacht Club Restaurant on Deck 18 and the MSC Yacht Club Grill & Bar on Deck 19.

Does MSC Virtuosa still have Cirque du Soleil at Sea?

No. The MSC × Cirque du Soleil at Sea partnership — which once ran bespoke shows on four Meraviglia-generation ships — has been phased out. The Carousel Lounge on MSC Virtuosa (Deck 7) was originally built for the Cirque du Soleil at Sea partnership and now hosts MSC's in-house Carousel Productions at Sea shows. MSC's main production shows on Virtuosa take place in Le Grand Théâtre on Decks 5-6, with secondary shows in the Carousel Lounge. If an older itinerary page or third-party reference still lists Cirque du Soleil for MSC Virtuosa, it reflects pre-phase-out marketing.

Who is Rob the robot bartender?

Rob is a humanoid robot bartender working at the MSC Starship Club on Deck 6 of MSC Virtuosa — the cruise industry's first humanoid robot bartender. The MSC Starship Club features Rob alongside 3D holographic projections, a digital art wall, and eight-language guest interaction. Rob accepts cocktail orders via touchscreen or voice and shakes and pours drinks on demand. The venue is unique to MSC Virtuosa in the current MSC Cruises fleet.

Does MSC Virtuosa have the MSC Yacht Club?

Yes. MSC Virtuosa has carried a full MSC Yacht Club ship-within-a-ship enclave from her 2021 delivery, spanning Decks 16/18/19. The enclave contains 95 Yacht Club suites (including a two-deck Royal Suite), the private Top Sail Lounge on Deck 16, the MSC Yacht Club Restaurant on Deck 18, the MSC Yacht Club Grill & Bar and Sundeck & Bar on Deck 19, and a private MSC Yacht Club Pool on Deck 19. Yacht Club suite guests have access to the Top 19 Exclusive Solarium and 24-hour private concierge service.

Where does MSC Virtuosa sail in 2026?

Winter 2025-26 MSC Virtuosa ran 7-night Southern Caribbean loops from Pointe-à-Pitre, Guadeloupe (Saint Vincent, Grenada, Saint Lucia, Saint Maarten, Antigua, Saint Kitts). She departed Pointe-à-Pitre on 5 April 2026 on a 14-night eastbound transatlantic repositioning voyage bound for Europe via Ponta Delgada in the Azores (expected arrival 12 April 2026). Summer 2026 she sails Northern Europe from Southampton, Le Havre, and Hamburg plus Mediterranean itineraries from Barcelona, Marseille, and Genoa. Her first statutory dry dock was 27 October – 19 November 2025 at Damen Verolme Rotterdam for routine class maintenance.
