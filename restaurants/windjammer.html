Windjammer Marketplace

Royal Caribbean — Complimentary Buffet

The fleet’s go-to casual spot from sunrise to late evening: global stations, carve-to-order favorites, fresh salads, desserts galore — all included.

Windjammer (a.k.a. “WJ”) is the everyday crowd-pleaser with rotating themes that mirror the Main Dining Room — think French, Italian, Caribbean, and more — plus breakfast classics and kid-friendly staples. Vegan & Indian Options

Vegan: Daily salad/grain bowls, roasted veg, bean dishes, dairy-free desserts; look for the vegetarian/vegan station and tagged labels.

Indian: Many ships offer an evening Indian corner with rotating vegetarian curries (chana masala, dal, aloo gobi) and rice; non-veg options appear on some nights.

Pro tip: Note preferences in the Royal App pre-cruise or speak with the Windjammer supervisor on Day 1 for nightly guidance.

4 ★ out of 5 Icon of the Seas • Sep 2025 • Breakfast, Lunch & Themed Dinners (Asian, Mexican) Price: Complimentary • Drinks packages ~$10–15/day for sodas/juices

Sailed aboard Icon of the Seas.

Royal Caribbean Windjammer Buffet Review 2025: Vibrant Casual Dining with Endless Variety

Introduction. The Windjammer Buffet is a lively, hassle-free haven for casual cruisers—wall-to-wall global flavors, ocean panoramas, and zero reservations. It’s abundance without the wait or the wallet hit: a customizable feast any time of day. I visited the Windjammer on Icon of the Seas during a 7-night Western Caribbean sailing in September 2025—breakfast around 8 AM, lunch at noon, and theme-night dinners (Asian and Mexican) near 7 PM. Core food is entirely complimentary; optional soda/juice packages run about $10–15/day for unlimited refills.

Food

Windjammer excels at variety and freshness. Breakfast started strong at the made-to-order omelet station—mine packed with spinach, mushrooms, peppers, and cheese—plus crisp bacon and blueberry pancakes with maple syrup and berries. Lunch meant DIY at the salad bar (a bright Greek bowl with feta and tangy vinaigrette) and a creamy fettuccine Alfredo from the pasta station, optionally topped with grilled chicken.

On Asian Night, the stir-fry station mixed noodles with broccoli, carrots, tofu, and a soy-ginger sauce with real umami punch, while a coconut-kissed veg curry paired perfectly with basmati rice. Mexican Night brought crowd-pleasers—build-your-own tacos, verde enchiladas, and churros. Desserts rotated; the mango sticky rice was a tropical standout.

Peak times can mean a lukewarm tray or two; the trick is wandering a bit—less busy stations hide gems like fresh fruit platters or vegan-friendly samosas. Clear labels make it easy to spot allergens, vegetarian, and vegan options. From kid-friendly burgers to seafood features on select nights, Windjammer’s breadth makes it a reliable 2025 buffet winner.

Service

For a self-serve venue, service is impressively efficient and personable. Chefs at interactive stations (omelets, carving) were friendly pros—on an American classics feature, the prime-rib carver nailed a juicy medium-rare slice and chatted about the herb rub. Attendants kept tables turning, pointed out fresh batches, and were proactive about gluten-free guidance—one staffer walked me to rice-noodle and veggie options and suggested pairings. It’s fast, helpful, and keeps your day moving.

Atmosphere / Ambiance

Think bright, ocean-view marketplace: floor-to-ceiling windows, nautical blues, and an easygoing soundtrack. Mornings feel sunny and energetic; evenings mellow into a cozy glow. Yes, peaks are lively, but seating turns quickly, and quieter corners (or al fresco spots) reward a short stroll. It’s ideal for embarkation-day lunches, quick bites between pool time, and late-night snacks.

Conclusion

Bottom line: Windjammer wins on flexibility, variety, and value—a complimentary, all-day flavor tour with something for everyone. Minor rush-hour hiccups just add to the exploratory fun. Rating: 4/5. Perfect for families, first-timers, and anyone craving global eats without fuss. Pro tip: go slightly off-peak for shortest lines—and snap the colorful stir-fries and dessert spreads for your album.
