Her Legacy

Carnival Fantasy wasn't just a cruise ship ‚Äî she was a revolution. When she debuted in 1990, her soaring glass-topped atrium changed expectations for cruise ship interiors forever. That single design innovation influenced every cruise ship built since.

As the lead ship of the Fantasy Class, she proved that mid-sized ships could deliver big experiences. Her success led to seven sister ships and established the template for Carnival's "Fun Ship" philosophy that continues today.

For 30 years, Carnival Fantasy introduced hundreds of thousands of first-time cruisers to the joy of cruising. Her final departure from Miami on July 15, 2020 marked the end of an era ‚Äî but her influence on modern cruise ship design lives on.

Frequently Asked Questions

When was Carnival Fantasy scrapped?

Carnival Fantasy was scrapped in July 2020. She left Miami on July 15, 2020, arrived at the Aliaga Ship Recycling Facility in Turkey on July 29, 2020, and dismantling began in late August 2020.

Why was Carnival Fantasy special?

Carnival Fantasy was the first cruise ship to feature a tall glass-topped atrium, revolutionizing cruise ship interior design. As the lead ship of the Fantasy Class, she set the template for eight successful sister ships and defined the "Fun Ship" concept.

How long did Carnival Fantasy operate?

Carnival Fantasy operated for exactly 30 years, from her maiden voyage on March 1, 1990 to her final voyage in March 2020 when cruise operations were suspended due to COVID-19.

Why was she sold for scrap?

The COVID-19 pandemic accelerated Carnival Corporation's fleet modernization plans. As the oldest ship in the fleet at 30 years, Carnival Fantasy was prioritized for retirement along with Carnival Inspiration to reduce costs during the industry shutdown.
