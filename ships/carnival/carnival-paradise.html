Overview

Historic Significance: Carnival Paradise made history in 1998 as the world's first completely non-smoking cruise ship, launching with a strict no-tobacco policy supported by anti-smoking and cancer-prevention groups.

Carnival Paradise is the 8th and final Fantasy-class cruise ship, delivered in 1998. As one of only two remaining Fantasy-class vessels still in service (alongside Carnival Elation), she represents the classic, intimate Carnival Fun Ship experience that launched an era of affordable cruising.

Built at Kværner Masa-Yards in Helsinki, Finland and christened by broadcast journalist Paula Zahn, Carnival Paradise serves Tampa with short Caribbean getaways. Originally named simply "Paradise," she received the "Carnival" prefix in 2007.

Ship Timeline

January 1998

Floated out at Kværner Masa-Yards Helsinki New Shipyard on January 29.

1998

Christened by godmother Paula Zahn as "Paradise." Launched as the world's first completely non-smoking cruise ship with strict $250 fine policy.

1998-2004

Sailed 7-day Caribbean cruises from Miami as the line's smoke-free option.

2004

Repositioned to Long Beach, California for 3-4 day cruises to Mexico and Catalina Island.

2007

Renamed "Carnival Paradise" along with all Fantasy-class sisters receiving the Carnival prefix.

2011

Repositioned to Tampa, Florida, replacing Carnival Inspiration on short Caribbean cruises.

2020

Operations suspended due to COVID-19. Six Fantasy-class sisters sold for scrap, leaving only Paradise and Elation.

2025

Continues sailing short Caribbean cruises from Tampa as one of only 2 remaining Fantasy-class ships.

Frequently Asked Questions

What class is Carnival Paradise?

Carnival Paradise is the 8th and final Fantasy-class ship, built in 1998. She is one of only 2 remaining Fantasy-class ships still in service, along with Carnival Elation.

Where does Carnival Paradise sail from?

Carnival Paradise is homeported in Tampa, Florida, offering short Caribbean cruises to destinations like Cozumel, Mexico and other Western Caribbean ports.

What made Carnival Paradise historically significant?

Carnival Paradise was the world's first completely non-smoking cruise ship when launched in 1998. The strict policy initially banned all smoking materials, with fines for violations.

How many Fantasy-class ships remain in service?

Only 2 of the original 8 Fantasy-class ships remain active: Carnival Paradise and Carnival Elation. The other 6 were sold or scrapped between 2020-2022.

Who was Carnival Paradise's godmother?

Carnival Paradise was christened by Paula Zahn, the acclaimed American broadcast journalist and news anchor, in 1998.
