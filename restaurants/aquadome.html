AquaDome

Royal Caribbean — Ship Neighborhood

Quick Answer: AquaDome is Icon Class's stunning glass-enclosed venue at the bow of the ship. By day, it houses the largest pool at sea with panoramic ocean views and a cascading waterfall. By night, it transforms into an entertainment venue hosting spectacular aquatic and aerial shows. Includes AquaDome Market for casual dining. All access is complimentary.

Best For: Everyone—daytime swimmers, ocean view seekers, show enthusiasts, casual diners, and anyone who appreciates architectural innovation at sea.

Key Facts

  • Location: Forward, Icon Class ships
  • Daytime: Largest pool at sea
  • Evening: Spectacular shows
  • Dining: AquaDome Market (casual)
  • Cost: Complimentary access

A transforming wonderland at the bow of the ship.

AquaDome represents Royal Caribbean's most ambitious venue to date. The glass dome structure provides unobstructed views from bow to horizon while housing multiple experiences. The pool and waterfall create a resort atmosphere by day; at night, the pool transforms into a stage for shows that combine diving, aerial acrobatics, and water effects in ways never before seen at sea. Special Accommodations

Accessibility Notes: AquaDome is wheelchair accessible with ramp access to viewing areas. Pool lifts may be available—check with Guest Services. Designated accessible seating for shows. AquaDome Market accommodates dietary restrictions with advance notice.

Where You'll Find It

AquaDome occupies the forward section of Icon Class ships, spanning multiple decks. The distinctive glass dome structure is visible from the ship's exterior and provides 220-degree ocean views from inside.

5 ★ out of 5 Icon of the Seas • Anniversary Cruise

AquaDome Review: The Ship's Crown Jewel

Introduction. AquaDome is why we chose Icon of the Seas. After seeing photos and videos, we had to experience it ourselves. It did not disappoint—this venue alone makes the ship special.

The Space

Walking into AquaDome for the first time is jaw-dropping. The glass dome lets in natural light while providing panoramic ocean views. The waterfall cascading multiple stories is mesmerizing. The pool is large enough to actually swim, not just soak.

The Shows

The evening shows are unlike anything we've seen at sea. The pool transforms, platforms rise, performers dive from incredible heights, aerial artists fly overhead, and water fountains dance to music. It's Cirque-level production. We saw every performance and each was slightly different.

The Dining

AquaDome Market was our go-to for casual lunch. Multiple stations, plenty of free options, and we could eat while looking at the ocean through the dome. Convenient and pleasant.

Conclusion

AquaDome is the heart of Icon of the Seas. Whether you're there for morning coffee, afternoon swimming, or evening shows, it delivers. The architectural achievement alone is worth the trip. Rating: 5/5.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is AquaDome free?

Yes, the pool, viewing areas, and shows are all complimentary. AquaDome Market has both free and premium dining options.

Do I need reservations for shows?

No reservations needed. Seating is open—arrive 20-30 minutes early for the best viewing spots.

Can I swim during shows?

No, the pool area transforms into the performance space. Swimming hours end before evening shows begin.

Is the pool heated?

Yes, the AquaDome pool is heated and the climate-controlled dome makes it comfortable regardless of weather.

What's the best spot for watching shows?

The upper levels provide good overview perspectives. Floor-level spots near the pool offer immersive close-up views. Both are excellent.
