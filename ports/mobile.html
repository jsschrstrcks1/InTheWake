Mobile

Mobile: Alabama's Historic Cruise Port

Mobile offers Gulf Coast cruisers a charming alternative to larger ports. Alabama's oldest city brings Southern hospitality, historic architecture, and the birthplace of Mardi Gras to your pre-cruise experience. Carnival sails from here to the Western Caribbean.

The cruise terminal sits on the Mobile River downtown, walking distance from the historic district. It's an easy drive from across the Southeast, making it popular with cruisers from Alabama, Mississippi, and the Florida Panhandle.

Southern Charm: Mobile's Mardi Gras is actually older than New Orleans' celebration. Visit during February for parades, or explore the year-round Mardi Gras Park and museum.

Getting to the Port

  • Address: 201 S Water Street, Mobile, AL
  • From Mobile Airport (MOB): 20 minutes
  • From Pensacola (PNS): 1 hour via I-10
  • From New Orleans: 2.5 hours via I-10
  • From Birmingham: 4 hours via I-65

Parking Options

  • On-site parking: Available at the cruise terminal
  • Cost: Reasonable rates for the region
  • Reserve in advance: Through your cruise line booking

Nearby Hotels

  • Renaissance Mobile Riverview Plaza: Connected to convention center
  • Battle House Renaissance: Historic luxury hotel
  • Hampton Inn Downtown Historic District: Good value, great location
  • Malaga Inn: Charming boutique in historic townhouses

Pre-Cruise Activities

  • USS Alabama Battleship Memorial Park: WWII battleship and submarine tours
  • Bellingrath Gardens: 65 acres of stunning gardens
  • Mobile Carnival Museum: Learn about America's original Mardi Gras
  • Historic District: Oak-lined streets and antebellum architecture
  • GulfQuest Maritime Museum: Interactive maritime history

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What cruise line sails from Mobile?
A: Carnival operates sailings from Mobile to the Western Caribbean.

Q: Is Mobile worth a pre-cruise day?
A: Yes! The historic district, USS Alabama, and Southern cuisine make it worth arriving early.

Q: How far is Mobile Airport from the cruise terminal?
A: Mobile Regional Airport (MOB) is about 20 minutes from the cruise terminal at 201 S Water Street.

Q: What are the best pre-cruise activities in Mobile?
A: Top activities include visiting the USS Alabama Battleship Memorial Park, exploring Bellingrath Gardens, touring the Mobile Carnival Museum, and walking the historic district with its oak-lined streets and antebellum architecture.
