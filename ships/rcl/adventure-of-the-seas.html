A First Look

Why Book Adventure of the Seas?

Imagine drifting into a world where every sunrise promises a fresh adventure, and the ocean’s rhythm syncs perfectly with your own. That’s the quiet magic of Adventure of the Seas, Royal Caribbean’s Voyager-class gem—classic in design, vibrant in experience, and easy to explore. With ocean views at every turn, live entertainment, and a crew that remembers your name, it’s a ship that feels personal yet packed with fun.

Families thrive here: kids conquer the Rock Climbing Wall, teens dominate the arcade, and everyone reunites for poolside movies under the stars. Couples savor slow sunsets and relaxed dinners—whether it’s a gourmet feast at Chops Grille or a quiet drink in the Solarium. First-timers and solo travelers love the manageable size, offering big-ship thrills without the overwhelm.

Dining is global, from fresh bites at Windjammer to sushi at Izumi, with the Main Dining Room as the evening’s centerpiece. Nights come alive with shows like City of Dreams, comedians, and live music in the lounges. It’s Royal’s signature blend of daytime energy and nighttime calm, perfect for value-seeking adventurers.

Sailings from ports like New Jersey or Galveston offer sunny Caribbean stops—Labadee, San Juan, or St. Maarten—plus sea days for recharging. In a world of mega-ships, Adventure shines with its balance of intimacy and excitement, delivering memorable moments with a crew that makes you feel at home.

A Note from Ken’s Logbook — A Personal Review

We sailed Adventure of the Seas in an ocean-view stateroom—bright with stunning sunrise views, a perfect morning start. Dining stood out: Chops Grille offered a melt-in-your-mouth filet, and the Main Dining Room impressed with its pacing and staff recommendations. The crew was exceptional—Maria at Chops provided warm, attentive service; Victor at Guest Services handled requests swiftly; and in Windjammer, Juan anticipated our coffee needs, a small touch that elevated the trip.

We enjoyed Taste of Royal: the tuna tartare was fresh, shrimp could use more spice, but the fried cheesecake was a delight. (Tip: Add a lemon twist to the Saint Peter’s spritzer for extra zing.) Our Western Caribbean itinerary included Cozumel, Grand Cayman, and Labadee. Snorkeling in Cozumel was a highlight, Grand Cayman’s Stingray City was unforgettable, and Labadee’s beach day was relaxing despite weather shifts. Local vendors were persistent, but a polite “no” worked wonders.

Embarkation in Bayonne was smooth, though parking was tricky—off-port options saved money. Evenings featured City of Dreams and live jazz in the lounge, both memorable. Adventure blends Royal’s big-ship fun with a personal touch, thanks to its caring crew.
