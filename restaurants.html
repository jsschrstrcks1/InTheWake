Frequently Asked Questions

What dining is included on Royal Caribbean cruises?

Royal Caribbean includes the Main Dining Room (multi-course dinner with rotating menus), Windjammer Marketplace (buffet), and various casual venues like Cafe Promenade, Sorrento's Pizza, and El Loco Fresh. Room service is also included for most items.

The Deluxe Beverage Package does NOT include specialty dining — those are separate charges ranging from $35-$185 per person.

How much do specialty restaurants cost on Royal Caribbean?

Specialty restaurant prices vary: Chops Grille (steakhouse) costs $65-72, 150 Central Park runs $85-95, Chef's Table is $185 for a multi-course experience, Izumi (sushi/hibachi) is $45-55, and Giovanni's Italian ranges $30-45.

Many cruisers pre-book one special dinner and stick to included venues otherwise. Prices occasionally increase, so check current rates when booking.

Do I need reservations for Royal Caribbean restaurants?

For specialty restaurants, yes — book through the Royal Caribbean app or Cruise Planner before sailing for best availability, especially for popular times like formal nights and sea days.

The Main Dining Room doesn't require reservations if you chose "My Time Dining" (flexible), but Traditional Dining has set times. Casual venues like Windjammer are first-come, first-served.

Which Royal Caribbean restaurants are best for families with kids?

For families: Johnny Rockets (burgers, shakes, 50s diner fun), Playmakers Sports Bar (arcade games, casual food), and the Windjammer (buffet variety for picky eaters) work well. The Main Dining Room accommodates kids with a children's menu.

Avoid Chef's Table and 150 Central Park with young children — these are intimate, lengthy dining experiences better suited for couples.

What bars are included with the drink package on Royal Caribbean?

The Deluxe Beverage Package covers drinks at all bars including Schooner Bar, Boleros, Playmakers, Pool Bar, and most lounges. Bionic Bar (robot bartenders) is included but may have wait times.

NOT included: Starbucks specialty drinks (only brewed coffee), some premium wines/spirits above the $14 limit, and mini-bar items. The package pays off if you drink 5-6 beverages daily.
