MSC Euribia — Deck Plans, Live Tracker, Dining & Videos

MSC Euribia is the third ship of MSC Cruises' Meraviglia-Plus sub-class, delivered on 31 May 2023 at Saint-Nazaire by Chantiers de l'Atlantique and christened in Copenhagen on 8 June 2023 by Sophia Loren. She measures 184,011 gross tons and 331 metres in length, carries 4,828 guests at double occupancy (6,327 maximum) across 2,419 cabins with a crew of approximately 1,711, and flies the Maltese flag from Valletta. She is the second LNG-powered ship in the MSC fleet, after MSC World Europa, and is named after the Greek sea goddess Eurybia.

Key Facts

  • Delivered: 31 May 2023, Saint-Nazaire
  • Godmother: Sophia Loren (8 June 2023, Copenhagen)

Quick Answer: MSC Euribia (184,011 GT, 4,828 double / 6,327 max, ~1,711 crew, delivered 2023) is the third Meraviglia-Plus ship — MSC's second LNG-powered vessel — sisters with MSC Grandiosa and MSC Virtuosa. Signature features include the 82-metre Himalayan Bridge on Deck 19, the Galleria Euribia LED-sky promenade, a 959-seat Delphi Theatre, four main dining rooms, and the MSC Yacht Club ship-within-a-ship enclave. Named after the Greek sea goddess Eurybia and christened by Sophia Loren in Copenhagen. Her 2026 Middle East winter season was cancelled due to regional conflict; she is repositioning to Northern Europe and the Mediterranean.

Who She's For: Families and entertainment lovers drawn to MSC's newest Meraviglia-Plus hardware with an environmental angle — LNG propulsion, bio-LNG demonstration heritage, and the Cádiz LNG-bunkering milestone. Strong pick for travellers wanting Norwegian Fjord or Baltic itineraries from Kiel and Copenhagen once her 2026 European season restarts. The 82-metre Himalayan Bridge and Ocean Cay Aquapark on Deck 19 appeal to active families; the four MDRs and Kaito trio (Sushi Bar, Teppanyaki, Robatayaki) give dining rotation through a full week. Not for guests expecting Cirque du Soleil at Sea (Euribia does not carry it) or Butcher's Cut (the steakhouse role here is filled by Le Grill).

MSC Euribia shares the Meraviglia-Plus platform with MSC Grandiosa (2019) and MSC Virtuosa (2021). Euribia's hull carries a distinctive painted ocean-themed artwork that wraps both sides of the ship and references the marine environment. Dining rotates across four main dining rooms (Aurora Boreale and Aurora Borealis on Deck 6, Colorado River on Deck 6, and Green Orchid on Deck 5), with Il Campo as a fifth main restaurant on Deck 7, plus the Marketplace Buffet on Deck 15 and specialty venues Le Grill, the Kaito trio (Sushi Bar, Teppanyaki, Robatayaki), and Hola! Tacos & Cantina. The signature Galleria Euribia promenade on Decks 6-7 is topped by an LED sky ceiling; the 959-seat Delphi Theatre handles production shows; the Himalayan Bridge and Ocean Cay Aquapark occupy Deck 19. MSC Euribia is not part of the World class — that is only MSC World Europa (2022) and MSC World America (2025).

Entertainment & Activities on MSC Euribia

Entertainment on MSC Euribia is anchored by the 959-seat Delphi Theatre spanning Decks 5-6, with nightly production shows from MSC's in-house creative team. The signature Galleria Euribia (Decks 6-7) is an indoor promenade with an LED sky ceiling that cycles through atmospheric scenes throughout the day, flanked by the Carousel Lounge, Helios Wine Maker, Euribia Bar & Lounge, Jean-Philippe Chocolat & Café, Jean-Philippe Crêpes & Gelato, and L'Atelier du Voyageur. The MSC Signature Casino sits on Deck 7 beside Masters of the Sea pub. Deck 19 carries the dramatic 82-metre Himalayan Bridge suspension bridge — a feature unique to Euribia in the Meraviglia-Plus sub-class — plus the Ocean Cay Aquapark (named after MSC's Bahamian private island). The Sportplex on Deck 16 houses bowling, basketball, the HoloGate Blitz VR arena, an XD Cinema, and an MSC Formula Racer F1 simulator. The aft Horizon Amphitheatre is an outdoor performance space on Decks 16-18. Note: MSC Euribia does NOT carry Cirque du Soleil at Sea — that programme is limited to MSC Meraviglia, Bellissima, Grandiosa, and Virtuosa. Families have MSC's multilingual youth programme covering toddlers through teens.

Key Facts About MSC Euribia

MSC Euribia Deck Plans

MSC Euribia occupies 19 decks (15 passenger) with 2,419 cabins. Deck 5 holds the Green Orchid MDR, Infinity Atrium, and the lower Delphi Theatre. Deck 6 is the main dining and Galleria axis — Aurora Boreale, Aurora Borealis, Colorado River MDRs, Le Grill, Hola! Tacos & Cantina, the Jean-Philippe cafés, L'Atelier du Voyageur, and the Euribia Bar & Lounge all live here. Deck 7 adds Il Campo, the Kaito trio (Sushi Bar, Teppanyaki, Robatayaki), Champagne Bar, Carousel Lounge, Masters of the Sea, the MSC Signature Casino, and TV Studio & Bar. Deck 15 carries the Marketplace Buffet and outdoor bars. Deck 16 has the Sports Bar, Sportplex, MSC Aurea Spa and Gym. The MSC Yacht Club ship-within-a-ship occupies Decks 16-19 with its Top Sail Lounge, Yacht Club Restaurant on Deck 18, Grill & Bar and private sundeck on Deck 19. Deck 19 also carries the 82-metre Himalayan Bridge, Ocean Cay Aquapark, and Ocean Cay Bar.

Frequently Asked Questions About MSC Euribia

What dining options are on MSC Euribia?

MSC Euribia has four main dining rooms — Aurora Boreale and Aurora Borealis (paired on Deck 6), Colorado River (Deck 6), and Green Orchid (Deck 5) — plus Il Campo as a fifth main restaurant on Deck 7, and the Marketplace Buffet on Deck 15. Specialty (extra-charge) venues include Le Grill steakhouse, Kaito Sushi Bar, Kaito Teppanyaki, Kaito Robatayaki, and Hola! Tacos & Cantina. Sweets and coffee come from Jean-Philippe Chocolat & Café, Jean-Philippe Crêpes & Gelato, and the Atmosphere Ice Cream Bar. Note: MSC Euribia does not carry Butcher's Cut — the steakhouse role on this ship is filled by Le Grill. See the Dining section above for the full list.

Which ships are in the same class as MSC Euribia?

MSC Euribia is the third ship of the Meraviglia-Plus sub-class, together with MSC Grandiosa (delivered 2019) and MSC Virtuosa (delivered 2021). All three were built by Chantiers de l'Atlantique at Saint-Nazaire. The base Meraviglia class is MSC Meraviglia (2017) and MSC Bellissima (2019). MSC Euribia is not part of the World class — that is only MSC World Europa (2022) and MSC World America (2025), and they are a larger separate hull.

Is MSC Euribia LNG-powered?

Yes. MSC Euribia is LNG (liquefied natural gas) powered and is the second LNG ship in the MSC fleet, after MSC World Europa which entered service in November 2022. She was the first cruise ship to bunker LNG at the Spanish port of Cádiz, and she operated a bio-LNG net-zero demonstration cruise from Saint-Nazaire to Copenhagen in June 2023 as MSC's first attempt at a net-zero greenhouse-gas cruise voyage. Her name comes from the Greek sea goddess Eurybia, chosen to reflect MSC's environmental focus.

Where does MSC Euribia sail in 2026?

MSC Euribia's 2026 schedule was significantly disrupted. She was operating a winter 2025-26 Arabian Gulf season from Dubai, Doha, and Abu Dhabi — her first Middle East deployment — but MSC cancelled the planned 2026-27 Middle East winter season due to regional conflict. Her 2 May 2026 European season-opening departure from Kiel was also cancelled. She was scheduled to reposition from Dubai to Northern Europe as a passenger-free crossing in April 2026, with a rescheduled Northern Europe programme (Norwegian Fjords from Kiel and Copenhagen) and a Mediterranean deployment from October 2026 onwards. Check the live tracker above for her current position.

Who christened MSC Euribia, and does she have Cirque du Soleil?

MSC Euribia was christened on 8 June 2023 in Copenhagen by Sophia Loren — her 19th MSC ship naming as MSC Cruises' long-standing godmother. The ceremony was hosted by Danish model Sarah Grünewald and featured the Royal Danish Ballet and Tony Hadley. MSC Euribia does not carry Cirque du Soleil at Sea — that programme is only on MSC Meraviglia, Bellissima, Grandiosa, and Virtuosa. Euribia's entertainment is produced in-house in the 959-seat Delphi Theatre. She is registered in Valletta, Malta and carries IMO 9901544.
