Seward

Seward: My Best Wildlife Day

We docked in Resurrection Bay with snow-capped mountains all around and bald eagles circling the mast. The air smelled of salt, glacier silt, and coffee from the harbor cafés. Seward is small, walkable, and proud. Founded in 1903 when John Ballaine brought the steamer Santa Ana into this deep, ice-free bay, the town was named for William H. Seward — the Secretary of State who negotiated the Alaska Purchase from Russia in 1867.

We did the 6-hour Kenai Fjords tour with Major Marine and it was the single best wildlife day of my life. Within 30 minutes we had orcas — a transient pod hunting Dall's porpoise. Then humpbacks lunge-feeding, puffins flying like confused bumblebees, stellar sea lions by the hundreds on Chiswell Islands, and harbor seals on ice. The captain nosed right up to Aialik Glacier and cut engines while it calved repeatedly — turquoise chunks exploding into the water. Had glacier margaritas (with 10,000-year-old ice chipped off a berg).

Back in town we visited the Alaska SeaLife Center — touched sea stars, watched puffins dive in the aviary, saw the resident Steller sea lion do flips for fish. Dinner was halibut cheeks at Ray's — buttery, sweet, better than lobster. Walking the waterfront afterward, I thought about how the 1964 Good Friday earthquake nearly destroyed this place — flattened docks, collapsed buildings, devastated economy — yet the town rebuilt itself as the gateway to Kenai Fjords National Park.

The pros: world-class wildlife viewing and charming small-town vibe.

The cons: weather can cause cancellations, and it's a long day if combining the full cruise with a transfer to Anchorage.

The Moment That Stays With Me: A pod of six orcas working together to separate a Dall's porpoise calf, the chase happening at full speed right beside the boat — raw, brutal, unforgettable nature.

Docking & Port Information

Ships dock at Seward Cruise Ship Terminal, located about 1 mile from historic downtown. The terminal is adjacent to the Small Boat Harbor and just a 5-minute walk from the train station. A new passenger dock became operational in 2024, improving accessibility and passenger flow.

Getting Around Seward

  • Free Shuttle: Yellow school buses run between the cruise terminal and downtown from 8am–6pm daily during summer season — you can't miss them.
  • Walking: Downtown is completely accessible on foot from the dock. It's about 1 mile, flat and scenic.
  • Taxi/Trolley: Available from the visitor center if you prefer not to walk.
  • Train: Alaska Railroad runs to Anchorage (2.5 hours) — one of the most scenic train rides in North America. The station is a 5-minute walk from the cruise dock.
  • Bus: USA Park Connection offers twice-daily service between Anchorage, Denali National Park, and Seward.
  • Pro tip: "Travel one direction by train and the other by bus" — you'll get the scenic views on the train and save time with the bus on the return leg.

Key Attractions

  • Kenai Fjords National Park: Seward is the gateway — this is why you're here.
  • Mt. Marathon: Dramatic 3,000-foot peak overlooking town, famous for the Fourth of July foot race (straight up and down — brutal and beloved).
  • Historic Downtown & Millionaire Row: Stroll the original homes of wealthy fishing and railroad barons along the waterfront.
  • Alaska SeaLife Center: $20 admission gets you up close with puffins, sea lions, octopuses, and rescued marine wildlife. Well worth it.

Top Excursions

  • Glacier & Wildlife Cruises: 6–8 hour tours into Resurrection Bay and Kenai Fjords with Kenai Fjords Tours or Major Marine. Expect orcas, humpbacks, sea lions, puffins, and tidewater glaciers calving into the sea. This is the single best excursion in Alaska.
  • Salmon Fishing Charters: Half- or full-day fishing for king, silver, or sockeye salmon.
  • Iditarod Sled-Dog Kennels: Visit working kennels and take a wheeled sled ride with champion mushers (summer version — no snow required).
  • Exit Glacier Tours: Hike to the face of a receding glacier in Kenai Fjords National Park. Accessible by road and shuttle.
  • $5/Day Shuttle: Hop-on, hop-off shuttle tours between the cruise dock and downtown attractions.

Depth Soundings Ashore

Practical tips before you step off the ship.

  • Currency: U.S. dollars. ATMs widely available in downtown Seward.
  • Tipping: Restaurants 15–20%, taxis 10–15%, bellhops $1–2 per bag.
  • Distance from Anchorage: Approximately 140 miles, 3.5 hours by car (or 2.5 hours by train).
  • WiFi: Starbucks at 1907 Seward Highway offers free WiFi.
  • Emergency: Dial 911 for police, fire, or medical emergencies.
  • Weather: Seas can be rough in the Gulf of Alaska — take Bonine or Dramamine if you're even slightly prone to seasickness. Layer up; it's cool even in summer.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is Seward worth it?
A: Best wildlife day of a lifetime.

Q: Best excursion?
A: 6–8 hour Kenai Fjords tour.

Q: How long is the boat tour?
A: 6–8.5 hours for the good ones.

Q: Can you walk from port?
A: Yes to everything.
