## Getting to the ship — and getting home

### Arrival: Friday recommended, Saturday required

**Friday, April 23 — fly in.** Same-day flights to a 3:30 PM all-aboard are risky. Pre-cruise hotel in Brickell, Downtown Miami, or Miami Beach gives you margin and lets you use Friday afternoon for South Beach, Wynwood, or Little Havana.

**Saturday, April 24 — embarkation.**

- **Terminal:** **MSC Miami Cruise Terminal** at PortMiami — the new MSC-built terminal that opened April 2025. *Different from where Royal Caribbean and Carnival board.* Confirm address with Tina.
- **MIA → MSC Terminal:** Uber/Lyft $30–45, 20–30 min. Taxi flat-rate $32.
- **FLL → MSC Terminal:** Uber/Lyft $60–90, 45–60 min. Cheaper if split with another group member.
- **If driving:** Terminal AA garage **$35/day** (~$245/week, firsthand May 2026 — verify); offsite Miami long-term lots $7–14/day with shuttle.
- **Embark window:** 11:30 AM – 3:30 PM. **All-aboard 4:00 PM.** Ship departs ~5:00 PM.
- **Online check-in via MSC for Me app** — complete it before arriving to skip a 20-minute queue.

### Day 8 debark and the trip home

- **The night before:** bags in the hall by ~11 PM with color-coded tag indicating your debark group.
- **6:00 – 8:00 AM:** Breakfast at Il Mercato or La Brasserie.
- **7:00 AM:** Customs clearance complete. Self-disembarkation begins.
- **7:30 – 10:00 AM:** Walk-off in waves. All passengers off by 10:00 AM.

**Carry-on with you:** change of clothes for the journey home, all medications, passport, phone charger, valuables — anything you can't lose if a checked bag is misrouted.

**Getting from PortMiami to MIA.** Uber/Lyft on Saturday afternoon, **$30–45, 25–30 minutes**. The math just works; don't overthink it.

**Killing 4–6 hours before an evening flight:**

- **Bayfront Park / Bayside Marketplace** — 10-min walk from the terminal. Outdoor shopping/dining waterfront.
- **Pérez Art Museum Miami (PAMM)** — 15-min walk. $20 entry. Air-conditioned.
- **Brickell** — short Uber. Cuban coffee, lunch at a real restaurant, Uber to MIA when ready.

**Airport-arrival timing.**

| Flight | Leave PortMiami by |
|--------|---------------------|
| Domestic, after 4 PM | 1:30 PM |
| International, after 6 PM | 2:30 PM |

MIA TSA lines run 30–60 min during Saturday afternoon cruise return.

## Areas of interest during the cruise

### On the ship — World America spots worth knowing

- **The World Promenade** (Deck 8 aft) — the seven-deck outdoor walkway around the Y-shaped stern. Signature space. Bars and seating directly above the wake.
- **The World Galleria** (Decks 6–8 mid-ship) — three-deck atrium with an LED ceiling that changes scenes through the day. Orienting landmark if you get turned around.
- **Panorama Lounge** (above the Promenade, glass-walled Y-stern) — wake view from indoors; the right spot for a glass of wine after a port day.
- **Venom Drop @ The Spiral** — 11-deck dry slide; longest at sea. First hour after opening (typically 10 AM) has the shortest line.
- **Aurea Spa thermal suite** — sea-day intro rates appear; ~90 minutes is the right dose.
- **The Loft** — new comedy and karaoke lounge debuting on World America. Good group venue.
- **La Brasserie** (Deck 19) — 24/7 French-style casual. Crepes, fresh bread, lighter food. Quieter alternative to Il Mercato.
- **Masters of the Sea Pub** (Deck 7) — onboard microbrewery + pub food. Late-night fallback.

### Sea-day priorities (Day 2 + Day 6)

1. **Sunrise coffee at the World Promenade** — the Y-stern wake view, full daylight by 7 AM.
2. **Climb the Venom Drop first thing** — empty in the first hour.
3. **Eataly at lunch** if you can't get a dinner reservation — pasta-making class some days.
4. **Aurea Spa thermal suite** — sea-day rates only appear sea-day mornings.
5. **Port talk for tomorrow's destination** — MSC schedules briefings; worth attending if it's your first time in Roatán or Cozumel.

### Specialty dining picks

- **Eataly** (Deck 8) — the first Eataly at sea. Fresh pasta, wood-fired pizza, gelato. **The one to book on Day 1.**
- **Paxos** (Deck 8) — Greek seafood, open-air seating on the World Promenade.
- **Butcher's Cut** — steakhouse. Cagney's-equivalent for veterans of NCL.
- **Kaito Teppanyaki / Kaito Sushi Bar** — Japanese; the teppanyaki is the showy hibachi room.
- **HOLA! Tacos & Cantina** — modern Mexican.

### Port-day picks (one each)

- **Roatán** — West Bay Beach via taxi ($6–8/person each way) with offshore reef snorkeling. The day most cruisers come for.
- **Costa Maya** — the pool complex inside the gated port is free with your cruise card; or Chacchoben Mayan ruins (~$50 indep / ~$80 ship).
- **Cozumel** — Palancar Reef snorkel tour (~$50 indep / ~$95 ship). Two hours, calm water, the trip that converts non-snorkelers. *The* Western Caribbean experience.
- **Ocean Cay** — beach choice depends on crowd tolerance: South Beach (busy, has the Beach Shack), Bimini/Pineapple (calmer, west side), Ocean House Beach (longest walk from pier, least crowded).

### The lighthouse show at Ocean Cay

**Runs only on overnight or late-departure calls.** Check your itinerary. If your sailing has the show, **Lighthouse Bay** (east side, next to the tower) is the seating area. The show: 15 minutes, free, the tower lights in red-and-white bands, then blue, then climbing gold; projections climb the cladding; Spanish guitar carries through the speakers. The reason to want an overnight call.

### Photo spots worth knowing

- **World Promenade Deck 8 aft sailaway** — wake beneath the Y-stern, downtown Miami fading.
- **The Galleria LED ceiling** at sunset (scene changes 4:30–5:30 PM most days).
- **Roatán West Bay Beach** — white sand, turquoise water, reef offshore.
- **Cozumel Palancar Reef** from the snorkel boat — underwater camera worth it.
- **Ocean Cay striped lighthouse** at golden hour — Lighthouse Bay beach has the angle.
