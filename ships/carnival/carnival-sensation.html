Carnival Sensation featured classic Fantasy Class dining venues including the main dining room and buffet. She received Fun Ship 2.0 upgrades in 2017 including Guy's Burger Joint and BlueIguana Cantina. The ship offered traditional dining times and flexible casual dining options.

The Neon Cathedral

Carnival Sensation was Joe Farcus at his most theatrical. The legendary cruise ship designer created a seven-deck atrium that transformed light into spectacle — neon tubes climbing the walls, colors shifting through evening transitions, a space that felt more like art installation than cruise ship interior.

The third Fantasy Class ship launched in 1993, bringing Farcus' enhanced design philosophy to Miami. Where Fantasy proved the concept and Ecstasy refined it, Sensation elevated it into theater.

For 29 years, she served as Miami's reliable short-cruise option, delivering affordable Caribbean escapes while her neon atrium dazzled first-time cruisers and veterans alike.

Carnival Sensation was retired in 2022. Live tracking is no longer available.

Accessibility Notes

Sensation's Miami homeport made her one of the most accessible Fantasy Class ships geographically. Her neon atrium created natural navigation landmarks for guests with cognitive disabilities, while her intimate size kept distances manageable for wheelchair users.

Frequently Asked Questions About Carnival Sensation

When was Carnival Sensation retired?

Carnival Sensation was retired in 2022 after 29 years of service. She was scrapped at Aliaga, Turkey.

What made Carnival Sensation unique?

Sensation featured Joe Farcus' dramatic seven-deck neon-lit atrium, one of the most theatrical interior spaces in cruise ship history.

Where was Carnival Sensation homeported?

Sensation primarily sailed from Miami throughout her 29-year career, offering short Caribbean cruises.

How many ships were in the Fantasy Class?

Eight ships comprised the Fantasy Class: Fantasy, Ecstasy, Sensation, Fascination, Imagination, Inspiration, Elation, and Paradise. Only Elation and Paradise remain in service today.

What was Carnival Sensation's final year of service?

Carnival Sensation's service was affected by the COVID-19 pandemic in 2020, and she was ultimately retired and sold for scrap in 2022.
