Overview

Carnival Splendor is a 113,323 gross ton cruise ship and the only Concordia-class vessel in Carnival's fleet. Built in 2008 at Fincantieri's Sestri Ponente shipyard in Italy, Splendor was originally designed for Costa Cruises but transferred to Carnival during construction, giving her unique design elements not found on other Carnival ships.

The ship was christened by Myleene Klass, British musician and television presenter, on July 10, 2008, in Dover, England. The ceremony featured a memorable moment when a Royal Navy diver climbed five decks on a rope to break the champagne bottle by hand.

Notable for featuring the largest spa in the Carnival fleet—a 21,000 square foot, two-deck facility—Splendor was also the first Carnival ship to sail around Cape Horn when relocating from Europe to Long Beach in 2009. Since October 2019, she has been homeported in Sydney, Australia.

Frequently Asked Questions

What class is Carnival Splendor?

Carnival Splendor is a Concordia-class ship, the only one in Carnival's fleet. The ship was originally designed for Costa Cruises but transferred to Carnival during construction. Her sisters include Costa Serena, Costa Pacifica, Costa Favolosa, and Costa Fascinosa.

Where does Carnival Splendor sail from?

Carnival Splendor is homeported in Sydney, Australia since October 2019. She offers year-round cruises to South Pacific destinations, New Zealand, and Australian coastal ports. Previously she sailed from Long Beach, California (2009-2019).

Who is the godmother of Carnival Splendor?

Myleene Klass, British musician, singer, and television presenter, christened Carnival Splendor on July 10, 2008, in Dover, England. The ceremony featured a unique moment where a Royal Navy diver climbed five decks on a rope to break the champagne bottle by hand.

What makes Carnival Splendor unique?

Carnival Splendor features the largest spa in the Carnival fleet—a 21,000 square foot, two-deck facility with a winter garden and traditional tea house. She was also the first Carnival ship to sail around Cape Horn when relocating from Europe to Long Beach in 2009.

Is Carnival Splendor related to Costa Concordia?

Yes, Carnival Splendor and Costa Concordia were sister ships, both built to the Concordia-class design at Fincantieri shipyard. Splendor was originally ordered for Costa Cruises but transferred to Carnival during construction. Costa Concordia tragically capsized off the coast of Italy in January 2012.
