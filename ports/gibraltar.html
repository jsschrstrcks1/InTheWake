Prices, hours, menus, and policies listed on this page were accurate as of 2025-12-26 based on official sources and verified firsthand. These details are subject to change without notice. Always verify current information with venue operators before visiting.

From the Pier

  • Main Street
    Duty-free shopping, pubs, British red phone boxes — walkable from the port
    🚶 10 min
  • Cable Car to Upper Rock
    Barbary macaques, St Michael's Cave, WWII tunnels — cable car plus Rock access included
    🚡 6 min ride
  • St Michael's Cave
    Natural limestone cave — stalactites, concert venue, inside the Rock
    🚡 at the top
  • Europa Point
    Southernmost tip — Africa is 14 km across the Strait. Lighthouse, mosque, views of Morocco
    🚌 15 min
  • The Runway
    You may have to cross the airport runway to enter town — traffic lights stop for planes
    ✈ depends

Ships dock at the Western Arm or North Mole. Gibraltar is tiny — 6.7 sq km — and walkable. Taxi tours of the Upper Rock run about $30. Currency is Gibraltar Pound (at parity with GBP); Sterling accepted everywhere. Do not feed the macaques.

Quick Answer: Gibraltar is a British Overseas Territory since 1704, home to the legendary Rock—one of Hercules' Pillars—rising 1,400 ft above the Strait. Just 15km from Africa, it harbours 150+ caves including St. Michael's (300m high, the Greeks' "Gates to Hades"), Europe's only wild apes, WWII tunnels, and views spanning two continents.
