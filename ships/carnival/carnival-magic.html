Overview

Carnival Magic is a Dream-class cruise ship that made history when she debuted in 2011 as the first vessel to feature the SkyCourse—an innovative suspended ropes course high above the deck. Built by Fincantieri at Monfalcone, Italy, she entered service on May 1, 2011.

At 130,000 gross tons with capacity for 3,690 guests, Magic offers the full Dream-class experience with her revolutionary layout featuring open-air promenade decks and the spectacular Lanai outdoor dining area. Her unique lifeboat configuration with 18 double-sized boats differs from sister ship Carnival Dream.

Magic operates from Norfolk, Virginia, offering Caribbean and Bahamas itineraries that make her a favorite among East Coast cruisers seeking the full Fun Ship experience close to home.

Frequently Asked Questions

When was Carnival Magic built?

Carnival Magic was built by Fincantieri at their Monfalcone shipyard in Italy. Her keel was laid November 20, 2008, she was launched August 27, 2010, and entered service on May 1, 2011.

What class is Carnival Magic?

Carnival Magic is the second Dream-class ship, joining sister ships Carnival Dream (2009) and Carnival Breeze (2012). Costa Diadema is also a Dream-class vessel operating under Costa Cruises.

How big is Carnival Magic?

Carnival Magic measures 130,000 gross tons and is 1,004 feet (306 meters) long. She has capacity for 3,690 guests at double occupancy across 1,845 staterooms on 15 decks, with a crew of 1,386.

What is the SkyCourse on Carnival Magic?

The SkyCourse is a suspended ropes course located about 80 feet above the deck near the funnel. Carnival Magic was the first cruise ship to feature this adventure attraction, which has since been added to other Carnival vessels including Carnival Breeze.

Where does Carnival Magic sail from?

Carnival Magic operates from Norfolk, Virginia, offering Caribbean and Bahamas itineraries. Norfolk's location near the Chesapeake Bay makes Magic convenient for East Coast cruisers from Virginia, Maryland, and surrounding states.
