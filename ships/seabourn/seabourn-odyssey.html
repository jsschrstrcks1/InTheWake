Seabourn Odyssey

Seabourn Odyssey is a Odyssey Class cruise ship operated by Seabourn Cruise Line. She entered service in 2009, measures 32,346 gross tons, and carries approximately 458 guests at double occupancy.

Key Facts

  • Class: Odyssey Class
  • Year: 2009
  • Tonnage: 32,346 GT
  • Guests: 458
  • IMO: 9417086

Quick Answer: Seabourn Odyssey is the first of three Odyssey Class sisters — 32,346 GT, 458 guests, launched in 2009. She established the modern Seabourn template: all-suite, all-veranda, with open-seating dining in an intimate setting. This page covers deck plans, live tracking, dining venues, and videos.

Best For: Travelers who want Seabourn's most intimate ocean-going experience. At 458 guests, the Odyssey Class is significantly smaller than the Encore Class (604 guests), offering a quieter, more personal atmosphere with an exceptional crew-to-guest ratio.

Seabourn Odyssey established the blueprint that the Encore Class later scaled up. At 458 guests she delivers the smallest Seabourn ocean experience — fewer people around the pool, shorter lines at the restaurants, and a crew that learns your name quickly. She shares her layout with sisters Sojourn and Quest; the choice between them is purely about itinerary. If you want more public space and a larger ship, the Encore Class (Encore, Ovation) carries 604 guests at 40,350 GT. If you want expedition adventure, the 264-guest Expedition Class is a completely different product.

Key Facts

  • Ship Class: Odyssey Class — 32,346 GT, 458 guests, 330 crew
  • Signature Features: All-suite/all-veranda, Seabourn's most intimate ocean ships, open-seating dining
  • Itineraries: Mediterranean, Caribbean, Northern Europe, Asia
  • Entered Service: 2009
