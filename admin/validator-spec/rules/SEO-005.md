## Rule
A page should have exactly one `<title>` and one `<meta name="description">`. Duplicates are almost always template-merge accidents (two `<head>` blocks from different generators concatenated). No validator currently counts title/description duplicates — orphan. ICP-012 covers the ai-summary duplicate case; this is the standard-SEO equivalent.

## Why (rationale)
Duplicate titles confuse search engines (which one wins?), produce incorrect browser-tab labels, and signal that the page was assembled from parts without a single-pass integrity check.

## Fix guidance
Search the page source for `<title` (should appear exactly once) and `<meta name="description"` (exactly once). If duplicated, determine which is correct and remove the other. The correct one is usually the most specific and most recently written.
