Vision of the Seas

The Vision Class is Royal Caribbean at its most human scale — floor‑to‑ceiling glass, a sun‑splashed Solarium, and a sweeping, light‑filled Centrum that feels like an atrium hotel at sea. These ships trade mega‑park thrills for classic ocean time: coffee with a view, string‑quartet sunsets, and promenades that smell like salt and teak.

Days naturally bend around the sea — lazy lunches at Windjammer, unhurried dinners in the Main Dining Room, live music drifting from the lounges. If you want boutique‑hotel‑at‑sea energy and itinerary‑first cruising, this class is your sweet spot.

Launched in 1998 and built at Chantiers de l’Atlantique, Vision of the Seas is the namesake and finale of her class — compact enough to feel friendly, yet big on glassy views. A significant revitalization brought contemporary venues, tech upgrades, and refreshed lounges, keeping her quietly stylish for port‑intense itineraries.

Travelers love Vision’s laid‑back rhythm: a serene adults‑only Solarium, the soaring Viking Crown Lounge for late‑night panoramas, and a music‑first evening scene that feels like a city of cozy venues rather than one giant show. She’s a wonderful match for Bermuda getaways, Caribbean warm‑ups, and European summers when deployed there.

Why book Vision? Because you want the journey to be as restorative as the destinations — sunlit spaces, attentive service that still remembers your name, and evenings that end with ocean instead of spectacle.
