A Note from Ken’s Logbook — A Personal Review

Borrowed Winds — Full disclosure: I have not yet sailed Adventure of the Seas. Notes here are distilled from trusted sources and traveler reports.

Adventure of the Seas is a Voyager-class crowd-pleaser: big enough for FlowRider and ice shows, compact enough to feel navigable after day one. If you like active days and classic Royal vibes, she’s an easy ship to love.

The Crew and Staff

Voyager-class ships tend to shine on service: bar teams that remember your go-to, dining staff who steer you to the sleeper hits, and stateroom attendants who keep everything shipshape.

Shows and Entertainment

Expect an ice spectacular in Studio B, live music across lounges, and a main-theater production built around nostalgia and vocals. Evenings can be as chill or as “turn-it-up” as you want.

Dining

Main Dining Room and Windjammer cover the classics; Chops Grille remains the reliable splurge. El Loco Fresh, Giovanni’s (where present), and soft-serve by the pool round out fan favorites.

Embarkation & Disembarkation

Lines move quickly when you arrive in your assigned window; self-assist disembark gets you off early if you pack light.

The Real Talk

Adventure shows her age in spots (carpets, fixtures), and peak-time elevators can be busy. Chair saving happens; ask crew to enforce the posted limits if needed.

Accessible Seas on Adventure

  • Elevator access to all public decks
  • Pool lifts for mobility assistance
  • Accessible staterooms with roll-in showers
  • Priority tendering for shore excursions

A Female Crewmate’s Perspective

“Solo walks for sunrise coffee felt comfortable. Bring a light layer for chilly theaters. Chops stays a solid bet; service warmth often makes the night.”

Closing Thoughts

Adventure balances action and ease. If your ideal day mixes pool time, a FlowRider set, and a great steak at night—you’re her people.
