# W6.2 Partnership Outreach — Target List

**Status:** Internal — do not share with partners.
**Companion document:** `admin/media-kit.md` (external-facing).
**Plan reference:** v2.5 W6.2, locked decisions per `/root/.claude/plans/resilient-dancing-turtle.md`.

**Pacing target (locked in plan):** one direct outreach email per week beginning Day 30 of the v2.5 execution window. Goal: one signed partnership within 90 days. Pacing is deliberate — quality of fit beats volume.

**Bright lines, repeated for the file:** no cruise lines, no shore-excursion operators, no cruise-booking platforms, no display-ad networks, no medication-product partners, no anything that lands on the pastoral surface.

## Tier 1 — Travel insurance (highest fit)

The single highest-relevance category. FTC-compliant disclosure is industry-standard, brand-spend budgets are real, and the audience profile (45–70, research-mode, household decision-maker) is exactly who travel insurers target most carefully.

### Allianz Global Assistance (allianztravelinsurance.com)

- **Why fit:** Largest U.S. travel insurance brand by volume; well-resourced BD team; existing partnerships with cruise-content sites prove the model works at small scale.
- **Their pain point:** Reaching the 8–12-week-out planner who hasn't yet bought insurance. That window is exactly our audience.
- **Talking points:**
  - 21K Copilot citations / 90 days; Allianz buys reach via display, but AI-citation reach is a category they don't currently access.
  - Our `/internet-at-sea.html`, `/first-cruise.html`, and the new `/reaching-someone-at-sea.html` all touch insurance-relevant decision moments.
  - Editorial-firewalled comparison page or sponsored review is the model we'd discuss.
- **Likely contact:** Their PR / Brand Partnerships team via the partnerships email on their corporate site, not through the consumer help line. Goal is the BD team, not customer service.
- **Outreach window priority:** First. Highest probability of a real conversation.

### Travelex Insurance Services (travelexinsurance.com)

- **Why fit:** Mid-sized, highly cruise-aware (their product positioning leans into cruise + airline-disruption coverage). Smaller brand than Allianz, more flexible on the partnership side.
- **Talking points:**
  - Same 21K AI-citation framing as Allianz.
  - Travelex has historically partnered with niche travel publishers; we fit their pattern.
  - Could be a more aggressive editorial relationship (multi-page review, year-long sponsorship of a specific tool like the Drink Calculator landing block) than Allianz would entertain.
- **Outreach window priority:** Second. Likely a faster yes than Allianz, smaller dollars but easier path to a signed agreement.

### Travel Guard / AIG Travel Insurance (travelguard.com)

- **Why fit:** Large brand, less cruise-specific positioning than Travelex but solid baseline coverage. Probable BD interest.
- **Talking points:**
  - Same framing.
  - Their cruise-specific product (Cruise Care or similar) is the natural anchor.
- **Outreach window priority:** Third. Solid candidate; less urgent than the first two.

### IMG (imglobal.com)

- **Why fit:** Specializes in international medical insurance for travelers, including evac. Excellent fit for our caregiver and accessibility audiences.
- **Talking points:**
  - Our content discusses sea-to-shore evacuation costs ($30K–$80K) and international air ambulance ($150K–$300K) — directly inside IMG's product wheelhouse.
  - The grief / accessibility audience profile is age-skewed in IMG's favor.
- **Outreach window priority:** Fourth. Strong fit but smaller brand.

### World Nomads (worldnomads.com)

- **Why fit:** Adventure-traveler bias, but their cruise add-ons are real and they market hard at the research-mode audience.
- **Outreach window priority:** Fifth. Worth a conversation; weaker fit than the first four.

## Tier 2 — Multi-currency cards / fintech

High audience-intent overlap. Cruisers spend in multiple currencies; we answer this question often. Brands typically don't pay for affiliate placements (FX-fee comparisons are a saturated affiliate niche), but several have CPA programs or direct partnership budgets.

### Charles Schwab Bank (schwab.com — Investor Checking account)

- **Why fit:** Schwab's no-foreign-transaction-fee Investor Checking debit card is the single most-recommended product on cruise forums for FX management. Already known to our audience.
- **Talking points:**
  - Existing community awareness is high; the partnership opportunity is to formalize the recommendation as a sponsored review or to license placement on a specific page (not editorial).
  - Schwab does not run a public affiliate program for the checking product; partnership conversation would go through their corporate PR / brand team.
- **Outreach window priority:** Open. Lower probability of a yes than insurance, but worth one outreach.

### Wise (wise.com — Multi-Currency Account / debit card)

- **Why fit:** Strong brand recognition with travelers; well-funded marketing; willing to partner.
- **Talking points:**
  - Wise's marketing budget targets exactly the 8–12-week pre-trip planner; AI-citation reach is a wedge.
  - They run an existing affiliate / partner program — but a direct editorial partnership would unlock more value.
- **Outreach window priority:** Strong. Wise is BD-friendly and pays.

### Revolut (revolut.com)

- **Why fit:** Mid-tier fit. Good partner if Wise doesn't materialize.
- **Outreach window priority:** Lower.

## Tier 3 — Premium travel credit cards

Only fits if the editorial firewall is absolute (no commissioned placement inside ship or port pages). Better suited to a single dedicated review page rather than embedded recommendations.

### Chase Sapphire Reserve / Sapphire Preferred (chase.com)

- **Why fit:** The dominant travel-rewards card in the U.S.; well-known among cruisers; existing affiliate model.
- **Talking points:**
  - We won't run within-page commissioned recommendations on ship/port pages, but a dedicated `/articles/cruise-credit-cards-the-ones-we-actually-use.html` editorial review with disclosed affiliate links is feasible.
  - Chase will not deal direct; affiliate path is via CardRatings, Bankrate, or similar networks. That conflicts with our "no ad networks" stance.
- **Outreach window priority:** Skip unless a direct relationship can be opened. Network-mediated approach is off-mission.

### American Express Platinum / Gold (americanexpress.com)

- **Why fit:** Same logic as Chase; same network-mediated path issue.
- **Outreach window priority:** Skip for now.

### Capital One Venture X (capitalone.com)

- **Why fit:** Capital One has historically been more willing to do direct partnerships than Chase or Amex. Possible direct path.
- **Outreach window priority:** Open. Worth one outreach if the BD email is reachable.

## Tier 4 — Accessibility equipment (high-value, low-volume)

Strong content fit; smaller brand budgets but more direct decision-makers; conversation-to-signed-agreement cycle is faster.

### Special Needs at Sea (specialneedsatsea.com)

- **Why fit:** Cruise-specific accessibility-equipment rental — wheelchairs, scooters, oxygen equipment, etc. They deliver to the cabin. Our `/disability-at-sea.html` and accessibility-tagged ship/port content is exactly their audience.
- **Talking points:**
  - We don't run advertising on accessibility content as advertising — but we can write a clearly-labeled "practical resource" sponsored review that identifies who they are, what they do, what they charge, and the honest tradeoffs.
  - The Disabled Traveler persona is our most under-served audience commercially; partnering with the right equipment company genuinely helps readers.
- **Outreach window priority:** Strong. Likely a fast conversation and a fast yes.

### Scootaround (scootaround.com)

- **Why fit:** Larger general-mobility-rental brand; also services cruise ports.
- **Outreach window priority:** Open. Slightly less cruise-specific than Special Needs at Sea but bigger brand.

### Special Needs Group / SNG (specialneedsgroup.com)

- **Why fit:** Mobility, oxygen, and scooter rentals; cruise-port delivery; smaller than Special Needs at Sea but a real player.
- **Outreach window priority:** Open. Tier-2 in this category.

## Tier 5 — VPN providers (operational fit)

Real product fit (cruise Wi-Fi is hostile; VPN is genuinely useful). Brand-saturated category with strong affiliate competition. Our angle is editorial credibility, not affiliate-volume; partners that respect that are the right partners.

### NordVPN (nordvpn.com)

- **Why fit:** Largest brand; known to engage in editorial sponsorships; cruise-WiFi-on-the-bridge use case is real.
- **Talking points:**
  - Direct editorial relationship around `/internet-at-sea.html`; sponsored "VPN at sea — what works, what doesn't" review.
- **Outreach window priority:** Open.

### ExpressVPN (expressvpn.com)

- **Outreach window priority:** Tier-2 fallback.

### ProtonVPN (protonvpn.com)

- **Why fit:** Privacy-positioning aligns more naturally with our no-tracking brand stance than NordVPN. Smaller brand, possibly more flexible.
- **Outreach window priority:** Worth one outreach; brand-fit reasoning could carry the conversation.

## Tier 6 — Editorial / data licensing (long-shot, high-leverage)

Lower probability of conversion but materially higher upside if it lands.

### Travel-content publishers (Skift, Travel + Leisure, Condé Nast Traveler)

- **Why fit:** Some larger travel publishers occasionally license niche structured data (port operating hours, ship deployment schedules) to fill gaps in their own content. Our 388-port + 300-ship data set is unusually clean.
- **Outreach window priority:** Defer to Phase B.3 of W6.3 (data-licensing prep). Not before.

### AI training partners (Anthropic, OpenAI, Perplexity, Bing/Microsoft)

- **Why fit:** The 21K AI-citation pattern means these companies already know our domain. Some of them have begun licensing publisher content (NYT/Amazon, News Corp/OpenAI, Reddit/Google). Most of these deals favor publishers above ~10M monthly visitors; we're below that threshold.
- **Outreach window priority:** Long shot, low priority. Revisit annually as the AI-publisher market matures (per the You.com research note in the v2.5 plan).

## Off-list — explicitly skipped

These crossed bright lines or are genuinely poor fits. Documented here so they don't re-enter consideration in 6 months:

- **Cruise-line affiliate networks** (CruiseDirect, Cruisedeals, etc.) — bright line.
- **Viator, GetYourGuide, shore-excursion booking** — bright line.
- **Mediavine, Raptive, AdThrive, any display-ad network** — bright line. "No ads" is in the trust badge; permanent.
- **Pre-cruise medication or medical-supplements brands** (motion-sickness patches, Sea-Bands, etc.) — FTC vulnerable-consumer rule. We dropped the Sea-Bands affiliate link in commit `[Phase A from prior session]`; we're not re-entering this category.
- **CardRatings, Bankrate, NerdWallet (as affiliate intermediaries)** — networked relationships violate the "direct, not networked" stance. Direct relationships with the issuing banks themselves are fine if reachable.
