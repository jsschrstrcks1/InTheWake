Support In the Wake

Reader-supported. No advertising. No cruise-line affiliations. Nothing on this site is gated.

Why this page exists

In the Wake is run by one person and a small army of language models, with occasional help from contributors. The goal isn't to sell you a cruise. We don't run display advertising. We don't take cruise-line affiliate commissions. We don't sell shore-excursion bookings. None of those are coming, and that decision is permanent — it lives in our affiliate disclosure.

What that means in practice is that the site has a small revenue gap. This page is the honest way to fill it: if the tools, ship guides, port pages, or the calmer voice have been useful to you, you can help keep them independent.

If you'd rather not, that's fine. Nothing changes for you. Everything on the site stays free.

Two ways to support

Tip jar (one-time)

Pick whatever feels right — $3, $5, $10, $25. One tap. No recurring obligation. We'll see your name (or your handle, or "Anonymous") in the next monthly footer thanks block.

Tip via Buy Me A Coffee

Buy Me A Coffee is a third-party platform. We see your tip; they handle payment. Their privacy policy applies during checkout.

The Duck Club ($3–5/month)

Named for the cruise duck-spotting tradition that runs through this site — small, joyful, community-shaped. Membership exists to support independent work, not to unlock anything.

What you get:

  • Optional duck sticker mailed once a year (let us know your address)
  • Monthly footer thanks block on the site, listing supporters
  • Early heads-up email when major new tools or articles launch
  • The continuing knowledge that the site stays ad-free

What you don't get: exclusive content, paywalled tools, "members-only" articles, or any other feature gate. We don't believe in paywalling planning information for cruisers — especially not the parts that disabled, grieving, or solo travelers rely on.

Join the Duck Club

Cancel anytime, no questions asked. Cancellations are handled directly through Buy Me A Coffee.

What support actually does

  • Pays hosting — domain, CDN, web infrastructure. Small but real.
  • Funds ongoing development — new tools, port-page rewrites, article updates, AI-research time.
  • Replaces ad and affiliate revenue that would otherwise compromise the brand promise.
  • Keeps the site independent — no cruise-line money, no shore-excursion commissions, no display ads.

There is no salary line item. This is supplemental work, not a livelihood, and that's by design — independence is part of the value.

What support does NOT buy

  • No exclusive content. Every existing article, port page, ship guide, and tool stays free, forever.
  • No early access to existing material. If it's published, it's open to everyone.
  • No paywalled tools. The Drink Calculator, Stateroom Check, port pages, ship comparisons — all of it stays free, on the open web.
  • No special editorial influence. Cruise lines can't buy positive coverage. Neither can supporters. Reviews and comparisons are honest because they have to be.
  • No cruise-line affiliations. Permanently off the table. See the affiliate disclosure.

Frequently asked

Why not just run ads?

The site's audience includes solo travelers, grieving widows, disabled adventurers, and exhausted caregivers — people who came here because they trust the voice. Display advertising would change the relationship. Reader support is the alternative funding path that keeps trust intact.

Why not paywall content?

Two reasons. First, the tools help people make real travel decisions, often in difficult life circumstances. Paywalling them would lock out exactly the readers who most need them. Second, AI engines (Bing Copilot, ChatGPT, Perplexity) cite our content tens of thousands of times a quarter — paywalled content doesn't get cited. Free, open content earns authority. Authority is more valuable than the small marginal revenue a paywall would bring.

What if my situation changes and I need to cancel?

Cancel anytime through Buy Me A Coffee. No questions asked, no email follow-up, no "are you sure?" friction. Your access to the site is identical before, during, and after — because there is no access tier in the first place.

Is this a tax-deductible donation?

No. In the Wake is not a registered nonprofit. Tips and Duck Club memberships are commercial transactions through Buy Me A Coffee, not charitable donations. If a tax-deductible alternative is important to you, we can suggest reputable cruise-accessibility nonprofits instead — just get in touch.

What about the Amazon affiliate links on a few articles?

A handful of articles include Amazon affiliate links — used the way they're meant to be used: as honest "here's the specific item I'd buy" references on gear-focused articles, never as the reason an article exists. The full list and our policy are in the affiliate disclosure. We do not, and will not, take cruise-line, shore-excursion, or cruise-booking affiliate commissions.

This site exists to be calmer water in the wake of another boat. If we've helped your planning at all, knowing that you found something useful is already a real kindness. Support is optional, never required, and never the cost of the door.
