First Look: Carnival Spirit

Ship Overview

Carnival Spirit is the lead ship of four Spirit-class vessels, entering service in 2001. Christened by Elizabeth Dole, former U.S. Senator and Secretary of Transportation, Spirit made history as the first Carnival ship permanently homeported outside North America when she arrived in Sydney, Australia in 2012.

After nearly a decade serving Australian waters, Spirit returned to the U.S. in 2023 with a major refurbishment adding the Heroes Tribute Bar. She now sails year-round from Mobile, Alabama.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the dining options on Carnival Spirit?

Carnival Spirit features the Empire Restaurant two-level main dining room with Your Time Dining flexibility. Complimentary casual options include Guy's Burger Joint, BlueIguana Cantina for fresh tacos and burritos, and the Lido Marketplace buffet. Specialty restaurants include Fahrenheit 555 steakhouse, Cucina del Capitano Italian dining, and Bonsai Sushi for fresh Japanese cuisine.

Where does Carnival Spirit sail from?

Carnival Spirit is homeported in Mobile, Alabama, offering year-round Caribbean, Mexico, and Bahamas cruises. She returned to the U.S. in 2023 after nearly a decade pioneering Carnival's Australian presence from Sydney and Brisbane (2012-2021).

Who is the godmother of Carnival Spirit?

Elizabeth Dole, former U.S. Senator and Secretary of Transportation, christened Carnival Spirit on April 27, 2001. As the lead ship of the Spirit Class, Spirit introduced several innovations that became Carnival standards.
