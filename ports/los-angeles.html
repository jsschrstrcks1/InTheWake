Los Angeles

Los Angeles: West Coast Cruising Capital

The Los Angeles area offers two major cruise terminals — the World Cruise Center in San Pedro and the Long Beach Cruise Terminal. Both serve as gateways to Mexican Riviera, Hawaii, and Pacific Coast itineraries, with easy access from LAX and SoCal's endless attractions.

San Pedro's terminal is the busier of the two, home to Carnival, Princess, and Norwegian. Long Beach hosts Carnival's Long Beach sailings near the Queen Mary. Both are about 25 miles south of downtown LA.

SoCal Secret: Arrive a day early and explore San Pedro's revitalized waterfront — great seafood, the Battleship Iowa museum, and killer sunset views without the Hollywood crowds.

Getting to the Ports

  • San Pedro (World Cruise Center): 100 Swinford Street, San Pedro
  • Long Beach Cruise Terminal: 231 Windsor Way, Long Beach
  • From LAX: 30-45 minutes to either terminal (traffic dependent)
  • From John Wayne (SNA): 30-40 minutes to Long Beach
  • Uber/Lyft: $40-60 from LAX to either port

Parking Options

  • San Pedro: On-site parking available, reserve in advance
  • Long Beach: Port parking adjacent to terminal
  • Cost: $20-25/day at either location
  • Off-site: Several third-party lots offer lower rates with shuttles

Nearby Hotels

  • DoubleTree San Pedro: Walking distance to World Cruise Center
  • Crowne Plaza San Pedro: Harbor views, close to port
  • Queen Mary Hotel (Long Beach): Historic ship hotel experience
  • Hyatt Regency Long Beach: Near convention center and waterfront

Pre-Cruise Activities

  • USS Iowa Museum: Battleship tours in San Pedro
  • Aquarium of the Pacific: Long Beach's world-class aquarium
  • Queen Mary: Tour the legendary ocean liner
  • Cabrillo Marine Aquarium: Free admission, great for families
  • Ports O'Call Village: Waterfront shopping and dining

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: San Pedro or Long Beach — which is better?
A: Both are fine; your cruise line determines which port you'll use. Check your booking confirmation.

Q: How bad is LA traffic?
A: It can be brutal. Allow extra time, especially during rush hour (7-9 AM, 4-7 PM).

Q: Can I visit Hollywood before my cruise?
A: Yes, but it's 25+ miles away. Better to explore the local waterfront areas.

At a Glance
