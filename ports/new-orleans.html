New Orleans

New Orleans: Where Every Cruise Starts with a Party

No cruise port in America offers a pre-cruise experience like New Orleans. Beignets at Café du Monde, jazz on Frenchmen Street, crawfish étouffée in the French Quarter — this is a city that demands you arrive early. The cruise terminal sits on the Mississippi River, minutes from the action.

Carnival, Norwegian, and Royal Caribbean sail from NOLA to the Western Caribbean, with the iconic streetcars and Garden District just a short ride from the port.

NOLA Truth: Bourbon Street is fine for a walk-through, but the real magic is on Frenchmen Street — live jazz pouring out of every doorway, locals mixing with visitors, and none of the tourist trap vibe.

Getting to the Port

  • Address: Port of New Orleans, 1350 Port of New Orleans Place
  • From MSY Airport: 30-40 minutes via I-10
  • From French Quarter: 10-15 minutes by taxi or rideshare
  • Streetcar: Riverfront line runs near the port
  • Uber/Lyft: $25-35 from airport, $10-15 from Quarter

Parking Options

  • Port parking: Erato Street Cruise Terminal parking garage
  • Cost: Around $20-25/day
  • Off-site: Several lots in the Warehouse District offer cruise parking
  • Book ahead: Especially during festival season

Nearby Hotels

  • Hotel Monteleone: Historic French Quarter landmark with rotating Carousel Bar
  • The Roosevelt New Orleans: Grand luxury hotel with famous Sazerac Bar
  • Ace Hotel New Orleans: Hip Warehouse District spot
  • Hampton Inn Convention Center: Budget-friendly near the port

Pre-Cruise Activities

  • French Quarter: Jackson Square, St. Louis Cathedral, historic architecture
  • Café du Monde: Beignets and chicory coffee — essential
  • Frenchmen Street: Live jazz every night, multiple venues
  • Garden District: Streetcar ride through stunning antebellum homes
  • WWII Museum: World-class museum, plan several hours
  • Food tour: Gumbo, po'boys, crawfish, oysters — pace yourself!

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How many days should I spend in NOLA pre-cruise?
A: At least 2-3 days. New Orleans deserves time — it's one of America's most unique cities.

Q: Is the French Quarter safe?
A: The main tourist areas are well-patrolled. Use normal city awareness, especially late at night.

Q: Best time to visit?
A: Spring (March-May) and fall (October-November) have the best weather. Summer is hot and humid.

Quick Answer: America's most unique pre-cruise city — beignets at Café du Monde, jazz on Frenchmen Street, the French Quarter, and culinary adventures make arriving days early mandatory.

Port Type: Homeport

Location: Mississippi River, Downtown

Airport: MSY (30-40 min)
