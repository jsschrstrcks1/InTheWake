Regatta — Deck Plans, Live Tracker, Dining & Videos

Regatta is a R Class cruise ship operated by Oceania Cruises. She entered service in 1998, measures 30,277 gross tons, and carries approximately 684 guests at double occupancy with a crew of 400.

Key Facts

  • Class: R Class
  • Year: 1998
  • Tonnage: 30,277 GT
  • Guests: 684
  • Crew: 400
  • IMO: 9156474

Quick Answer: Regatta is Oceania's original R Class ship — 30,277 GT, 684 guests, launched in 1998 and extensively refurbished. She helped establish Oceania's reputation for exceptional food on an intimate ship. This page covers deck plans, live tracking, dining venues, and videos.

Who She's For: Travelers who value Oceania's culinary heritage on the most intimate ships in the fleet. Regatta suits repeat cruisers who appreciate the R Class charm — a loyal crew, small-ship ports, and specialty dining included without the formality of luxury lines.

Regatta was one of the original ships that built Oceania's reputation. At 684 guests she delivers the same R Class experience as Insignia, Nautica, and Sirena — Toscana and Polo Grill included, relaxed dress code, no formal nights. The four sisters are interchangeable in quality; choose by itinerary. If you want more public space and more dining options, Marina or Riviera (1,250 guests) offer the full Oceania experience. For the newest hardware, Vista (2023) and Allura (2025) are the Allura Class new-builds.

Frequently Asked Questions About Regatta

What dining options are on Regatta?

Five venues, all included: Grand Dining Room, Toscana (Italian), Polo Grill (steakhouse), Terrace Café (buffet), Waves Grill (poolside). See the Dining section above.

How big is Regatta?

30,277 GT with 684 guests and 400 crew. One of four R Class sisters (with Insignia, Nautica, Sirena).

How does the R Class compare to Marina/Riviera?

R Class (684 guests, 30,277 GT) is half the size of the O Class — Marina and Riviera carry 1,250 guests at 66,084 GT with more dining. R Class is more intimate.

Where does Regatta sail?

Seasonal itineraries including the Mediterranean, Caribbean, South America, and world cruise segments. Her smaller size allows access to ports larger ships cannot reach. Check the live tracker above.
