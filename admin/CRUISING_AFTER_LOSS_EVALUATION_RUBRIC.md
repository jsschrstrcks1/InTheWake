### 5. Tone & Voice Quality

**Grief-Appropriate Writing:**

- [ ] **Pastoral without preaching** - Comforting, not prescriptive
- [ ] **Permission-giving** - "You're allowed to..." language
- [ ] **Realistic about pain** - Doesn't minimize grief or rush healing
- [ ] **Hope without toxic positivity** - "This will help" not "This will fix"
- [ ] **Practical and specific** - Actionable guidance, not platitudes
- [ ] **Respectful of different timelines** - No "should be over it by now"

**Faith Integration:**

- [ ] Scripture used for comfort, not correction
- [ ] Accessible to both churched and unchurched readers
- [ ] Theology of lament, not just triumph
- [ ] Permission to grieve AND permission to experience joy

### 9. Differentiation from Solo Travel Article

**"Cruising After Loss" Should Be:**

- Grief-specific, not general solo travel
- Addresses loss, not just traveling alone
- Covers widow/widower community-finding
- Includes Scripture/faith comfort
- Permission to feel joy again after loss
- Practical about emotional triggers

**"Solo Cruising" Article Covers:**

- General solo travel (not grief-specific)
- First-time solo cruisers nervous about being alone
- Introverts, anxiety sufferers
- Solo by choice (not forced by loss)

**Note:** Both articles should cross-link, but serve different readers.
