Seattle: Your Alaska Adventure Starts Here

Seattle serves as the primary gateway for Alaska cruises, with multiple terminals along the waterfront welcoming ships from Norwegian, Royal Caribbean, Holland America, Princess, and Celebrity. The Emerald City's world-class food scene, iconic Pike Place Market, and stunning mountain views make it worth arriving early.

Whether you're sailing a round-trip Alaska cruise or beginning a one-way voyage to/from Vancouver, Seattle offers the perfect combination of urban sophistication and Pacific Northwest nature.

Seattle Essential: Pike Place Market at opening time — watch the fish throwers, grab a latte from the original Starbucks, and load up on local treats for your stateroom.

Getting to the Port

Cruise Terminals:

  • Smith Cove Terminal (Pier 91): Serves Carnival, Holland America, Princess, and Royal Caribbean cruise lines. Located about 10 minutes from downtown Seattle and the Space Needle area. Transportation required to reach city attractions.
  • Bell Street Pier Terminal (Pier 66): Hosts Celebrity and Norwegian cruise ships. Conveniently situated downtown within walking distance of major attractions.

Transportation:

  • From SEA-TAC Airport: Taxi service typically costs $25-40 and takes 20-30 minutes
  • Link Light Rail: Airport to downtown ($3.25, 40 min) — Westlake Station is closest to cruise terminals; taxi/Uber for final leg. Sound Transit
  • Uber/Lyft: $40-55 from airport to cruise terminals
  • Washington State Ferries: Connects to Bainbridge Island, Bremerton, and more — world's largest ferry fleet. WSDOT Ferries
  • Terminal Pick-up: Both cruise terminals offer passenger pick-up services

Parking Options

  • Pier 91 (Smith Cove): On-site parking managed by Platinum Parking — 1,100 secure spots including accessible stalls. $27+/day. Reserve at cruiseseattleparking.com
  • Bell Street Pier: Very limited; downtown garages recommended
  • RV/Overheight: Available at Pier 91 for $40+/day; height limit 7'4" in garage
  • Off-site: Several third-party lots offer cruise parking with shuttle service at lower rates

Nearby Hotels

  • Hilton Seattle: Downtown tower with easy access to Pike Place and waterfront attractions
  • DoubleTree by Hilton Seattle Downtown: Spacious rooms near the convention center with famous cookies
  • The Edgewater: Iconic waterfront hotel on Pier 67 — the Beatles stayed here in 1964 (and fished from their window)
  • Four Seasons Seattle: Downtown luxury near Pike Place with Elliott Bay views
  • Inn at the Market: Boutique hotel overlooking Pike Place — prime location for market exploring
  • Hampton Inn & Suites Downtown: Budget-friendly with free breakfast, walking distance to attractions

Pre-Cruise Activities

  • Pike Place Market: Historic public market featuring more than 200 year-round vendors selling fresh seafood, flowers, crafts, and produce. Home to fish throwing demonstrations and the original Starbucks
  • Space Needle: Seattle's iconic landmark offers a 520-foot observation deck with panoramic views of the city, mountains, and Puget Sound, plus a revolving restaurant
  • Seattle Aquarium: Located at Pier 59 on the waterfront, featuring Pacific Northwest marine life
  • Woodland Park Zoo: Home to more than 1,000 animals representing approximately 300 species from around the world
  • Seattle Central Library: Striking architectural landmark designed by renowned architect Rem Koolhaas
  • Tillicum Village: Experience Native American culture with traditional performances and a salmon feast
  • Chihuly Garden and Glass: Stunning glass art installations
  • Museum of Pop Culture (MoPOP): Music, sci-fi, pop culture
  • Capitol Hill: Trendy neighborhood with great dining

Tours & Excursions

  • Olympic National Park: UNESCO World Heritage Site with three distinct ecosystems — rainforest, mountains, and wild Pacific beaches — all in one park. Full-day tours from Seattle available. Book through Viator
  • Hop-On Hop-Off Buses: Double-decker buses with departures every 30 minutes, covering major city attractions
  • Ride the Ducks: Amphibious vehicle tours through city streets and into Lake Union — wacky and fun
  • Bainbridge Island Ferry: 35-minute ferry ride with stunning city skyline views — charming shops and cafes on the island
  • Underground Tour: Explore the buried city beneath Pioneer Square — Seattle rebuilt on top of itself after the 1889 fire
  • Boeing Factory Tour: See 787 Dreamliners being assembled at the world's largest building by volume (40 min north)

History & Heritage

Seattle's cruise story is inseparable from the Klondike Gold Rush of 1897, when the city marketed itself as the "Gateway to Alaska" — a title it still holds today. Ships loaded with prospectors departed from Elliott Bay, and the fortunes made outfitting those expeditions built modern Seattle. The Klondike Gold Rush National Historical Park in Pioneer Square preserves this era.

Pike Place Market opened in 1907 to connect farmers directly with consumers, and it has operated continuously ever since — making it one of the oldest farmers' markets in the nation. The original Starbucks opened here in 1971. The Space Needle, built for the 1962 World's Fair, remains Seattle's most recognized landmark.

Practical Information

  • Currency: US dollars; credit and debit cards widely accepted throughout the city
  • Sales Tax: 4-6% added to purchases
  • Tipping: Standard rates are 15-20% at restaurants, 10-15% for taxi rides, and $5-20 for tour guides
  • Emergency Services: Dial 911 for police, fire, or medical emergencies
  • WiFi: Widely available in hotels, cafes, restaurants, and public areas

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Which terminal will my ship use?
A: Check your cruise documents — Pier 91 and Bell Street Pier serve different cruise lines.

Q: How's Seattle weather before Alaska cruises?
A: May-September is usually pleasant (60s-70s°F). Always bring layers — Seattle weather can change quickly.

Q: One-way vs. round-trip Alaska cruises?
A: One-way cruises (Seattle to Vancouver or vice versa) see more of the Inside Passage, visiting ports like Juneau, Ketchikan, and Skagway. Round-trips are more convenient if flying.

Quick Answer: The Emerald City's gateway to Alaska — Pike Place Market fish throwers, the Space Needle, world-class coffee, and stunning mountain views make arriving early a must.

Port Type: Major Alaska Homeport

Terminals: Pier 91 & Bell Street
