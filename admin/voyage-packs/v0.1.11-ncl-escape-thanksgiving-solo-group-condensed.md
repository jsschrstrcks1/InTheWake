**The Caribbean packing rule is layers-light.** Quick-dry shirts, swimsuit rotation, light rain shell, water shoes for Damajagua. Skip heavy clothes — late-November Caribbean is 78–86°F daytime.

## Getting to the ship — and getting home

### Arrival: Thursday recommended, Friday required

**Thursday Nov 19 — fly into MCO.** Same-day flights to a 3:30 PM Friday all-aboard during **Thanksgiving week — the busiest US travel week of the year** are risky. Thursday-night hotel in Cocoa Beach (15 min from port) or Orlando/MCO area (45 min from port).

**If you can absorb the extra night, Wednesday Nov 18 arrival** gives the largest weather + delay buffer.

**Friday — embarkation.**

- **Terminal:** **Port Canaveral, Cruise Terminal 10** (verify in NCL app 14 days before).
- **MCO → Port Canaveral:** Uber/Lyft 45–60 min, $60–80. Shared cruise shuttle ~$30/person, slower.
- **If driving:** Port Canaveral garage ~$17–22/day (significantly cheaper than PortMiami).
- **Embark window:** 11:30 AM – 3:30 PM. **All-aboard 3:30 PM.** Ship departs ~4:00–5:00 PM.
- **NCL app online check-in** before arrival — completes faster than paper.

### Day 8 debark and the trip home

- **The night before** (Thanksgiving evening): bags in the hall with color-coded tag by ~11 PM.
- **6:30 AM:** Customs clearance.
- **7:00 – 9:30 AM:** Walk-off in waves; self-disembark starts ~7 AM.
- **10:00 AM (latest):** All passengers off.

**Black Friday traffic timing.** Nov 27 is Black Friday — Florida highway traffic is light early morning but spikes by mid-afternoon. Build the buffer in. Cocoa Beach outlets see the worst of it.

**Getting from Port Canaveral to MCO.** Uber/Lyft $60–80, 45–60 min. Cocoa Beach Shuttle (~$30/person, advance booking) is the budget option.

**Airport-arrival timing for MCO.**

| Flight | Leave Port Canaveral by |
|---|---|
| Domestic, after 4 PM | 12:30 PM |
| International, after 6 PM | 2:00 PM |

MCO TSA on Black Friday afternoon runs heavy — give margin.

**Killing 4–6 hours before an evening flight:** Cocoa Beach pier (15 min, beach + lunch); Kennedy Space Center (20 min, $75 entry, half-day minimum); Cocoa Beach outlets if Black Friday shopping is your thing.

## Areas of interest during the cruise

### On the ship — Escape spots worth knowing

- **The Observation Lounge** (Deck 15 forward) — wraparound glass; panoramic. Sunrise coffee here is the day's best 30 minutes; quiet at 6 AM.
- **Studio Lounge** — solo-cruiser social anchor. Free coffee, hosted hours, scheduled meet-and-greets. NCL hosts a Day 1 gathering.
- **The Aqua Park** — Aqua Racer (twin-tube racing slides), Free Fall slides, The Whip. All free.
- **The ropes course + Sky Walk** — a multi-story rope course (the largest at sea when Escape launched in 2015), with a plank cantilevered out over the ship's side. Free. *(Note: Escape has no go-kart track — that's a Prima/Joy/Encore feature, not this ship.)*
- **Vibe Beach Club** — adults-only enclave, $99/day or $399/week. Quietest pool on the ship.
- **The Haven** — ship-within-a-ship suite enclave (if your cabin is in there).
- **Guy Harvey hull artwork** — Escape's marlin-and-billfish mural is the iconic pier photo.

### Sea-day priorities (Day 2 and Day 6)

1. **Sunrise coffee in the Observation Lounge** — the trip's best 30 minutes.
2. **Book Great Tides Waterpark slot** — Thanksgiving sailings fill 60+ days pre-cruise; book Day 1 if not done.
3. **Specialty dining reservation** for the second half of the week.
4. **NCL app port talks** for Puerto Plata (Day 2) and Great Stirrup Cay (Day 6).
5. **Mandara Spa thermal-suite day pass** — sea-day intro rate.

### Theme nights and the holiday calendar

NCL runs a lighter theme-night cadence than Royal. Watch for **"Norwegian's Night Out"** (the one optional slightly-dressier evening — not formal night; jacket not required) and the **White Hot Party** (white attire, deck party). The **Thanksgiving evening (Day 7)** is the dressier-than-usual night by tradition rather than NCL mandate — many cruisers dress slightly nicer for the holiday meal. **Smartest packing move:** one all-white outfit (works for White Hot + doubles as smart-casual) + one autumn-toned accessory (scarf, wrap, sequined top) for Thanksgiving. Specific nights confirm in the daily program once aboard.

### Day 7 — Great Stirrup Cay + Thanksgiving aboard

**Cay (9 AM – 5 PM):**

- **Great Tides Waterpark** (opened Sept 2026) — Tidal Tower racing slides, Wandering River, Cliffside Cove platform-jumping, Splash Cay/Harbor family zone, 19 slides total. Reservation-based, paid add-on.
- **Free with fare:** Beach access (multiple zones), BBQ buffet lunch, Great Life Lagoon (28,000-sq-ft pool with swim-up bars), tram service from Welcome Plaza, lounge chairs/umbrellas (first-come).
- **Paid add-ons:** Flighthouse Zipline (~$20), Underwater Snorkel Garden, Vibe Shore Club ($79+, adults-only), cabanas ($300–700+), Silver Cove (ultra-premium adults-only).

**Thanksgiving dinner (5:30 – 9:00 PM):**

- **The traditional feast** — roast turkey, stuffing, sweet potatoes, cranberry sauce, green bean casserole, pumpkin/pecan/apple pie — runs across the [Manhattan Room](/restaurants/ncl/the-manhattan-room.html), Taste, and Savor. **No extra charge.**
- **Pam's group typically gathers at Manhattan Room 5:30 PM** — verify in the Facebook group chat.
- **Specialty restaurants stay open with regular menus** if you'd rather have steak or French. Book 60+ days pre-cruise.
- **The shower-and-rest window** between 4 PM (back-aboard from cay) and 5:30 PM dinner is the unspoken rhythm.

### Specialty dining picks

- **[Cagney's Steakhouse](/restaurants/ncl/cagneys-steakhouse.html)** — NCL's standard steakhouse; reliably good.
- **[Le Bistro](/restaurants/ncl/le-bistro.html)** — French; the most romantic specialty room.
- **[Teppanyaki](/restaurants/ncl/teppanyaki.html)** — Japanese hibachi; group-friendly.
- **[Food Republic](/restaurants/ncl/food-republic.html)** — pan-Asian small plates; one of NCL's most distinctive specialty rooms.
- **[Q Texas Smokehouse](/restaurants/ncl/q-texas-smokehouse.html)** — Texas BBQ.
- **[La Cucina](/restaurants/ncl/la-cucina.html)** — Italian.

### Port-day picks (one each)

- **Puerto Plata (Amber Cove)** — Damajagua Waterfalls (27 cascades to climb + slide; $50–95) is the iconic experience. If you want low-stress, Amber Cove's pools + beach + lunch is fine.
- **St. Thomas** — Magens Bay (one of the most-photographed beaches in the world, ~$17 r/t taxi + $5 entry) is the classic; ferry to St. John's Trunk Bay is the postcard upgrade.
- **Tortola** — Cane Garden Bay (~$30 r/t) for the beach day; The Baths on Virgin Gorda ($150–200 all-in) for the granite-boulder swim caves.
- **Great Stirrup Cay** — see Day 7 section above. Most cruisers do morning waterpark/Flighthouse + afternoon beach.

### Photo spots worth knowing

- **Sailaway from the Pool Deck** — Cape Canaveral as the ship pulls away; sunset light on the Florida coast.
- **Observation Lounge sunrise** — panoramic windows; Eastern Caribbean dawn at sea.
- **Amber Cove from the upper decks** — green Dominican mountains behind the cruise port.
- **Magens Bay from Mountain Top** — the iconic St. Thomas harbor postcard.
- **The Baths on Virgin Gorda** — granite boulders, turquoise pools.
- **Great Stirrup Cay from the tender** — the marlin hull artwork in profile against Bahamian water.
