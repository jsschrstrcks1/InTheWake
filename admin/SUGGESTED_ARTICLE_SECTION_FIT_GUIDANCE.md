# SUGGESTED ARTICLE SECTION: Fit-Guidance (Who This Is For)

**Placement:** Immediately after H1/dek, before opening story begins

---

## Who This Article Is For

**This article is for you if:**

- You're a widow or widower considering your first cruise after losing your spouse
- You're facing your first holidays without them and the thought of staying home feels unbearable
- You've been a "we" for decades and don't know how to be an "I"
- You're nervous about traveling alone after years of traveling as a couple
- You feel guilty every time you smile or imagine beauty
- You need permission to feel joy without betraying their memory
- You're looking for gentle community that doesn't require you to explain your grief
- You want practical guidance from someone who understands both grief and cruising
- You're wondering if it's "too soon" (and tired of other people's timelines)

**This article may not be for you if:**

- You're in deep shock—typically the first few months after loss (there is no shame in waiting)
- You're looking for general solo travel advice without the grief component—see our [Solo Cruising Guide](/solo/articles/why-i-started-solo-cruising.html)
- You need professional grief counseling or couples therapy—this is pastoral travel guidance, not clinical treatment
- You're trying to escape financial or family responsibilities—travel can create space to breathe, but it cannot solve those problems
- You're seeking a "cure" for grief—cruising won't erase your loss, but it may help you carry it differently

**A note on faith:**

This article includes Scripture and pastoral reflections because faith has been a comfort to many grieving travelers—including the author. If that's not your tradition, take what helps and leave what doesn't. The practical guidance stands on its own.
