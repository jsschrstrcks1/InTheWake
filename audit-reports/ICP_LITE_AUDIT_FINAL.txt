SECTION 9: KEY INSIGHTS & PATTERNS
================================================================================

INSIGHT #1: The Cruise-Lines Model Works
  The cruise-lines/ directory shows 90% compliance (9/10 files), suggesting:
  - When pages ARE designed with ICP-Lite in mind, compliance is achievable
  - The template and process for cruise-line pages is effective
  - This approach should be replicated for other categories

INSIGHT #2: Meta Tags Are Done, Content Structure Is Not
  Meta tag implementation (99.5%) vs. content structure (12-54%) shows:
  - The site has completed the "metadata layer" of ICP-Lite
  - The "content structure layer" is largely unimplemented
  - These are separate efforts that need different approaches
  - Meta tags are easier (automated), content requires editorial work

INSIGHT #3: FAQ Sections Are the Bottleneck
  FAQPage schema is low (18.6%) because FAQ sections are rare (12.7%):
  - FAQ content can't be auto-generated; it requires deliberate creation
  - Restaurants (129 files) have zero FAQ, representing the biggest gap
  - Ships lack FAQ despite being factual/reference material (could be added)
  - FAQ is both important and labor-intensive

INSIGHT #4: Content Structure Moves as a Unit
  The answer-line/fit-guidance/key-facts classes always appear together:
  - Suggesting they're part of a template
  - When one is present, all three are
  - This makes them easier to implement (add all or none)
  - Makes it harder to retrofit incrementally

INSIGHT #5: Page Type Matters More Than Directory
  Compliance isn't evenly distributed by directory:
  - Comparison/Landing pages: High compliance (cruise-lines = 90%)
  - Reference pages: Low compliance (ships = mostly 0-8%)
  - Service pages: Zero compliance (restaurants = 0%)
  - This suggests a page-type-based implementation strategy
