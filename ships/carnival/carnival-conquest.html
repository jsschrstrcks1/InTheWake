Carnival Conquest — Deck Plans, Live Tracker, Dining & Videos

Looking for Carnival Conquest planning info? This page covers deck plans, live ship tracking, dining venues, and video tours to help you plan your Carnival cruise.

Carnival Conquest tends to suit travelers who want a mid-sized ship with New Orleans flair. It's ideal if you want Conquest-class reliability, French Impressionist décor, and Gulf Coast departures. If you're looking for newer mega-ship features or water parks, explore the Dream or Excel Class ships instead.

The Logbook — Tales From the Wake

Carnival Conquest holds a special place in Carnival's history as the lead ship of the Conquest Class — a design so successful it spawned four sister ships and set the template for Carnival's mid-sized fleet for over a decade.

When she entered service in November 2002, Conquest was Carnival's largest ship ever built. Her French Impressionist-themed interiors pay elegant tribute to New Orleans, with main dining rooms named after Renoir and Monet. Approximately 60% of staterooms have ocean views, and 37% feature private balconies — generous proportions for her era.

Today, Carnival Conquest continues her connection to New Orleans, frequently sailing from the Big Easy on Western Caribbean itineraries. Her intimate size (compared to newer mega-ships) offers easier navigation and a more relaxed pace, while still delivering the Fun Ship entertainment and dining options that Carnival is known for.

Frequently Asked Questions

What makes Carnival Conquest unique compared to other Carnival ships?

Carnival Conquest is the lead ship of the Conquest Class, featuring French Impressionist-style décor that pays tribute to New Orleans. At 110,239 GT with 2,980 guests, she established the blueprint for Carnival's mid-sized ships with 37% of staterooms featuring private balconies.

Which ships are in the same class as Carnival Conquest?

Carnival Conquest is the lead ship of the Conquest Class, which includes Carnival Freedom (2007), Carnival Glory (2003), Carnival Liberty (2005), and Carnival Valor (2004). All five ships share similar size and layouts.

What dining options are available on Carnival Conquest?

Carnival Conquest offers multiple dining venues including the Renoir and Monet Main Dining Rooms, Lido Marketplace buffet, Guy's Burger Joint, BlueIguana Cantina, pizzeria, and specialty options like the steakhouse.

Where does Carnival Conquest typically sail?

Carnival Conquest typically sails from New Orleans, Louisiana, offering Western Caribbean itineraries including Cozumel, Costa Maya, Belize, Roatan, and other Gulf of Mexico destinations.
