Nordic Prince — Historical Information & Legacy

Looking for Nordic Prince information? This page covers the history, legacy, and details of Nordic Prince, one of Royal Caribbean's founding vessels from 1971.

Nordic Prince was one of Royal Caribbean's founding vessels, serving from 1971 to 1995. The ship offered classic Caribbean cruising with about 876 guests at double occupancy, embodying the pioneering spirit of Royal Caribbean's early years. Though retired decades ago, Nordic Prince holds historical significance as part of the line's original fleet.

Nordic Prince — A Founding Ship

Nordic Prince was a historic Royal Caribbean ship, part of the cruise line's original fleet alongside Song of Norway and Sun Viking. Launched in 1971, Nordic Prince helped establish Royal Caribbean International as a premier cruise line.

The ship served Royal Caribbean faithfully for 24 years, offering Caribbean cruises and helping to pioneer the modern cruise industry. Nordic Prince was known for her elegant Scandinavian design and warm hospitality.

The ship was retired from Royal Caribbean service in 1995, marking the end of an era for one of the cruise line's founding vessels.

A Legacy of Innovation

Nordic Prince was part of Royal Caribbean's founding fleet that revolutionized Caribbean cruising in the 1970s. Along with her sister ships Song of Norway and Sun Viking, Nordic Prince helped establish many of the cruise traditions and service standards that Royal Caribbean is known for today.

The Song of Norway Class ships were designed specifically for Caribbean cruising, featuring innovative design elements that maximized ocean views and passenger comfort in the warm Caribbean climate.

After her retirement from Royal Caribbean service, Nordic Prince sailed under different names and operators before ultimately being scrapped.
