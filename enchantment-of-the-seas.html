Enchantment of the Seas

Enchantment of the Seas stands out in the Vision class thanks to a dramatic 2005 lengthening that inserted a mid‑section—expanding pool areas, adding suspension bridges above the main pool, and creating room for extra staterooms and venues. The result is a compact ship with a touch more elbow room and energy on deck.

Indoors, you’ll find Royal Caribbean’s familiar mix of lounges, a classic theater, and Main Dining Room service backed by Windjammer staples. Depending on deployment, you may also see casual or specialty venues added during later updates.

Staterooms are practical; balconies and ocean‑views shine on warm‑weather routes. The layout is simple to learn, making the ship a solid choice for first‑timers and short‑getaway cruisers who want an easy rhythm.

If you like a smaller ship but welcome just a bit more top‑deck activity, Enchantment splits the difference nicely within the Vision class.
