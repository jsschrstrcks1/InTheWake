Carnival Ecstasy (1991–2022) — A Historical Tribute

Remembering Carnival Ecstasy — In her 31 years of service, Carnival Ecstasy carried nearly 5.5 million guests and helped define the "Fun Ship" experience.

Carnival Ecstasy was the second of eight Fantasy-class ships and served as a gateway to cruising for millions of first-time cruisers. Her final voyage departed Mobile, Alabama on October 10, 2022, and she was beached for scrapping in Aliaga, Turkey on November 8, 2022.

The Logbook — Tales From the Wake

Carnival Ecstasy holds a special place in cruise history. Built in 1991 at Kværner Masa-Yards in Helsinki, Finland, at a cost of $275 million, she was the second Fantasy-class ship and helped establish Carnival's reputation for bringing cruising to the masses.

The Fantasy class represented a revolution in cruise ship design. At 70,526 GT with capacity for over 2,000 guests, these ships proved that cruising could be accessible, fun, and affordable. Carnival Ecstasy featured the line's signature "Fun Ship" atmosphere with neon lights, the distinctive whale-tail funnel, and a party-forward approach that attracted a younger demographic to cruising.

Throughout her career, Ecstasy sailed from various homeports including Mobile, Alabama — where she became a beloved fixture of the Gulf Coast cruise scene. Her final voyage in October 2022 was an emotional farewell, with passengers and crew sharing memories of three decades of service.

While she's no longer sailing the seas, the nearly 5.5 million guests who stepped aboard Carnival Ecstasy carry with them the memories of birthdays, anniversaries, honeymoons, and family vacations that made her more than just a ship — she was a floating community of joy.

Frequently Asked Questions

What happened to Carnival Ecstasy?

Carnival Ecstasy was retired in October 2022 after 31 years of service. Her final voyage departed Mobile, Alabama on October 10-15, 2022, with port calls in Cozumel and Progreso. The ship was beached for scrapping in Aliaga, Turkey on November 8, 2022.

When was Carnival Ecstasy built?

Carnival Ecstasy was built in 1991 at Kværner Masa-Yards in Helsinki, Finland. She was floated out on January 6, 1991 and christened by television hostess Kathie Lee Gifford.

How many passengers did Carnival Ecstasy carry?

Over her 31 years of service, Carnival Ecstasy carried nearly 5.5 million guests. The ship had a capacity of 2,040 passengers at double occupancy with 1,028 staterooms.

What class was Carnival Ecstasy?

Carnival Ecstasy was a Fantasy Class ship, the second of eight ships in that class. Sister ships included Carnival Fantasy, Carnival Sensation, Carnival Fascination, Carnival Imagination, Carnival Inspiration, Carnival Elation, and Carnival Paradise — all now retired.

Why was Carnival Ecstasy retired?

Carnival retired the Fantasy Class ships as part of fleet modernization. After 31 years of service, Carnival Ecstasy and the other Fantasy Class vessels were replaced by newer, more efficient ships with modern amenities. The COVID-19 pandemic accelerated these retirement decisions.
