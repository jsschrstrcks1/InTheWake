The First Purpose-Built SuperLiner

Historic Significance: MS Holiday was Carnival's first purpose-built cruise ship. All previous Carnival vessels were converted ocean liners. Holiday proved the "Fun Ship" concept could scale to larger, modern ships.

Delivered in 1985 from Denmark's Aalborg Vaerft shipyard, Holiday established the template for Carnival's expansion through the late 1980s. Her sisters Jubilee (1986) and Celebration (1987) followed the same successful formula.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why was MS Holiday significant?

MS Holiday was Carnival's first purpose-built cruise ship. All previous Carnival ships (Mardi Gras, Carnivale, Festivale) were converted ocean liners. Holiday proved the "Fun Ship" concept could work on a larger, modern scale.

What happened to the Holiday-class ships?

All three were scrapped: MS Jubilee in 2017, MS Holiday and MS Celebration both in 2021. The COVID-19 pandemic accelerated the retirement of these older vessels.
