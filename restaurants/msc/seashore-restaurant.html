Seashore Restaurant

MSC Cruises — Main Dining Room (MSC Seaside)

Quick Answer: The larger of MSC Seaside’s two main dining rooms. About 922–928 seats on Deck 5 aft in the Cape Cod area, paired with the smaller Ipanema Restaurant on Deck 6 Copacabana. Seashore is also the breakfast and lunch MDR (Ipanema is dinner only). New England maritime design language. Included with cruise fare. Not to be confused with the MSC Seashore ship.

Best For: Travelers who want a real sit-down breakfast or brunch instead of the buffet upstairs, plus cruisers who prefer Seashore’s cooler maritime room aesthetic over Ipanema’s warmer Brazilian theme. Families with the early dinner seating; couples for the late.

Key Facts

  • Ship: MSC Seaside (not MSC Seashore)
  • Deck: 5 aft (Cape Cod area)
  • Capacity: ~922–928 seats
  • Price: Complimentary (included in cruise fare)
  • Hours: Breakfast 7:30–9:00 port days · Brunch 8:30–13:30 sea days · Dinner 5:30/7:30/9:30 PM or My Choice flex
  • Dress Code: Casual breakfast/brunch; smart casual dinner; jacket/cocktail dress Gala Night
  • Reservations: Assigned at booking; walk-in for breakfast/brunch

The larger of MSC Seaside’s two complimentary main dining rooms, and the only one open for breakfast and brunch. Same kitchen and menu as Ipanema upstairs; the choice between them is room aesthetic and dining-assignment logistics. Return to the Restaurants hub →

Menu & Prices

Price: Complimentary (included in cruise fare). Drinks beyond water, iced tea, lemonade, juice at breakfast, and basic coffee are billed separately or covered by your MSC drinks package.

Breakfast (port days, 7:30–9:00 AM): sit-down a la carte with European-style pastries, fresh fruit, made-to-order eggs, an Italian-leaning cured-meat and cheese board, smoked salmon, and a small hot-buffet adjunct of bacon, sausage, and roasted potatoes. The smoked salmon and the European cheese board are the items that justify skipping the buffet upstairs.

Brunch (sea days, 8:30 AM–1:30 PM): the combined breakfast-and-lunch service that’s the calmer alternative to the Marketplace Buffet on Deck 8. Same breakfast items plus a small mid-morning lunch menu (pasta, a Caesar, a daily fish). Less crowded than the buffet, table-service pacing, real espresso.

Dinner (every night): identical menu to Ipanema on Deck 6 — antipasti or appetizer, soup or salad, pasta course, main rotation (beef, chicken, fish, vegetarian), and dessert. A Classic Favorites section is available every night for travelers who want fixed-option steak, salmon, or chicken. On Gala Night the menu lifts a step (escargots, rock lobster bisque, duck a l’orange, beef short rib ravioli have all appeared on recent sailings).

Menu rotates by sailing. Confirm specific dishes and hours in the MSC for Me app onboard.

Special Accommodations

Allergen & Dietary Notes: MSC handles allergies through the maitre d’ ahead of the meal rather than at the table-side order. Identify allergies at check-in on Day 1; the kitchen will pull the next night’s menu options the evening before so the chef can pre-plate around gluten, shellfish, dairy, and nut allergies. Vegetarian options appear on every menu; vegan is on request. Kosher and halal meals require advance notice at booking, not onboard.

Where You’ll Find It

Seashore Restaurant exists only on MSC Seaside. Deck 5 aft, accessed from the Cape Cod corridor and the Seaside Bar atrium. Its sister room Ipanema Restaurant sits one deck up. Naming note: “Seashore Restaurant” on MSC Seaside is a venue; MSC Seashore is a separate, larger ship from the Seaside EVO sub-class. They share a name root by coincidence and the venues on MSC Seashore the ship are different.

3.7 ★ out of 5 MSC Seaside • 2023–2025

Seashore Restaurant: Why Sit-Down Breakfast Quietly Wins on MSC Seaside

Seashore is the bigger of MSC Seaside’s two main dining rooms and the only one that opens before dinner. Deck 5 aft, Cape Cod area, cool blue-and-cream palette, large stern-facing windows that look back along the wake on sea days. The room runs about 922–928 seats, three fixed dinner seatings, plus a flexible window for Aurea Experience and higher. Same kitchen and menu as Ipanema; the difference is mostly room, hours, and the breakfast-and-brunch program that doesn’t happen one deck up.

Breakfast and brunch — the underused move

Most cruisers default to the Marketplace Buffet for breakfast because it’s closer to the cabins on Deck 8 and faster on a port morning. Seashore at 7:30 AM is the better play for travelers who want a real breakfast. Sit-down service, a server who brings real espresso (not the carafe coffee the buffet pours), made-to-order eggs that haven’t sat under a lamp, smoked salmon and an Italian cured-meat plate that you’d pay for at a hotel ashore. The room is quiet at this hour — the kid contingent is still upstairs at the cereal station, and the line cooks have time to plate properly. On sea days the brunch service runs to 1:30 PM and bridges into a light lunch menu (pasta, Caesar, fish of the day) without the buffet’s circus.

Dinner — the same plate, a different room

Dinner is where the comparison with Ipanema becomes purely aesthetic. Same antipasti, same pasta course, same Gala Night lift. Seashore’s room is bigger and the noise floor is correspondingly higher when the 7:30 PM seating fills; the 9:30 PM late is the quieter sit if conversation matters. The pasta course remains the most-consistently-strong plate — al dente, sauce that hasn’t broken, an appropriate undersize portion so the main still has room. Mains follow the same pattern as Ipanema: stronger at lower volume, weaker when the kitchen is plating 900+ covers and the chicken or fish hits the rail late. Gala Night is the night to plan around — lobster bisque, escargots, duck a l’orange, short rib ravioli, and the kitchen seems to take more time per plate.

Service

The fixed-seating model puts the same waiter and assistant at your table for the full sailing, which is the right way to do MSC. By Night 2 they know your wine, your pace, your bread preference; by Night 3 they’re bringing a second pasta before you’ve asked. The brunch and breakfast service is less personal — rotation crews, walk-up seating — but pace and attention are honest. Multi-language announcements drift through the room throughout the meal in the standard MSC five-language rotation; they’re part of the culture rather than an intrusion.

Atmosphere

The Cape Cod design is more restrained than Ipanema’s warmer tropical theme — rope-and-driftwood maritime motifs, cooler blues, white-painted millwork. The stern windows are the room’s real feature on sea days, especially the 9:30 PM late dinner as the wake glows phosphorescent in the ship’s lights. On breakfast service the same windows catch first sun in the eastward-cruising Caribbean season, which is reason enough to swap a buffet morning for a Seashore one. Acoustic load is heavier than Ipanema at full seating because the room is bigger; the carpet absorbs some but adjacent eight-tops carry.

Pro Tips

  • At least one port morning, eat breakfast at Seashore instead of the buffet. You’ll leave the ship calmer and better-fed.
  • Sea-day brunch beats the buffet on three vectors: pace, coffee, and elbow room. The buffet’s only real edge is variety.
  • For dinner, the 9:30 PM late seating in the stern windows on a sea night is the quietest, prettiest meal Seashore offers.
  • If you were assigned Ipanema at booking but prefer the Cape Cod aesthetic, Guest Services on Day 1 handles the swap in minutes.
  • Keep the breakfast plate small if you’re heading ashore — the temptation is to over-order at sit-down, and Bahamian heat punishes a heavy stomach.

Conclusion

Rating: 3.7/5. Seashore is the more useful of MSC Seaside’s two MDRs because of the breakfast and brunch program, not because the dinner is meaningfully different from Ipanema’s. The kitchen is honest on the Italian front courses; mains thin out under volume; Gala Night earns its name. Tip: on a 4-night sailing, plan to eat at Seashore at least once before dinner — a Seashore breakfast on Day 2 sets up the rest of the cruise with the right tone.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Seashore Restaurant on MSC Seaside?

Seashore Restaurant is the larger of MSC Seaside's two main dining rooms. It seats roughly 922–928 on Deck 5 aft in the Cape Cod area, paired with the smaller Ipanema Restaurant on Deck 6. Seashore handles breakfast, lunch (sea days only), and dinner; Ipanema serves dinner only. Both run the same nightly menu under one kitchen, and the dining assignment is made at booking with flex available through Guest Services on Day 1.

Is Seashore Restaurant the same as the MSC Seashore ship?

No — and this is a common point of confusion. Seashore Restaurant is a dining venue on MSC Seaside (the lead 2017 Seaside-class ship). MSC Seashore is a separate, larger 2021 ship in the Seaside EVO sub-class. The two share a name root but are unrelated: Seashore Restaurant is the Deck 5 MDR on Seaside; MSC Seashore is its own ship with its own different dining program.

How much does Seashore Restaurant cost?

Seashore Restaurant is complimentary — included in your MSC Seaside cruise fare. There is no cover charge for any meal, port day or sea day. Bottled wine, cocktails, premium coffee, and bottled water are billed separately or covered by your MSC drinks package.

When is Seashore Restaurant open?

Breakfast 7:30 AM – 9:00 AM on port days. Brunch 8:30 AM – 1:30 PM on sea days. Dinner with three fixed seatings (typically 5:30 PM, 7:30 PM, and 9:30 PM) or My Choice flexible dining 5:30 PM – 9:30 PM for Aurea Experience tier and above. Hours adjust on embarkation and debarkation days — confirm in the MSC for Me app for your specific sailing.

What's the difference between Seashore and Ipanema?

Same kitchen, same menu, different rooms. Seashore (Deck 5 Cape Cod, ~922 seats) leans New England maritime in its design — cooler tones, sea references; Ipanema (Deck 6 Copacabana, 766 seats) leans Brazilian and tropical — warmer tones, beach-resort references. Seashore is the breakfast and lunch MDR; Ipanema is dinner only. For dinner, both are equally good — pick by aesthetic preference or by which one you were assigned at booking.
