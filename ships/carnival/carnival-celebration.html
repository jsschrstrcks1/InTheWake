Carnival Celebration debuted in November 2022, marking Carnival Cruise Line's golden anniversary milestone.

Carnival Celebration

Carnival Cruise Line • Excel-Class

Active Fleet Excel-Class Largest Ship LNG Powered

Overview

Carnival Celebration is Carnival Cruise Line's largest ship at 183,521 GT and the second vessel in the revolutionary Excel-class. Debuting in November 2022 to mark Carnival's 50th anniversary, she features six uniquely themed zones, the BOLT roller coaster, and environmentally-friendly LNG propulsion.

Homeported at PortMiami, she offers 6-8 day cruises to the Eastern, Western, and Southern Caribbean. Her Miami-inspired design includes the exclusive 820 Biscayne zone, celebrating the city's vibrant culture and cuisine.

Godmother: Cassidy Gifford

Cassidy Gifford, American actress and daughter of TV personality Kathie Lee Gifford, christened Carnival Celebration on November 20, 2022 at PortMiami. Her mother Kathie Lee was godmother of the original MS Celebration in 1987 and served as Carnival's TV spokesperson from 1984-2002.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Carnival Celebration the largest Carnival ship?

Yes, Carnival Celebration is Carnival's largest ship at 183,521 gross tons. She is the second Excel-class vessel, sister to Mardi Gras (2021) and Jubilee (2023). The Excel-class ships are the largest in Carnival's fleet.

Where does Carnival Celebration sail from?

Carnival Celebration is homeported at PortMiami, Florida, offering 6-8 day cruises to the Eastern Caribbean (San Juan, St. Thomas, Amber Cove), Western Caribbean (Cozumel, Costa Maya, Roatan), and Southern Caribbean year-round.

Who is the godmother of Carnival Celebration?

Cassidy Gifford, American actress and daughter of TV personality Kathie Lee Gifford, serves as godmother. The naming ceremony was held November 20, 2022 at PortMiami. Her mother Kathie Lee was godmother of the original MS Celebration in 1987.

What are the six zones on Carnival Celebration?

Carnival Celebration features six themed zones: 820 Biscayne (Miami-inspired dining/entertainment), The Gateway (entertainment hub), Celebration Central (main atrium), Summer Landing (poolside), Lido (casual dining), and The Ultimate Playground (BOLT coaster, sports).

Does Carnival Celebration have a roller coaster?

Yes, Carnival Celebration features BOLT: Ultimate Sea Coaster, the first roller coaster at sea (introduced on sister ship Mardi Gras). This electric-powered coaster reaches speeds up to 40 mph on a track above the ship's top deck. Reservations recommended.
