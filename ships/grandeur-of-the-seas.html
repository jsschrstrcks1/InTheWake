A First Look

Why book Grandeur of the Seas?

Imagine drifting into a world where every sunrise promises a fresh adventure and the ocean’s rhythm syncs with your own. That’s the subtle magic of Grandeur of the Seas, Royal Caribbean’s intimate Vision-class gem—classic in size, vibrant in spirit—designed for sun-soaked escapes, effortless relaxation, and the thrill of discovery. On deck, tropical horizons frame your day while timeless charm meets modern touches that make each moment feel tailor-made.

Families find easy together-time: kids scale the Rock Climbing Wall for sweeping views and friendly bragging rights, teens hit the arcade, and everyone gathers for poolside movies under the stars. Couples slip into quieter rhythms—cocktails in the Solarium, a romantic tasting at Chef’s Table, or a lingering dinner at Chops Grille. First-timers and solo travelers love the ship’s approachable scale: world-class entertainment and dining without feeling overwhelmed.

Dining flows as freely as the sea—casual favorites at Windjammer, sushi artistry at Izumi, and a classic Main Dining Room that still delivers “wow.” Evenings come alive with Royal Caribbean productions—showstoppers like Broadway Rhythm & Rhyme—plus live bands, comedians, and adults-only games. It’s that balanced signature: a bit of adrenaline by day; serene, starlit unwinds by night.

From Tampa, itineraries whisk you to Western Caribbean color—Cozumel’s reefs and Roatán’s beaches—or to Bahamian blues and off-the-beaten-path gems. In a world buzzing with mega-innovations, Grandeur proves that intimate adventures deliver premium value without the fuss. Guests rave about the attentive crew and personal touches—the kind that turn a good getaway into a great one.

A note from Ken’s Logbook — a personal review

We sailed Grandeur of the Seas in an ocean-view stateroom, and the natural light set the tone—bright mornings, blue horizons, and an easy, coastal calm. Service stood out from day one: Miguel (Philippines) at Chops Grille delivered outstanding, personable care (ask for him if you can); Vincent d’Cruz at Guest Services resolved a snag quickly and professionally; and Vergel in Windjammer noticed my mom was waiting on eggs, then found us and brought them the moment they were ready. That kind of attention is where Grandeur shines.

Dining highlights: the filet at Chops was fantastic. At Taste of Royal, the tuna tartare hit the spot, and the fried cheesecake stole the show. The shrimp could have used more seasoning, but the Saint Peter’s Spritzer was lovely—especially with the orange wedge squeezed in.

Our Eastern Caribbean route had a rainout in Labadee. The local vendors of arts and crafts are persistent but respectful—“no, thank you” works in any language. In Aruba was the star: we snorkeled near La Palma—clear water, flamingos, and solid bites onsite. Curaçao brought the distillery and a striking above-ground cave (dress for heat). Bonaire: we taxied to Buddy Dive, rented gear affordably (prices vary), and explored where the reef begins plan for deeper snorkel dives (20-25 meters, 60-80ft) or consider scuba. Evenings back onboard meant live music and shows—Tango Buenos Aires was our favorite for world-class dancers and vocals. Embark/debark in Fort Lauderdale was smooth; driving the port felt confusing and on port parking pricey. off-port plus a taxi may save money and frustration.

Overall? A great cruise on a ship with heart. Grandeur trades mega-ship flash for personal warmth—crew who notice, entertainers who connect, and sunsets that feel just for you.
