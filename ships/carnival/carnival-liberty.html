Overview

Carnival Liberty is a Conquest-class cruise ship that pioneered onboard entertainment innovation when she debuted in 2005 as the first vessel to feature the Seaside Theater—a massive poolside LED screen for movies under the stars. Built by Fincantieri in Monfalcone, Italy, she was christened by Academy Award-winning actress Mira Sorvino in Civitavecchia on July 19, 2005.

In 2011, Liberty made history again as the first ship to undergo Carnival's ambitious "Fun Ship 2.0" refurbishment program, testing new dining and entertainment concepts that would later spread fleet-wide. At 110,320 gross tons with capacity for nearly 3,000 guests, she offers the full Conquest-class experience with a proven track record of continuous improvement.

Liberty operates year-round from Port Canaveral, Florida, offering Eastern and Western Caribbean itineraries that make her a favorite among Florida cruisers.

Frequently Asked Questions

When was Carnival Liberty built?

Carnival Liberty was built by Fincantieri at their Monfalcone shipyard in Italy. She was christened by Academy Award-winning actress Mira Sorvino on July 19, 2005, in Civitavecchia, Italy.

What class is Carnival Liberty?

Carnival Liberty is the fourth of five Conquest-class ships, joining sisters Carnival Conquest (2002), Carnival Glory (2003), Carnival Valor (2004), and Carnival Freedom (2007). All were built by Fincantieri.

How big is Carnival Liberty?

Carnival Liberty measures 110,320 gross tons and is 952 feet (290 meters) long. She has capacity for 2,978 guests at double occupancy across 1,487 staterooms on 13 decks, with a crew of 1,150.

What is the Seaside Theater?

Carnival Liberty was the first ship to feature the Seaside Theater—a massive 12×22 foot LED screen located by the midship pool. It shows movies, concerts, and sporting events under the stars, and has since been added to other ships in the fleet.

Where does Carnival Liberty sail from?

Carnival Liberty operates year-round from Port Canaveral, Florida, offering Eastern and Western Caribbean itineraries. Her proximity to Orlando makes her popular with theme park visitors combining cruise and land vacations.
