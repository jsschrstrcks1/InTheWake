A Cruise Traveler’s Logbook

Vital Travel‑Doc Reminder

Keep passports/IDs, visas, boarding passes, and medications in your carry‑on. Store photos of your documents on your phone for emergencies. Print key contacts and your cruise‑line emergency number.

Tips

1) Plan around flights and liquid rules

Know your airline’s baggage limits. Liquids/gels in carry-ons must be ≤ 3.4 oz (100 ml) inside one quart-size bag.

2) Pack a smart carry‑on

Keep meds, IDs, a change of clothes, swimwear, and essentials with you. Bags can reach cabins hours after boarding.

3) Edit your checked luggage

Lay out everything, then remove duplicates. Split family items across bags so no one is left without clothing if a bag is lost.

4) Know dress codes

Most lines = resort casual at night; formal nights vary. Premium lines may be stricter—check your cruise line’s policy.

5) Use packing aids

Packing cubes and compression bags keep items organized and save space. Roll clothes to reduce creases.

6) Laundry options

Some ships offer self-serve or paid laundry. Hand‑washing works in a pinch; drying space is limited.

7) Toiletries reality check

Mainstream ships often provide only basic soap/shampoo. Bring your preferred conditioner, lotion, and skincare.

8) Pack for destination & activities

Research weather and cultural norms. Bring modest options for religious sites and footwear suited to excursions.

9) Leave room for souvenirs

Bring a foldable duffel for the trip home.

10) Re‑wear smartly

Mix and match tops with bottoms. Accessories refresh outfits without extra bulk.

11) Don’t forget the basics

OTC meds, batteries, memory cards, sunscreen, and adaptors are pricier onboard.

12) Keep IDs with you

Carry passports/IDs, visas, and immunization proof—never in checked bags.

13) Prohibited items

Items like irons, candles, power strips without surge protection, and weapons are typically banned. Check your cruise line.

14) Photo your documents

Store photos of passports and IDs on your phone. They’re invaluable if you need embassy assistance.

15) Emergency contacts

Carry printed contacts and cruise‑line emergency numbers.

16) PPE varies by port

Rules change. Check ports for any mask or health requirements.

17) Pack lighter than you think

Choose only what’s relevant to you. Most forgotten items can be bought onboard (markup) or in port (often cheaper).

18) Temperatures on board

Indoor temps are often low 70s °F (21–22 °C). If you run cold, bring a light layer. Check ports for climate.

Notes & Disclosures

  • Disability: Bring any adaptive gear or supplies you rely on; contact your cruise line for specific accommodation requests.
  • Airline vs. Cruise Limits: Airlines usually restrict luggage more than cruise lines. Pack to your flight limits.
  • Converters/Adapters: Needed if departing from or staying in international ports pre/post‑cruise.
