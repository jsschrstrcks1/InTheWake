Quick Answer: Belize is the port where you skip the city and go full adventure – cave tubing through bat-filled caves, Great Blue Hole scenic flights, and Mayan ruins. This is Indiana Jones territory.

Belize City: My Jungle & Reef Adventure Day

Tendering through the turquoise flats to Belize City with manatees popping up beside the boat is magical – this is the port where you forget the city and go full adventure. My perfect day: off the first tender and straight on an airplane to the Great Blue Hole (Lamanai Air or Tropic Air – $250 but life-changing) or cave tubing and zip-lining combo with Cavetubing.bz ($110 including lunch).

Lunch is always stew chicken, rice & beans with Marie Sharp's hot sauce. Afternoon floating down the Caves Branch River in an inner tube through bat-filled caves – coolest thing I've ever done. Back in time for a Belikin beer at the Wet Lizard before the last tender.

The Moment That Stays With Me: Floating silently through a pitch-black cave with only my headlamp, then emerging into sunlight with waterfalls everywhere – felt like Indiana Jones.

Getting Around Belize City

Tenders to Fort Street Tourism Village. All tours pick up right at the terminal. No need for taxis unless staying in town.

  • Cave Tubing: 45-min drive, all tours include transport
  • Great Blue Hole: Scenic flight from local airport
  • Altun Ha Ruins: 1-hour drive, great half-day option

Positively Worded Word of Warning

The tender and longer transfer times just build anticipation for the wildest adventure day of your cruise – Belize saves the best for those who go all in!
