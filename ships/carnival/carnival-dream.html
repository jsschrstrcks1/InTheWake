Carnival Dream — Deck Plans, Live Tracker, Dining & Videos

Looking for Carnival Dream planning info? This page covers deck plans, live ship tracking, dining venues, and video tours to help you plan your Carnival cruise.

Carnival Dream tends to suit travelers who want Dream-class innovation with the Fun Ship atmosphere. It's ideal if you want WaterWorks aqua park, Serenity adult retreat, and diverse specialty dining options. If you're looking for the newest mega-ship features like BOLT roller coaster, explore the Excel Class ships instead.

The Logbook — Tales From the Wake

Carnival Dream represents a pivotal moment in Carnival Cruise Line's history. When she entered service in September 2009, built at Fincantieri's Monfalcone shipyard in Italy, she was the largest ship Carnival had ever constructed — a title she held until her sister Carnival Magic arrived in 2011.

Dream pioneered several features that have since become Carnival signatures: the WaterWorks aqua park with its thrilling water slides, Serenity (the adults-only retreat), and SportSquare outdoor recreation area. She also introduced the Ocean Plaza — a stunning indoor/outdoor social hub that spans the ship's width.

Today, sailing primarily from Galveston, Texas, Carnival Dream continues to deliver the innovations that made her class-leading. Her 1,823 staterooms include many with private balconies, and her 14 passenger decks offer everything from Thrill Theater 4D experiences to the serenity of the Cloud 9 Spa. She remains an excellent choice for guests who want Dream-class features from a Texas homeport.

Frequently Asked Questions

What makes Carnival Dream unique compared to other Carnival ships?

Carnival Dream is the lead ship of the Dream Class and was Carnival's largest ship when she debuted in 2009. At 128,250 GT with 3,646 guests, she pioneered features like WaterWorks aqua park, Serenity adult-only retreat, and SportSquare outdoor fitness area that became standard on newer Carnival ships.

Which ships are in the same class as Carnival Dream?

Carnival Dream is the lead ship of the Dream Class, which includes Carnival Magic (2011) and Carnival Breeze (2012). Costa Diadema is also a Dream-class variant. All three Carnival ships share similar size and layouts with approximately 130,000 GT.

What dining options are available on Carnival Dream?

Carnival Dream offers multiple dining venues including the Crimson and Scarlet Main Dining Rooms, Lido Marketplace buffet, Guy's Burger Joint, BlueIguana Cantina, pizzeria, Cucina del Capitano Italian restaurant, Fahrenheit 555 steakhouse, and Bonsai Sushi.

Where does Carnival Dream typically sail?

Carnival Dream sails primarily from Galveston, Texas, offering Western Caribbean itineraries including Cozumel, Costa Maya, Roatan, and Belize.
