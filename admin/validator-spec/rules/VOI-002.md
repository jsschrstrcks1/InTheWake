## Rule
Content prose must not stack hedging phrases. One hedge per paragraph is human; two or more is a machine tell. The voice-audit skill scans for: "It's important to note that", "One might argue", "It's worth considering", "It seems like", "Perhaps", "It could be argued". Like-a-human says: "Kill the conditional voice — is/does/will, not might/could/may/tends to."

## Fix guidance
Replace hedges with direct assertions. "It's worth noting that Nassau can feel unsafe after dark" → "Nassau feels unsafe after dark east of Bay Street." The hedge was protecting the writer from commitment; the reader needs the commitment.
