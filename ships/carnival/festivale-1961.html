TSS Festivale

1978 — 1996  |  Carnival's Third Ship

Ex-Transvaal CastleScrapped 2003

Tribute page. TSS Festivale served Carnival 1978-1996 and was scrapped in 2003-2004.

~27,000 GT

Original
Transvaal Castle

Pioneer of Fun at Sea

Historic Significance: TSS Festivale introduced the first waterslide on a cruise ship — an early sign of Carnival's commitment to fun and entertainment that would define the brand.

Timeline

1961: Built as RMS Transvaal Castle for Union-Castle Line mail service to South Africa.
1977: Acquired by Carnival from South African Marine Corporation.
1978: Enters service as TSS Festivale after $30M refit in Japan. First waterslide at sea!
1996: Chartered to Dolphin Cruise Line as IslandBreeze.
1998: Becomes Big Red Boat III for Premier Cruises.
2000: Premier files bankruptcy; ship laid up.
2003-04: Scrapped at Alang, India.
