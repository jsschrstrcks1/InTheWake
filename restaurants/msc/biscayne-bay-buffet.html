Biscayne Bay Restaurant & Buffet

MSC Cruises — Casual Dining (MSC Seaside)

Quick Answer: MSC Seaside’s upper-deck buffet on Deck 16 Miami Beach. 430 seats, family-oriented, with a made-to-order pizzeria, fast-food stations, and the better view than the Marketplace Buffet on Deck 8. Breakfast and lunch on most days. Branded “Colonial Buffet & Pizzeria” at the 2017 launch. Included with cruise fare.

Best For: Families with kids who default to pizza and burgers, sea-day diners who want a less crowded buffet, breakfast-by-the-pool cruisers, and anyone who wants a quieter, more open buffet room than Marketplace can offer.

Key Facts

  • Ship: MSC Seaside
  • Deck: 16 (Miami Beach), near the main pool
  • Size: 430 seats, ~1,050 m²
  • Price: Complimentary (included in cruise fare)
  • Hours: Breakfast ~6:30–10:30 AM · Lunch ~12:00–14:30 · pizza/grill into mid-afternoon (dinner generally at Marketplace on Deck 8)
  • Dress Code: Casual; cover-up over swimwear preferred
  • Reservations: Walk-up
  • Launch name: Colonial Buffet & Pizzeria (2017)

The upper-deck buffet on MSC Seaside — smaller, calmer, family-oriented, with a made-to-order pizzeria and better views than the Marketplace Buffet downstairs. Once you find it, it becomes the sea-day lunch you actually prefer. Return to the Restaurants hub →

Menu & Prices

Price: Complimentary (included in cruise fare). Drinks beyond water, iced tea, lemonade, juice at breakfast, and basic coffee are billed separately or covered by your MSC drinks package.

The room is built around four food zones rather than one long line:

  • Pizzeria: pizza by the slice on the warming rail plus made-to-order pies in a dedicated oven. The pizza is the single best buffet item on MSC Seaside — thin crust, real char, fresh basil. Repeat travelers come here for it specifically.
  • Fast-food / grill station: burgers, hot dogs, panini, wraps. Built for the after-pool lunch.
  • Salad bar and fresh-fruit station: the smaller-but-honest alternative to the Marketplace buffet's bigger spread. Vegan station included.
  • Breakfast counter: made-to-order omelets, pastries, fruit, breakfast meats, cereals. The line stays shorter than upstairs through 8:00 AM.

Hours and station rotation vary by sailing. Confirm in the MSC for Me app onboard.

Special Accommodations

Allergen & Dietary Notes: Buffet allergen handling is harder than table-service, but MSC marks station signage with major allergens (gluten, dairy, nuts) and the kitchen will plate a fresh portion off the line on request — ask the station attendant rather than reaching from the warming tray. A dedicated vegan station runs daily. For severe allergies, the Seashore Restaurant downstairs is the safer option for breakfast and brunch — the maitre d' can pre-plate around the allergen.

Where You’ll Find It

Biscayne Bay Restaurant & Buffet exists on MSC Seaside only. Deck 16 Miami Beach, mid-to-aft, near the Miami Beach Pool and within a short walk of the specialty-dining cluster (Butcher’s Cut, Asian Market Kitchen, Ocean Cay). One layout note: there is no direct walk-through from the forward end of Deck 16 to the buffet — you may need to descend a level, walk aft, and ascend. Use the aft elevator bank to skip the detour. The sister MSC Seaview carries a similar upper-deck buffet on the same deck plan.

The Logbook — Real Guest Soundings

Depth Sounding: This is a composite account from multiple guest experiences across MSC Seaside sailings 2023–2025, edited to our venue standards for clarity. Individual sailings vary by ship, itinerary, and crew.

3.8 ★ out of 5 MSC Seaside • 2023–2025

Biscayne Bay: The Quieter Buffet Most MSC Seaside Cruisers Miss on Day 1

By Day 2, the savvy MSC Seaside cruiser learns there are two buffets. Marketplace on Deck 8 is the obvious one — bigger, busier, all the international stations, the one you find on Embarkation Day because it’s right where you arrive. Biscayne Bay is the one you have to be told about. Deck 16, Miami Beach area, near the pool. 430 seats. Smaller. Calmer. Better light. Once you know it exists, it becomes the sea-day lunch you actually prefer.

Food

The standout is the pizza. Multiple reviews flag the Biscayne Bay pizza as the best item on the entire MSC Seaside buffet program, ship-wide — thin crust, real char, fresh basil, made-to-order pies in addition to the slices on the warming rail. The pizzaiolo will customize on request, and the pies come out fast enough that asking for fresh rather than reaching for the tray is a no-cost upgrade. The burger-and-hot-dog grill is what it claims to be — serviceable, fast, the right answer for kids after the slides. The breakfast counter does made-to-order omelets at speed because the line is rarely as long as the Marketplace line is at the same time. The smoked salmon and European-cheese plate from Marketplace doesn’t cross over to Biscayne Bay; if those are what you came for, the Deck-8 buffet has them. Where Biscayne Bay falls short: the breadth is narrower, the seafood station is smaller, and dinner is generally not served here.

Service

Station-attended stations have crew on shift; the buffet line is mostly self-service with bussers clearing the dining room. The dining-room staff at Biscayne Bay tends to be the lighter rotation — you’ll see fewer of the same faces day-to-day than at Seashore or Marketplace, but the table-clearing is faster because the room is smaller. The pizzaiolo at the dedicated pie oven is the one staff position to remember by name — tip in cash at the end of the cruise if you’ve been there four or five times.

Atmosphere

The room reads as an indoor-outdoor lunch space rather than a cruise-ship cafeteria. Lighter colour palette than Marketplace, more windows, a smaller dining-room footprint that doesn’t echo when full. The outdoor seating annex faces the Miami Beach Pool deck, which is the pleasant version of a poolside lunch — less wind than Deck 19, less spray than the Waterfront Boardwalk on Deck 8. On a sea day, the 1:00 PM window catches good light and a manageable crowd. Layout note travelers complain about: there is no direct walk-through from the forward end of Deck 16. If you’re coming from the forward cabins, take the aft elevator bank rather than walking the full deck.

Pro Tips

  • Order the pizza made-to-order. Ask the pizzaiolo for a fresh pie rather than taking a slice off the warming rail. The wait is short and the pizza is meaningfully better.
  • Use Biscayne Bay for the sea-day lunch crush. Marketplace at 12:30 PM on a sea day is a queue; Biscayne Bay at the same time is half-full and the food is functionally the same plus the pizza.
  • Skip Biscayne Bay for dinner. It generally isn’t open for the evening service; Marketplace on Deck 8 is the dinner buffet, and the MDR is the better dinner anyway.
  • The forward-deck-16 layout quirk is real. If you’re in a forward cabin, plan to detour through the aft elevators or descend one deck and walk along Deck 15. It’s easier the second time.
  • Tip the pizzaiolo in cash at the end of the week. They’re making the best item on the ship from a buffet station; the recognition matters and the service the next sailing will be better for it.

Conclusion

Rating: 3.8/5. Biscayne Bay is the underused buffet on MSC Seaside — smaller than Marketplace, less crowded, with the best pizza on the ship and the better light for lunch. It loses marks for the narrower selection (the European-cheese and smoked-salmon spreads stay at Marketplace) and the forward-deck access quirk, but the pizza and the calmer crowd add up to the better choice for most sea-day lunches and many port-morning breakfasts. Tip: if you’ve never tried it, go for lunch on a sea day, order a fresh pizza from the oven, and sit at the window. You’ll wonder why you waited until Day 3.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Biscayne Bay Restaurant & Buffet on MSC Seaside?

Biscayne Bay is MSC Seaside's second buffet — the upper-deck one. It seats 430 in a 1,050 m² room on Deck 16 in the Miami Beach area near the main pool, with a family-oriented food layout that includes a pizzeria (pizza by the slice and made-to-order pies), fast-food stations (panini, wraps, burgers, hot dogs), a salad and fresh-fruit bar, and a vegan station. It opens for breakfast and lunch with virtually the same hot offerings as the Marketplace Buffet downstairs, but with shorter lines, a smaller crowd, and the better view.

Was Biscayne Bay called something else originally?

Yes. When MSC Seaside was announced and through her early sailings, this Deck 16 venue carried the working name “Colonial Buffet & Pizzeria.” The Biscayne Bay name aligns the venue with MSC Seaside's Miami-themed deck-naming scheme (Cape Cod, Copacabana, South Beach, Ocean Cay, Miami Beach) and is what guests will see on signage and in the MSC for Me app today. Older reviews on third-party sites may still use the launch name.

How is Biscayne Bay different from the Marketplace Buffet?

Marketplace Buffet is the main buffet — Deck 8 Ocean Cay, larger, busier, the only buffet open for dinner. Biscayne Bay is the upper-deck overflow buffet on Deck 16 — smaller (430 seats), family-oriented, with a pizzeria and fast-food stations rather than the full international station rotation. Biscayne Bay is the calmer breakfast and lunch room with a view; Marketplace is the bigger room and the dinner option. Many MSC Seaside cruisers default to Marketplace and never find Biscayne Bay — once you do, it becomes the quieter sea-day lunch.

Does Biscayne Bay serve dinner?

Usually no. Biscayne Bay is the breakfast and lunch buffet on most sailings — typically 6:30 AM to 10:30 AM for breakfast and noon to 2:30 PM for lunch, with the pizzeria and grill running into mid-afternoon. Marketplace Buffet on Deck 8 is the dinner buffet (until ~9:30 PM, with late-night pizza after that). Schedules adjust on embarkation, debarkation, and excursion days — confirm in the MSC for Me app for your sailing.

How much does Biscayne Bay cost?

Biscayne Bay is complimentary — included in your MSC cruise fare. The pizza, the grill items, the buffet stations, and the made-to-order omelets are all part of the included food program. Drinks beyond water, iced tea, lemonade, and basic coffee are billed separately or covered by your MSC drinks package.
