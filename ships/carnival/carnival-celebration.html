First Look

Carnival Celebration debuted in November 2022 as Carnival Cruise Line's 50th anniversary flagship. The second Excel-class vessel at 183,521 GT features LNG propulsion, BOLT roller coaster, and the exclusive Miami-inspired 820 Biscayne zone.

Homeported at PortMiami, she celebrates her namesake city with vibrant Cuban and Miami-inspired cuisine, entertainment, and design. Sister to Mardi Gras (2021) and Jubilee (2023), she shares the revolutionary Excel-class innovations while offering her own unique character.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Carnival Celebration the largest Carnival ship?

At 183,521 GT, Carnival Celebration is tied with Mardi Gras and Jubilee as Carnival's largest ships. All three are Excel-class vessels with LNG propulsion and similar layouts.

Where does Carnival Celebration sail from?

Carnival Celebration is homeported at PortMiami, Florida, offering 6-8 day cruises to Eastern Caribbean (San Juan, St. Thomas), Western Caribbean (Cozumel, Costa Maya), and Southern Caribbean destinations year-round.

What is 820 Biscayne?

820 Biscayne is a Miami-inspired zone exclusive to Carnival Celebration. Named for the famous Miami boulevard, it features Cuban and Latin cuisine, Miami-themed cocktails, and entertainment celebrating the ship's homeport city.

Who is the godmother of Carnival Celebration?

Cassidy Gifford, American actress and daughter of TV personality Kathie Lee Gifford, christened the ship on November 20, 2022. Her mother was godmother of the original MS Celebration in 1987.

Does Carnival Celebration have BOLT roller coaster?

Yes, Carnival Celebration features BOLT: Ultimate Sea Coaster, an electric-powered coaster reaching 40 mph on a track above the ship's top deck. Reservations recommended.

What makes Carnival Celebration different from Mardi Gras?

While sharing the same Excel-class platform, Carnival Celebration has the unique 820 Biscayne zone (Miami-themed) instead of Mardi Gras's French Quarter zone (New Orleans-themed). Each ship celebrates its homeport city.
