Overview

Carnival Mardi Gras is a revolutionary Excel-class cruise ship that redefined what's possible at sea. As the lead ship of Carnival's new flagship class, she debuted with groundbreaking innovations including BOLT—the first roller coaster at sea—and pioneering LNG propulsion technology. Built by Meyer Turku in Finland, she was delivered December 18, 2020, and entered service July 31, 2021.

Named to honor the original Mardi Gras (1972), Carnival's very first ship, this modern namesake represents a quantum leap in cruising. At 180,000 gross tons with capacity for 5,282 guests across six distinctive themed zones, she offers unprecedented variety and experiences.

Mardi Gras operates from Port Canaveral, Florida, offering Eastern and Western Caribbean itineraries that let guests experience her revolutionary features while exploring tropical destinations.

Signature Features

BOLT Roller Coaster

The first roller coaster at sea—800 feet of track, speeds up to 40 mph, 187 feet above the ocean.

LNG Propulsion

First LNG-powered cruise ship in the Americas with four Caterpillar MaK engines producing 57.2 MW.

Family Feud Live

Official "Family Feud" game show experience hosted in the theater.

WaterWorks

Massive aqua park with multiple waterslides and the AquaDunk free-fall slide.

Cloud 9 Spa

Full-service spa with thermal suites, Thalassotherapy pool, and Cloud 9 Spa staterooms.

Excel Suite

Massive 1,120 sq ft suite with wraparound balcony—the largest accommodations in Carnival history.

Frequently Asked Questions

When was Carnival Mardi Gras built?

Carnival Mardi Gras was built by Meyer Turku in Finland. Her keel was laid December 20, 2018, she floated out August 31, 2019, and was delivered December 18, 2020. She entered service July 31, 2021, after pandemic-related delays.

What class is Carnival Mardi Gras?

Carnival Mardi Gras is the lead ship of the Excel-class (Carnival's name for the Excellence-class). Sisters include Carnival Celebration (2022), Carnival Jubilee (2023), with Carnival Festivale (2027) and Carnival Tropicale (2028) on order.

Does Carnival Mardi Gras have a roller coaster?

Yes! BOLT is the first roller coaster at sea, located atop the Ultimate Playground zone. Riders race around 800 feet of track at speeds up to 40 mph, 187 feet above the ocean. It's an extra-fee attraction.

What makes Carnival Mardi Gras eco-friendly?

Mardi Gras is powered by LNG (liquefied natural gas), the first such cruise ship in the Americas. LNG produces significantly lower emissions than traditional marine fuel, including virtually no sulfur oxide and reduced nitrogen oxide and particulate matter.

Where does Carnival Mardi Gras sail from?

Carnival Mardi Gras operates from Port Canaveral, Florida, offering Eastern and Western Caribbean itineraries. Port Canaveral's proximity to Orlando makes her accessible for guests combining cruise and theme park vacations.
