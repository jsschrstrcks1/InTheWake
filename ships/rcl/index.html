Royal Caribbean Fleet

The vacation revolution spans 28 active ships across 8 distinct classes — from the record-breaking Icon Class (the world's largest cruise ships at 250,800 GT) to the glass-wrapped Radiance Class vessels purpose-built for Alaska's glaciers. Whether you're chasing adrenaline on the FlowRider, exploring seven neighborhoods on an Oasis-class giant, or savoring intimate sea days on a Vision-class explorer, Royal Caribbean delivers experiences that make you say "I can't believe this is on a cruise ship."

Below you'll find every ship organized by class, from the newest flagships to the beloved classics. Each class has its own personality, signature venues, and sweet spots for different travelers.

Royal Caribbean Guide Ship Quiz Packing Lists

Icon Class

The newest flagships — floating cities with neighborhoods in the sky. At 250,800 GT and 7,600 guests, these are the world's largest cruise ships. AquaDome shows, Surfside family zones, Category 6 waterpark, Central Park with real trees, and 40+ dining and drinking options. The pinnacle of Royal Caribbean innovation.

Signature venues: AquaDome, Central Park, Surfside, Category 6 Waterpark, The Pearl, Thrill Island

  • Icon of the Seas
  • Star of the Seas
  • Legend of the Seas (2026)
  • Icon Class TBN (2027)
  • Icon Class TBN (2028)

Oasis Class

The original gamechangers that redefined cruising. Seven neighborhoods, AquaTheater diving shows, and the Boardwalk's hand-carved carousel. At 225,000+ GT and 5,400–6,600 guests, these mega-ships deliver jaw-dropping variety without feeling crowded. Perfect for families who want "wow" with their wake.

Signature venues: AquaTheater, Boardwalk, Central Park, Royal Promenade, Ultimate Abyss, FlowRider

  • Utopia of the Seas
  • Wonder of the Seas
  • Symphony of the Seas
  • Harmony of the Seas
  • Allure of the Seas
  • Oasis of the Seas
  • Oasis Class TBN (2028)

Quantum Ultra Class

Quantum evolved for tropics and nightlife. SeaPlex glow parties, iFly skydiving, and North Star pods — same tech-forward DNA with warmer itineraries. At 169,000 GT and 4,900 guests, these ships bring Quantum innovation to Caribbean and Mediterranean routes.

Signature venues: SeaPlex, North Star, Sky Pad, Playmakers Sports Bar, Music Hall

  • Odyssey of the Seas
  • Spectrum of the Seas
  • QU Class TBN (2028)
  • QU Class TBN (2029)

Quantum Class

Technology meets exploration. North Star observation pods, RipCord skydiving, robotic bartenders at Bionic Bar. At 168,000 GT and 4,900 guests, smart weather flexibility meets surprising serenity. These ships pioneered the tech-forward cruising revolution.

Signature venues: North Star, RipCord by iFly, Bionic Bar, Two70, SeaPlex

  • Ovation of the Seas
  • Anthem of the Seas
  • Quantum of the Seas

Freedom Class

The bridge between classic and colossal. FlowRider, promenade parades, and family-friendly scale without mega-ship crowds. At 154,000 GT and 3,600 guests, these proven favorites deliver spirited decks and excellent value.

Signature venues: FlowRider, Royal Promenade, H2O Zone, Sabor, Giovanni's Table

  • Independence of the Seas
  • Liberty of the Seas
  • Freedom of the Seas

Voyager Class

The trailblazers. First to skate at sea and stroll a true promenade. At 137,000 GT and 3,100 guests, these ships deliver a condensed mega-ship experience with warmth. Perfect for first-timers who want ice shows without overwhelming scale.

Signature venues: Royal Promenade, Ice Skating Rink, Rock Climbing Wall, Studio B, Schooner Bar

  • Mariner of the Seas
  • Navigator of the Seas
  • Adventure of the Seas
  • Explorer of the Seas
  • Voyager of the Seas

Radiance Class

Glass-wrapped and purpose-built for scenery. Excels on Alaska glaciers and coastal itineraries. At 90,000 GT and 2,100 guests, bright atriums, Solarium calm, and excellent service meet smaller crowds and larger windows.

Signature venues: Solarium, Chops Grille, Izumi, Viking Crown Lounge, Rock Climbing Wall

  • Jewel of the Seas
  • Serenade of the Seas
  • Brilliance of the Seas
  • Radiance of the Seas

Vision Class

The fleet's explorers — smaller, older, deeply loved. At 78,000 GT and 2,400 guests, intimacy and itinerary matter more than bells and whistles. Ideal for port-intensive voyages where connection matters most.

Signature venues: Viking Crown Lounge, Schooner Bar, Solarium, Centrum

  • Vision of the Seas
  • Enchantment of the Seas
  • Grandeur of the Seas

Star Class (Future)

Details emerging. Royal Caribbean's next frontier in ship design, expected to push boundaries once again.
