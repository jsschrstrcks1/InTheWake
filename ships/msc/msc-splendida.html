MSC Splendida — Deck Plans, Live Tracker, Dining & Videos

MSC Splendida is the second ship of MSC Cruises' Fantasia Class, ordered in June 2005 and delivered on 4 July 2009 at Saint-Nazaire by STX France (the yard had been renamed from Aker Yards the previous October; STX France is period-accurate for her 2009 delivery). She was christened eight days later on 12 July 2009 in Barcelona by Sophia Loren, with tenor José Carreras performing. She measures 137,936 gross tons and 333.30 metres in length, carries 3,274 guests at double occupancy (4,363 maximum) across 1,637 cabins with a crew of approximately 1,370, and flies the Panamanian flag. Her two main dining rooms are La Reggia and Villa Verde, her theatre is The Strand Theatre, her casino is the Royal Palm Casino, and a full MSC Yacht Club enclave on Decks 14/15/18 has been aboard from delivery with 71 suites and the L'Olivo Restaurant. Her most recent drydock (10 January – 7 February 2026 at Palumbo Malta Shipyard, Valletta) refreshed the MSC Yacht Club Restaurant with a modern redesign and updated carpets, upholstery, and Future Cruise offices fleet-wide.

Quick Answer: MSC Splendida (137,936 GT, 3,274 double / 4,363 max, ~1,370 crew, delivered 2009) is the second Fantasia Class ship — direct sister to MSC Fantasia. She carries two main dining rooms (La Reggia and Villa Verde), two buffets (Bora Bora and Pago Pago on Deck 14), Butcher's Cut, Santa Fe Tex Mex, and Dim Sum Bar & Tea House specialty restaurants, the 4D Cinema, MSC Formula Racer F1 simulator, and a full MSC Yacht Club with 71 suites, the L'Olivo Restaurant, and the panoramic Top Sail Lounge. Her main theatre is The Strand Theatre, her casino is the Royal Palm Casino, and her pools include the Aqua Park, the covered L'Equatore Indoor Pool, and the private Yacht Club One Pool. Mediterranean summer 2026 from Barcelona; Rio de Janeiro / Buenos Aires winter 2026-27.

Who She's For: European cruisers wanting a proper mega-ship out of Barcelona or Marseille in summer, South American travellers for winter sailings from Rio de Janeiro or Buenos Aires, and suite travellers drawn to the 71-suite MSC Yacht Club with a freshly-redesigned L'Olivo restaurant (refreshed in January-February 2026). Families using the Aqua Park, 4D Cinema, and F1 simulator. Not for cruisers expecting Cirque du Soleil at Sea (Meraviglia-class only) or the La Palmeraie Yacht Club restaurant (that's on MSC Preziosa — Splendida uses L'Olivo).

MSC Splendida is the second of four ships in the Fantasia Class — together with her direct sister MSC Fantasia (2008), plus the later 'modified Fantasia' pair MSC Divina (2012) and MSC Preziosa (2013) — but note that strictly speaking, only MSC Fantasia is Splendida's direct sister (same 137,936 GT, same 1,637-cabin hull). The later Divina/Preziosa pair are larger 'modified' derivatives at 139,072 GT with 1,751 cabins. Splendida was ordered in June 2005, built at Saint-Nazaire in 27 months from first steel cut, and delivered on 4 July 2009 by STX France. Her naming ceremony took place eight days later on 12 July 2009 in Barcelona, with Sophia Loren as godmother and tenor José Carreras performing. Each Fantasia-class ship uses unique MDR, theatre, and Yacht Club restaurant names: Splendida's main theatre is The Strand Theatre (distinct from Fantasia's Teatro L'Avanguardia, Divina's Pantheon Theatre, and Preziosa's Platinum Theatre); her MDRs are La Reggia and Villa Verde; her Yacht Club restaurant is L'Olivo — the same L'Olivo name is shared with sister MSC Fantasia, making L'Olivo the Yacht Club restaurant signature of the original Fantasia pair (Divina and Preziosa use different Yacht Club restaurant names; Preziosa has the Moroccan-themed La Palmeraie). Her specialty dining on Deck 7 is the trio Butcher's Cut, Santa Fe Tex Mex, and Dim Sum Bar & Tea House. Her most recent drydock, at Palumbo Malta Shipyard in Valletta from 10 January to 7 February 2026, included routine class inspections plus a modern redesign of the Yacht Club L'Olivo Restaurant with darker colours, soft textures, and updated lighting (the restaurant name was retained). Splendida resumed service on 7 February 2026.

Entertainment & Activities on MSC Splendida

Entertainment on MSC Splendida is built around The Strand Theatre (Decks 6-7) — a two-level production theatre unique to Splendida among the Fantasia class. The Deck 6 public-room axis adds the Royal Palm Casino, the Royal Palm Casino Bar, La Piazzetta indoor plaza, L'Aperitivo Bar, and The Cigar Lounge. Deck 7 is the dining and entertainment spine with the specialty-dining trio (Butcher's Cut, Santa Fe Tex Mex, Dim Sum Bar & Tea House), L'Enoteca wine bar, L'Espresso Coffee Bar, La Prua Piano Bar, The Purple Jazz Bar, The Aft Lounge, Sport Fun Bar, Bowling Alley, and Galleria D'Arte. Deck 14 is the pool and spa deck with the Bora Bora Buffet and Pago Pago Buffet, the Aqua Park, the covered L'Equatore Indoor Pool, the MSC Aurea Spa, and multiple outdoor bars (Bar del Riccio, L'Equatore Bar, Tartaruga Bar). Deck 15 adds the Children's Pool, Playa del Sol whirlpool area, Power Walking Track, Movida Bar, and the kids' clubs. Deck 16 is the topmost entertainment level with Club 33, 4D Cinema, MSC Formula Racer (F1 simulator), Virtual World arcade, MSC Arena multi-sport court, squash court, and shuffleboard. Deck 18 holds the private MSC Yacht Club sundeck with The One Pool, The One Bar, and the Top 18 Exclusive Solarium. Note: MSC Splendida does NOT carry Cirque du Soleil at Sea (Meraviglia-class only).

MSC Splendida Deck Plans

MSC Splendida's 18-deck layout uses a Fantasia-class plan with dining and entertainment on the lower and mid decks and pools and recreation topping the ship. Deck 5 holds the Splendida Bar atrium and the lower La Reggia Restaurant. Deck 6 adds the upper La Reggia Restaurant, Villa Verde Restaurant, Royal Palm Casino, La Piazzetta, L'Aperitivo Bar, The Cigar Lounge, the lower Strand Theatre, and the Video Games Arcade. Deck 7 is the specialty-dining and lounge cluster with Butcher's Cut, Santa Fe Tex Mex, Dim Sum Bar & Tea House, L'Enoteca, L'Espresso Coffee Bar, La Prua Piano Bar, The Purple Jazz Bar, The Aft Lounge, Sport Fun Bar, Bowling, Galleria D'Arte, and the outdoor Promenade Deck. Deck 14 is the pool deck with Bora Bora and Pago Pago buffets, the Aqua Park, the covered L'Equatore Indoor Pool, MSC Aurea Spa, Spa Bar, and the outdoor Bar del Riccio, L'Equatore Bar, and Tartaruga Bar. Deck 15 adds the Children's Pool, Playa del Sol whirlpools, Power Walking Track, Movida Bar, and the forward MSC Yacht Club enclave (Concierge Area, Library, Top Sail Lounge, L'Olivo Restaurant). Deck 16 holds Club 33, 4D Cinema, MSC Formula Racer, Virtual World, MSC Arena, squash, and shuffleboard. Deck 18 is the private MSC Yacht Club sundeck with The One Pool, The One Bar, and the Top 18 Exclusive Solarium.

Frequently Asked Questions About MSC Splendida

What dining options are on MSC Splendida?

MSC Splendida has two main dining rooms — La Reggia Restaurant (Decks 5-6) and Villa Verde Restaurant (Deck 6) — plus two buffets on Deck 14: Bora Bora Buffet and Pago Pago Buffet. Specialty dining on Deck 7 includes Butcher's Cut steakhouse, Santa Fe Tex Mex, and the Dim Sum Bar & Tea House (Splendida's Asian venue — there is no separate Kaito Sushi Bar on Splendida). The L'Espresso Coffee Bar serves Italian espresso on Deck 7. Yacht Club suite guests have the private L'Olivo Restaurant on Deck 15 — the L'Olivo name is shared with sister MSC Fantasia, while the later modified-Fantasia ships Divina and Preziosa use different Yacht Club restaurant names. L'Olivo was refreshed with a modern redesign during the January-February 2026 Palumbo Malta drydock.

Which ships are in the same class as MSC Splendida?

MSC Splendida is the second ship of the Fantasia Class, together with her direct sister MSC Fantasia (2008). MSC Divina (2012) and MSC Preziosa (2013) form a separate 'modified Fantasia' sub-group at 139,072 GT with 1,751 cabins (vs Splendida's 137,936 GT and 1,637 cabins) — they share design DNA but are not direct sisters. Each Fantasia-class ship uses unique MDR, theatre, and Yacht Club restaurant names: Splendida's main theatre is The Strand Theatre, distinct from Fantasia's Teatro L'Avanguardia, Divina's Pantheon Theatre, and Preziosa's Platinum Theatre.

Does MSC Splendida have the MSC Yacht Club?

Yes. MSC Splendida has carried a full MSC Yacht Club ship-within-a-ship enclave from her 2009 delivery — the concept itself debuted on sister ship MSC Fantasia in 2008 and was built into Splendida from day one. The enclave on Decks 14/15/18 contains 71 Yacht Club suites (cabins 15001-15035 and 16001-16035 per the current deck plan), the panoramic Top Sail Lounge on Deck 15, a private Concierge Area and Library, the L'Olivo Restaurant on Deck 15 (refreshed in the January-February 2026 Palumbo Malta drydock), private access to a section of the MSC Aurea Spa on Deck 14, and a Deck 18 private sundeck with The One Pool, The One Bar, and the Top 18 Exclusive Solarium.

Where does MSC Splendida sail in 2026?

In spring and summer 2026 MSC Splendida runs 7-night Western Mediterranean round-trips from Barcelona calling at Marseille, Genoa, Naples, Palermo, and Tunis. As of 11 April 2026 VesselFinder shows her approaching Barcelona to complete such a cruise. For winter 2026-27, MSC Cruises announced in February 2026 that Splendida will reposition to South America, homeporting out of Rio de Janeiro and Buenos Aires for 8- and 9-night itineraries to Brazil, Argentina, and Uruguay. Her most recent drydock was at Palumbo Shipyard in Valletta, Malta from 10 January to 7 February 2026, where the MSC Yacht Club Restaurant was refreshed and carpets, upholstery, and Future Cruise offices were updated.

Who christened MSC Splendida?

MSC Splendida was christened on 12 July 2009 in Barcelona by Sophia Loren — MSC Cruises' long-standing godmother. The ceremony featured a performance by tenor José Carreras. She had been delivered eight days earlier, on 4 July 2009, by STX France at Saint-Nazaire (STX Europe had acquired the former Aker Yards in October 2008, so STX France is the period-accurate yard name for this 2009 delivery). She was ordered in June 2005, built in 27 months from first steel cut, and came at a cost of approximately USD 550 million. She is registered in Panama and carries IMO number 9359806.
