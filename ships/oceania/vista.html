Vista — Deck Plans, Live Tracker, Dining & Videos

Vista is an Allura Class cruise ship operated by Oceania Cruises. She entered service in 2023, measures 67,000 gross tons, and carries approximately 1,200 guests at double occupancy with a crew of 800.

Key Facts

  • Class: Allura Class
  • Year: 2023
  • Tonnage: 67,000 GT
  • Guests: 1,200
  • Crew: 800
  • IMO: 9876957

Quick Answer: Vista is Oceania's first new-generation ship — 67,000 GT, 1,200 guests, launched in 2023. She introduces the Allura Class with refreshed public spaces, new dining concepts, and the line's most spacious suites. This page covers deck plans, live tracking, dining venues, and videos.

Who She's For: Oceania fans who want the newest ship with the latest design. Vista suits food-focused travelers who value new-ship condition and want Oceania's culinary pedigree with updated public spaces and a refreshed dining lineup.

Vista represents Oceania's first new-build in over a decade, evolving the Oceania Class blueprint with fresh design and slightly fewer guests (1,200 vs. 1,250). She retains the culinary DNA — specialty restaurants included in the fare, no formal nights, destination-rich itineraries — while adding new venue concepts. If you want the proven Oceania Class experience, Marina and Riviera remain excellent. If you want Oceania's most intimate ships, the R Class (684 guests) offers a different feel entirely. Allura (2025) will be Vista's identical sister.

Frequently Asked Questions About Vista

What dining options are on Vista?

Vista has the most dining venues in the Oceania fleet: Grand Dining Room, Toscana, Polo Grill, Red Ginger, Ember (new American-inspired, Allura Class exclusive), Aquamar Kitchen (wellness), Terrace Café, Waves Grill. All included. See Dining above.

How big is Vista?

67,000 GT, 1,200 guests, 800 crew. Lead ship of the Allura Class (2023). Sister Allura follows in 2025.

What's new on Vista compared to Marina/Riviera?

Vista introduces Ember and Aquamar Kitchen as new dining concepts not on the O Class. She's slightly smaller (1,200 vs 1,250 guests) with refreshed public spaces and the most spacious suites in the fleet.

Where does Vista sail?
