Giovanni’s Italian — Kitchen vs. Table

Two takes on trattoria comfort. One handy guide.

Giovanni’s comes in two flavors: the newer Giovanni’s Italian Kitchen (pizza-forward, modern trattoria) and the classic Giovanni’s Table (family-style, old-world plates). Use this page to compare menus, prices, ships, and which one fits your cruise night.

Kitchen or Table: which suits your night?

Pizza people & grazers → Kitchen

Hand-tossed pies, antipasti, and modern twists. Lunch on sea days is the sleeper value.

Occasion dinner & sharers → Table

Old-world classics in bigger, family-style portions—great for slow, celebratory meals.

Kids & groups

Both work. Kitchen runs louder & quicker; Table feels cozier for date night.

Reservations & value tips

  • Book early: Prime dinner slots go fast—especially on Oasis/Icon.
  • Dining packages: “3-Night” or “Unlimited” can drop per-meal cost 30–40%.
  • Lunch plays: On sea days, Kitchen lunch (~$15) is a superb value.
  • Families: Ask for kids’ menus; shareable plates stretch value.
  • Allergies: Kitchens can adjust many dishes (gluten-free pasta on request).

What cruisers say (2024–2025)

Italian Kitchen — ~4.3/5

  • Loved: Pizzas, lobster ravioli, gigantic meatball; casual vibe.
  • Meh: Portion size vs. price on some apps; occasional pasta doneness misses.

Giovanni’s Table — ~4.3/5

  • Loved: Hearty family-style plates, osso buco, cozy ambiance.
  • Meh: Can feel heavy; pacing slow on peak nights.

Our take: Kitchen for lively, pizza-forward nights; Table for classic, share-heavy dinners.

FAQ

Is either venue included?

Both are specialty dining with a cover charge. Dining packages lower the effective price. Kids often have reduced pricing.

Which one is on my ship?

Newer and amplified ships tend to feature Italian Kitchen; many legacy ships keep Giovanni’s Table. Check your Cruise Planner.

Dress code?

Smart casual. No jacket required. Formal nights welcome a dressier look.
