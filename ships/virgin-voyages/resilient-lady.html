Resilient Lady — Deck Plans, Live Tracker, Dining & Videos

Resilient Lady is the third of Virgin Voyages' four Lady Class ships, delivered by Fincantieri in Genoa on 7 December 2022 after an 11-month delay from her original August 2022 slot. At 110,000 gross tons she carries 2,770 guests at double occupancy (2,860 maximum) and a crew of 1,160. Like all Virgin Voyages ships she is strictly adults-only (18+), with over 20 included restaurants and cafés in place of a traditional main dining room or buffet. As of April 2026 she runs year-round 7-night Western Caribbean round-trips from PortMiami.

Strictly 18+, all dining and basic beverages included.

Best For: Adults and couples looking for a 7-night Western Caribbean round-trip from Miami with Bimini Beach Club (Virgin's private beach club), on a ship that has logged one of the most storied deployment histories in the Virgin fleet (Greek Isles inaugural, Australia/NZ, Mediterranean, Puerto Rico, now Caribbean). If you want an adults-only cruise with none of the traditional cruise conventions — no buffet, no formal nights, no children, no upcharge for dining — Resilient Lady is the Virgin ship currently closest to most US travellers.

Resilient Lady is hardware-identical to her three Lady Class sisters: the same 110,000 GT hull, the same 1,408 cabins, the same Fincantieri Sestri Ponente yard, the same 20+ all-included restaurants. Her differentiators are history and deployment: she was Virgin Voyages' inaugural Greek Isles ship (14 May 2023), Virgin's first ship to sail Australia and New Zealand (Sydney arrival 4 December 2023, with Sir Richard Branson in attendance), and Virgin's first trans-Indian-Ocean repositioning (Athens → Melbourne). Virgin cancelled her planned 2024-25 Australia season on 26 February 2024, citing Red Sea / Suez Canal security (Houthi attacks) making the long repositioning unviable. After a 2024 Mediterranean season from Piraeus and a winter 2024-25 season from San Juan, she is now homeported at PortMiami for her winter 2025-26 and spring 2026 Caribbean season. Signature on-board events include Scarlet Night (fleet-wide red party), the Runway fashion show, and Drag Brunch. Gratuities, basic WiFi, soft drinks, still/sparkling water, and group fitness classes are all included in the fare.

A First Look at Resilient Lady

Who Resilient Lady Is For

You'll love Resilient Lady if: you want a 7-night Western Caribbean round-trip from PortMiami with Bimini Beach Club as the private island call; you want an adults-only cruise with none of the traditional cruise conventions — no buffet, no formal nights, no children, no upcharge for dining; you like the idea of 20+ included restaurants and cafés instead of a main dining room; you're drawn to the Virgin festival/club atmosphere (The Manor nightclub, Scarlet Night party, Drag Brunch); and you want a Virgin ship that has sailed more of the world than her sisters — Greek Isles, Australia, Mediterranean, Puerto Rico, Caribbean — while still being hardware-identical to them.

You may prefer a different ship if: you are travelling with children (Virgin Voyages is strictly 18+); you want a traditional main dining room experience with set seatings; you want Broadway-style production shows (Resilient Lady's Red Room runs immersive theatre instead); you were specifically hoping for an Australia/NZ cruise (Virgin cancelled that programme in February 2024); you want the Panama Canal and West Coast option (only Brilliant Lady has the adapted frame for that); or you want Valiant Lady's new Ariya Indian restaurant (that is Valiant-exclusive from 25 May 2026).

The Logbook: Resilient Lady (Lady Class)

Virgin Voyages Lady Class — Sister Ships

Resilient Lady is the third of four Lady Class ships, all built by Fincantieri at its Sestri Ponente yard in Genoa. The sisters share an identical hull, propulsion, and cabin layout (1,408 cabins, 2,770 guests, 1,160 crew) — they differ mainly in deployment, yard number, and a few per-ship venue exclusives.

  • Scarlet Lady — Lead ship, IMO 9804801, yard 6287, delivered 14 February 2020. Caribbean from PortMiami, Mediterranean summer. Scarlet-unique: Lucky Lotus by Razzle Dazzle Chinese evening concept.
  • Valiant Lady — Second ship, IMO 9805336, yard 6288, delivered 1 July 2021. US East Coast / Mediterranean. Valiant-unique (from 25 May 2026 after drydock): Ariya — Virgin's first Indian restaurant at sea, by chef Maneet Chauhan.
  • Resilient Lady — Third ship, IMO 9805348, yard 6289, delivered 7 December 2022. Currently Caribbean from PortMiami after Virgin cancelled her 2024-25 Australia programme in February 2024. Sailed Greek Isles (2023 inaugural) and Australia (2023-24, one season only).
  • Brilliant Lady — Fourth ship, IMO 9870654, yard 6304, entered commercial service 5 September 2025 after an 18-month lay-up. Brilliant-unique: the only Lady with a Panama-Canal-capable adapted frame, enabling West Coast and Alaska sailings. Currently on Virgin's first West Coast deployment ex-Los Angeles (from 6 April 2026), repositioning to Seattle for Virgin's first Alaska season in May 2026.

All four ships are strictly 18+ adults-only, with 20+ included restaurants, the Manor nightclub, Richard's Rooftop, Squid Ink tattoo parlor, Voyage Vinyl record store, and included gratuities/WiFi/fitness classes. None have Cirque du Soleil — Virgin has never had that partnership and uses in-house production shows.

Frequently Asked Questions About Resilient Lady

What dining options are available on Resilient Lady?

Resilient Lady has over 20 included restaurants and cafés — not a traditional main dining room or buffet. Complimentary specialty venues include The Wake (steakhouse, raw bar, tableside martini service), Pink Agave (contemporary Mexican with Tom Dixon interiors and a fully gluten-free menu), Extra Virgin (Italian), Gunbae (Korean BBQ with round griddle tables), Razzle Dazzle (vegetable-forward breakfast, brunch, and dinner), and Test Kitchen (chef's experimental tasting menu inspired by Escoffier). Quick-serve options include The Pizza Place, The Galley food hall, Sun Club Cafe, and Grounds Club. All dining is included at no extra charge. Note: Resilient Lady is hardware-identical to her three sisters — the only class differentiators are the Scarlet-exclusive Lucky Lotus Chinese concept (on Scarlet Lady only) and the Valiant-exclusive Ariya Indian restaurant (on Valiant Lady from May 2026).

Is Resilient Lady adults-only?

Yes — strictly. Resilient Lady is adults-only: every guest must be 18 years of age or older at time of sailing. Virgin Voyages has applied this policy across all four Lady Class ships since launch. There are no children's clubs, no kids' menus, no family staterooms, and no waterslides. The adult-only policy is the central design choice of the Virgin Voyages concept.

Does Resilient Lady have Cirque du Soleil?

No. Virgin Voyages has never had a Cirque du Soleil partnership. Resilient Lady's entertainment is produced in-house in collaboration with creative partners including House of Yes (The Manor nightclub) and theatre producers such as Randy Weiner. Productions in The Red Room include Persephone, Booked!, Another Rose, Duel Reality, and signature immersive experiences. Signature ship-wide events include Scarlet Night, the once-per-sailing red-themed party — this is a fleet-wide class event, not a Scarlet-Lady exclusive.

Who was Resilient Lady's godmother?

Resilient Lady does not have a traditional christening godmother. Virgin Voyages has broken with cruise-industry convention and does not hold formal christening ceremonies. Resilient Lady was keel-laid on 27 April 2017 at Fincantieri Sestri Ponente, floated out on 2 July 2021, and formally delivered on 7 December 2022 after an 11-month delay. Her maiden voyage was a 7-night 'Greek Island Glow' from Piraeus on 14 May 2023 — Virgin held a celebratory launch event but no christening ceremony. In March 2022, Virgin appointed Jennifer Lopez as fleet-wide "Chief Entertainment and Lifestyle Officer" — this is a brand-partnership title, not a ship godmother role.

Where does Resilient Lady sail in 2026?

As of April 2026, Resilient Lady is sailing 7-night Western Caribbean round-trips from PortMiami (Terminal V), calling at Bimini Beach Club (Virgin's private beach club in The Bahamas), Cozumel, and alternating calls at Roatán (Honduras), Costa Maya (Mexico), or Puerto Plata / Amber Cove (Dominican Republic). April 2026 departures: 4, 11, 18, and 25 April. She is NOT an Australia/Asia-Pacific ship as of 2026. Although Resilient Lady completed one Australia/NZ season from Melbourne (11 December 2023 – 27 March 2024, with a Sydney Harbour arrival on 4 December 2023), Virgin Voyages cancelled the planned 2024-25 Australian programme on 26 February 2024 citing Red Sea / Suez Canal security concerns (Houthi attacks) making the long repositioning unviable. Her subsequent seasons have been Mediterranean (2024), San Juan Puerto Rico (winter 2024-25), and Miami (winter 2025-26 and spring 2026).

What is included in a Resilient Lady fare?

Every Virgin Voyages fare on Resilient Lady includes: all 20+ restaurants and cafés (no cover charges), all gratuities, basic WiFi, still and sparkling water, non-pressed juices, sodas, and group fitness classes at the Athletic Club. Not included: premium WiFi tiers, specialty coffees and alcoholic beverages, Bar Tab drink packages, spa treatments, and shore excursions. Virgin's "Sailor Loot" shipboard credit promotions are frequently offered at booking.
