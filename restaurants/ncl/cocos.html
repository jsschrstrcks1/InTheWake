Coco's Review: Tropical Drinks in a Beachy Setting

Introduction. Coco's is a tropical-themed bar found on select Norwegian ships, serving island-inspired cocktails in a beach-bar setting. It's positioned as a step up from the pool bar in terms of cocktail variety, though still firmly casual. Drinks are standard pricing or covered by the beverage package.

Drinks & Service

The tropical menu is the draw here. The Coco Loco (coconut rum, coconut cream, pineapple, lime) is the house specialty and it's genuinely good — creamy without being cloying. The Painkiller is another strong choice: dark rum, pineapple, orange, coconut cream, with a nutmeg dusting. The Blue Lagoon is for Instagram more than flavor. Beyond the tropicals, it's the same NCL bar menu you've seen everywhere. Draft beer is ice-cold, which matters when you're sitting in the sun. The fruit garnishes are fresh. Ask for "less sweet" on any frozen drink — it makes a noticeable difference.

Service

Service is casual and friendly — the bartenders lean into the island vibe and are generally enthusiastic about the tropical menu. Speed is good; the bar doesn't get as overwhelmed as the main pool bar. During sea days, expect a short wait but nothing excessive. The staff will customize sweetness levels if you ask, which is a nice touch.

Atmosphere

Tiki torches (LED, not real), bamboo accents, palm-leaf décor, and rattan seating — Coco's commits to the island bar aesthetic. It's a step above the pool bar in terms of vibe: more relaxed, slightly fewer kids, better seating. On Bliss and Breakaway, it's tucked away enough to feel like a discovery. The music leans reggae and steel drum, played at conversation-friendly volume. Sunset hours are the sweet spot — the tropical décor actually matches the ocean views. The seats fill up during Caribbean itineraries, so grab one early on sea days.

Conclusion

Rating: 3.5/5. Coco's delivers a fun tropical bar experience — the Coco Loco and Painkiller are worth seeking out, and the beachy atmosphere is a nice escape from the main bar scene. It's limited by the same fleet-wide beer and wine list, and the non-tropical drinks are nothing special. Tip: Ask for your cocktail "less sweet" — the bartenders are used to this request and it improves most tropical drinks significantly.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Coco's on Norwegian Cruise Line?

Cross between ice cream parlor and candy shop — chocolates, milkshakes, crepes, mini chocolate fountain, and pralines. Open day and night.

How much does Coco's cost?

Pricing varies — check the Norwegian Cruise Line app or NCL app for current Coco's pricing.

What is the dress code for Coco's?

Come as you are. Coco's is a casual venue — pool attire and casual clothes are fine.

Is Coco's included in drink packages?

Most drinks at Coco's are covered by Norwegian Cruise Line's Premium Beverage Package. Premium and specialty selections may have an upcharge. Non-alcoholic options are available.

What are the signature drinks at Coco's?

Coco's features Mad Milkshakes (Dark Chocolate Oreo, Snickers Bar, Fudge and Cheesecake), Chocolate Fondue for Two, Celebration Platter (gelato, ice cream, fruit, candy — serves 3-4), Assorted Bonbons and Pralines, and more. Ask the bartender for the house specialty.
