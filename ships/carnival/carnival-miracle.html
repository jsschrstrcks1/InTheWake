A First Look at Carnival Miracle

Carnival Miracle offers a memorable cruise experience as the final Spirit-class ship in Carnival's fleet. Christened by Jessica Lynch in 2004, she combines the intimate feel of an 88,500 GT vessel with modern Fun Ship 2.0 upgrades including Guy's Burger Joint, BlueIguana Cantina, and the Alchemy Bar.

Ship Overview

Carnival Miracle is the 4th and final Spirit-class ship, christened by Jessica Lynch — the retired U.S. Army Private renowned for her dramatic rescue during the Iraq War — in Jacksonville, Florida in 2004.

As the culmination of the Spirit-class, Miracle combines intimate ambiance with classic Fun Ship amenities. Her 2015 Fun Ship 2.0 upgrade added Guy's Burger Joint, BlueIguana Cantina, Red Frog Rum Bar, and Alchemy Bar. She now sails Alaska from Seattle and Caribbean from Tampa and Galveston.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the dining options on Carnival Miracle?

Carnival Miracle offers the Bacchus Restaurant two-level main dining room with Your Time Dining flexibility. Complimentary casual options include Guy's Burger Joint with hand-crafted burgers, BlueIguana Cantina for fresh tacos and burritos, and the Lido Marketplace buffet. Specialty dining includes Nick & Nora's Steakhouse and the exclusive Chef's Table experience with galley tour.

Where does Carnival Miracle sail from?

Carnival Miracle operates seasonally from multiple homeports. During summer months, she sails Alaska's Inside Passage from Seattle with 7-day itineraries visiting Juneau, Ketchikan, and Glacier Bay. During winter and shoulder seasons, she operates Caribbean cruises from Tampa and Galveston, Texas.

What is Fun Ship 2.0 and does Miracle have it?

Yes! Carnival Miracle received the comprehensive Fun Ship 2.0 refurbishment in 2015, which added Guy's Burger Joint, BlueIguana Cantina, Red Frog Rum Bar for Caribbean cocktails, and the Alchemy Bar with pharmacy-themed craft cocktails. These venues transformed the ship's dining and bar scene.

Who is the godmother of Carnival Miracle?

Jessica Lynch, the retired U.S. Army Private famous for her dramatic rescue during the Iraq War, christened Carnival Miracle in Jacksonville, Florida in 2004.
