Wonderland

Royal Caribbean — Imaginative Dining Experience

Quick Answer: Wonderland is Royal Caribbean's whimsical, Alice in Wonderland-inspired restaurant featuring molecular gastronomy, theatrical presentations, and imaginative cuisine. Cover charge $49–55. A multi-sensory dining adventure unlike anything else at sea.

Best For: Adventurous eaters seeking unique experiences, couples celebrating special occasions, foodies who love theatrical presentations, and anyone wanting Instagram-worthy dining moments.

Key Facts

  • Price: Cover charge $49–55 (+18% gratuity)
  • Hours: Dinner only (check Royal Caribbean app)
  • Dress Code: Smart Casual to Dressy
  • Reservations: Highly recommended (popular venue)
  • Signature: Painting appetizer, liquid nitrogen desserts, dry ice presentations

The Wonderland Experience

Pricing: Cover charge $49–55 includes multi-course tasting menu with theatrical presentations. Wine pairings available for additional charge.

Unlike traditional restaurants, Wonderland offers a curated multi-course journey. The menu changes seasonally, but expect these signature elements:

Signature Starters

  • The Painting — edible paints on a blank canvas you "paint" yourself
  • Molecular Bites — foams, spheres, and unexpected textures
  • Dry Ice Presentations — smoke and mystery
  • Deconstructed classics with playful twists

Main Courses

  • Surf and Turf — reimagined with sous vide techniques
  • Fish "Under the Sea" — artistic plating
  • Duck or Lamb — with unexpected accompaniments
  • Vegetarian Options — creative plant-based presentations
  • Each dish tells a story and surprises the senses

Magical Desserts

  • Liquid Nitrogen Ice Cream — made tableside
  • Color-Changing Desserts
  • Edible Art Installations
  • Surprise sweet bites throughout the meal

The Experience

  • 2–2.5 hour dining journey
  • Interactive elements throughout
  • Whimsical Alice-inspired decor

Note: Wonderland is about the experience as much as the food. Come with an open mind, a sense of wonder, and your camera ready. Menu items change frequently to keep the magic fresh.

Special Accommodations

Allergen & Dietary Notes: Royal Caribbean follows SAFE Food Policy. Please disclose allergens to your server before ordering. Gluten-free, dairy-free, vegetarian, and many vegan adjustments are available on request.

Wonderland Review: Guest Experience Summary

Introduction. Wonderland is unlike anything else on a cruise ship. This is molecular gastronomy meets dinner theater — a multi-course tasting menu where dishes arrive with dry ice fog, color-changing cocktails, and edible art you paint yourself. Guests either love the theatricality or find it style over substance.

Food & Drinks

The tasting menu runs 6–8 courses of small, inventive plates. Standouts include The Painting (an edible canvas with sauces as paints), liquid nitrogen ice cream prepared tableside, and spherified bites that pop on the tongue. Flavors lean creative rather than rich — this is not the place for a massive steak. The cocktail program matches the whimsy, with color-shifting drinks and smoking concoctions. Portions are intentionally small across many courses; most guests leave satisfied but not stuffed.

Service

Servers double as guides through the experience, explaining each course and its preparation technique. Pacing runs 2–2.5 hours — deliberately slow to let each course land. Staff are trained on the molecular gastronomy methods and can walk you through what you are eating. Dietary accommodations are handled well with advance notice.

Atmosphere / Ambiance

The room is Alice in Wonderland brought to life — oversized chairs, whimsical murals, and a menu that asks you to "pick a color" rather than choose a dish. Lighting shifts through the meal. It is immersive, theatrical, and designed to disorient in the best way. Couples and adventurous foodies get the most out of it; very young children may find the long format challenging.

Conclusion

Rating: 4.5/5. Wonderland delivers a one-of-a-kind dining experience that justifies its $49–55 cover charge through sheer creativity. Not for guests who want a traditional meal — this is dinner as performance art. Book early; it fills up fast on sea days.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need reservations for Wonderland?

Reservations are essential — this is one of the most sought-after dining experiences on the ship. Book early through the Cruise Planner.

What is the dress code for Wonderland?

Elegant attire recommended. Collared shirts and dress pants for men; cocktail or elegant attire for women.

Is Wonderland included in my cruise fare?

There's a premium cover charge (typically $65-125 per person) for this elevated dining experience.
