Ipanema Restaurant

MSC Cruises — Main Dining Room (MSC Seaside)

Quick Answer: One of two main dining rooms on MSC Seaside. 766 seats on Deck 6 Copacabana, dinner only, paired with the Seashore Restaurant on Deck 5. Brazilian-themed design language; the menu is shared between the two MDRs and changes nightly. Included with cruise fare.

Best For: Cruisers who want a real European-style dinner that takes two hours, with Mediterranean and Italian-leaning courses, table service, and the option to linger over wine. Couples and small groups who prefer the warmer Copacabana aesthetic to Seashore’s cooler Cape Cod design.

Key Facts

  • Ship: MSC Seaside
  • Deck: 6 (Copacabana area)
  • Capacity: 766 seats
  • Price: Complimentary (included in cruise fare)
  • Hours: Dinner only — three fixed seatings ~5:00/7:00/9:00 PM, or My Choice flexible 5:30–9:30 PM for Aurea+
  • Dress Code: Smart casual; jacket/cocktail dress for Gala Night
  • Reservations: Assigned at booking; flex through Guest Services

One of MSC Seaside’s two complimentary main dining rooms. Brazilian/tropical design, Mediterranean and Italian-leaning menu, multi-course pace, shared with the Seashore Restaurant on Deck 5. Return to the Restaurants hub →

Menu & Prices

Price: Complimentary (included in cruise fare). Bottled wine, cocktails, premium coffee, and specialty drinks are billed separately or covered by your MSC drinks package.

Ipanema and Seashore share the same dinner menu, which rotates nightly across a 4- to 7-night sailing. Standard structure: antipasti or appetizer, soup or salad, pasta course, main (a rotation of beef, chicken, fish, and a vegetarian option), and dessert. The Italian heritage of MSC shows up most clearly in the bread basket, the pasta plate, and the antipasti — these are the courses to lean into. Mains are competent rather than ambitious; the kitchen is plating 700+ covers per seating. On Gala Night (typically Thursday on a 4-night sailing) the menu lifts a step: escargots, rock lobster bisque, duck a l’orange, and beef short rib ravioli have all turned up on recent sailings. A Classic Favorites section — available every night — lets reluctant eaters fall back on steak, salmon, or chicken without ordering from the rotation.

Menu rotates by sailing. Some signature dishes appear on a fixed weekly schedule; ask your waiter on Day 1 which night carries the dishes you most want to try. Confirm in the MSC for Me app.

Special Accommodations

Allergen & Dietary Notes: MSC handles allergies through the maitre d’ rather than at the table-side order. Identify allergies at check-in on Day 1 and the kitchen will pull the next night’s menu options for review the evening before — this lets the chef pre-plate around gluten, shellfish, dairy, or nut allergies safely. Vegetarian options appear on every menu; vegan dishes are on request. Kosher and halal meals require advance notice at booking, not onboard. Speak with the maitre d’ or your server for assistance.

Where You’ll Find It

Ipanema Restaurant exists only on MSC Seaside. It sits on Deck 6 Copacabana, mid-ship, accessed from the Seaside Bar atrium. The sister MDR, Seashore Restaurant, is on Deck 5 Cape Cod, one deck below and aft. The two restaurants run the same menu and the same kitchens — the choice between them is design preference and dining-assignment logistics. MSC Seaview, Seaside’s only direct sister ship, carries similarly-named main dining rooms with the same dual-MDR layout.

The Logbook — Real Guest Soundings

Depth Sounding: This is a composite account from multiple guest experiences across MSC Seaside sailings 2023–2025, edited to our venue standards for clarity. Individual sailings vary by ship, itinerary, and crew.

3.6 ★ out of 5 MSC Seaside • 2023–2025

Ipanema Restaurant: The Deck-6 Half of MSC Seaside’s Two-MDR Setup

Ipanema is one of two doors into MSC Seaside’s main dining program. From the Deck 6 Copacabana corridor it reads as a warm, beach-resort-themed room — carved wood, palm-leaf motifs, lighting tuned to the lower end of the dimmer. The room runs about 766 seats, three fixed dinner seatings, plus a flexible window for Aurea Experience guests. The menu is identical to Seashore on Deck 5; the choice between them is mostly aesthetic and logistical.

Food

MSC’s Italian heritage carries the meal more than any single signature dish. The bread basket arrives early and refills willingly — the focaccia and grissini are real, not industrial. The antipasti course is consistent: cured meats with proper aging, a marinated seafood plate that delivers, a caprese that uses tomatoes worth the trip. The pasta plate is the dish to order first — an undersize portion by design, so it doesn’t fill you before the main, but most sailings produce a properly al dente plate with sauce that hasn’t broken. Mains are the weaker course on regular nights: the kitchen is plating 700+ covers per seating, and chicken and fish suffer from hold-time more than the Italian dishes. Gala Night is the lift — lobster bisque, duck a l’orange, and short rib ravioli appear on most recent sailings, and the kitchen seems to plate fewer covers in less time, so quality steps up. Desserts hit a middle band: a tiramisu that’s respectable, an Italian cheese plate that’s the smarter close, layered cakes that look better than they taste.

Service

Service is efficient rather than warm. The same waiter and assistant work your table for the full sailing under fixed-seating, which lets a relationship build by Night 2 — they learn your wine, your bread preference, your pace. The flex-dining model trades that continuity for time-of-day freedom; if the relationship matters to you, request fixed seating. Pacing on a full room is brisk on the front courses (appetizer to pasta in 25 minutes) and slower between pasta and main (sometimes a 15-minute wait while the kitchen catches up). The sommelier on the floor is overworked but knowledgeable; if you ask for a specific Italian region or grape variety, you’ll get a real recommendation rather than a wine-list page-flip.

Atmosphere

The Copacabana theme is committed but not gimmicky — the room feels warmer than Seashore on the deck below, more dinner-jacket than maritime-blazer. Large windows along the sides face the sea; sunset seatings (5:00 PM and the early end of My Choice) catch the better light. The 9:00 PM late seating is the quietest and least kid-heavy if that matters to you. Acoustic load is high when the room is full — the carpet absorbs some, but two adjacent six-tops can carry over your table. Multi-language announcements drift through the room periodically; they’re part of MSC’s European culture and they don’t intrude on conversation if you let them sit.

Pro Tips

  • If you have a strong preference between Ipanema and Seashore, request the swap at Guest Services on Day 1. They handle dozens of these requests daily and the process is quick.
  • Order the pasta course every night. It’s the most-consistently-strong plate and the smallest miss when the kitchen is under load.
  • Do not skip Gala Night dinner for a specialty restaurant. The MDR menu lifts a meaningful step that evening and the photos with the captain happen here.
  • If you’re fixed-seating, learn your waiter’s name on Night 1. The week is better when they know you back.
  • The 5:00 PM seating is the family-and-early-evening crowd; the 9:00 PM is the quieter, often older-couple room. Pick by which energy you want.

Conclusion

Rating: 3.6/5. Ipanema is a competent European-style main dining room serving a menu the Italian-heritage kitchen does honestly. It earns its score on the front courses (bread, antipasti, pasta) and Gala Night; it loses points where high-volume mains and prefab desserts thin out. Tip: if you remember nothing else, lean into the pasta course. It’s the truest expression of what MSC’s kitchen is built to do.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Ipanema Restaurant on MSC Seaside?

Ipanema is one of MSC Seaside's two main dining rooms. It seats 766 on Deck 6 in the Copacabana area, paired with the Seashore Restaurant on Deck 5 Cape Cod. The two share the same nightly menu — guests are assigned one MDR at booking but can flex through Guest Services. Ipanema serves dinner only with three fixed seatings (typically 5:00 PM, 7:00 PM, and 9:00 PM) or My Choice flexible dining for Aurea and higher Experience tiers.

How much does Ipanema Restaurant cost?

Ipanema is complimentary — included in your MSC Seaside cruise fare. There is no cover charge, no a la carte pricing, no surcharge. Drinks beyond water, iced tea, lemonade, and coffee are billed separately or covered by your drinks package.

What's the dress code at Ipanema Restaurant?

Smart casual most evenings — trousers and a collared shirt, a casual dress, dressy linen separates. Shorts and tank tops are discouraged at dinner. MSC runs one Gala Night per sailing (typically Thursday on a 4-night Bahamas itinerary) where a jacket for men and cocktail dress for women fit the room better, though MSC's enforcement is lighter than Cunard or Holland America.

Do I need a reservation for Ipanema Restaurant?

Not in the day-to-day sense. Your dining assignment (which MDR, which seating) is made at booking. You can request a change at Guest Services on Day 1 if your assigned seating doesn't suit. Aurea Experience tier and higher receive My Choice flexible dining — show up any time within the dinner window and the host will seat you, no fixed time required.

What's the difference between Ipanema and Seashore restaurants on MSC Seaside?

Same menu, different rooms. Ipanema (Deck 6 Copacabana, 766 seats) leans Brazilian and tropical in its design — warmer tones, beach-resort references; Seashore (Deck 5 Cape Cod, 922-928 seats) leans New England maritime — cooler tones, sea references. Ipanema serves dinner only; Seashore is the breakfast and lunch MDR as well. If you have a preference based on aesthetic, request it at Guest Services on Day 1; otherwise the food is identical.
