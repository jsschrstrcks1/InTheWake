Why I Started Solo Cruising (and Built a Community)

From that first solo gangway to a thriving community — lessons, safety, and the unexpected joy of sailing on your own terms.

I booked my first solo cruise because I was tired of waiting for everyone else’s schedule. I stayed for the freedom — coffee at sunrise, new friends by lunch, and a quiet deck chair when I needed to breathe.

Author’s note: These are lessons from real sailings and real meetups — what worked, what didn’t, and what I tell every first-timer who messages the group.

Why I Went Solo

I wanted a reset — a trip that didn’t hinge on anyone else’s calendar. Onboard, I found something more: a community of people who weren’t afraid to try new things, and a rhythm I could set for myself.
