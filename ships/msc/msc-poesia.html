MSC Poesia — Deck Plans, Live Tracker, Dining & Videos

MSC Poesia is the third ship of MSC Cruises' Musica Class, delivered on 5 April 2008 at Saint-Nazaire by Aker Yards and christened the same day at the Port of Dover by Sophia Loren — the first MSC ship named outside Italy. She measures 92,627 gross tons and 294 metres in length, carries 2,550 guests at double occupancy (3,060 maximum) with a crew of 1,039, and flies the Panamanian flag from Colón. During a February 19 – April 6, 2026 refit at Palumbo Malta Shipyard she received a brand-new MSC Yacht Club enclave (69 suites across five categories), a Butcher's Cut steakhouse, a renewed Kaito Sushi Bar, and a redesigned MSC Aurea Spa. She makes her Alaska debut from Seattle in May 2026, then moves to Miami for year-round Caribbean itineraries beginning winter 2026-27.

Quick Answer: MSC Poesia (92,627 GT, 2,550 double / 3,060 max, 1,039 crew, delivered 2008) is the third Musica Class ship — sisters MSC Musica, MSC Orchestra, and MSC Magnifica. She carries two main dining rooms (Le Fontane and Il Palladio), two separate buffets (L'Obelisco and Villa Pompeiana), Kaito Sushi Bar, the new Butcher's Cut steakhouse, the two-deck Teatro Carlo Felice, Casino Royal, and the MSC Aurea Spa. Following her February-April 2026 Palumbo Malta refit she now carries a new MSC Yacht Club enclave with 69 suites, Top Sail Lounge, and private sundeck. Seattle / Alaska summer 2026; Miami / Caribbean winter 2026-27.

Who She's For: Alaska cruisers who want an Italian-design mid-size option out of Seattle starting May 2026, suite travellers seeking one of MSC's newest Yacht Club installations on a smaller hull, and Caribbean cruisers who prefer a 2,550-guest ship over the mega-scale of the Seaside or Meraviglia classes. Good pick for guests who want a Musica-class atmosphere with a brand-new Yacht Club ship-within-a-ship. Not for those wanting Cirque du Soleil at Sea or waterslides — neither is aboard.

MSC Poesia is the third of four Musica Class ships — MSC Musica (2006, first-in-class), MSC Orchestra (2007), and MSC Magnifica (2010) complete the class. All four were built at Saint-Nazaire in the yard that passed through Alstom Marine → Aker Yards → STX France ownership (Poesia delivered under Aker Yards in April 2008 — period-accurate naming, since STX Europe's acquisition of Aker Yards did not close until October 2008). Each sister uses unique MDR and theatre names — Poesia's Le Fontane and Il Palladio are distinct from Musica's L'Oleandro and Belle Époque, Orchestra's Villa Borghese and L'Ibiscus, and Magnifica's L'Edera and Quattro Venti. Poesia's main theatre is the two-deck Teatro Carlo Felice (named after the historic opera house in Genoa), her casino is Casino Royal, her late-night venue is S32 Disco on Deck 14, and her wine bar is Il Grappolo d'Oro ('The Golden Grape'). Her February 19 – April 6, 2026 Palumbo Malta refit made her the second Musica-class ship to receive an MSC Yacht Club enclave, following sister MSC Magnifica in late 2025; MSC Musica and MSC Orchestra are scheduled to follow in late-2026 and Q1 2027 refits.

Entertainment & Activities on MSC Poesia

Entertainment on MSC Poesia centres on the Teatro Carlo Felice, a two-deck production stage named after the historic opera house in Genoa. The Deck 6 public-room axis carries five themed bars — Bar dei Poeti (the signature 'Poets' Bar'), Bar Smeraldo, Giada Bar, The Zebra Bar, and the atrium-style Le Rendez-Vous Bar on Deck 5. Deck 7 is the dining and entertainment spine with the Il Grappolo d'Oro Wine Bar, The Hitchcock Lounge (with its adjoining Cigar Room), Pigalle Lounge, the renewed Kaito Sushi Bar, the new Butcher's Cut steakhouse, Cyber Café, and the Casino Royal. Outdoor Deck 13 carries the Mojito Bar, Piraña Bar, and the Spa Bar, plus the Cayo Levantado and Coral Bay pool areas. Late-night life moves to the S32 Disco on Deck 14. Wellness lives at the newly redesigned MSC Aurea Spa with a new Gentleman's Barber, workout studios, and an expanded Outdoor Aurea area, plus the Top 15 Exclusive Solarium for Aurea Experience guests. Following the February-April 2026 Palumbo Malta refit, suite guests have access to the private MSC Yacht Club enclave with its Top Sail Lounge, Yacht Club Restaurant, and private sundeck pool area. Families have MSC's multilingual youth programme covering toddlers through teens. Note: MSC Poesia does NOT carry Cirque du Soleil at Sea.

Key Facts About MSC Poesia

MSC Poesia Deck Plans

MSC Poesia's 15-deck layout runs from Deck 5 up through the Top 15 Solarium. Deck 5 holds the Le Fontane Restaurant and Le Rendez-Vous Bar; Deck 6 is the main public-room axis with the Il Palladio Ristorante, the four themed bars (Bar dei Poeti, Bar Smeraldo, Giada Bar, The Zebra Bar), and the lower Teatro Carlo Felice. Deck 7 is the dining and entertainment spine with Butcher's Cut, the renewed Kaito Sushi Bar, Il Grappolo d'Oro Wine Bar, The Hitchcock Lounge, Pigalle Lounge, Cyber Café, Via delle Arti (interior promenade), and Casino Royal. Deck 13 is the pool deck with L'Obelisco and Villa Pompeiana buffets, the Mojito, Piraña, and Spa outdoor bars, the Cayo Levantado and Coral Bay pools, and the MSC Aurea Spa. Deck 14 carries the S32 Disco; Deck 15 has the Top 15 Exclusive Solarium. The new forward-upper-deck MSC Yacht Club enclave added in the April 2026 refit contains 69 suites, a private Top Sail Lounge, a Yacht Club Restaurant, and a private sundeck pool area. Caveat: MSC's official ship map PDF (po_en-gb.pdf) predates the April 2026 refit and does not yet reflect the Yacht Club, Butcher's Cut, or the new spa layout.

Frequently Asked Questions About MSC Poesia

What dining options are on MSC Poesia?

MSC Poesia has two main dining rooms — Le Fontane Restaurant (Deck 5) and Il Palladio Ristorante (Deck 6) — plus two separate buffets on Deck 13: L'Obelisco Buffet and Villa Pompeiana Buffet. Specialty dining includes Kaito Sushi Bar (renewed and relocated in the April 2026 refit) and the new Butcher's Cut steakhouse added in the same refit. Yacht Club suite guests also have the private MSC Yacht Club Restaurant. See the Dining section above for the full list, including all bars and lounges.

Which ships are in the same class as MSC Poesia?

MSC Poesia is the third ship of the Musica Class, together with MSC Musica (2006, first-in-class), MSC Orchestra (2007), and MSC Magnifica (2010) — four ships total. All four were built at Saint-Nazaire through the yard's Alstom Marine → Aker Yards → STX France ownership transitions. Poesia was delivered under Aker Yards branding in April 2008 — period-accurate, since the STX Europe acquisition of Aker Yards did not close until October 2008. Each sister uses unique MDR and theatre names — Poesia's Le Fontane, Il Palladio, and Teatro Carlo Felice distinguish her from Musica's L'Oleandro + Belle Époque + Teatro La Scala, Orchestra's Villa Borghese + L'Ibiscus + Covent Garden Theatre, and Magnifica's L'Edera + Quattro Venti + Royal Theatre.

Does MSC Poesia have the MSC Yacht Club?

Yes, as of April 2026. MSC Poesia received a brand-new MSC Yacht Club enclave during her February 19 – April 6, 2026 drydock at Palumbo Malta Shipyard in Valletta. Per MSC's official press release the new Yacht Club adds 69 suites across five categories (Yacht Club Interior, Deluxe, Grand, Executive Family, and Whirlpool Royal), a private Top Sail Lounge, a dedicated Yacht Club Restaurant, a private sundeck with pool area, and 24-hour butler service. She is the second Musica-class ship to receive a Yacht Club — following MSC Magnifica in her October-December 2025 refit — with sisters MSC Musica (late 2026) and MSC Orchestra (Q1 2027) scheduled to follow.

Where does MSC Poesia sail in 2026?

After completing her February-April 2026 Palumbo Malta refit, MSC Poesia repositioned transatlantically from Civitavecchia to Miami in April 2026, then runs a Panama Canal cruise before beginning her Alaska debut from Seattle in early May 2026 — seven-night round-trips covering the Inside Passage, Tracy Arm Fjord, Ketchikan, Icy Strait Point, Juneau, and Victoria, British Columbia, running through September. In winter 2026-27 she returns to Miami for Caribbean and Central America itineraries. Her current AIS position (April 10, 2026) places her in the Strait of Gibraltar en route to Funchal, Portugal — the mid-Atlantic leg of her repositioning voyage.

Who christened MSC Poesia?

MSC Poesia's naming ceremony took place on 5 April 2008 at the Port of Dover, Kent. She became the first MSC ship ever christened outside Italy — a milestone for the 2008 delivery class. Her godmother was Sophia Loren, MSC Cruises' long-standing godmother. She was delivered by Aker Yards at Saint-Nazaire on 5 April 2008. She is registered in Panama (Colón) and carries IMO number 9387073.
