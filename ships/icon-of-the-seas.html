Icon of the Seas

Icon of the Seas launched a new generation for Royal Caribbean, emphasizing purpose‑built neighborhoods for families (Surfside), adults (The Hideaway), and thrill‑seekers (Thrill Island). The waterpark‑style top deck and multi‑pool layout spread crowds well on sea days.

Entertainment blends classic theaters with immersive venues, while dining ranges from casual grab‑and‑go to upgraded specialty experiences. Expect modern cabins with family‑minded categories and plenty of connecting options.

The ship’s design focuses on choice—quiet nooks and high‑energy zones co‑exist—so different travel styles can find their pace. It’s the most resort‑like experience in the fleet and a natural pick for travelers who want the latest hardware and headline attractions.

Book Icon if you’re after the ‘new ship’ wow factor, cutting‑edge water features, and a neighborhood model that makes a big ship feel navigable.

Public perception at launch centered on how well the ship delivered the class promise—mixing amenities, entertainment, and service—while early reviews often highlighted crew friendliness and the ship’s signature venues. As the vessel matured, refits and programming kept it aligned with guest expectations.

Good Fit For

  • Travelers who match the Icon-class profile described above
  • Guests who value the specific mix of amenities, entertainment, and itineraries typical of this class
  • Families, couples, or groups seeking Royal Caribbean’s style at the ship’s scale
