Seven Seas Navigator — Deck Plans, Live Tracker, Dining & Videos

Seven Seas Navigator is a Navigator Class cruise ship operated by Regent Seven Seas Cruises. She entered service in 1999, measures 28,550 gross tons, and carries approximately 490 guests at double occupancy with a crew of 340.

Key Facts

  • Class: Navigator Class
  • Year: 1999
  • Tonnage: 28,550 GT
  • Guests: 490
  • Crew: 340
  • IMO: 9064126

Quick Answer: Seven Seas Navigator is Regent's smallest and most intimate ship — 28,550 GT, 490 guests, launched in 1999. All-suite and fully inclusive, she offers the highest crew-to-guest ratio in the fleet. This page covers deck plans, live tracking, dining venues, and videos.

Who She's For: Travelers who prioritize intimacy and personal attention above all else. Navigator suits experienced luxury cruisers who find even 750-guest ships too large — with 490 guests and 340 crew, you are known by name.

Seven Seas Navigator is the fleet's intimate outlier — at 490 guests she carries roughly two-thirds the capacity of the other Regent ships. Not all suites have balconies (some have windows), which is unique in the current Regent fleet. The all-inclusive fare is identical to the larger ships — specialty dining, drinks, shore excursions, Wi-Fi, and gratuities are all included. Her smaller size allows her to dock in ports that the Explorer Class ships cannot reach. If you want balcony-guaranteed, Mariner or Voyager at 700 guests are the next step up. If you want the newest Regent experience, Grandeur (2023) is the current flagship.

Frequently Asked Questions About Seven Seas Navigator

What dining options are on Seven Seas Navigator?

Seven Seas Navigator has five dining venues — all complimentary and included in Regent's all-inclusive fare. Compass Rose is the open-seating main dining room (lunch menus include dishes inspired by Prime 7, Chartreuse, and Pacific Rim concepts, but those exist on Navigator only as menu items within Compass Rose — not as standalone restaurants). Prime 7 is the steakhouse (reservations required). La Veranda serves buffet breakfast and lunch and transforms into Sette Mari at La Veranda for Italian dinner service each evening. Pool Grill handles casual outdoor dining poolside. Navigator does not carry Chartreuse or Pacific Rim as separate venues — those are on other Regent ships. See the Dining section above.

How big is Seven Seas Navigator and how many guests does she carry?

Seven Seas Navigator is the smallest ship in the Regent fleet at 28,550 gross tons with 245 suites and approximately 490 guests at double occupancy, served by a crew of 340. She is the only current Regent ship where not all suites have balconies — some have large picture windows. Her small size gives her one of the highest crew-to-guest ratios in luxury cruising and allows her to dock in ports that larger ships cannot reach.

How does Seven Seas Navigator compare to the other Regent ships?

Seven Seas Navigator (1999, 28,550 GT, 490 guests) is significantly smaller than the rest of the Regent fleet. Seven Seas Mariner (2001) and Seven Seas Voyager (2003) carry 700 guests each. The Explorer Class — Explorer (2016), Splendor (2020), and Grandeur (2023) — carries 750 guests at 55,254 GT. Navigator's smaller size means more personal attention but older hardware and fewer dining options (5 venues vs 7+ on the Explorer Class).

What is included in Regent's all-inclusive fare on Seven Seas Navigator?

Regent's all-inclusive fare covers all dining (main and specialty restaurants with no surcharges), premium drinks and fine wines, unlimited shore excursions in every port, pre-paid gratuities, Wi-Fi, and a 1-night pre-cruise hotel stay in concierge suites and above. The inclusions are identical across the Regent fleet.

Where does Seven Seas Navigator sail?

Seven Seas Navigator sails seasonal luxury itineraries — her smaller size allows access to smaller ports across the Mediterranean, Northern Europe, Alaska, Canada/New England, Caribbean, and repositioning voyages. Regent also deploys Navigator on multi-segment world cruise routes. Check the live tracker above for current position and Regent's official site for departure dates.
