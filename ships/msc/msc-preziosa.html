MSC Preziosa — Deck Plans, Live Tracker, Dining & Videos

MSC Preziosa is the fourth and final ship of MSC Cruises' Fantasia Class, delivered on 14 March 2013 at Saint-Nazaire by STX France and christened nine days later on 23 March 2013 in Genoa by Sophia Loren. She measures 139,072 gross tons and 333.30 metres in length, carries 3,502 guests at double occupancy (4,345 maximum) across 1,751 cabins with a crew of approximately 1,388, and flies the Panamanian flag from Colón. Her 18-deck layout includes the L'Arabesque and The Golden Lobster main dining rooms, the 1,645-seat Platinum Theatre, the stern-top Galaxy Restaurant / Lounge / Disco, the 120-metre Vertigo waterslide, and a forward MSC Yacht Club enclave that has been aboard since delivery. In summer 2026 she runs Northern Europe round-trips from Hamburg; in winter 2026-27 she remains in Western Europe from the same home port.

Quick Answer: MSC Preziosa (139,072 GT, 3,502 double / 4,345 max, ~1,388 crew, delivered 2013) is the fourth and final Fantasia Class ship — sisters MSC Fantasia, MSC Splendida, and MSC Divina. She carries two main dining rooms (L'Arabesque and The Golden Lobster), two separate buffets (Inca and Maya on Deck 14), Butcher's Cut steakhouse, the stern-top Galaxy Restaurant (which also serves sushi and converts to the Galaxy Disco), the 1,645-seat Platinum Theatre, Millennium Star Casino, the signature Vertigo 120-metre waterslide, and a forward MSC Yacht Club enclave with the Moroccan-themed La Palmeraie restaurant and the Top Sail Lounge. Hamburg / Northern Europe summer 2026; Western Europe winter 2026-27.

Who She's For: Northern European cruisers who want a 1,751-cabin Italian-design ship out of Hamburg, Germany, with real Fantasia-class scale. Families who will use the Vertigo 120-metre waterslide and the Doremi Castle Aquapark. Suite travellers who want a fully-built-in MSC Yacht Club with 69 forward suites and private sundeck. Not for cruisers looking for Cirque du Soleil at Sea (not on Preziosa) or Eataly (also not on Preziosa, despite what older articles may suggest).

MSC Preziosa is the fourth ship of the Fantasia Class — MSC Fantasia (2008, first-in-class), MSC Splendida (2009), and MSC Divina (2012) complete the class. Preziosa and Divina form the "modified" sub-group of Fantasia class: both sail at 139,072 GT (vs the ~137,936 GT of Fantasia/Splendida) and carry 1,751 cabins — 114 more than the original pair — plus two extra elevators. Each Fantasia-class sister uses different venue names: Preziosa's L'Arabesque and The Golden Lobster MDRs are distinct from her sisters' dining rooms, her Platinum Theatre is unique (vs Fantasia's Teatro L'Avanguardia, Splendida's Strand Theatre, and Divina's Pantheon Theatre), and her Yacht Club restaurant is the Moroccan-themed La Palmeraie — not the L'Olivo found on MSC Fantasia and Splendida. Her hull has an unusual contract history: originally ordered in 2010 by Libya's GNMTC for Hannibal Gaddafi and planned to be named Phoenicia, the contract was cancelled by STX France during the 2011 Libyan Civil War; MSC Cruises acquired the hull in March 2012 for approximately €550 million and completed construction as the fourth Fantasia-class ship. She was delivered under the STX France name (period-accurate — the yard reverted to Chantiers de l'Atlantique only in 2018).

Entertainment & Activities on MSC Preziosa

Entertainment on MSC Preziosa centres on the Platinum Theatre, a 1,645-seat two-deck production stage on Decks 6-7 — unique to Preziosa among the Fantasia class. Deck 6 Diamante carries the Millennium Star Casino, the Diamond Bar & Library, and the Phoenician Plaza themed square with live music. Deck 7 Rubino is the entertainment and specialty-dining axis with Butcher's Cut, the El Dorado Piano Bar, La Locanda wine bar, the 313-seat Safari Lounge with dance floor, The Green Sax Jazz Bar, Sports Bar & Bowling, La Cantina di Bacco, and Il Cappuccino coffee bar. The signature late-night venue is on Deck 16 at the stern, where the Galaxy Restaurant converts to the Galaxy Disco. Families have the signature 120-metre Vertigo Waterslide (Deck 18 entrance down to Deck 15 exit, with a transparent over-sea tube section), plus the Doremi Castle Aquapark on Deck 16, the Doremiland kids' clubs and I Graffiti Teen Club on Deck 15, and four separate pools (the main Aquapark, the covered La Playa Preciosa Pool with sliding roof, the aft Garden Pool, and the private Yacht Club One Pool on Deck 18). Wellness lives at the 1,700 m² MSC Aurea Spa on Deck 14 with thermal area, balneo, thalasso room, MediSpa, barber shop, and salons. MSC Yacht Club suite guests have a complete forward ship-within-a-ship enclave with the Top Sail Lounge, the Moroccan-themed La Palmeraie Restaurant, The One Bar and One Pool on Deck 18, the Top 18 Exclusive Solarium, and 24-hour butler service. Note: MSC Preziosa does NOT carry Cirque du Soleil at Sea, and despite some older articles she does not have an Eataly venue.

Key Facts About MSC Preziosa

MSC Preziosa Deck Plans

MSC Preziosa's 18-deck layout uses gemstone-themed deck names: Corallo (5), Diamante (6), Rubino (7), Smeraldo (14), Cristallo (15), Acquamarina (16), and Topazio (18) are the primary public decks. Deck 5 Corallo holds the atrium and The Golden Lobster lower level; Deck 6 Diamante is the main public-room axis with the L'Arabesque Restaurant, Diamond Bar & Library, Phoenician Plaza, Millennium Star Casino, and the lower Platinum Theatre. Deck 7 Rubino is the dining and entertainment spine with Butcher's Cut, El Dorado Piano Bar, La Locanda, Safari Lounge, The Green Sax Jazz Bar, Sports Bar & Bowling, Il Cappuccino, and La Cantina di Bacco. Deck 14 Smeraldo is the pool deck with Inca and Maya buffets, the Aquapark and the covered La Playa Preciosa Pool, the Saphir, Turquoise, La Playa Preciosa and Spa bars, and the MSC Aurea Spa. Deck 15 Cristallo has The Garden Pool and Garden Bar aft, and forward holds the MSC Yacht Club with Top Sail Lounge and La Palmeraie restaurant. Deck 16 Acquamarina carries Galaxy Restaurant / Disco at the stern and the Doremi Castle Aquapark. Deck 18 Topazio tops the ship with the Vertigo waterslide entrance, The One Pool and One Bar, and the Top 18 Exclusive Solarium.

Frequently Asked Questions About MSC Preziosa

What dining options are on MSC Preziosa?

MSC Preziosa has two main dining rooms — L'Arabesque (Deck 6 Diamante) and The Golden Lobster (split across Decks 5 and 6) — plus two separate buffets on Deck 14 Smeraldo: Inca Buffet and Maya Buffet. Specialty dining includes Butcher's Cut steakhouse (Deck 7 Rubino), the stern-top Galaxy Restaurant (Deck 16 Acquamarina — which also serves sushi and converts to Galaxy Disco late-night; there is no separate Kaito Sushi Bar on Preziosa), La Cantina di Bacco wine venue (Deck 7), and Il Cappuccino coffee bar (Deck 7). Yacht Club suite guests have access to the private Moroccan-themed La Palmeraie Restaurant on Deck 15 Cristallo. See the Dining section above for the full list.

Which ships are in the same class as MSC Preziosa?

MSC Preziosa is the fourth and final ship of the Fantasia Class, together with MSC Fantasia (2008, first-in-class), MSC Splendida (2009), and MSC Divina (2012). Preziosa and Divina form the "modified" sub-group: both are 139,072 GT with 1,751 cabins (vs ~137,936 GT and 1,637 cabins on Fantasia/Splendida), with two extra elevators and slightly different venue programming. Each sister uses different MDR and theatre names — Preziosa's Platinum Theatre is distinct from Fantasia's Teatro L'Avanguardia, Splendida's Strand Theatre, and Divina's Pantheon Theatre. Her Yacht Club restaurant is the Moroccan-themed La Palmeraie, NOT the L'Olivo found on MSC Fantasia and Splendida.

Does MSC Preziosa have the MSC Yacht Club?

Yes. Unlike the Musica-class ships which are receiving Yacht Club enclaves through 2025-2027 refits, MSC Preziosa has carried a full MSC Yacht Club from her 2013 delivery — the Fantasia class had Yacht Club from build. The forward enclave on Decks 15/16/18 contains 69 Yacht Club suites, the Top Sail Lounge (Deck 15 Cristallo, 177 seats), the Moroccan-themed La Palmeraie Restaurant (Deck 15, 110 seats), The One Pool and The One Bar on Deck 18 Topazio, a private concierge reception and library, 24-hour butler service, and dedicated access to a private section of the MSC Aurea Spa. A refresh of the Yacht Club Restaurant is scheduled for February 2028.

Where does MSC Preziosa sail in 2026?

As of April 2026 MSC Preziosa is mid-Atlantic, repositioning from her 2025-26 South America season (Santos/Maceió) to her 2026 Northern Europe season. Her summer 2026 home port is Hamburg, Germany, where she runs 5-, 7-, 11-, and 14-night round-trip itineraries to the Norwegian Fjords, Iceland, the British Isles, and the North Cape, with secondary embarkation from Southampton, Rotterdam, Zeebrugge, and Le Havre. For winter 2026-27 she stays in Western Europe from Hamburg — a rotation change from previous years when she wintered in Santos. Example itineraries include a 14-night Hamburg round-trip on 9 August 2026 and 11-night Iceland loops on 11 June and 23 August 2026.

Who christened MSC Preziosa?

MSC Preziosa was christened on 23 March 2013 in Genoa, Italy by Sophia Loren, MSC Cruises' long-standing godmother. She had been delivered nine days earlier on 14 March 2013 by STX France at Saint-Nazaire — STX France is the period-accurate yard name for the 2013 build, since the yard reverted to Chantiers de l'Atlantique only in 2018. Her hull has an unusual history: originally ordered in 2010 by Libya's General National Maritime Transport Company for Hannibal Gaddafi and planned as "Phoenicia", the contract was cancelled by STX France during the 2011 Libyan Civil War. MSC Cruises acquired the half-built hull in March 2012 for approximately 550 million euros and completed construction as the fourth Fantasia-class ship. She is registered in Panama (Colón) and carries IMO number 9595321.
