Gli Archi Buffet Review: Mediterranean Spread with Mixed Results

Introduction. Gli Archi Buffet is one of MSC's standard ship buffets, named for the arched design elements that frame the serving stations. Found on MSC Meraviglia-class and other vessels, it offers the typical cruise buffet proposition — complimentary, open for extended hours, and built to serve a high volume of guests. Like all MSC buffets, Italian and Mediterranean staples form the backbone of the offering.

Food & Drinks

The Italian stations remain the buffet's strongest asset — fresh-rolled pasta with bolognese or pesto, wood-fired style pizza that arrives frequently from the oven, and antipasti platters with cured meats and marinated vegetables that reflect genuine Mediterranean flavors. Breakfast features a solid European spread: good croissants, cold cuts, cheeses, and a cooked station with eggs and bacon. The carved roast at dinner (when available) is worth queuing for — properly rested and seasoned. Where it falls short: the international stations attempt too much and deliver too little — Asian-inspired stir-fries taste generic, the Indian curries lack depth, and seafood items under heat lamps quickly lose texture and appeal. The salad bar is adequate but uninspired, with dressings that lean toward bottled rather than house-made. Desserts follow the familiar cruise buffet pattern: visually appealing cakes that taste uniform, with the occasional gem in the fruit tarts.

Service

Buffet service is functional. Staff keep stations reasonably stocked and tables are cleared with decent frequency, though peak lunch on sea days tests the system — expect crowded aisles, depleted stations awaiting refresh, and competition for clean tables. The drink station staff are efficient but transactional. No table service for beverages is provided consistently.

Atmosphere

The arched architectural theme gives the space slightly more character than a standard cruise buffet, but the reality is a large, bright, high-traffic dining hall. Noise levels at peak hours are significant. The outdoor seating area (weather permitting) is the better choice — pool-adjacent tables with natural light and fresh air transform the meal from cafeteria to casual al fresco. On Mediterranean itineraries, eating outdoors with a sea view nearly compensates for any food shortcomings.

Conclusion

Rating: 3.3/5. Gli Archi Buffet delivers what cruise buffets deliver — volume, variety, and convenience — with Italian stations that outperform the rest of the offering by a noticeable margin. It loses marks for generic international options, inconsistent freshness during peak hours, and a dining hall atmosphere that favors efficiency over enjoyment. Tip: head straight for the Italian and Mediterranean stations, grab a pizza fresh from the oven, and eat outside whenever weather allows — it's a meaningfully better experience.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Gli Archi Buffet on MSC Cruises?

Complimentary buffet restaurant on MSC Musica with Italian-inspired arched decor and international cuisine stations for all meals.

How much does Gli Archi Buffet cost?

Gli Archi Buffet is complimentary — included in your MSC cruise fare at no extra charge.

What is the dress code for Gli Archi Buffet?

Come as you are. Gli Archi Buffet is a casual venue — pool attire and casual clothes are fine. MSC has Smart Casual and Elegant nights where slightly dressier options are appreciated.

Do I need reservations for Gli Archi Buffet?

No reservations needed. Gli Archi Buffet is walk-up service — just show up and enjoy.

What are the menu highlights at Gli Archi Buffet?

Gli Archi Buffet offers a curated selection that changes by ship and sailing. Check the MSC for Me app onboard for the latest menu.
