From the Logbook

From the back of the boat

The finest view at sea isn’t what’s coming—it’s what you’ve left behind, carved in white across the water. After 150 nights at sea, that’s where most of these notes were written: at the stern, with the wake going quiet behind us and the next port still a day away.

This page is a layout demo. It shows how a ship guide, a port guide, or an article reads in this design: a column of plain, readable text, room for photographs, and a sidebar for the things you reach for at a glance.

What you’ll find here

Every entry tries to do three things, in plain words:

  • Tell you what is actually there, not what a brochure promises.
  • Give you a number, a name, or a date you can plan around.
  • Say plainly when we haven’t done something, so you know the edge of what we know.

Why the back of the boat

Looking astern is looking at where you have already been. That is what a logbook is: a record kept so the next traveler’s way forward is a little easier. You sail in our wake, and the water is calmer for it.

We sail ahead, collect the real details, and write them down. The goal isn’t to sell you anything — it’s to take the friction out of the decision.
