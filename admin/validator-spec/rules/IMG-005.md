## Rule
A port page must contain at least 11 `<img>` elements. Fewer than 11 fails as BLOCKING. This is a minimum-content-richness check — port pages that try to ship with just a hero and one gallery image usually indicate incomplete content.

## Why (rationale)
A port page earns its reader by showing, not just telling. A hero + 10 body/gallery images gives enough visual detail to convey what the port actually looks like — terminal, downtown, key landmarks, beach option, market, food. Falling below this floor almost always means the page was shipped before visual research was done.

The 11-image floor is the validator's policy; standards docs don't describe the specific number. Backfill needed.

## Pass example
Port page with hero (1) + 10 gallery/body images = 11 images total. Passes.

## Fail example
Port page with hero + 3 body images = 4 images total. Validator emits: `Page has 4 images, minimum is 11`.

## Fix guidance
Source more images (following ATTR-001 attribution discipline — do NOT fabricate attributions to fill the count). If real images aren't available for a port, the port page is not ready to ship; don't pad with unrelated/duplicate images.
