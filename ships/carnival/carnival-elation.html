What Makes Carnival Elation Special

As one of Carnival's Fantasy-class vessels, Elation offers a more intimate cruise experience at 70,367 gross tons. Sailing from Jacksonville, she provides easy access to Bahamas and Eastern Caribbean destinations for cruisers in Florida and the Southeast.

Her compact size means shorter walking distances and a cozier atmosphere, while still featuring Carnival signatures like the WaterWorks aqua park, Piano Bar, and BlueIguana Cantina. The ship appeals to first-time cruisers and those who prefer a less overwhelming ship experience.

Originally built at Kværner Masa-Yards in Helsinki, Finland, Carnival Elation made history as the first cruise ship with AZIPOD propulsion, offering improved maneuverability and fuel efficiency. Multiple refurbishments in 2017 and 2020 added modern Carnival favorites while preserving the classic Fantasy-class charm that guests love.

Frequently Asked Questions About Carnival Elation

What are the dining options on Carnival Elation?

Carnival Elation offers the Imagination and Inspiration main dining rooms serving multi-course dinners each evening with Your Time Dining flexibility. Complimentary casual options include Guy's Burger Joint with hand-crafted burgers, BlueIguana Cantina for fresh tacos and burritos, the Lido Marketplace buffet, and Pizzeria del Capitano for hand-tossed pizza around the clock.

Where does Carnival Elation sail from?

Carnival Elation is homeported in Jacksonville, Florida, offering 4- and 5-day Bahamas cruises year-round. Typical itineraries include stops at Nassau and Carnival's private island Half Moon Cay. Jacksonville is convenient for cruisers from the Southeast United States.

What makes Carnival Elation historically significant?

Carnival Elation made maritime history as the first cruise ship equipped with AZIPOD propulsion when she launched in 1998. This podded propulsion technology offers superior maneuverability and fuel efficiency, and is now standard on most modern cruise ships. Actress Teri Hatcher served as godmother at the christening ceremony in New York City.
