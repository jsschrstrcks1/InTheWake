Cruise Tech & Photography Guide: Capture Every Moment

Honest notes on what tech actually helps you document a voyage and pass time at sea.

by Ken Baker | January 21, 2026

Disclosure: This page contains affiliate links. Purchases may earn a commission at no extra cost. Learn more.

A cruise offers countless photo opportunities: sunsets over the ocean, colorful port towns, wildlife encounters, and shipboard moments with family and friends. The right tech makes the difference between cherished memories and missed opportunities.

This guide covers the tech that actually helps on cruises — from protecting your phone at the beach to staying entertained on sea days.

Waterproof Protection

If your trip includes a beach day, a snorkel excursion, or even a rainy port walk, a waterproof phone pouch lets you carry your phone without worrying about it. Skip if your existing phone case is already rated for water immersion.

Waterproof Phone Pouch

A pouch like the UNBREAKcable Waterproof Phone Pouch (IPX8, fits phones up to 7", touchscreen still works through the plastic) is enough for most cruise water situations. The category is genuinely commoditized — any IPX8-rated pouch from a reputable seller works the same way.

Test before you travel: seal up a wad of tissue paper, submerge in the sink for 30 minutes. If the tissue stays dry, you're good.

Waterproof Pouches for Documents

Keep passports, ship cards, and cash safe from splashes during beach excursions.

Entertainment at Sea

Sea days are perfect for relaxing with a good book or catching up on podcasts. But cruise WiFi is often slow and expensive. The solution: download everything before you sail.

E-Reader

For cruise reading, the Kindle Paperwhite remains the best all-around choice — waterproof for poolside reading, glare-free in direct sun, and the battery lasts weeks rather than hours. If you want color (magazines, comics, kids' books) the Kindle Colorsoft and Kobo Clara Colour are reasonable alternatives, but for plain text the Paperwhite is the established sweet spot.

Before you sail: download books from your library app (Libby, OverDrive) or buy from your preferred bookseller. Most U.S. public libraries offer free e-book loans through Libby.

Noise-Canceling Earbuds

Cabin hallways, pool decks, and engine rooms are all louder than a hotel; if you're sensitive to noise or want to enjoy podcasts during sea days, active noise-canceling earbuds make a real difference. The Bose QuietComfort Ultra Earbuds are the universal recommendation here — they hold their ranking across most independent reviews. AirPods Pro and Sony WF-1000XM5 sit in the same tier; if you already use one of those ecosystems, stay there. EarFun Air Pro is a budget alternative if the flagship pricing doesn't fit.

Portable Power

Your phone is your camera, boarding pass, excursion tickets, and communication device. Don't let it die during a port day.

Portable Power Bank

The Anker PowerCore 20,000mAh is reliable, charges a phone three to four times, and the brand has a long track record. Higher-wattage compact banks (the INIU 65W is the most-cited) charge laptops too if you carry one — pick that route only if you actually need it.

Carry-on only: TSA and most airlines require lithium battery packs to fly in the cabin, never in checked luggage.

Multi-Port USB Charger

Most cabins have one or two outlets. A multi-port wall charger like the Anker 6-Port USB Charger turns a single outlet into a charging station for everyone in the cabin. Apple, Belkin, and other reputable brands make functionally similar versions; the category is mature.

Photography Tips

You don't need expensive camera equipment for great cruise photos. Modern smartphones rival DSLRs for most situations. Here are tips for better shots.

Golden Hour is Your Friend

The hour after sunrise and before sunset provides the most flattering light for ocean photos. If you can plan deck time around it, the difference is real.

Stabilize Your Shots

Ships move. Use these techniques for sharper photos:

  • Brace your elbows against the railing
  • Use burst mode and pick the sharpest frame
  • Enable optical image stabilization (OIS) if your phone has it

Composition Tips

  • Rule of thirds: Place your subject off-center for more interesting photos
  • Leading lines: Use railings, decks, and walkways to draw the eye
  • Include scale: Put a person in landscape shots to show how vast the ocean is
  • Clean your lens: Seriously — salt spray and sunscreen smudges ruin photos

Port Day Photography

  • Arrive early: Beat the crowds at popular spots
  • Look up: Architecture and signs often tell the story of a place
  • Capture details: Textures, food, street art, signage
  • Respect locals: Ask permission before photographing people

Motion Sickness

If you're prone to motion sickness, several over-the-counter and drug-free options exist: Bonine (meclizine, generally less drowsy than Dramamine), Sea-Bands (acupressure wristbands), ginger candies, and prescription scopolamine patches if you need them. Talk to your pharmacist or doctor about which fits your situation — we don't link product purchases here because the right answer depends on your medical context, not on what's convenient to buy.

Tech Packing Checklist

  • Waterproof phone pouch — see waterproof protection
  • E-reader (loaded with books) — see entertainment at sea
  • Noise-canceling earbuds — see entertainment at sea
  • Portable power bank (carry-on only) — see portable power
  • Multi-port USB charger — see portable power
  • Charging cables for all devices
  • Lens cleaning cloth
  • Microfiber towel (for camera/phone)
