Voyage Packs

Itinerary-specific cruise planning, in one document, for a one-time price.

A Voyage Pack is what you'd get if a friend who knows cruise planning sat down for two evenings and wrote you everything they'd tell you about your specific cruise. Day-by-day rhythm, ship details, port-by-port practical advice, packing calibrated to that itinerary, an honest budget breakdown, and the contact information your family will need if something goes wrong at sea — all in one document, designed to print on paper and to work offline on your phone.

Each pack is built around one specific sailing. The packing list knows the climate. The ports section knows which ones are tender ports. The budget section knows the cruise line's auto-gratuity. The emergency-contacts section knows which embassies cover which port. Generic cruise advice can do none of those things.

One-time purchase. No subscription. Yours forever. Designed to be shared with one trusted person at home before you sail.

What's in every pack

  • The ship. Cabin choice guidance, dining venues, drinks math, connectivity options, the rhythm of a typical sea day, which features actually matter and which don't.
  • Day by day. An hour-by-hour rhythm for each port day and sea day, written for this specific itinerary — not a generic template.
  • The ports. What's at the pier, how to get into town, taxi unions and licensing, what to skip, what's worth a paid excursion, when to be back onboard.
  • The packing list. Calibrated to the climate, the dress code, and the specific ports. Not "everything you might possibly need."
  • The budget. Honest math on what you'll actually spend onboard, line item by line item, with the cruise line's current auto-gratuity built in.
  • Seasickness, practical logistics, accessibility. Multi-paragraph practical content for the questions cruisers actually have.
  • The family Emergency Contacts section. A fillable handoff card (booking number, cabin number, insurance policy, trusted-contact details), the U.S. State Department's 24/7 line, the cruise line's contact numbers framed honestly (paid ship-phone vs free emergency desk), and the U.S. embassies and consulates for every port on this specific itinerary. Designed to be filled in once and shared with one trusted person at home — and to work offline at sea.
Offline-capable. After the first time you load a Voyage Pack, it's available without Wi-Fi. Useful at sea, in port, on a plane, or anywhere your family loses signal. The PWA caches the pack automatically.

Custom Voyage Pack for your cruise

If your sailing isn't covered by the prototypes above, a custom pack tailored to your specific cruise is available. Same eight-section structure, calibrated to your ship, your itinerary, your dates, your audience.

Custom packs run $49–$79 depending on itinerary complexity (more ports, more sea days, more bespoke content like group-cruise rhythms or accessibility-deep content). Lead time is typically 2–4 weeks.

To inquire about a custom pack, email through the contact link with your cruise line, ship, sailing dates, and a brief note about anything specific you want addressed (mobility considerations, multi-generational travel, religious or dietary observance, etc.).

What you should know before you buy

Format. Each pack is delivered as both a PDF (for printing and easy sharing) and an offline-capable HTML version (works in your phone or laptop browser without Wi-Fi after first load, via In the Wake's PWA). The HTML version contains the fillable Emergency Contacts handoff card with localStorage persistence — fill it in once, share the page with one trusted person at home, and they have it offline too.

One-time purchase. No subscription. No upsell. Yours forever. If In the Wake updates the pack with newly verified information (cruise-line policy changes, embassy phone updates, etc.), you'll get the updated version at no additional cost — provided the same email is still active.

Refund policy. Full refund within 14 days of purchase, no questions asked. If the pack doesn't fit your needs, write back and we'll process the refund. After 14 days, refunds are case-by-case; we err on the side of yes.

What this is not. A Voyage Pack is not a travel agent service. It does not book your cruise. It does not coordinate transfers, excursions, dining reservations, or seating arrangements. (For booking on the v0.1.2 NCL Aqua hosted group pack, that's Maulsby Travel Co.'s job, separately.) The pack is a planning companion, not a concierge.

Why we sell these. In the Wake is reader-supported. We don't take cruise-line affiliate commissions, we don't sell shore-excursion bookings, we don't run display advertising. The full posture lives in the affiliate disclosure. Voyage Packs are how the planning labor that lives behind the free site gets compensated. The base site stays free, forever; the packs are the funded layer that keeps that promise possible.

FAQ

How is this different from the free content on the rest of the site?

The free site is broad — 388 ports, 300+ ships, 9 planning tools, hundreds of articles. A Voyage Pack pulls the relevant subset of that knowledge into one calibrated document for your specific cruise, plus content that doesn't live on the public site (the family Emergency Contacts handoff card, the day-by-day rhythm, the budget breakdown for that specific sailing).

Will my pack be updated if cruise-line policies change?

Yes. When meaningful updates happen (cruise-line auto-gratuity changes, embassy contact-information updates, currency-handling changes, new accessibility infrastructure at a port), we update the pack and re-send to all buyers via the email on file.

What if I bought the pack and the cruise gets cancelled?

If your cruise is cancelled within 14 days of purchase, full refund. If it's cancelled later than that, write to us and we'll work it out — we typically offer either a refund or a credit toward a different pack you might use for a future cruise.

Can I share my Voyage Pack with my travel companions?

Yes — please. The pack is designed to be shared with one trusted person at home (the family Emergency Contacts handoff is the entire point), and traveling parties can share within the household. We trust you not to redistribute the pack publicly. The packs include some unique content not duplicated on the free site, and that's the part the small purchase price funds.

Why are the prices so low? ($19–$29)

Because we'd rather a pack be in the hands of every cruiser who'd benefit than priced for a small audience. The pack is the funded layer that keeps the rest of the site free; modest pricing matches that posture. The custom-pack tier ($49–$79) is priced higher because it requires bespoke labor.

What about accessibility, neurodivergent, or sensitive-content considerations?

Each pack is written with the same calm voice as the free site. No urgency framing, no fear-based hooks, no banned-vocabulary "must-haves." Accessibility content is integrated throughout (it's not a separate section that pretends inclusion as an afterthought). If you have specific considerations you want addressed in a custom pack, mention them in your inquiry email — we adjust accordingly.
