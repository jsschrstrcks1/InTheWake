Brilliant Lady — Deck Plans, Live Tracker, Dining & Videos

Brilliant Lady is the fourth and newest of Virgin Voyages' four Lady Class ships. Fincantieri counted a technical delivery in 2023 and formally handed her over in February 2024 from Sestri Ponente (yard 6304), but she was placed in warm lay-up for approximately 18 months and did not enter commercial service until 5 September 2025, when she sailed a 5-night maiden voyage from New York City to King's Wharf, Bermuda. At 110,000 gross tons she carries 2,770 guests at double occupancy (2,860 maximum) and a crew of 1,160. She is strictly adults-only (18+). On 6 April 2026 she became the first Virgin Voyages ship to dock at the Port of Los Angeles.

Strictly 18+, all dining and basic beverages included.

Best For: Adults and couples looking for a 4- or 5-day West Coast / Mexican Riviera round-trip from Los Angeles, or Virgin Voyages' first-ever Alaska season from Seattle (starting May 2026). Brilliant Lady is the only Lady with Panama Canal clearance, so she is also the right Virgin ship for any Panama Canal itinerary. If you want an adults-only cruise with no children, no buffet, no formal nights, and 20+ restaurants included in the fare — and you want the newest Virgin ship — this is the one.

Brilliant Lady has the most complicated history in the fleet. She was ordered from Fincantieri on 31 October 2018 for approximately €700 million, with hull sections fabricated at Castellammare di Stabia and assembled at Sestri Ponente. She floated out on 25 November 2022. In September 2023, Virgin Voyages cancelled all her planned cruises while completing a new round of financing — and placed her in warm lay-up for approximately 18 months, drifting between anchorages off Cyprus, Civitavecchia, and Málaga. Her delay forced Virgin to pull Valiant Lady's Winter 2024 Caribbean season and Resilient Lady's Summer 2024 Greek Isles season to backfill itineraries. A pre-launch drydock at Fincantieri Palermo from 20 July to 1 August 2025 finally prepared her for service, and on 5 September 2025 she made her commercial debut from New York on a 5-night maiden voyage to King's Wharf, Bermuda — 21 months after her originally announced first voyage. She is hardware-identical to her three sisters except for one critical difference: her adapted frame, with modified deck and lifeboat overhangs, allows her to transit the Panama Canal. Her sisters cannot. This is what enables her West Coast and Alaska deployment. Her bow carries a distinctive mermaid figurehead designed by Toronto illustrator Janice Sung, blending Renaissance and Japanese/Chinese art influences. Signature on-board events include Scarlet Night (the fleet-wide red party), the Runway fashion show, and Drag Brunch; all dining, gratuities, basic WiFi, and group fitness classes are included in the fare.

A First Look at Brilliant Lady

Who Brilliant Lady Is For

You'll love Brilliant Lady if: you want the newest ship in the Virgin Voyages fleet; you want a West Coast departure from Los Angeles, or Virgin Voyages' first-ever Alaska season from Seattle (starting May 2026); you're planning a Panama Canal cruise (Brilliant is the only Lady that can transit the Canal); you want an adults-only cruise with no buffet, no formal nights, no children, and 20+ restaurants included in the fare; you want to sail on the ship with the Janice Sung mermaid figurehead, a Brilliant-exclusive design touch; or you're curious about a ship whose 21-month delivery delay made her one of the more storied ship-launch sagas of the decade.

You may prefer a different ship if: you are travelling with children (Virgin Voyages is strictly 18+); you want a traditional main dining room experience with set seatings; you want Broadway-style production shows (Brilliant Lady's Red Room runs immersive theatre instead); you are looking for year-round Caribbean from Miami (consider Scarlet Lady or Resilient Lady); you are specifically booking for a Mediterranean summer (consider Scarlet, Valiant, or Resilient); or you want Valiant Lady's new Ariya Indian restaurant (Valiant-exclusive from 25 May 2026).

The Logbook: Brilliant Lady (Lady Class)

Virgin Voyages Lady Class — Sister Ships

Brilliant Lady is the fourth and newest of four Lady Class ships, all built by Fincantieri at its Sestri Ponente yard in Genoa. The sisters share an identical hull, propulsion, and cabin layout (1,408 cabins, 2,770 guests, 1,160 crew) — they differ mainly in deployment, yard number, and a few per-ship venue exclusives. Brilliant Lady is the only sister with a Panama-Canal-capable adapted frame.

  • Scarlet Lady — Lead ship, IMO 9804801, yard 6287, delivered 14 February 2020. Caribbean from PortMiami, Mediterranean summer. Scarlet-unique: Lucky Lotus by Razzle Dazzle Chinese evening concept.
  • Valiant Lady — Second ship, IMO 9805336, yard 6288, delivered 1 July 2021. US East Coast / Mediterranean. Valiant-unique (from 25 May 2026 after drydock): Ariya — Virgin's first Indian restaurant at sea, by chef Maneet Chauhan.
  • Resilient Lady — Third ship, IMO 9805348, yard 6289, delivered 7 December 2022. Currently Caribbean from PortMiami after Virgin cancelled her 2024-25 Australia programme in February 2024.
  • Brilliant Lady — Fourth and newest ship, IMO 9870654, yard 6304, entered commercial service 5 September 2025 after an 18-month lay-up. Brilliant-unique: the only Lady with a Panama-Canal-capable adapted frame, enabling West Coast and Alaska sailings, plus the Janice Sung mermaid figurehead. Currently on Virgin's first West Coast deployment ex-Los Angeles (from 6 April 2026), repositioning to Seattle for Virgin's first Alaska season in May 2026.

All four ships are strictly 18+ adults-only, with 20+ included restaurants, the Manor nightclub, Richard's Rooftop, Squid Ink tattoo parlor, Voyage Vinyl record store, and included gratuities/WiFi/fitness classes. None have Cirque du Soleil — Virgin has never had that partnership and uses in-house production shows.

Frequently Asked Questions About Brilliant Lady

What dining options are available on Brilliant Lady?

Brilliant Lady has over 20 included restaurants and cafés — not a traditional main dining room or buffet. Complimentary specialty venues include The Wake (steakhouse, raw bar, tableside martini service), Pink Agave (contemporary Mexican with Tom Dixon interiors and a fully gluten-free menu), Extra Virgin (Italian), Gunbae (Korean BBQ with round griddle tables), Razzle Dazzle (vegetable-forward breakfast, brunch, and dinner), and Test Kitchen (chef's experimental tasting menu inspired by Escoffier). Quick-serve options include The Pizza Place, The Galley food hall, Sun Club Cafe, and Grounds Club. All dining is included at no extra charge. Brilliant Lady is hardware-identical to her sisters on the dining side — the only class differentiators are the Scarlet-exclusive Lucky Lotus Chinese concept and the Valiant-exclusive Ariya Indian restaurant (from May 2026). Brilliant's distinguishing feature is her Panama-Canal-capable frame and her West Coast / Alaska deployment.

Is Brilliant Lady adults-only?

Yes — strictly. Brilliant Lady is adults-only: every guest must be 18 years of age or older at time of sailing. Virgin Voyages has applied this policy across all four Lady Class ships since launch. There are no children's clubs, no kids' menus, no family staterooms, and no waterslides. The adult-only policy is the central design choice of the Virgin Voyages concept.

Does Brilliant Lady have Cirque du Soleil?

No. Virgin Voyages has never had a Cirque du Soleil partnership. Brilliant Lady's entertainment is produced in-house in collaboration with creative partners including House of Yes (The Manor nightclub) and theatre producers such as Randy Weiner. Productions in The Red Room include Persephone, Booked!, Another Rose, Duel Reality, and signature immersive experiences. Signature ship-wide events include Scarlet Night, the once-per-sailing red-themed party — this is a fleet-wide class event, not a Scarlet-Lady exclusive.

Who was Brilliant Lady's godmother?

Brilliant Lady does not have a publicly named traditional christening godmother. Virgin Voyages has broken with cruise-industry convention and does not hold formal christening ceremonies. Virgin did not publish ceremony details for her 5 September 2025 commercial debut in New York. In March 2022, Virgin appointed Jennifer Lopez as fleet-wide "Chief Entertainment and Lifestyle Officer" — this is a brand-partnership title, not a ship godmother role. Brilliant Lady's most distinctive naming detail is her mermaid figurehead designed by Toronto illustrator Janice Sung, blending Renaissance and Japanese/Chinese art influences.

Where does Brilliant Lady sail in 2026?

On 6 April 2026 Brilliant Lady became the first Virgin Voyages ship (as of 2026) to dock at the Port of Los Angeles — the inaugural berth of Virgin's first-ever West Coast deployment (as of 2026). She is currently running 5-day round-trip sailings from LA down the Mexican Riviera calling at Ensenada and other Baja ports. In May 2026 she will reposition to Seattle to launch Virgin Voyages' inaugural Alaska season (also calling Vancouver). This West Coast and Alaska programme is only possible because Brilliant Lady is the only Lady Class ship with an adapted frame (modified deck and lifeboat overhangs) that allows Panama Canal transit — her three sisters cannot. Her previous seasons were NYC maiden (September–October 2025) and Miami Caribbean (October 2025–April 2026).

What is included in a Brilliant Lady fare?

Every Virgin Voyages fare on Brilliant Lady includes: all 20+ restaurants and cafés (no cover charges), all gratuities, basic WiFi, still and sparkling water, non-pressed juices, sodas, and group fitness classes at the Athletic Club. Not included: premium WiFi tiers, specialty coffees and alcoholic beverages, Bar Tab drink packages, spa treatments, and shore excursions. Virgin's "Sailor Loot" shipboard credit promotions are frequently offered at booking.
