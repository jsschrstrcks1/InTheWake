Carnival Jubilee — Deck Plans, Live Tracker, Dining & Videos

Looking for Carnival Jubilee planning info? This page covers deck plans, live ship tracking, dining venues, and video tours to help you plan your Carnival cruise aboard Carnival Jubilee.

Carnival Jubilee tends to suit travelers who want mega-ship amenities with Texas-themed entertainment and BOLT roller coaster. It's ideal if you want Carnival's signature fun atmosphere with Excel-class innovation and year-round sailings from Galveston. If you're looking for a smaller, more intimate experience or different homeports, you may want to explore Vista class or Dream class instead.

Frequently Asked Questions

What makes Carnival Jubilee unique compared to other Carnival ships?

Carnival Jubilee is the third Excel-class ship (183,521 GT, 5,374 guests) featuring BOLT roller coaster, LNG propulsion, and the unique Texas-themed Shores zone. Homeported year-round in Galveston, it brings mega-ship amenities to Texas cruisers without flying to Florida.

Which ships are in the same class as Carnival Jubilee?

Carnival Jubilee is part of the Excel Class, which includes Carnival Mardi Gras (2021), Carnival Celebration (2022), and upcoming Carnival Festivale (2027). All ships share BOLT coaster and LNG propulsion.

What dining options are available on Carnival Jubilee?

Jubilee offers main dining rooms, Guy's Burger Joint, BlueIguana Cantina, specialty restaurants including Fahrenheit 555, Bonsai Sushi, Emeril's Bistro 1396, plus The Brass Magnolia in the Texas-themed Shores zone. See the Dining Venues section above for current pricing and full details.

Where does Carnival Jubilee typically sail?

Carnival Jubilee is homeported year-round in Galveston, Texas, offering 7-day Western Caribbean cruises to destinations including Cozumel, Costa Maya, Roatan, and Mahogany Bay.

How can I see Carnival Jubilee's current location?
