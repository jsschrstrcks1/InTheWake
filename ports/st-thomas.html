St. Thomas

St. Thomas: My Absolute Favorite Caribbean Day Where America Meets Paradise

Region: US Virgin Islands  |  Port: Charlotte Amalie  |  Currency: USD (US Territory)

My St. Thomas Logbook

Sailing into Charlotte Amalie harbor surrounded by emerald hills dotted with rainbow-colored houses is pure magic — there's a reason this view has graced a thousand postcards. The buildings cascade down the steep hillsides like stacked Lego blocks painted in every shade of coral, yellow, and seafoam, centuries of Danish colonial architecture blending with Caribbean vibrancy. I'm always on the top deck screaming when Magens Bay appears around the bend, that heart-shaped beach backed by palm groves that consistently lands in "World's Best" lists.

But before I chase the beach, I usually spend an hour exploring Charlotte Amalie's historic heart. Fort Christian is impossible to miss — that distinctive brick-red tower has stood guard over the harbor since 1680, making it the oldest standing structure in the US Virgin Islands. The Danes built it when this was their Caribbean colony, and it's served as a church, courthouse, governor's mansion, and jail over the centuries. The small museum inside offers a fascinating window into the island's complex history, from the Taino people through Danish rule to American acquisition in 1917. Standing on those ramparts looking out at your cruise ship, you're literally standing where pirates once surveyed their targets.

My perfect St. Thomas day: off the ship by 7:30 a.m. and straight into a shared safari taxi bound for Magens Bay. These open-air converted trucks are pure Caribbean — wind in your hair, reggae on the radio, driver pointing out celebrity mansions as you wind up the mountain road. The fare to Magens is surprisingly reasonable when split among passengers, and the ride itself (with those vertiginous cliff views) is half the adventure.

Magens Bay is everything you've heard — voted one of the top-10 beaches in the world for good reason. That impossibly turquoise water stays calm because the bay is protected on three sides, making it perfect for floating endlessly while staring at the sky. I rent two loungers and an umbrella (great value for the whole day) and alternate between swimming, floating, and reading until my soul feels reset. The small entrance fee includes clean facilities, and there's a beach bar when you need a cold Presidente or frozen drink.

The Moment That Stays With Me: Floating on my back in Magens Bay at 10 a.m., completely alone in that crystal water, with zero sound except my own heartbeat and the distant laughter of children building sandcastles. The sun warm on my face, the salt holding me up, and realizing I'm exactly where I'm supposed to be. That's what St. Thomas gives you — permission to just exist.

Around noon I head to Paradise Point, taking the skyride tram from Havensight — a scenic seven-minute ascent 700 feet up Flag Hill. The views from the top are insane: 360-degree panoramas of the harbor, neighboring islands, and your ship looking like a toy in the water below. The bar up top is famous for the Bushwacker — a frozen concoction (rum, Kahlúa, cream, and secret magic) that's become a St. Thomas institution. The combination of those views and that signature drink is why Paradise Point stays packed with cruisers.

If you're feeling adventurous, Blackbeard's Castle is a short walk from the skyride base. This 17th-century stone watchtower allegedly once belonged to the infamous pirate himself — though historians debate whether Edward Teach ever actually set foot here. Either way, the tower offers spectacular views, and the surrounding gardens are filled with quirky sculptures and historical plaques. The climb up the "99 Steps" (actually 103 — go ahead and count) from the main road is a quad-burner, but the photo ops and pirate lore make it worthwhile.

Lunch is lobster rolls at Mafolie Hotel's hilltop restaurant — insane views of Magens Bay from the terrace, reasonable prices, and that satisfied exhaustion of a morning well-spent. Or head downtown to Gladys' Cafe for authentic Virgin Islands cuisine (the conch in butter sauce is revelatory).

Afternoon is duty-free shopping along Main Street and the side alleys — St. Thomas is famous for jewelry, watches, liquor, and perfume at prices that actually beat stateside. I stock up on rum (the $1,200 duty-free allowance for US citizens is generous) and usually grab a piece of larimar jewelry, that sky-blue stone found only in the Caribbean. No passport hassle, no currency exchange, no language barrier — just good old American vacation with palm trees.

If there's still time, a sunset sail on The VI Cat or Heaven & Hell catamaran is pure magic — snorkeling, open bar, and watching the sky turn orange while the islands go silhouette. I'm always last back to the ship, sunburned shoulders and the biggest grin, already planning my next visit.

Getting Around St. Thomas

St. Thomas has two cruise piers — Havensight (closer to town) and Crown Bay (requires short taxi ride). Both areas have shopping right at the pier.

  • Havensight to Downtown Charlotte Amalie: 15-minute walk approximately 11 football fields, 40 blue whales end-to-end, or 880 emperor penguins stacked skyward or quick taxi
  • Havensight to Magens Bay: Shared safari taxi (great value, ~20 minutes)
  • Paradise Point Skyride: Walking distance from Havensight
  • Crown Bay to Downtown: 5-minute taxi
  • Fort Christian: Downtown, 5-minute walk from Main Street approximately 33 school buses, 16 blue whales end-to-end, or 352 emperor penguins forming an improbable tower
  • Blackbeard's Castle: Above downtown, climb 99 Steps or taxi to top

Pro tip: Safari taxis (open-air trucks) are the authentic St. Thomas experience and very affordable when shared. They run regular routes to Magens Bay and other popular spots.

Depth Soundings Ashore

Practical tips before you step off the ship.

The steep hills and open-air safari trucks are St. Thomas's way of giving you a free roller-coaster ride with million-dollar views! Wear comfortable shoes if you're exploring downtown — those historic streets are hilly. Everything is in USD, cell service works normally, and it feels remarkably like home despite the palm trees. The only "warning" is that you'll want to move here.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a passport for St. Thomas?

No — St. Thomas is a US territory, so American citizens only need a valid ID. It's like visiting Hawaii, just with better beaches. US dollars everywhere, your cell phone works, and you can bring back $1,200 in duty-free goods.

What's the best beach?

Magens Bay is consistently rated top-10 in the world — calm, clear, protected water with excellent facilities. Small entrance fee is worth it. For a closer option, Coki Beach near Coral World has great snorkeling but can get crowded.

Where's the best Bushwacker?

Paradise Point at the top of the skyride is legendary. Mountain Top (highest point on island) claims to have invented the Bushwacker's cousin, the Banana Daiquiri. Both are worth the trip for the views alone.

Is the duty-free shopping actually good?

Yes — liquor, jewelry (especially tanzanite and larimar), watches, and perfume are genuinely discounted. US citizens can bring back $1,200 duty-free. Main Street and side alleys have the best selection. Compare prices before you buy.

At a Glance

Quick Answer: St. Thomas is my absolute favorite Caribbean day — Magens Bay (top-10 beach in the world), Paradise Point Bushwackers with 360° views, duty-free shopping, and the convenience of US territory. No passport, USD everywhere, feels like home with palm trees.
