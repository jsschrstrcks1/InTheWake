## Welcome {#welcome}

This pack exists to make one specific cruise easier: **Norwegian Escape**, sailing 7 nights from Port Canaveral, **November 20 – 27, 2026** — Friday to Friday, with **Thanksgiving Day (Thursday November 26) at sea or on the cay**. The itinerary calls at **Puerto Plata (Amber Cove), St. Thomas, Tortola, and Great Stirrup Cay**. It's a **hosted solo cruisers group cruise** organized by **Pam Walters**.

**Pam is your on-ship host.** Communication for this group runs through the [Thanksgiving Group Cruise Host Pam Facebook chat](https://www.facebook.com/share/1DuHTTtgdc/) — join before sailing for pre-cruise logistics, the sailaway gathering, group dinner timing, and the daily where-are-we-meeting rhythm. Once aboard, Pam is the in-person organizer. For booking questions and what's bundled with your group rate, contact your travel agent.

**A note on the Thanksgiving angle.** This sailing is built around the holiday. NCL serves a full Thanksgiving feast on Thursday Nov 26 — traditional turkey, stuffing, sweet potatoes, pumpkin pie — typically across all main dining rooms with no extra charge. **Specialty restaurants stay open with their regular menus** if you'd rather have steak or Italian. For solo cruisers, Thanksgiving aboard is often quieter and warmer than Thanksgiving alone at home — that's part of the editorial draw of this sailing.

**A note on Friday-to-Friday.** Most cruise weeks run Saturday-to-Saturday. NCL repositions Escape's schedule to Friday-to-Friday this week so that Thanksgiving lands mid-itinerary rather than on debark day. Plan flights and pre-cruise hotels accordingly.

**A note on November Caribbean weather.** The official Atlantic hurricane season runs through November 30, but the late-November risk is low — peak passes in mid-October. November typically delivers calmer seas and lower humidity than peak summer cruising. Bring a light rain layer; otherwise, this is one of the calmer Caribbean weeks of the year. See [Section 7](#section-7) for the seasickness backstop.

It is calm by design. No urgency, no upsell, no countdown timer. The site that produced this pack is independent — no cruise-line affiliations, no shore-excursion commissions, no display ads.

**Includes an offline-capable Emergency Contacts section** — a fillable family handoff card, cruise-line and consulate numbers tailored to this itinerary, available offline after first load via [In the Wake](/index.html)'s PWA. Designed to share with one trusted person ashore before sailing.

If you're sailing solo for the first time, you are not the only one in this group doing so. The [solo cruising stories on this site](/solo.html) are worth reading whether before, during, or after this trip.

## Section 1 — Your Ship: Norwegian Escape {#section-1}

### The lead Breakaway Plus ship — now in her eleventh year

[Norwegian Escape](/ships/norwegian/norwegian-escape.html) was delivered October 22, 2015 by Meyer Werft at Papenburg, Germany — the **lead ship of NCL's Breakaway Plus class** (the larger evolution of the Breakaway class). Her sisters arrived later: Norwegian Joy (2017), [Norwegian Bliss](/ships/norwegian/norwegian-bliss.html) (2018), and [Norwegian Encore](/ships/norwegian/norwegian-encore.html) (2019). By your November 2026 sailing she's eleven years into service: thoroughly settled, crew rhythms long established, all the new-ship issues long resolved.

She was christened **November 9, 2015 at PortMiami** by **Pitbull** (Armando Christian Pérez) — the Miami-born rapper who became the **first male godfather ever bestowed in cruise-ship christening history**. The convention had been "godmother" for centuries — NCL broke it with Escape. Quietly distinctive in cruise lore.

If you've sailed [Norwegian Bliss](/ships/norwegian/norwegian-bliss.html) or [Norwegian Encore](/ships/norwegian/norwegian-encore.html), **Escape is the same Breakaway Plus hull and the same general layout** with a few refinements that distinguish her from the later sisters. The Alaska-themed Wyland hull mural on Bliss is replaced on Escape by **Guy Harvey's marlin-and-billfish hull artwork** — visible the moment the ship is at the pier. If you're new to Breakaway Plus, the [Ship Size Atlas](/tools/ship-size-atlas.html) shows how she compares against the newer Prima Plus class and other megaships.

### What's distinctive about Escape

- **The Guy Harvey hull artwork** — Escape's hull mural depicts a leaping marlin, sea turtle, swordfish, and other Caribbean game fish. Visible from any pier shot. Guy Harvey is a Caribbean marine artist; the partnership is fitting for an Eastern Caribbean ship.
- **The Sky Walk ropes course** — a three-story rope-and-plank course running the width of the upper decks, with two narrow planks that extend eight feet over the side of the ship and five Sky Rail zip tracks that fly over open ocean. At launch in 2015 NCL marketed this as the largest ropes course at sea — Escape's defining outdoor signature.
- **The Aqua Park** — at launch, marketed as the largest at sea: **the Aqua Racer** (twin-tube racing slides), **Free Fall slides** (steep enclosed drops), **The Whip** (a tubular spiral slide), two pools, four hot tubs, and a dedicated kids' area.
- **[Observation Lounge](/restaurants/ncl/observation-lounge.html)** — wraparound glass lounge; sunrise coffee here is the day's best 30 minutes on any Caribbean morning.
- **Vibe Beach Club** — adults-only enclave with private pool, two hot tubs, dedicated bar, shaded loungers. ~$99/day or $399/week pre-cruise.
- **The Haven by Norwegian** — ship-within-a-ship suite enclave with private restaurant, lounge, pool, and butler service.
- **Pincho Tapas Bar** — a small Spanish tapas room added in Escape's refurbishment cycles; specialty dining at small-bites cadence.

### What's NOT on Escape

- **No go-kart track / Speedway** — NCL introduced the at-sea Speedway with [Norwegian Joy](/ships/norwegian/norwegian-joy.html) in 2017, two years after Escape. The later three Breakaway Plus sisters (Joy, [Bliss](/ships/norwegian/norwegian-bliss.html), [Encore](/ships/norwegian/norwegian-encore.html)) all got the two-level Speedway; Escape did not.
- **No Indulge Food Hall** — that's a Prima-class signature. Escape predates the Prima Class by seven years.
- **No Penrose Atrium** — also Prima-class.
- **No Aqua Slidecoaster** — Prima Plus only (Aqua, Luna).

### Cabin categories — the solo calculus

The group flyer shows three price points: **Solo Studio $1,249**, **Ocean View $1,569**, **Balcony $1,839**. Honest reads:

- **Solo Studio at $1,249** — NCL's signature solo offering. Escape has **82 dedicated Studio cabins** — one of the largest Studio inventories on any cruise ship. Roughly 100 sq ft, windowless, with **no single supplement** and **[Studio Lounge](/restaurants/ncl/studio-lounge.html) access** (free coffee, social space, hosted hours, scheduled meet-and-greets for solo cruisers). The Studio Lounge is the daily social anchor; solos cluster there independent of the group meetups. **At $1,249, this is the price-leader for solo travel.**
- **Ocean View at $1,569** — a window cabin, daylight in the morning, the world feels different. The $320 differential buys real space + a window. **The value pick for most solo cruisers.**
- **Balcony at $1,839** — a private outdoor space, which on a Caribbean cruise matters more than on Alaska. Sea-day sunrises, port-day return-from-shore decompression, evenings with a book. At a $270 differential over Ocean View, **the splurge that pencils out** if the math works.

**Before you commit, run the [stateroom sanity check](/articles/stateroom-sanity-check.html)** — a four-question filter that catches the bookings most likely to leave a cruiser unhappy. Studios screen well for confident sleepers; the filter flags the few cases where the windowless interior is a problem. Once chosen, the [cabin organization guide](/articles/cruise-cabin-organization.html) covers small-space pragmatics.

### Dining

Escape's dining map:

**Included with your fare (no extra charge):**

- **[Manhattan Room](/restaurants/ncl/the-manhattan-room.html)** (Deck 7 aft) — main dining room with a small dance floor and live music most evenings. Most atmospheric of the included rooms.
- **Taste** and **Savor** (Deck 6) — paired main dining rooms; same menu, different room atmosphere.
- **[Garden Café](/restaurants/ncl/garden-cafe.html)** (Deck 15) — buffet; long hours; upper-deck view earns its keep on sea days.
- **[The Local](/restaurants/ncl/the-local.html)** — pub food + classics; long hours; the fallback for any time.
- **O'Sheehan's** — Irish pub-style; included; quieter than The Local late at night.

**Specialty dining (extra charge or via Specialty Dining Package):**

- **[Cagney's Steakhouse](/restaurants/ncl/cagneys-steakhouse.html)** — NCL's standard steakhouse; reliably good.
- **[Le Bistro](/restaurants/ncl/le-bistro.html)** — French; the most romantic specialty room on NCL ships.
- **[Teppanyaki](/restaurants/ncl/teppanyaki.html)** — Japanese hibachi; the showy room; group-friendly seating.
- **[Q Texas Smokehouse](/restaurants/ncl/q-texas-smokehouse.html)** — Texas BBQ.
- **[Los Lobos](/restaurants/ncl/los-lobos.html)** — modern Mexican.
- **[Ocean Blue](/restaurants/ncl/ocean-blue.html)** — Geoffrey Zakarian-partnered seafood (verify whether running in 2026).
- **[La Cucina](/restaurants/ncl/la-cucina.html)** — Italian.
- **[Food Republic](/restaurants/ncl/food-republic.html)** — pan-Asian small plates; one of NCL's most distinctive specialty rooms. Worth the visit even if you're not a sushi-and-pho person.
- **Pincho Tapas Bar** — Spanish tapas; small bites and wine.

**Drinks.** NCL's drink-package math: the Premium Beverage Package is commonly included in the **"Free at Sea"** promotional bundle when booking. If your group fare included Free at Sea, drinks may already be covered — **verify what's bundled with your booking agent**. Otherwise the package runs ~$109/day per person + 20% gratuity at pre-cruise rates; breaks even at ~5–6 drinks per day. The [drink-calculator](/drink-calculator.html) lets you put in your real consumption pattern and see the breakeven. The companion article [Is the drink package worth it?](/articles/is-drink-package-worth-it.html) walks through worked examples.

**The honest first-day mistake to avoid:** Don't sign up for an unlimited drink package at the front desk on Day 1 unless you've already done the drink-calculator math. Decide before you board, ideally as part of your Free At Sea bundle. Mid-sail upgrades cost more per day and give you fewer days of usage.

### Connectivity at sea

NCL's Wi-Fi packages are tier-based and frequently bundled into Free at Sea. The [internet at sea guide](/internet-at-sea.html) covers cross-line baselines and how to avoid cellular roaming charges in port.

For most solo cruisers in this group:

- Use included basic Wi-Fi if your fare bundles any
- Don't try to stream video at sea
- Use port-day cellular when possible (DR uses Claro/Altice; USVI uses US carriers normally; BVI uses Flow/Digicel; Bahamas has no terrestrial coverage at Great Stirrup Cay)
- Keep one trusted person ashore informed via a daily one-line message — see [Section 6](#section-6)

## Section 2 — Day by Day {#section-2}

The day-by-day below uses the typical NCL Escape Eastern Caribbean rhythm. **Verify your specific times in the NCL app** — the published sequence sometimes shifts between sailings.

### Day 1 — Friday, November 20 — Port Canaveral (embarkation)

**Terminal:** **Port Canaveral, Cruise Terminal 10** (NCL's typical Central Florida berth — verify in the NCL app 14 days before sailing).

**Getting to Port Canaveral:**

- **Orlando International (MCO) → Port Canaveral:** Uber/Lyft $60–80, 45–60 min. Shared cruise shuttle ~$30/person, slower.
- **If driving:** Port Canaveral garage ~$17–22/day (significantly cheaper than PortMiami's $35); reservable in advance via the Port Canaveral website.
- **Embark window:** Typically 11:30 AM – 3:30 PM. **All-aboard 3:30 PM.** Ship departs ~4:00–5:00 PM.

**Pre-cruise hotel — strongly recommended.** Same-day flights to a 3:30 PM Friday all-aboard are risky given Thanksgiving-week travel volume. Two viable bases:

- **Cocoa Beach** (15 min from port) — beach town, walking distance to the pier and beach. Right call if you want to be near the port.
- **Orlando MCO area** (45 min from port) — more hotel options, longer Friday-morning transfer.

**Thursday Nov 19 — fly into MCO.** This is the day before your Friday embarkation — and, usefully, a full week ahead of the Thanksgiving travel crush (which peaks on the Wednesday before Thanksgiving, Nov 25, while you're already at sea). Still, book flights early and consider arriving Wednesday Nov 18 for extra buffer. Pam will confirm any pre-cruise gathering plans in the Facebook group chat.

**The realistic Day 1 timeline:**

- **11:00 AM:** Terminal opens. NCL online check-in via the NCL app — complete it before arrival to skip a 20-minute queue.
- **12:00 PM:** Aboard. Cabins typically open by 1:30 PM; until then, lunch at the buffet ([Garden Café](/restaurants/ncl/garden-cafe.html), Deck 15) or [The Local](/restaurants/ncl/the-local.html) (Deck 6).
- **1:30 PM:** Cabin open. Meet your stateroom steward.
- **3:00 PM:** Mandatory muster drill — partly app-based (watch the safety video in the NCL app) plus an in-person check-in at your assigned station.
- **4:00 PM:** **Sailaway.** Pam's group gathers at the **[Observation Lounge](/restaurants/ncl/observation-lounge.html)** (Deck 15 forward, panoramic) — verify in the Facebook group chat. Less crowded than the main pool deck.
- **5:30 – 8:00 PM:** Dinner. Pam's nightly group dinner is at the **Manhattan Room (or one of the main dining rooms)** at 5:30 PM — verify in the Facebook group chat. Show up to whichever nights work for you.
- **Later evening:** NCL hosts a **Studio meet-and-greet** sometime on Day 1 in the [Studio Lounge](/restaurants/ncl/studio-lounge.html) — check the NCL app's daily program.

**What to pack in your carry-on for Day 1:** swimsuit (pools open before checked bags arrive), light layer (Florida November evenings can be 65–75°F outdoors but ship interior runs ~72°F), all medications, change of clothes, **passport** (required — DR, USVI, BVI are foreign or special-jurisdiction; passport is the cleanest documentation), phone charger, refillable water bottle. Your checked bags may not arrive until 7–9 PM.

**Money/card:** Your NCL keycard is your ID, room key, and onboard charge. **Currency aboard: US Dollar.**

### Day 2 — Saturday, November 21 — Cruising at sea

The settling-in day. The ship is heading southeast across the Florida Straits and Gulf Stream toward the Dominican Republic.

**Evening — group dinner.**

Pam's standard 5:30 PM group dinner. The Manhattan Room seats groups easily.

### Day 3 — Sunday, November 22 — Puerto Plata (Amber Cove), Dominican Republic {#day-puerto-plata}

**Arrival:** Approximately 8:00 AM. **Depart:** 5:00 PM. Escape docks at **Amber Cove** — Carnival Corporation's purpose-built cruise port complex on the Dominican Republic's north coast, about 30 minutes from the city of Puerto Plata.

**The shape of Puerto Plata / Amber Cove.** Amber Cove is the cruise-port complex; Puerto Plata is the actual city. Most cruisers stay at Amber Cove and never see Puerto Plata; some venture inland. Amber Cove has a beach, multiple pools, a zip line, restaurants, shops, and a paid waterpark. The complex was built specifically for cruise visitors and is highly developed. If you want a low-stress "stay near the ship" day, Amber Cove delivers.

**Free with your cruise card (at Amber Cove):**

- Multiple pool zones (free; first-come for chairs)
- Beach access (small but real)
- Shops and the duty-free zone
- Restaurants for purchase (most affordable in USD)

**Paid (decide ahead):**

- **Damajagua Waterfalls** — the inland excursion that justifies leaving Amber Cove. A series of 27 cascades you can climb and slide through. Ship excursion ~$95/person; independent operators ~$50–60. 4–5 hour day total. Wet shoes required.
- **Puerto Plata City Tour** — visit the colonial fortress (San Felipe), the amber museum, the malecón waterfront. Half-day; ~$40–60 with transport.
- **Ocean World Adventure Park** — dolphins, sea lions, tigers, sharks. ~$70 entry; controversial ethics for some travelers (research before booking).
- **Mount Isabel de Torres cable car** — short ride to the top of the mountain with the Christ statue, botanical gardens, panoramic views. ~$25.
- **Cofresí Beach** — public beach 15 minutes from Amber Cove; quieter than the cruise-port crowd.

**For first-time Puerto Plata:** if it's your first Caribbean DR call, Damajagua Waterfalls is the experience that justifies leaving the cruise port. If you'd rather stay calm, Amber Cove's pools + beach + lunch is a fine day.

**Pack for Puerto Plata:** swimsuit, water shoes (Damajagua requires them), sunscreen (SPF 30+), sun hat, light cover-up, $40–60 USD cash in small bills (USD widely accepted; Dominican peso not necessary), insect repellent for inland excursions.

**Worth a look on the way in:** Build your day in the [Port Day Planner](/tools/port-day-planner.html); record what actually happened in the [Port Logbook](/tools/port-tracker.html) on the way back.

### Day 4 — Monday, November 23 — St. Thomas, US Virgin Islands {#day-st-thomas}

![Charlotte Amalie, St. Thomas — colorful Danish colonial buildings cascading down the hillside above the harbor](/ports/img/st-thomas/city-view.webp)

**Arrival:** Approximately 10:00 AM. **Depart:** 6:00 PM. Escape typically docks at **Crown Bay** or **Havensight (WICO)** — both in the harbor at Charlotte Amalie. Crown Bay is ~2 miles west of downtown; Havensight is at the eastern end of downtown.

**What St. Thomas actually is.** Charlotte Amalie is the largest cruise port in the Caribbean by call volume — on a busy day, 5–6 ships of 3,000+ guests each are in harbor. Downtown is well-organized for the cruise crowd: duty-free shopping (jewelry is the marquee category; St. Thomas was the duty-free engine of US Caribbean commerce for decades), the historic Synagogue, Blackbeard's Castle (overpriced, climb-the-stairs), and ferries to the surrounding islands.

**Free, walkable (from Havensight; Crown Bay requires shuttle/taxi):**

- **Downtown Charlotte Amalie** — Main Street is the duty-free spine; Government Hill has the historic homes; the Synagogue (1833 — the second-oldest in continuous use in the Western Hemisphere) is worth a quiet half-hour.
- **The waterfront walking path** — quiet, scenic.

**Paid (decide ahead):**

- **Magens Bay** — one of the most-photographed beaches in the world. ~$12 r/t per person via taxi-shuttle; $5 entry. The standard St. Thomas beach day.
- **Sapphire Beach** — quieter alternative, ~$15 r/t taxi; better snorkeling at the east end.
- **Mountain Top** — short drive to the highest point on the island; the harbor view that ends up on most postcards. Banana daiquiri tradition. ~$25 r/t.
- **Ferry to St. John** — Cruz Bay ferries leave Red Hook every 30 min; ~$8 each way. Trunk Bay beach in St. John's national park is one of the Caribbean's signature beaches.
- **Skyride to Paradise Point** — gondola from Havensight; ~$24 r/t.
- **Coral World Ocean Park** — sea-life encounters; ~$25 entry.

**For first-time St. Thomas:** Magens Bay or the ferry to St. John are the two great calls. Magens is the standard St. Thomas beach day; St. John's Trunk Bay is the "this is the Caribbean of postcards" experience but takes longer (the ferry adds an hour each way).

**Pack for St. Thomas:** swimsuit, water shoes, reef-safe sunscreen, sun hat, $60–80 USD cash, snorkel gear if you have your own.

**Worth a look:** the [St. Thomas port page](/ports/st-thomas.html), its [gallery](/ports/st-thomas.html#gallery), and the [St. Thomas logbook](/ports/st-thomas.html#logbook).

### Day 5 — Tuesday, November 24 — Tortola, British Virgin Islands {#day-tortola}

**Arrival:** Approximately 8:00 AM. **Depart:** 5:00 PM. Escape docks at **Tortola Pier Park** in Road Town, the BVI capital — directly adjacent to the cruise terminal complex, no tender required.

**What Tortola actually is.** The largest of the British Virgin Islands; population ~24,000; a British Overseas Territory still using the US Dollar despite the British flag. The BVI is *quieter* than St. Thomas — fewer ships, fewer aggressive vendors, a more deliberate pace. The island is mountainous; the postcard destinations are reached by safari taxi over winding roads, not on foot.

**Free, walkable from the pier:**

- **Road Town** — Main Street walkable; the J.R. O'Neal Botanic Gardens (~10-min walk, ~$5 entry — quietly excellent); the old courthouse and government complex.
- **Pier Park** — a planned cruise-port shopping/dining complex; restaurants and shops at the gangway.

**Paid (decide ahead):**

- **Cane Garden Bay** — one of the BVI's signature beaches; a curve of white sand backed by green hills. ~$20–30 taxi each way. Calm water, restaurants, bars. **For most cruisers in Tortola, this is the day.**
- **The Baths on Virgin Gorda** — the postcard of the BVI. Massive granite boulders forming sea caves and natural pools you swim through. Requires a ferry from Tortola to Virgin Gorda + taxi. A full half-day; with the 5:00 PM all-aboard there's more margin here than on shorter Tortola calls. ~$150–200/person all in.
- **Sage Mountain National Park** — Tortola's high point and rainforest. Hiking; cool temperatures; less-crowded. Half-day excursion.
- **Sailing or snorkeling day trip** — catamarans to Norman Island or the BVI's small reef islands. $130–180/person; standout for water-lovers.

### Day 7 — Thursday, November 26 — Great Stirrup Cay, Bahamas (THANKSGIVING) {#day-great-stirrup-cay}

**What Great Stirrup Cay is right now (November 2026).** NCL leased Great Stirrup Cay in 1977 — the first cruise line in the industry to take exclusive use of a private island in the Caribbean — and bought it outright in 1986. You'll arrive at the new **Welcome Plaza** (the redesigned tender-pier arrival area) and the cay is the most developed it has ever been.

## Section 3 — Pre-Cruise Countdown {#section-3}

### 6+ months before — booking and big decisions

- **Confirm the booking with your travel agent.** Cabin category, final price, what's bundled with Free at Sea.

### The Friday-to-Friday PTO + holiday-week travel reality

NCL repositioned Escape to Friday-to-Friday for this week so that Thanksgiving lands mid-itinerary instead of on debark day. That creates two real complications for a working solo cruiser, and the pack would be hiding the ball if it didn't name them:

- **Friday Nov 20 is not a US federal holiday.** Most employers do not give the Friday before Thanksgiving off, and Thursday Nov 26 (the actual holiday) you're already at sea. If you take the full cruise window you're burning four weekdays of PTO (Fri Nov 20, plus Mon Nov 23, Tue Nov 24, Wed Nov 25) since Thanksgiving Day itself is already a paid holiday at most employers. That Fri-Mon-Tue-Wed pattern is the absolute minimum.
- **The Thanksgiving travel peak is the Wednesday before the holiday (Nov 25) and the Sunday after — and both fall while you're at sea, so they can't touch your outbound flight.** What you're managing on Nov 19–20 is ordinary pre-holiday-week volume plus late-November weather at whatever northern hub you connect through. The sane play: take Wednesday Nov 18 off too, fly in Wednesday afternoon, and give yourself a full buffer day. Add one day to PTO, save the entire trip from a single delayed flight cascading.
- **Working from Cocoa Beach Thursday is the compromise** if your employer is flexible. Drive or fly in Wednesday, work remote from a Cocoa Beach hotel Thursday morning, then walk to the port Friday. Same number of PTO days as a non-cruise Thanksgiving week.
- **The return is Friday Nov 27 — Black Friday.** Domestic MCO traffic on Black Friday afternoon is the worst of the year in either direction. **Book a morning flight Friday Nov 27 if you can** — anything after 1 PM is exposed to the afternoon spike. International flights have more margin (international concourses don't crush the way domestic does), but the parking-and-rideshare bottleneck still bites.

## Section 7 — Seasickness + November Caribbean Weather {#section-7}

Late November Eastern Caribbean is among the **calmest cruise weeks of the year**. The hurricane season officially ends November 30, but peak risk passed in October. The Gulf Stream and Caribbean transits this week are usually mild.

**If you're prone to motion sickness:**

- **Bonine (meclizine)** — once-daily, less drowsy than Dramamine.
- **Sea-bands (acupressure wristbands)** — placebo or not, many find them effective.
- **Ginger** (capsules, tea, candies) — mild but real.
- **Scopolamine patches** (prescription, behind-the-ear) — strongest option.
- **Stay mid-ship and low** in your cabin choice. Mid-ship Deck 9–12 has the least felt motion.

**If you get sick onboard:** the ship's medical center stocks IV anti-nausea medication. Bring travel insurance — onboard medical bills are real.

NCL serves a full Thanksgiving dinner across the main dining rooms — **the Manhattan Room, Taste, and Savor** — on Thanksgiving evening. Typical menu (verify in the daily program once aboard):

- **Roast turkey** with stuffing, mashed potatoes, gravy
- **Cranberry sauce** (typically both whole-berry and jellied)
- **Candied sweet potatoes / yams**
- **Green bean casserole or roasted vegetables**
- **Dinner rolls**
- **Pumpkin pie, pecan pie, apple pie** for dessert

**No extra charge** — this is part of your included main-dining-room experience.

**Pam's group typically gathers at the Manhattan Room 5:30 PM seating** for the holiday meal. Verify in the Facebook group chat.

### If you'd rather NOT do traditional Thanksgiving

**Specialty restaurants stay open with their regular menus.** [Cagney's Steakhouse](/restaurants/ncl/cagneys-steakhouse.html), [Le Bistro](/restaurants/ncl/le-bistro.html), [Teppanyaki](/restaurants/ncl/teppanyaki.html), and [Food Republic](/restaurants/ncl/food-republic.html) all serve their usual menus on Thanksgiving Day. **Reservations for specialty venues on Thanksgiving Day book out earliest** — if you want a non-turkey night, book 60+ days pre-cruise.

The [Garden Café](/restaurants/ncl/garden-cafe.html) buffet also runs a Thanksgiving spread for the lunch crowd if you'd rather have your turkey early in the day.

### Lunch on Thanksgiving Day (at Great Stirrup Cay)

The cay's **BBQ buffet lunch** runs as usual — jerk chicken, ribs, burgers. NCL typically adds Thanksgiving touches at the buffet too (maybe a small turkey station). Most cruisers eat lunch on the cay and dinner aboard. The shower-and-rest window between 4 PM and 5:30 PM is the unspoken rhythm of Thanksgiving Day on this sailing.

### For solo cruisers specifically

**Thanksgiving aboard is often quieter and warmer than Thanksgiving alone at home** — this is part of why solo cruisers book holiday sailings. Pam's group dinner is the answer to "what do I do on Thanksgiving as a single person." The Studio Lounge crowd typically gathers there too around 4–5 PM for a "before dinner" coffee or drink.

**No one will judge if you want to spend Thanksgiving evening at the Observation Lounge with a book and a glass of wine.** That's the version some cruisers come for.

### The Thanksgiving gratitude tradition (informal)

A small but recurring solo-cruiser tradition: **share what you're grateful for** with the table at the start of the Thanksgiving meal. Pam may invite this at the group dinner. If you'd rather skip, smart-casual table conversation is fine. Both rhythms work.

### The 5 PM moment

The hardest minute of the day for many solo Thanksgiving cruisers is around 4:30–5:00 PM ship time. Back home in the Eastern time zone, the family is sitting down. The grandkids are at the table. The chair you usually sit in is occupied by someone else, or it's empty. You can feel that 1,200 miles away.

This is the part nobody briefs you for, and the part this pack will not pretend doesn't happen.

Some options for that hour:

- **Be at Pam's group dinner at 5:30 PM.** The Manhattan Room with 40 other solo cruisers, most of whom are doing the same emotional accounting at the same hour, is the version of Thanksgiving the cruise actually delivers. The table is full. The conversation is light. You will eat turkey with people whose names you will know by dessert.
- **Step away if you need to.** A solo cruiser in tears on the Observation Lounge balcony at 5 PM on Thanksgiving is not unusual and not weak. Take ten minutes. Call your daughter on the ship's Wi-Fi if you can; send a text if you can't. Then come down to dinner.
- **Skip dinner entirely if that's the answer.** Room service is included with your fare; the cabin door locks; the pillow is yours. Some solo cruisers come on Thanksgiving sailings specifically because they don't want to perform celebration. That is also a valid version of this trip.

Pam knows the rhythm of this hour. You don't have to explain anything.
