## Rule
When `last-reviewed` is more than 180 days in the past, validators emit a WARNING flagging potential staleness. The rule is enforced in all three validators (port, ship, venue) with the same 180-day threshold. Standards docs do not currently describe this threshold; backfill required during Phase 6.

## Why (rationale)
Port logistics shift with cruise-line itinerary changes. Ship deployments rotate quarterly. Venue menus change seasonally. A 180-day-old review is the rough midpoint where content quality starts visibly drifting. 90 days is too noisy (warning on nearly every page); 365 is too lax (some facts go stale in under a year).

Phase 6 regenerated standards should adopt the 180-day threshold so writers know it's not arbitrary.

## Pass example
```html
<!-- reviewed in the last 180 days -->
<meta name="last-reviewed" content="2025-12-01"/>
```
(If today is 2026-04-15, that's 135 days — under threshold, no warning.)

## Fail example
```html
<meta name="last-reviewed" content="2025-08-01"/>
```
(If today is 2026-04-15, that's 257 days — validator emits `last-reviewed date is 257 days old (2025-08-01) — content may be stale`.)

## Fix guidance
When a page passes 180 days, open it, re-read it, verify the prices/distances/venues cited are still accurate, then bump `last-reviewed` to today's date. Do NOT bump the date without actually reviewing — fabricating freshness is dishonesty. If you can't do a proper review, leave the date alone and the warning acts as a queue signal.
