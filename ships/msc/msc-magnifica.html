MSC Magnifica — Deck Plans, Live Tracker, Dining & Videos

MSC Magnifica is the fourth and final ship of MSC Cruises' Musica Class, delivered on 25 February 2010 at Saint-Nazaire by STX France (the yard now operating as Chantiers de l'Atlantique) and christened on 6 March 2010 in Hamburg by Sophia Loren. She measures 95,128 gross tons and 293.8 metres in length, carries 2,506 guests at double occupancy (3,007 maximum) across approximately 1,317 cabins with a crew of 1,038, and flies the Panamanian flag. Between October and December 2025 she completed a major reconstruction at Palumbo Malta Shipyard that added a 63-suite MSC Yacht Club enclave — the first Musica-class ship to carry one — plus Butcher's Cut steakhouse, Kaito Sushi Bar, and an upgraded MSC Aurea Spa. In 2026 she is MSC's World Cruise ship (4 January to 14 May 2026, 130 days) before a Baltic summer season from Warnemünde.

Quick Answer: MSC Magnifica (95,128 GT, 2,506 double / 3,007 max, 1,038 crew, delivered 2010) is the fourth and final Musica Class ship — sisters MSC Musica, MSC Orchestra, and MSC Poesia. The October-December 2025 Palumbo Malta refit added a new 63-suite MSC Yacht Club (the first on a Musica-class hull), Butcher's Cut steakhouse, and Kaito Sushi Bar. She carries the L'Edera and Quattro Venti MDRs, Royal Theatre, and MSC Aurea Spa. 2026 is a World Cruise year (4 January – 14 May) followed by Baltic sailings from Warnemünde in summer 2026.

Who She's For: World-cruise travellers and long-voyage enthusiasts wanting MSC's most experienced grand-voyage ship — Magnifica has been MSC's World Cruise vessel for multiple seasons, with itineraries that rotate the headline ports year to year. Strong pick for guests drawn to the newly added MSC Yacht Club enclave (2025 refit, first on a Musica hull), and for travellers preferring a mid-size ship (2,506 guests) over the 4,000+ on Meraviglia-class and 4,800+ on Meraviglia-Plus hulls. Good choice for Baltic and Norwegian Fjord itineraries from Warnemünde in summer 2026. Not for guests expecting Cirque du Soleil at Sea, waterslides, or the indoor LED-dome promenade of MSC's newer ships.

MSC Magnifica is the fourth of four Musica Class ships — MSC Musica (2006), MSC Orchestra (2007), MSC Poesia (2008), and MSC Magnifica (2010). She is the largest of the four and was the last to be delivered. Her main dining rooms are L'Edera (Deck 5) and Quattro Venti (Deck 6), with the Sahara Buffet as the single pool-deck buffet on Deck 13. The October-December 2025 Palumbo Malta Shipyard reconstruction was a landmark refit — it added a complete MSC Yacht Club ship-within-a-ship enclave (63 suites in five categories, including a Royal Suite with private hot tub, plus dedicated Yacht Club Restaurant, Top Sail Lounge, private pool and sundeck, concierge, and butler service) — the first Yacht Club ever installed on a Musica-class hull. The same refit added the Butcher's Cut steakhouse, Kaito Sushi Bar, an upgraded MSC Aurea Spa, a Technogym fitness centre, and a Gentlemen's Barber. Magnifica does NOT carry Cirque du Soleil at Sea. She has built a reputation as MSC's World Cruise ship — the 2026 edition sailed 4 January, is 130 nights, and returns 14 May.

Entertainment & Activities on MSC Magnifica

Entertainment on MSC Magnifica centres on the Royal Theatre spanning Decks 6-7, running MSC's in-house production shows and guest acts. The Deck 7 entertainment axis includes the Atlantic City Casino and its L'Olimpiade Sports Bar, the Onix Lounge, the Cuba Cigar Room, and the Cyber Café. Deck 6 has the Poker Room and four themed bars — the Topazio Bar, Ruby Bar, Purple Bar, and Tiger Bar. Deck 5 adds Le Gocce Bar. The October-December 2025 Palumbo Malta refit added the Butcher's Cut steakhouse (Deck 13), Kaito Sushi Bar (Deck 7), an upgraded MSC Aurea Spa, a Technogym fitness centre, and a Gentlemen's Barber. The outdoor pool deck on Deck 13 carries the La Barchetta Bar and La Conchiglia Bar. MSC Yacht Club guests have their own Top Sail Lounge on Deck 13, a private pool and sundeck on Deck 16, and the Yacht Club Bar & Grill — all added in the 2025 refit. Note: MSC Magnifica does NOT carry Cirque du Soleil at Sea.

Key Facts About MSC Magnifica

MSC Magnifica Deck Plans

MSC Magnifica's 13-deck passenger layout places the two main dining rooms on Deck 5 (L'Edera) and Deck 6 (Quattro Venti). Deck 6 is also the public-room axis with the Royal Theatre (lower level), Poker Room, and four themed bars. Deck 7 concentrates entertainment and the new specialty venues: Atlantic City Casino, Kaito Sushi Bar, MSC Yacht Club Restaurant, Cuba Cigar Room, Onix Lounge, and Cyber Café. Pool Deck 13 has the Sahara Buffet, Butcher's Cut steakhouse, the two outdoor pool bars, and the Top Sail Lounge (Yacht Club exclusive). Decks 8-12 are passenger cabins. Deck 16 houses the newly added Yacht Club private pool, sundeck, and Yacht Club Bar & Grill. Following the October-December 2025 Palumbo Malta refit she has approximately 1,317 cabins across 22 categories, including 63 new MSC Yacht Club suites.

Frequently Asked Questions About MSC Magnifica

What dining options are on MSC Magnifica?

MSC Magnifica has two main dining rooms — L'Edera Restaurant (Deck 5) and Quattro Venti Restaurant (Deck 6) — plus the Sahara Buffet on Deck 13. Specialty (extra-charge) venues added in the October-December 2025 Palumbo Malta refit include Butcher's Cut steakhouse (Deck 13) and Kaito Sushi Bar (Deck 7). The new MSC Yacht Club enclave adds a private Yacht Club Restaurant on Deck 7 and a Yacht Club Bar & Grill on Deck 16 for suite guests. Cyber Café on Deck 7 handles coffee and internet. Older references to L'Oasis French specialty or Shanghai Chinese predate the current ship map and are no longer on board. See the Dining section above for the full list.

Which ships are in the same class as MSC Magnifica?

MSC Magnifica is the fourth and final ship of the Musica Class, together with MSC Musica (2006), MSC Orchestra (2007), and MSC Poesia (2008). All four were built at Saint-Nazaire by Chantiers de l'Atlantique under the yard's various ownerships (Alstom → Aker Yards → STX France); Magnifica was delivered under the STX France name in February 2010. The yard reverted to its historic Chantiers de l'Atlantique name in 2018. Following the 2025 refit, MSC Magnifica is the only Musica-class ship currently carrying an MSC Yacht Club enclave.

Does MSC Magnifica have the MSC Yacht Club?

Yes — as of the October-December 2025 Palumbo Malta refit. MSC Magnifica became the first Musica-class ship to carry an MSC Yacht Club enclave; the Yacht Club concept previously existed only on Fantasia-class and later ships. The new Magnifica Yacht Club has 63 suites across five categories (including a Royal Suite with private hot tub and roughly 839 sq ft terrace), plus a dedicated Yacht Club Restaurant (Deck 7), Top Sail Lounge (Deck 13), private pool and sundeck (Deck 16), Yacht Club Bar & Grill, concierge, and 24/7 butler service. The full Yacht Club experience launches for summer 2026.

Where does MSC Magnifica sail in 2026-2027?

Early 2026 is the MSC World Cruise 2026 (4 January to 14 May, 130 days, from Civitavecchia via Genoa, Marseille, and Barcelona). The revised itinerary uses Los Angeles in place of San Francisco and Hilo in place of Honolulu. Summer 2026 she moves to Warnemünde (Germany) for Baltic Capitals and Norwegian Fjord itineraries, though the 16 May 2026 inaugural departure was cancelled to align with the world cruise return. Winter 2026-27 she returns to the Western Mediterranean before starting the 2027 MSC World Cruise in January 2027. Check the live tracker above for her current position.

Who christened MSC Magnifica?

MSC Magnifica was christened on 6 March 2010 in Hamburg, Germany by Sophia Loren — MSC Cruises' long-standing godmother. She was delivered at Saint-Nazaire on 25 February 2010 by STX France and completed her maiden voyage from Amsterdam on 9 March 2010. She is registered in Panama and carries IMO number 9387085.
