Explora II — Deck Plans, Live Tracker, Dining & Videos

Explora II is an Explora Class cruise ship operated by Explora Journeys. She entered service in 2024, measures 63,900 gross tons, and carries approximately 922 guests at double occupancy with a crew of approximately 700.

Quick Answer: Explora II is the second ship from Explora Journeys — 63,900 GT, 922 guests, launched in 2024. An identical sister to Explora I with all ocean-front suites and a near 1:1 guest-to-crew ratio. This page covers deck plans, live tracking, dining venues, and videos.

Best For: Luxury travelers who want Explora's all-suite concept with the freshest interiors in the current fleet. Explora II suits those planning Mediterranean or Northern European itineraries who want new-ship condition on a brand still establishing its identity.

Frequently Asked Questions About Explora II

How is Explora II different from Explora I?

Explora II is the second ship in the Explora Class, a near-identical sister to Explora I. Same 63,900 GT hull, same all-ocean-view suite configuration, same eight dining venues. She entered service in 2024 (IMO 9869887), giving her slightly newer interiors and any service refinements Explora Journeys learned from Explora I's inaugural season.

What dining is included on Explora II?

Five main restaurants are included in the fare: Fil Rouge (French-inspired international), Med Yacht Club (Mediterranean), Sakura (pan-Asian), Marble & Co. Grill (steakhouse), and Emporium Marketplace (all-day marketplace). Anthology is a fine-dining tasting-menu specialty restaurant with an extra fee and reservation required. Crema Café and Gelateria & Creperie are also included, along with twelve bars and lounges.

Who builds Explora Journeys ships?

All Explora Journeys ships are built by Fincantieri in Italy. Explora II (IMO 9869887) entered service in 2024. She is Malta-flagged with 922 guests at double occupancy and a crew of approximately 700 (sources range 640–738).

How does Explora II compare to other luxury cruise lines?

At 63,900 GT Explora II is larger than most ultra-luxury peers (Silversea, Seabourn, Regent) with more public space per guest. The all-suite, all-inclusive model competes directly with Regent Seven Seas and Silversea, while the Italian design sensibility and open-plan public spaces set a distinct tone.

Where does Explora II sail?

Current itineraries and departure ports are published on the Explora Journeys website. Explora Journeys emphasises destination-rich Mediterranean and transatlantic voyages across the fleet.
