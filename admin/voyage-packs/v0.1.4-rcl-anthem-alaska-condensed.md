**The Alaska packing rule is layering.** Thermal base, fleece mid, waterproof shell. Skip the umbrella (coastal wind blows them inside out — a hooded shell is superior).

**Endicott Arm runs 10–15°F colder than the previous day's ports** — the glacier face creates its own cold-air pool. Hand warmers earn their place.

**Currency aboard:** USD. **Auto-gratuity:** ~$18.50/day per person; verify if pre-paid.

**Decide before you board.** Run the [drink-calculator](/drink-calculator.html) on your real drinking pattern, the [budget-calculator](/tools/cruise-budget-calculator.html) for the total-fare picture, and the [tipping-calculator](/tools/cruise-tipping-calculator.html) for auto-grats + cash tips — mid-sail upgrades cost more per day and give you fewer days of usage.

## Getting to the ship — and getting home

### Arrival: Sunday recommended, Monday required

**Sunday — fly into SEA.** Same-day flights to a 4:30 PM Monday all-aboard are risky given Seattle weather. Sunday-night hotel in Downtown, Belltown, or Queen Anne puts you walking-distance to coffee Monday morning.

**Monday — embarkation.**

- **Terminal:** **Pier 91, Port of Seattle** (on the Magnolia/Interbay waterfront — NOT Pier 66 where NCL/MSC/Princess board).
- **Sea-Tac (SEA) → Pier 91:** Uber/Lyft 25–35 min, $40–55.
- **If driving:** Pier 91 parking garage ~$30/day. Bring proof of cruise reservation.
- **Embark window:** 11:30 AM – 4:30 PM. **All-aboard 3:30 PM.** Ship departs ~4:00–5:00 PM.
- **Royal app online check-in** before arrival — completes faster than paper.

### Day 8 debark and the trip home

- **The night before:** bags in the hall with color-coded tag by ~11 PM.
- **6:30 AM:** Bags off ship to terminal.
- **7:00 – 9:30 AM:** Walk-off in waves; self-disembark starts ~7 AM.
- **10:00 AM (latest):** All passengers off.

**Port Valet — use it.** Port of Seattle's free service transfers your checked bag from ship directly to your final-destination airline check-in. Walk off Anthem with only your carry-on; bag meets you at home. Sign-up mid-cruise (Day 5 or 6 — watch the daily program). **The single most useful Seattle post-cruise tip.**

**Getting from Pier 91 to SEA.** Uber/Lyft $40–55, 30–40 min. Pier 91 is NOT walkable to a Link light rail station like Pier 66 is — you're committed to Uber/Lyft/taxi or a shuttle.

**Airport-arrival timing.**

| Flight | Leave Pier 91 by |
|---|---|
| Domestic, after 4 PM | 1:30 PM |
| International, after 6 PM | 2:30 PM |

SEA TSA lines run 30–60 min during Monday-afternoon cruise return.

## Areas of interest during the cruise

### On the ship — Anthem spots worth knowing

- **The Observation Lounge** — heated, glass-roofed, panoramic. Sunrise coffee here is the day's best 30 minutes; busy mid-morning during glacier.
- **The North Star** — observation pod that rises 300 ft above the deck on a glass-walled arm. Free with reservation; book Day 1 in the Royal app.
- **SeaPlex** — bumper cars, roller skating, basketball; the indoor activity zone for rainy sea days.
- **Solarium** — adults-only glass-roofed pool. Quietest in Alaska.
- **Two70°** — wraparound aft glass lounge with morphing video walls; the evening show venue.
- **The Bionic Bar** — robot bartenders. Touristy but worth one drink.

### Sea-day priorities (Day 2)

1. **Sunrise coffee in the Observation Lounge** — the trip's best 30 minutes.
2. **Book North Star slot for tomorrow's glacier day** — do it Day 1 if possible, otherwise Day 2 morning.
3. **Mandara Spa thermal-suite day pass** — sea-day morning intro rate.
4. **Royal IQ app port talk for Sitka** — knows the tender timing and shore options.
5. **Specialty dining reservation** for the second half of the week.

### Theme nights (rotating across the week)

Four themed evenings on this sailing: **80s Night** (leg warmers, neon), **70s Night** (bell-bottoms, sequins, disco), **Caribbean Night** (tropical/floral, on the Royal Promenade), and **Let's Dance Street Party** (all-white outfit). Participation is optional. **Smartest packing move: one all-white outfit** (works for Street Party + doubles as smart-casual) + **one bright accessory** (scarf, sunglasses, sequined top) that gestures toward 70s/80s/tropical. Specific nights are set in the *Cruise Compass* once aboard.

### Day 5 — the Endicott Arm + Dawes Glacier hours

Best deck spots:
- **Your own balcony** (warm coffee, your own pace) if you have one.
- **Observation Lounge** — heated, glass-roofed.
- **Solarium** — heated, partial outdoor.
- **Deck 5 Promenade** — open-air, wind protection on the leeward side.

**Pack for the rail:** thermal, fleece, waterproof shell, hat, gloves, scarf, hand warmers, hot coffee in a thermal mug, binoculars.

### Specialty dining picks

- **Chops Grille** — steakhouse, Royal's standard.
- **Jamie's Italian** — Jamie Oliver pasta and pizza; family-friendly.
- **Wonderland** — Royal's "imaginative cuisine" tasting menu; the splurge experience.
- **Izumi** — Japanese; sushi + hibachi rooms.

### Port-day picks (one each)

- **Sitka** — totem trail at Sitka National Historical Park (free, 30 min). Pair with St. Michael's Cathedral and the Sheldon Jackson Museum.
- **Skagway** — White Pass railway (~$130, ~3 hrs) is the iconic Skagway ride. Or dog-sledding helicopter trip (~$650) as the splurge.
- **Juneau** — Mendenhall Glacier via Blue Bus shuttle (~$30 r/t), or whale-watching with Harv & Marv's (~$150) — humpback season peaks Jun–Sep. Mount Roberts Tramway is the third option.
- **Victoria** — Inner Harbour walk + dinner ashore. 4-hour evening stop; don't overplan.

### Photo spots worth knowing

- **Sailaway from the Solarium** — Mt. Rainier on clear days.
- **Sitka totem trail** — old-growth forest at 10 AM has the best light.
- **Dawes Glacier face from the Observation Lounge** — ice fills the windows.
- **North Star pod over Endicott Arm** — booked in the Royal app.
- **Victoria Inner Harbour at night** — Parliament Buildings illuminated.
