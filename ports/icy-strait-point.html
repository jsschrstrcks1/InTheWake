Icy Strait Point

Icy Strait Point: My Wild Alaska

The ship docks literally in wilderness. The pier is a restored 1930s salmon cannery, and the air smells of salt, spruce, and alder smoke from beach fires. Everything here is owned by the Huna Tlingit — profits go directly to the community.

We did whale watching first thing — within 20 minutes we were surrounded by humpbacks. One breaching male launched completely clear of the water seven times in a row — the guide said she'd never seen anything like it. The sound of 40 tons hitting the water is thunderous. Also saw stellar sea lions, tufted puffins rafting like little footballs, and Dall's porpoise riding our bow wave.

Then the ZipRider — 5,330 ft, 1,300 ft vertical drop, 65 mph terminal velocity. You sit in a harness chair and just... fall. The view over Port Frederick and Icy Strait is insane; I screamed the entire 90 seconds and immediately wanted to do it again.

Lunch was fresh Dungeness crab cracked tableside at the Crab Station and reindeer sausage grilled over alder wood. Sat on the beach eating while ravens tried to steal fries.

Afternoon forest trail walk — boardwalks through old-growth spruce and hemlock, interpretive signs in Tlingit and English, bear scat everywhere (but no bears, thankfully).

The pros: least commercial and most authentic Alaska experience, incredible wildlife, and all profits stay local with the Huna Tlingit community.

The cons: only one or two ships visit per day, so book early.

Practical tips: Do the zip first thing or last — lines get long mid-day.

The Moment That Stays With Me: That humpback breaching over and over, twisting mid-air so we saw its white belly and barnacles, while our Tlingit guide quietly said "he's showing off for you — that's a gift."

Getting Around Icy Strait Point

The multi-million dollar cruise dock opened in May 2016, with Norwegian adding a second pier in 2020. The port sits about 22 miles from Glacier Bay National Park and just 1 mile from Hoonah village.

  • Walking: Downtown Hoonah is about 1.5 miles from the dock on a flat, scenic route with beautiful views along the way
  • Shuttle Service: Complimentary shuttle buses run between the port and Hoonah village
  • On-Site: Most attractions are at the dock complex itself — the historic cannery, shops, restaurants, and tour departures are all steps from the ship

Most cruisers stay at the port area — everything you need is right there.

Depth Soundings Ashore

Practical tips before you step off the ship.

Safety: Bears are real here — make noise on trails and carry the provided bear spray. This is genuine wilderness.

Currency & Money: US Dollars accepted everywhere. ATMs available at the port. All major credit cards accepted.

Tipping: 15-20% at restaurants, 10-15% for services, $5-10 per person for half-day tours.

Shopping & Dining: The historic cannery complex has shops (locally/American owned) steps from the dock. Landing Zone Restaurant & Bar offers free WiFi and waterfront views. Cannery Cookhouse serves fresh seafood including Dungeness crab and reindeer sausage.

Wildlife: Expect to see humpback whales, orcas, Dall's porpoises, seals, sea otters, and salmon runs depending on season.

Booking: Popular excursions (especially whale watching and ZipRider) sell out quickly. Book through your cruise line in advance to guarantee spots — this is one of Alaska's most sought-after ports with limited daily capacity.

Emergency: Dial 911 for all emergencies.

Top Excursions

The best shore excursions and activities at Icy Strait Point.

Historic Cannery Tour: Explore the beautifully restored 1930s salmon cannery with cultural exhibits, Tlingit storytelling, and authentic Alaska history. Dining and shopping on-site.

ZipRider: The world's longest zip line at 5,330 feet with a breathtaking 1,300-foot vertical drop. Reach speeds up to 60 mph soaring over the rainforest and Port Frederick. An absolute must-do — reserve early.

Whale Watching: World-class humpback whale viewing in Icy Strait and Point Adolphus. Also see orcas, Dall's porpoises, seals, sea otters, and seabirds. Many consider this the best whale watching in all of Alaska.

Brown Bear Viewing: Small-group excursions to remote viewing areas where Alaska brown bears fish for salmon. Unforgettable wildlife photography opportunities.

Glacier Bay Aerial Tours: Flightseeing tours over Glacier Bay National Park, just 22 miles away. See massive tidewater glaciers, fjords, and wildlife from above.

Sea Kayaking: Paddle the protected waters of Port Frederick surrounded by old-growth forest. Calm conditions perfect for beginners, with wildlife sightings common.

Tlingit Cultural Experiences: Learn about the Huna Tlingit people through storytelling, traditional dance performances, and cultural demonstrations. All profits benefit the local community.

Charter Fishing: Fish for salmon, halibut, and rockfish in some of Alaska's most productive waters. Half-day and full-day charters available.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is Icy Strait Point worth it?
A: The most "real Alaska" experience you'll get on a megaship.

Q: Best excursion?
A: Whale watching (better than Juneau, locals say).

Q: How long is the zip?
A: 90 seconds of terror/bliss.

Q: Can you walk from port?
A: Yes — everything is at the dock.
