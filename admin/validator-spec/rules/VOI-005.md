## Rule
The site's pastoral voice requires emotional precision — calling hard things by their real names. Like-a-human defines a swap table:
- widow (not "navigating loss")
- died (not "passed")
- cabin felt empty (not "bittersweet")
- grief (not "journey through loss")
- loneliness (not "season of solitude")
- fear (not "apprehension")
- sin (not "brokenness")
- repentance (not "fresh start")

These swaps are not style preferences — they are pastoral commitments. A widow reading "navigating loss" hears a writer who can't say the word. She came here because the site promised honesty.

## Fix guidance
When the voice-audit flags a euphemism, replace it with the direct word. If the sentence feels too blunt afterward, the problem is usually that the surrounding context doesn't earn the directness yet — add specificity rather than softening the word.
