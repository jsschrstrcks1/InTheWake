Full Disclosure

Full disclosure: I have stood the watch on Grandeur of the Seas, for 9 days logged in her logbook and mine. This is no tide of borrowed winds, but depth soundings taken in my own wake.

A First Look

Why Book Grandeur of the Seas?

Grandeur is Royal Caribbean’s Vision-class sweetheart—small enough to feel neighborly, big enough for real variety. Ocean views are everywhere, venues are easy to reach, and crew say hello like they mean it.

Families get easy wins: Rock Climbing Wall bragging rights, arcade downtime, and poolside movies under the stars. Couples lean into slow sunsets and unhurried dinners—maybe Chef’s Table for a splurge, or a quiet evening in the glass-covered Solarium. First-timers and solo travelers appreciate the scale: less walking, more doing.

Dining runs from easy bites at Windjammer to sushi at Izumi, with the Main Dining Room anchoring each evening. After dinner, it’s shows like Broadway Rhythm & Rhyme, comedians, game shows, and live music. It’s the daytime-energy / nighttime-unwind balance Royal does best.

Sailings from Tampa keep itineraries sunny and simple—Western Caribbean hops and relaxed Bahamas loops—plus sea days for recharging.

Ken’s Logbook — A Personal Review

We booked an ocean-view stateroom and woke to wide-water sunrises and the kind of quiet that pairs well with coffee. Service was the standout: Miguel at Chops Grille paced the meal like a pro; Vincent D’Cruz at Guest Services solved problems before they grew legs; and in Windjammer, Vergel noticed our morning routine and brought fresh eggs without us asking—a small gesture that says everything about this crew.

  • Chops Grille: A filet so good it nearly had its own standing ovation, with sides like truffle fries that vanished faster than they arrived. Miguel had the timing of a captain easing into a smooth docking.
  • Taste of Royal: Tuna tartare was bright and lively; shrimp could use more spice; fried cheesecake? Worth writing home about.

Our Eastern Caribbean run hit Aruba, Curaçao, Bonaire, and Labadee. Highlights: snorkeling in Aruba’s bathtub-clear water; Curaçao’s distillery (and a sun-warmed cave); and in Bonaire, a quick taxi to Buddy Dive Center for great-value rentals and a drop-off reef that rewards confident snorkelers and divers. Labadee still delivered a good beach day despite weather canceling our dive.

Chair policy note: Royal is actively posting “no chair saving” signs fleet-wide. Enforcement varies by sailing, but staff respond well if you flag an obviously held chair. Our family uses one “base” chair—shared, with our things stowed neatly—so we’re present often enough that nothing gets re-homed to the towel station.
