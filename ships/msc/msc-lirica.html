MSC Lirica — Deck Plans, Live Tracker, Dining & Videos

MSC Lirica is the first-in-class ship of MSC Cruises' Lirica Class — the first purpose-built ship delivered under the MSC Cruises name. Built at Saint-Nazaire by Chantiers de l'Atlantique (then under Alstom Marine ownership) and delivered in March 2003, she was christened by Sophia Loren in her first MSC Cruises naming ceremony. She measures 65,591 gross tons and 274.9 metres in length (post-lengthening), carries 1,984 guests at double occupancy (2,658 maximum) across 992 cabins with a crew of approximately 721, and flies the Panamanian flag from Colón. From 31 August to 9 November 2015 she was cut in half and lengthened by a 24-metre midsection at Fincantieri Palermo under MSC's Renaissance Programme, adding 193 passenger cabins, 59 crew cabins, and the Doremi Spray Park. In 2026 she sails Eastern Mediterranean itineraries from Venice (Marghera) and Ancona.

Quick Answer: MSC Lirica (65,591 GT, 1,984 double / 2,658 max, ~721 crew, delivered 2003) is the first-in-class Lirica Class ship — sisters MSC Opera, MSC Sinfonia, and MSC Armonia. She carries two main dining rooms (La Bussola and L'Ippocampo), the 713-seat Broadway Theatre, the MSC Aurea Spa, the Doremi Spray Park added in the 2015 Fincantieri Palermo lengthening, and classic Italian-heritage public rooms. She does NOT carry the MSC Yacht Club. Eastern Mediterranean 2026 from Venice and Ancona; 2026-27 Brazil season cancelled — Eastern Med continues.

Who She's For: Mediterranean cruisers drawn to a smaller, more intimate ship than MSC's mega-class hardware — ideal for Adriatic, Aegean, and Eastern Med ports where the newer Meraviglia and World class ships feel overwhelming. Good pick for travellers interested in MSC's earliest purpose-built ship, for families wanting the Doremi Spray Park at a budget price, and for guests preferring a classical multi-deck atrium layout. Not for guests looking for the MSC Yacht Club (not installed on Lirica class), Cirque du Soleil at Sea (Meraviglia/Meraviglia-Plus only), waterslides, or the indoor-promenade LED-dome feel of newer MSC ships.

MSC Lirica is the lead ship of four Lirica Class vessels — MSC Opera (2004), MSC Sinfonia (2002), and MSC Armonia (2001) — all of which received 24-metre midsection insertions at Fincantieri Palermo between 2014 and 2015 under MSC's Renaissance Programme. Lirica's main dining rooms are La Bussola (primary MDR, Deck 6) and L'Ippocampo (secondary MDR, Deck 6 aft). Her theatre is named the 713-seat Broadway Theatre rather than following the Italian opera-house names used on some other MSC ships. The signature outdoor space is the Lido Catalano–style pool deck with La Pergola Outside Cafeteria, Pizza & Pasta and Hamburger Paradise stations, and the Doremi Spray Park added in the 2015 refit. Note that MSC Lirica's "La Pergola" venues are the poolside buffet/snack family — not the suite-exclusive MDR called La Pergola on sister MSC Armonia. Lirica does not carry the MSC Yacht Club ship-within-a-ship enclave; that concept debuted on MSC Fantasia in 2008 and was never retrofitted to Lirica-class ships.

Entertainment & Activities on MSC Lirica

Entertainment on MSC Lirica is centred on the 713-seat Broadway Theatre on Decks 5-6 forward, running MSC's production shows, guest acts, and classical performances. The nightlife axis on Deck 6 includes Le Cabaret Lounge, the Beverly Hills Bar and L'Atmosphère Piano Bar, plus the Las Vegas Casino and its Casino VIP Lounge. Lord Nelson Pub on Deck 5 handles English-style pub service. Late-night life moves to The Blue Club Disco on Deck 11 aft. The 2015 Renaissance refit added the Doremi Spray Park on Deck 11 — an outdoor splash zone for children. Upper decks carry two saltwater pools with whirlpools, mini-golf (Deck 13), shuffleboard, and a power walking track (Deck 12). Wellness is handled by the MSC Aurea Spa on Deck 11 with its thermal suite, beauty parlour, relaxation room, and MSC Gym. Families have a Baby Club, Mini Club, Junior Club, and Teens Karaoke club on Deck 11, all run by MSC's multilingual youth staff. MSC Lirica does NOT carry Cirque du Soleil at Sea, waterslides, or the MSC Yacht Club.

MSC Lirica Deck Plans

MSC Lirica's 13-deck layout uses musical deck names — Deck 5 Verdi, Deck 6 Puccini, Deck 7 Scarlatti, Deck 8 Paganini, Deck 9 Albinoni, Deck 10 Bellini, Deck 11 Vivaldi, Deck 12 Rossini. Deck 5 holds the Lord Nelson Pub, Card Room, Library, and Internet Café. Deck 6 is the main dining and entertainment axis — La Bussola and L'Ippocampo MDRs, The Broadway Theatre (lower level), The Coffee Corner, The Beverly Hills piano bar, L'Atmosphère, Le Cabaret Lounge, Kaito Sushi Bar, Las Vegas Casino and Casino VIP Lounge. Decks 7-10 are passenger cabins. Deck 11 (Vivaldi) is the pool deck with Le Bistrot Buffet, La Pergola Outside Cafeteria, two saltwater pools, MSC Aurea Spa and thermal suite, gym, Doremi Spray Park, kids' clubs, and The Blue Club Disco. Deck 12 carries the Top 13 Exclusive Solarium and power walking track; Deck 13 has minigolf and shuffleboard.

Frequently Asked Questions About MSC Lirica

What dining options are on MSC Lirica?

MSC Lirica has two main dining rooms on Deck 6 — La Bussola Restaurant (primary MDR, ~660 seats) and L'Ippocampo Restaurant (secondary MDR, ~400 seats aft) — plus the Le Bistrot Buffet on Deck 11 and the open-air La Pergola Outside Cafeteria with Pizza & Pasta and Hamburger Paradise stations. On Lirica, "La Pergola" is the poolside buffet/snack family — not a main dining room (Armonia's La Pergola is a Yacht-Club-style suite restaurant; do not conflate the two). The only specialty extra-charge restaurant on MSC's current deck plan is the Kaito Sushi Bar (Deck 6, ~40 seats). Casual café venues include The Coffee Corner, The Italian Ice Cream Bar, and The Vitamin Bar. See the Dining section above for the full list.

Which ships are in the same class as MSC Lirica?

MSC Lirica is the first-in-class Lirica Class ship, together with MSC Opera (2004), MSC Sinfonia (ex-European Stars, 2002), and MSC Armonia (ex-European Vision, 2001). All four were cut in half and lengthened by a 24-metre midsection at Fincantieri Palermo between 2014 and 2015 under MSC's Renaissance Programme. The yard where Lirica was originally built — Chantiers de l'Atlantique at Saint-Nazaire — was then under Alstom ownership; the Aker Yards acquisition that eventually became STX France/Europe happened later in January 2006.

Where does MSC Lirica sail in 2026-2027?

In summer 2026 MSC Lirica sails Eastern Mediterranean itineraries from Venice (Porto Marghera) and Ancona, with 7-night round-trips calling at Kotor, Mykonos, Syros, and Adriatic ports. Earlier in spring 2026 she sailed Eastern Mediterranean cruises from Piraeus (Athens). For winter 2026-27, MSC cancelled her previously announced South America/Brazil season in March 2026 for "operational reasons" and redeployed her to continue in the Eastern Mediterranean. She is NOT sailing to South Africa for 2026-27 — that deployment is covered by MSC Armonia replacing MSC Opera, beginning with a repositioning cruise from Marghera on 1 November 2026 to Durban.

Does MSC Lirica have the MSC Yacht Club?

No. The MSC Yacht Club ship-within-a-ship concept debuted on MSC Fantasia in 2008 and has never been retrofitted to Lirica-class ships. MSC Lirica does offer Aurea Experience suite categories with MSC Aurea Spa privileges, but that is a fare class bundling spa access and premium amenities rather than a Yacht Club enclave with its own restaurant, lounge, and private sundeck. The Top 13 Exclusive Solarium on Deck 12 is available to Aurea guests.

Who christened MSC Lirica?

MSC Lirica was christened in 2003 by Sophia Loren — this was her first MSC Cruises christening, beginning a long relationship that has since spanned 19 MSC ship namings. She was delivered by Chantiers de l'Atlantique in Saint-Nazaire, France in March 2003 (at that time the yard was under Alstom Marine ownership). MSC Lirica is registered in Panama (Colón) and carries IMO number 9246102. She was cut in half and lengthened by a 24-metre midsection at Fincantieri Palermo between 31 August and 9 November 2015 as part of MSC's Renaissance Programme, gaining 193 passenger cabins, 59 crew cabins, the Doremi Spray Park, and an expanded poolside La Pergola buffet family.
