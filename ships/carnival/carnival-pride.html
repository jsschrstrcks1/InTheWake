First Look: Carnival Pride

Ship Overview

Carnival Pride is the 2nd Spirit-class cruise ship, christened by NASA astronaut Tamara Jernigan at Port Canaveral on January 7, 2002. She pioneered year-round Caribbean cruising from Baltimore starting in 2009.

Designer Joe Farcus created her stunning Icons of Beauty interior theme, celebrating beauty in art, architecture, and the human form. The Normandie Restaurant evokes Art Deco elegance across two decks.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the dining options on Carnival Pride?

Carnival Pride features the Normandie Restaurant main dining room with Your Time Dining flexibility. Complimentary casual options include Guy's Burger Joint, BlueIguana Cantina for fresh tacos and burritos, and the Lido Marketplace buffet. Specialty restaurants include Fahrenheit 555 steakhouse, Bonsai Sushi, and Cucina del Capitano Italian dining.

Where does Carnival Pride sail from?

Carnival Pride is homeported year-round in Baltimore, Maryland, offering Caribbean, Bahamas, Bermuda, and Canada/New England cruises. Baltimore is convenient for Mid-Atlantic travelers and offers free parking options.

Who is the godmother of Carnival Pride?

Kathie Lee Gifford, the television personality and former Today show host, christened Carnival Pride on January 12, 2002 in Fort Lauderdale. The ship features Art Deco décor inspired by 1930s ocean liners.
