## Rule
A logbook entry should contain the narrative anatomy defined in LOGBOOK_ENTRY_STANDARDS_v2.300: opening hook → emotional tension → ship/venue/port in action → emotional pivot → reflection + lesson. These are not labeled sections — they are movements in continuous prose. An LLM reviewer detects their presence or absence.

## Why (rationale)
The narrative anatomy is what makes a logbook entry a story rather than a brochure. Without the emotional pivot (the moment where the experience turns into meaning), the entry is a descriptive paragraph pretending to be a story. The standard says: "Story, not brochure."

## Fix guidance
Read LOGBOOK_ENTRY_STANDARDS_v2.300.md for the complete anatomy with examples. The most common miss is the emotional pivot — writers describe the experience in detail but never turn toward what it meant.
