Rhapsody of the Seas

The Vision Class is Royal Caribbean at its most human scale — floor‑to‑ceiling glass, a sun‑splashed Solarium, and a sweeping, light‑filled Centrum that feels like an atrium hotel at sea. These ships trade mega‑park thrills for classic ocean time: coffee with a view, string‑quartet sunsets, and promenades that smell like salt and teak.

Days naturally bend around the sea — lazy lunches at Windjammer, unhurried dinners in the Main Dining Room, live music drifting from the lounges. If you want boutique‑hotel‑at‑sea energy and itinerary‑first cruising, this class is your sweet spot.

Rhapsody made her debut in 1997 and, after a US$54M bow‑to‑stern refit in 2012, emerged with modern comforts — poolside movie screen, refreshed dining like Giovanni’s Table and Izumi, upgraded lounges, and ship‑wide tech improvements.

She leans into the intimate side of cruising: a lively but not overwhelming pool deck, a glass‑lined Centrum where evening music pulls people together, and thoughtful bar programs that pair perfectly with sail‑away views. On port‑rich routes — Greek Isles, the Adriatic, Caribbean — she feels purpose‑built for days that start early and end well.

Choose Rhapsody if you want the classic cruise vibe done right — polished but friendly, compact but complete, with just enough nightlife to keep you out under the stars.
