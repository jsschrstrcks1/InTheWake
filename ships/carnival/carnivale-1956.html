The Ship That Doubled Carnival's Fleet

Historic Significance: TSS Carnivale joined the fleet in 1975 as Carnival's second ship, doubling capacity and proving that the "Fun Ship" concept had legs.

Timeline

1956: Built as RMS Empress of Britain for Canadian Pacific.
1975: Purchased by Carnival, renamed TSS Carnivale.
1975-1993: Serves Carnival for 18 years alongside Mardi Gras and Festivale.
1993: Sold; sails as Queen Anna Maria, then Fiesta Marina.
2000s: Renamed Olympic, then The Topaz.
2008: Scrapped after 52 years of total service.
