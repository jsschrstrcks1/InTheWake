Frequently Asked Questions

What is BOLT roller coaster on Carnival Mardi Gras?

BOLT: Ultimate Sea Coaster is the first roller coaster at sea, debuting on Carnival Mardi Gras in 2021. The electric-powered coaster reaches speeds up to 40 mph on an 800-foot track above the ship's top deck. Riders control their own speed through twists, dips, and drops with stunning ocean views.

Which ships are in the Excel Class with Carnival Mardi Gras?

Carnival Mardi Gras is the lead Excel-class ship, joined by Carnival Celebration (2022), Carnival Jubilee (2023), and Carnival Festivale (2027). All feature BOLT coaster, LNG propulsion, and themed zones.

What is the French Quarter zone on Carnival Mardi Gras?

The French Quarter is a New Orleans-themed zone exclusive to Carnival Mardi Gras, featuring Emeril's Bistro 1396 (Creole cuisine), Brass Magnolia jazz bar, Fortune Teller Bar, and NOLA-inspired entertainment celebrating New Orleans culture.

Where does Carnival Mardi Gras sail from?

Carnival Mardi Gras is homeported at Port Canaveral, Florida year-round, offering 6-8 day Caribbean cruises to Eastern Caribbean (San Juan, St. Thomas, St. Maarten) and Western Caribbean (Cozumel, Costa Maya) destinations.

What are the six themed zones on Carnival Mardi Gras?

Carnival Mardi Gras features six distinct zones: French Quarter (New Orleans theme, exclusive to Mardi Gras), Grand Central (atrium with shops), Summer Landing (outdoor pool area), La Piazza (Italian marketplace), Lido (pool deck and buffet), and Ultimate Playground (BOLT coaster, waterslides, sports).
