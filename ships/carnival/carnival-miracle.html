Overview

Carnival Miracle is the 4th and final Spirit-class cruise ship, delivered in 2004. As the culmination of Carnival's successful mid-sized series, she combines intimate ambiance with classic Fun Ship amenities, making her ideal for Alaska adventures from Seattle and Caribbean escapes from Tampa and Galveston.

Christened by Jessica Lynch, the retired US Army Private renowned for her dramatic rescue during the Iraq War, Carnival Miracle represents courage and perseverance. Her 2015 Fun Ship 2.0 upgrade brought modern favorites like Guy's Burger Joint, BlueIguana Cantina, and the Alchemy Bar.

Ship Timeline

2004

Delivered by STX Finland (Kvaerner Masa-Yards, Hull 503) on January 17. Christened by godmother Jessica Lynch, retired US Army Private, in Jacksonville, Florida.

2004

Maiden voyage February 27 from Jacksonville to the Bahamas, with a call at Freeport.

2015

Fun Ship 2.0 upgrade completed, adding Guy's Burger Joint, BlueIguana Cantina, Red Frog Rum Bar, and Alchemy Bar.

2020

Operations suspended due to COVID-19 pandemic. Crew repatriation and lay-up period.

2021

Returned to service with enhanced health protocols, sailing Caribbean itineraries.

2025

Currently sailing Alaska cruises from Seattle and Caribbean voyages from Tampa/Galveston seasonally.

Frequently Asked Questions

What class is Carnival Miracle?

Carnival Miracle is the 4th and final Spirit-class ship, built in 2004. Sister ships include Carnival Spirit, Carnival Pride, and Carnival Legend.

Where does Carnival Miracle sail from?

Carnival Miracle sails Alaska cruises from Seattle, Washington, and Caribbean itineraries seasonally from Tampa, Florida and Galveston, Texas.

How many passengers does Carnival Miracle hold?

Carnival Miracle has a capacity of 2,124 guests at double occupancy, with a crew of approximately 930.

What dining options are on Carnival Miracle?

After the Fun Ship 2.0 upgrade, Carnival Miracle features Guy's Burger Joint, BlueIguana Cantina, Red Frog Rum Bar, Alchemy Bar, and the Lido Marketplace buffet, plus the Bacchus main dining room.

Who was Carnival Miracle's godmother?

Carnival Miracle was christened by Jessica Lynch, the retired US Army Private famous for her rescue during the Iraq War. The christening took place in Jacksonville, Florida in 2004.
