Seven Seas Explorer — Deck Plans, Live Tracker, Dining & Videos

Seven Seas Explorer is an Explorer Class cruise ship operated by Regent Seven Seas Cruises. She entered service in 2016, measures 55,254 gross tons, and carries approximately 750 guests at double occupancy with a crew of 552.

Key Facts

  • Class: Explorer Class
  • Year: 2016
  • Tonnage: 55,254 GT
  • Guests: 750
  • Crew: 552
  • IMO: 9703150

Quick Answer: Seven Seas Explorer is Regent's flagship — 55,254 GT, 750 guests, launched in 2016. Billed as "the most luxurious ship ever built" at launch, she is all-suite, all-balcony, and fully inclusive (drinks, dining, shore excursions, Wi-Fi). This page covers deck plans, live tracking, dining venues, and videos.

Who She's For: Luxury travelers who want everything included in the fare — no surprise charges for drinks, specialty dining, shore excursions, or gratuities. Explorer suits those who prefer an intimate 750-guest ship with the space ratio and finish of a boutique hotel.

Seven Seas Explorer tends to suit experienced cruisers who have outgrown premium lines and want no-decision-required luxury. The all-inclusive fare means you never sign a check onboard — specialty restaurants, premium spirits, curated shore excursions, and Wi-Fi are all included. At 750 guests with 552 crew, the service ratio is among the best in the industry. If you want even smaller ships, Seven Seas Navigator (490 guests) or Seabourn's fleet offers more intimacy. If all-inclusive matters less than price, Oceania provides a similar culinary focus at a lower fare with fewer inclusions.

Frequently Asked Questions About Seven Seas Explorer

What dining options are on Seven Seas Explorer?

Seven Seas Explorer has seven dining venues — all complimentary and included in Regent's all-inclusive fare. Compass Rose is the open-seating main dining room. Specialty restaurants include Prime 7 (steakhouse, reservations required), Chartreuse (French cuisine), Pacific Rim (Pan-Asian), and Sette Mari at La Veranda (Italian, evening service). La Veranda serves buffet breakfast and lunch. Pool Grill handles casual outdoor dining. Pacific Rim debuted with the Explorer Class and is not on the older Regent ships. See the Dining section above.

How big is Seven Seas Explorer and how many guests does she carry?

Seven Seas Explorer measures 55,254 gross tons with 375 suites (all with private balconies) and approximately 750 guests at double occupancy, served by a crew of 552. She launched in 2016 as the lead ship of the Explorer Class and was billed by Regent as "the most luxurious ship ever built" at introduction.

How does Seven Seas Explorer compare to other Regent ships?

Seven Seas Explorer (2016, 55,254 GT, 750 guests) is the lead ship of the Explorer Class, followed by Seven Seas Splendor (2020) and Seven Seas Grandeur (2023) — all share the same hull platform, tonnage, and capacity with incremental refinements. She is larger than the older Mariner and Voyager (700 guests) and much larger than Navigator (490 guests).

What is the Regent Suite on Seven Seas Explorer?

The Regent Suite on Explorer is the ship's single top-tier accommodation at approximately 3,875 sq ft — among the largest suites at sea as of 2026. It includes in-suite spa facilities, a Steinway grand piano, a private balcony with a whirlpool, and dedicated butler and concierge service. A similar concept exists on Splendor and Grandeur.

Where does Seven Seas Explorer sail?

Seven Seas Explorer sails seasonal luxury itineraries including the Mediterranean, Caribbean, South America, Northern Europe, Alaska, and repositioning voyages. Regent also deploys Explorer on multi-segment world cruise routes. Check the live tracker above for current position.
