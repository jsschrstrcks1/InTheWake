## Rule
Logbook entries on ship and port pages should include a `## Full disclosure` section near the opening. This discloses the basis of the review (personal experience, composite research, AI-assisted, etc.) and establishes trust with the reader. The 2026-02 logbook audit found only 1% of 2,126 stories followed the full standard including this disclosure.

No validator enforces this — orphan.

## Why (rationale)
The site serves readers making real money decisions. A reader deserves to know whether they're reading "I was on this ship last November" vs "this was compiled from 200 reviews." The disclosure is the honesty floor.

## Fix guidance
Add `## Full disclosure` after the opening paragraph. State basis: "Based on our November 2024 sailing on Allure of the Seas" or "Compiled from verified guest reviews across 2023-2024 sailings." Keep to 1-2 sentences. Avoid hedging — say what it is, not what it "might" be.
