A First Look

Celebrity Infinity entered service in 2001 as the second Millennium-class vessel and underwent Celebrity's fleet-wide Revolution modernization in 2019. The refit introduced The Retreat — a ship-within-a-ship suite-class enclave with dedicated sundeck, lounge, and restaurant — along with completely reimagined staterooms, updated public spaces, and a refreshed pool deck. At 965 feet long, she carries 2,170 guests with a 2.2:1 guest-to-crew ratio.

Dining standouts include the glamorous two-story main dining room, Tuscan Grille (Italian steakhouse), Sushi on Five, and the exclusive Blu restaurant for AquaClass guests. Luminae at The Retreat serves suite-class guests with a bespoke menu. The Canyon Ranch SpaClub, Persian Garden thermal suite, rooftop terrace, and Martini Bar round out her public spaces. Infinity is particularly beloved for Alaska glacier routes where her mid-size profile allows closer approach to tidewater glaciers.

Frequently Asked Questions

What dining options are available on Celebrity Infinity?

Celebrity Infinity features the two-story Metropolitan Restaurant, Luminae at The Retreat (suite-class), Blu (AquaClass), Tuscan Grille, Sushi on Five, Café al Bacio, and Ocean View Café. Specialty dining carries a cover charge or is included with certain suite categories.

How do I find the deck plans for Celebrity Infinity?

Deck plans are available through the links on this page. You can also find official deck plans on the Celebrity Cruises website or in the cruise planner app.

Where does Celebrity Infinity sail?

Ship deployments vary by season. Check the Celebrity Cruises website for current itineraries and departure ports for Celebrity Infinity.

Is this information official?

This page provides planning resources and community insights. Always confirm details with Celebrity Cruises or your travel advisor before booking.

Is Celebrity Infinity good for Alaska?

Celebrity Infinity is one of the top-rated ships for Alaska itineraries. Her mid-size Millennium-class profile allows closer glacier approaches, and the ship's floor-to-ceiling windows in the dining room and observation areas provide exceptional views of the Inside Passage and Glacier Bay.
