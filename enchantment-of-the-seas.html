Enchantment of the Seas

The Vision Class is Royal Caribbean at its most human scale — floor‑to‑ceiling glass, a sun‑splashed Solarium, and a sweeping, light‑filled Centrum that feels like an atrium hotel at sea. These ships trade mega‑park thrills for classic ocean time: coffee with a view, string‑quartet sunsets, and promenades that smell like salt and teak.

Days naturally bend around the sea — lazy lunches at Windjammer, unhurried dinners in the Main Dining Room, live music drifting from the lounges. If you want boutique‑hotel‑at‑sea energy and itinerary‑first cruising, this class is your sweet spot.

In 2005, Enchantment literally grew up — the ship was cut in two and a 73‑foot (22 m) mid‑section was added, expanding public spaces and creating a larger pool deck with signature suspension bridges. A 2012 dry‑dock brought further refreshes across bars, lounges, and cabins.

The result is the peppiest of the Vision quartet: bright pool days, easygoing nightlife, and a Centrum that’s lively without being chaotic. She’s a favorite for short getaways and just‑add‑ocean vacations where the ship is your sunny base camp.

Sail Enchantment if you want compact done energetic — more elbow room on deck, a breezy resort feel, and itineraries that make weekend escapes feel like real vacations.
