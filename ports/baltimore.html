Baltimore: Charm City's Gateway to the Caribbean

Baltimore's cruise terminal at the South Locust Point Marine Terminal offers Mid-Atlantic cruisers easy access to Caribbean itineraries without flying south. The Inner Harbor's iconic waterfront, historic ships, and world-famous crab cakes make this homeport a destination in itself.

The port sits just minutes from downtown Baltimore, with easy access from I-95, the Baltimore-Washington Parkway, and BWI Airport. Whether you're driving from D.C., Philadelphia, or beyond, Baltimore's central location makes it an ideal departure point for East Coast cruisers.

Local Tip: Arrive a day early and explore the Inner Harbor — catch a game at Camden Yards, visit the National Aquarium, and enjoy Maryland blue crabs at one of the waterfront restaurants.

Getting to the Port

The cruise terminal is located at 2001 East McComas Street in South Baltimore, about 3 miles from the Inner Harbor and 10 miles from BWI Airport.

  • From BWI Airport: 15-20 minutes via I-295 South
  • From Washington D.C.: About 1 hour via Baltimore-Washington Parkway
  • From Philadelphia: About 1.5 hours via I-95 South
  • Uber/Lyft: Widely available; $15-25 from BWI Airport

Parking Options

The port offers on-site parking with both covered and uncovered options.

  • On-site parking: Reserve in advance through your cruise line
  • Off-site lots: Several third-party lots offer shuttle service
  • Pro tip: Book early for peak sailing dates — spaces fill quickly

Nearby Hotels

  • Four Seasons Baltimore: Luxury waterfront option with harbor views
  • Sagamore Pendry Baltimore: Historic Fells Point location with modern amenities
  • Hilton Baltimore Inner Harbor: Connected to convention center, easy access
  • Hampton Inn & Suites Inner Harbor: Budget-friendly with good location

Pre-Cruise Activities

  • National Aquarium: World-class exhibits and dolphin shows
  • Fort McHenry: Birthplace of "The Star-Spangled Banner"
  • Fells Point: Historic cobblestone streets, shops, and restaurants
  • Camden Yards: Catch an Orioles game at the legendary ballpark
  • Maryland Blue Crabs: Don't leave without trying them — LP Steamers or Thames Street Oyster House

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What cruise lines sail from Baltimore?
A: Royal Caribbean and Carnival offer seasonal sailings from Baltimore to the Bahamas, Bermuda, and Caribbean.

Q: Is there a shuttle from the airport?
A: No official cruise line shuttle, but taxis and rideshares are readily available. Some hotels offer airport shuttles.

Q: Can I walk to attractions from the port?
A: The port is a few miles from Inner Harbor — a taxi or rideshare is recommended.

At a Glance

Quick Answer: Charm City cruise gateway offering Caribbean escapes without flying south — world-famous crab cakes, the Inner Harbor, Camden Yards, and the National Aquarium make arriving early essential.
