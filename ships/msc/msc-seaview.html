MSC Seaview — Deck Plans, Live Tracker, Dining & Videos

MSC Seaview is the second and final ship of MSC Cruises' original Seaside Class, delivered on 4 June 2018 at Monfalcone, Italy by Fincantieri (yard hull 6257) and christened five days later on 9 June 2018 at the Port of Genoa by Sophia Loren. She measures 153,516 gross tons and 323 metres in length, carries 4,134 guests at double occupancy (5,331 maximum) across 2,066 cabins with a crew of 1,413, and flies the Maltese flag from Valletta. Her 18-passenger-deck layout shares Seaside's 'Miami Beach condo' aesthetic — the outdoor wrap-around Waterfront Boardwalk on Deck 8, the glass-floored Bridge of Sighs on Deck 16, twin 120-metre ziplines across Deck 19, the Forest Aquaventure waterpark, and a full MSC Yacht Club enclave — but uses completely different interior names from her sister. Seaview's two main dining rooms are the Silver Dolphin and Golden Sand, her main theatre is the Odeon Theatre, her casino is the Platinum Casino, and her signature pool is the Panorama Pool with a retractable Magrodome roof. Her 2026 deployment splits between a winter South America season from Santos (Brazil) and a summer Mediterranean season, with MSC announcing in April 2026 that she will return to Brazil and Argentina for winter 2026-27.

Who She's For: South American cruisers wanting a 2018-build MSC ship out of Santos in winter, European cruisers wanting a Seaside-style ship in the Mediterranean summer, and families who will use the Forest Aquaventure Park and twin ziplines. Suite travellers drawn to a full MSC Yacht Club on a Seaside-class hull at a price point below the larger Seaside EVO sisters. Not for cruisers expecting Cirque du Soleil at Sea (Meraviglia-class only).

MSC Seaview is the second and final ship of the original Seaside Class, together with her only direct sister MSC Seaside (2017). The enlarged Seaside EVO sub-class — MSC Seashore (2021) and MSC Seascape (2022) — are derivatives at 170,412 GT on 339 m hulls, not direct sisters. Both original Seaside ships were built at Fincantieri Monfalcone. Seaview is hull 6257, delivered on 4 June 2018, and christened five days later at the Port of Genoa by Sophia Loren — MSC's long-standing fleet godmother since MSC Musica in 2006. Although she shares Seaside's hull, layout, and class-signature features — the Waterfront Boardwalk, Bridge of Sighs, twin 120-metre ziplines, Forest Aquaventure Park, and the Asian Market Kitchen by Roy Yamaguchi chef collaboration — her interior naming is distinct: Silver Dolphin and Golden Sand main dining rooms (vs Seaside's Ipanema and Seashore Restaurant), the Odeon Theatre (vs Seaside's Metropolitan Theater), the Platinum Casino (vs Seaside's Miami Casino), and the Seaview Bar atrium. Her signature pool is the Panorama Pool on Deck 16, covered by a retractable Magrodome roof for all-weather sailing. A class-wide MSC Yacht Club enclave on Decks 16/18/19 has been aboard from delivery. Her 2026 deployment runs a winter South America season from Santos, Brazil (through April 2026), a summer Mediterranean season from Barcelona, Marseille, Genoa, Naples, and Palermo, and — per an April 2026 MSC announcement — returns to South America for winter 2026-27 with Brazil and Argentina itineraries rather than the originally-planned Southern Caribbean season.

Entertainment & Activities on MSC Seaview

Entertainment on MSC Seaview is built around the Odeon Theatre (Decks 6-7) for main production shows — distinct from sister ship MSC Seaside's Metropolitan Theater — and the Platinum Casino on Deck 7 (also unique to Seaview). Alongside the casino on Deck 7 are the Champagne Bar, Haven Lounge, The Billiard Room, the late-night Garage Club disco, the Interactive XD Cinema, the MSC Formula Racer F1 simulator, the Bowling & Arcade, and the outdoor Sunset Beach Pool and Sunset Beach Bar. Deck 8 Waterfront Boardwalk carries the main Marketplace Restaurant & Buffet, L'Atelier Bistrot, Seaside Lounge, Sports Bar, The Piazza, the Infinity Bridge glass walkway, the aft Infinity Bar, and the 2,410 m² MSC Aurea Spa. Deck 16 is the dining and pool zone with the specialty restaurants (Asian Market Kitchen by Roy Yamaguchi, Butcher's Cut, Ocean Cay Restaurant), the Ocean Point Restaurant & Buffet, the Wine & Cocktails tasting venue, the Panorama Pool with its retractable Magrodome roof, the glass-floored Bridge of Sighs, and the forward Yacht Club enclave with the Top Sail Lounge and MSC Yacht Club Restaurant. Deck 18 Playa Paraiso carries the Jungle Pool Lounge. Deck 19 Biarritz tops the ship with the twin 120-metre Ziplines, the Forest Aquaventure Park waterpark, the Top 19 Exclusive Solarium, the Aurea and Miramar bars, and the private MSC Yacht Club Pool. Deck 20 Sun Deck adds the MSC Sports Arena and the Adventure Trail ropes course. Note: MSC Seaview does NOT carry Cirque du Soleil at Sea (that concept is Meraviglia-class only).

MSC Seaview Deck Plans

MSC Seaview's 18-passenger-deck layout runs from Deck 4 through Deck 20. Deck 5 holds the Golden Sand Restaurant (one of two MDRs), the Seaview Bar atrium, and the Emotions Immersive Gallery. Deck 6 adds the Silver Dolphin Restaurant, the Venchi 1878 Chocolate Bar, the Shine Bar, the Piazza Grande plaza, and the lower Odeon Theatre. Deck 7 is the indoor entertainment axis with the Platinum Casino, Champagne Bar, Haven Lounge, The Billiard Room, Garage Club disco, the Interactive XD Cinema, MSC Formula Racer, Bowling & Arcade, and the outdoor Sunset Beach Pool and Bar. Deck 8 is the signature outdoor Waterfront Boardwalk level with the Marketplace Restaurant & Buffet, L'Atelier Bistrot, Seaside Lounge, Sports Bar, The Piazza, the 2,410 m² MSC Aurea Spa, the Infinity Bridge glass walkway, and the aft Infinity Bar. Deck 16 is the specialty-dining cluster with the Asian Market Kitchen by Roy Yamaguchi, Butcher's Cut, Ocean Cay Restaurant, Ocean Point Restaurant & Buffet, Wine & Cocktails, the glass-floored Bridge of Sighs, the Panorama Pool (with retractable Magrodome roof), the Panorama Bar, and the forward MSC Yacht Club Concierge Area and Top Sail Lounge. Deck 18 Playa Paraiso carries the Jungle Pool Lounge, Jungle Beach Bar, and the MSC Yacht Club Restaurant. Deck 19 Biarritz has the Forest Aquaventure waterpark, the twin 120-metre ziplines, the Top 19 Exclusive Solarium, the Aurea and Miramar bars, and the private MSC Yacht Club Pool and Grill & Bar. Deck 20 is the topmost Sun Deck with the MSC Sports Arena and Adventure Trail.

Frequently Asked Questions About MSC Seaview

What dining options are on MSC Seaview?

MSC Seaview has two main dining rooms — Silver Dolphin Restaurant (Deck 6) and Golden Sand Restaurant (Deck 5) — plus two buffets: Marketplace Restaurant & Buffet (Deck 8) and Ocean Point Restaurant & Buffet (Deck 16, aft). Specialty dining on Deck 16 includes the Asian Market Kitchen by Roy Yamaguchi chef collaboration (Pan-Asian, sushi, teppanyaki), Butcher's Cut steakhouse, and the Ocean Cay Restaurant seafood specialty. L'Atelier Bistrot is a French bistro/bar on Deck 8. Sweet venues include Venchi 1878 Chocolate Bar (Deck 6) and Venchi 1878 Gelato & Crêperie (Deck 7). Yacht Club suite guests have the private MSC Yacht Club Restaurant (Deck 18) and MSC Yacht Club Grill & Bar (Deck 19).

Which ships are in the same class as MSC Seaview?

MSC Seaview is the second and final ship of the original Seaside Class, together with her only direct sister MSC Seaside (2017). The enlarged Seaside EVO sub-class — MSC Seashore (2021) and MSC Seascape (2022) — are derivatives at 170,412 GT on 339 m hulls but not direct sisters. Seaside and Seaview share the 323 m Fincantieri hull and the Seaside-family signature features (Waterfront Boardwalk, Bridge of Sighs, twin 120 m ziplines, Forest Aquaventure Park, Asian Market Kitchen by Roy Yamaguchi) but use different interior naming: Seaview has the Silver Dolphin + Golden Sand MDRs, the Odeon Theatre, and the Platinum Casino, while Seaside uses Ipanema + Seashore Restaurant, the Metropolitan Theater, and the Miami Casino.

Does MSC Seaview have the MSC Yacht Club?

Yes. MSC Seaview has carried a full MSC Yacht Club ship-within-a-ship enclave from her 2018 delivery — the Yacht Club concept had debuted earlier on the Fantasia-class ships and was integrated into the Seaside class from build. The enclave spans Decks 16, 18, and 19 with Yacht Club suites and butler service, a 24-hour Concierge Area (Deck 16), the panoramic Top Sail Lounge (Deck 16), the MSC Yacht Club Restaurant (Deck 18), and the MSC Yacht Club Pool and Grill & Bar on Deck 19.

Where does MSC Seaview sail in 2026?

MSC Seaview is wrapping up a 2025-26 winter season of 7-night South America cruises round-trip from Santos (São Paulo), Brazil — as of 11 April 2026 she is approaching Santos for one of her final Brazil-season rotations. She then repositions to the Mediterranean for summer 2026, homeporting from Barcelona, Marseille, Genoa, Naples, and Palermo. For winter 2026-27, MSC Cruises announced in April 2026 that she will return to South America (Brazil and Argentina) rather than the originally-planned Southern Caribbean season, as part of MSC's five-ship deployment in South America for 2026-27.

Who christened MSC Seaview?

MSC Seaview was christened at the Port of Genoa on 9 June 2018. Her godmother was Sophia Loren, MSC Cruises' long-standing fleet godmother since MSC Musica in 2006. She had been delivered by Fincantieri at Monfalcone five days earlier, on 4 June 2018 (yard hull 6257). She is registered in Malta (Valletta) and carries IMO number 9745378 — distinct from her sister MSC Seaside, whose IMO is 9745366. A recent incident: on 12 January 2026 a fire safety system water line burst on Deck 10 during a South America cruise, flooding approximately 40 cabins; no injuries, guests were compensated and relocated, and the ship continued her itinerary.
