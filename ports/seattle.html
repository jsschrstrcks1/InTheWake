Seattle

Seattle: Your Alaska Adventure Starts Here

Seattle serves as the primary gateway for Alaska cruises, with multiple terminals along the waterfront welcoming ships from Norwegian, Royal Caribbean, Holland America, Princess, and Celebrity. The Emerald City's world-class food scene, iconic Pike Place Market, and stunning mountain views make it worth arriving early.

Whether you're sailing a round-trip Alaska cruise or beginning a one-way voyage to/from Vancouver, Seattle offers the perfect combination of urban sophistication and Pacific Northwest nature.

Seattle Essential: Pike Place Market at opening time — watch the fish throwers, grab a latte from the original Starbucks, and load up on local treats for your stateroom.

Getting to the Port

  • Terminals: Pier 91 (Smith Cove) and Bell Street Pier (Pier 66)
  • From SEA-TAC Airport: 30-45 minutes via I-5 or Light Rail + rideshare
  • Light Rail: To downtown, then taxi/Uber to terminal
  • Uber/Lyft: $40-55 from airport to cruise terminals

Parking Options

  • Pier 91 (Smith Cove): On-site parking available
  • Bell Street Pier: Limited; nearby lots recommended
  • Cost: $20-30/day
  • Off-site: Several lots offer cruise parking with shuttles

Nearby Hotels

  • The Edgewater: Iconic waterfront hotel, Beatles stayed here
  • Four Seasons Seattle: Downtown luxury near Pike Place
  • Inn at the Market: Boutique hotel overlooking Pike Place
  • Thompson Seattle: Modern downtown with rooftop bar
  • Hampton Inn Downtown: Budget-friendly, good location

Pre-Cruise Activities

  • Pike Place Market: Fish throwing, flowers, crafts, the original Starbucks
  • Space Needle: Iconic views of the city and mountains
  • Chihuly Garden and Glass: Stunning glass art installations
  • Museum of Pop Culture (MoPOP): Music, sci-fi, pop culture
  • Seattle Waterfront: Aquarium, ferris wheel, seafood restaurants
  • Capitol Hill: Trendy neighborhood with great dining

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Which terminal will my ship use?
A: Check your cruise documents — Pier 91 and Bell Street Pier serve different cruise lines.

Q: How's Seattle weather before Alaska cruises?
A: May-September is usually pleasant (60s-70s°F). Always bring layers — Seattle weather can change quickly.

Q: One-way vs. round-trip Alaska cruises?
A: One-way cruises (Seattle to Vancouver or vice versa) see more of the Inside Passage, visiting ports like Juneau, Ketchikan, and Skagway. Round-trips are more convenient if flying.

Quick Answer: The Emerald City's gateway to Alaska — Pike Place Market fish throwers, the Space Needle, world-class coffee, and stunning mountain views make arriving early a must.

Port Type: Major Alaska Homeport

Terminals: Pier 91 & Bell Street

Airport: SEA-TAC (30-45 min)
