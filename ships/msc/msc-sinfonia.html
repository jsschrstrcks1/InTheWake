MSC Sinfonia — Deck Plans, Live Tracker, Dining & Videos

MSC Sinfonia is the fourth and final ship of MSC Cruises' Lirica Class. She was originally built by Chantiers de l'Atlantique at Saint-Nazaire and delivered on 18 April 2002 as MS European Stars for Festival Cruises. After Festival's 2004 bankruptcy MSC Cruises acquired both European Stars and her sister European Vision in July 2004 for approximately EUR 220 million, and European Stars entered MSC service in March 2005 as MSC Sinfonia. In January-March 2015 she returned to the yard at Fincantieri Palermo as Phase 2 of MSC's Renaissance Programme, where she was cut in half and a 24-metre midsection was inserted — adding 193 passenger cabins and 59 crew cabins and bringing her to 65,542 gross tons and 274.9 metres in length. She now carries approximately 1,554 guests at double occupancy (2,546 maximum) with a crew of about 721, flies the Panamanian flag from Colón, and currently operates Mediterranean itineraries from Bari in summer 2026.

Quick Answer: MSC Sinfonia (65,542 GT, ~1,554 double / 2,546 max, ~721 crew, MSC service 2005, lengthened 2015) is the fourth and final Lirica Class ship — sisters MSC Lirica, MSC Opera, and MSC Armonia. She was originally built in 2002 as MS European Stars for Festival Cruises and joined MSC in 2004. Her two main dining rooms are Il Galeone (Deck 5, ~1,039 seats) and Il Covo (Deck 6, ~353 seats, dinner only); her buffet is La Terrazza; her main theatre is the Teatro San Carlo; her casino is the Sanremo Casino; her nightclub is the Pasha Club Disco. Bars include Buddha Bar, Shelagh's House (Irish pub), Manhattan Lounge, Café le Baroque, Ambassador Cigar Room, and the Sinfonia Lounge & Library. She has MSC Aurea Spa (943 m²), Doremi Spray Park (added 2015), two saltwater pools, and the Top 13 Exclusive Solarium. Summer 2026 Greek Isles from Bari; winter 2026-27 South America.

Who She's For: Value-conscious European cruisers who want MSC's smaller, more intimate 65,542 GT format for Greek Isles itineraries from Bari, families who will use the Doremi Spray Park water-play area and the Pinocchio Playroom, and travellers intrigued by the ship's history as the last Festival Cruises newbuild. Not for cruisers expecting an MSC Yacht Club (none on the Lirica class), Cirque du Soleil at Sea (Meraviglia-class only), or large-ship amenities like ropes courses and ziplines (those are on the Seaside class).

MSC Sinfonia is the fourth and final ship of the Lirica Class, together with MSC Lirica (2003), MSC Opera (2004), and MSC Armonia (originally MS European Vision 2001, joined MSC 2004). Sinfonia and Armonia are the two Lirica-class ships with a Festival Cruises origin: Sinfonia was built by Chantiers de l'Atlantique at Saint-Nazaire and delivered in April 2002 as MS European Stars — the last newbuild for Festival Cruises before the line's 2004 bankruptcy. MSC Cruises acquired her (and European Vision, now MSC Armonia) in July 2004 for approximately EUR 220 million, and she entered MSC service in March 2005 after a rebranding drydock. Between 20 January and 25 March 2015 she underwent MSC's Renaissance Programme Phase 2 at Fincantieri Palermo: the ship was cut in half, a 24-metre midsection weighing 2,200-2,400 tonnes was floated in and welded between the two halves, and the resulting hull added 193 passenger cabins and 59 crew cabins. The lengthening also introduced a Doremi Spray Park interactive water-play area, LEGO and Chicco kids' branding, and additional retail and public-space refreshes. Each of the four Lirica-class sisters uses unique Italian-themed venue names: Sinfonia's signature theatre is the Teatro San Carlo (named after the historic opera house in Naples), distinct from Lirica's Broadway Theatre, Opera's Teatro dell'Opera, and Armonia's Teatro La Fenice. Her main dining rooms Il Galeone and Il Covo are likewise unique to Sinfonia within the class. None of the Lirica-class ships carry the MSC Yacht Club.

Entertainment & Activities on MSC Sinfonia

Entertainment on MSC Sinfonia centres on the Teatro San Carlo, a main production theatre with approximately 557-600 seats named after the historic opera house in Naples — unique to Sinfonia among the four Lirica-class sisters. The Sanremo Casino on Deck 6 (~127 seats) is the main gaming venue, with the Sanremo Casino Bar inside. Late-night life moves to the Pasha Club Disco, a panoramic nightclub. Bars and lounges include the signature Buddha Bar (Asian-themed, ~91 seats), the traditional Irish Shelagh's House, the Manhattan Bar, the baroque-themed Café le Baroque, the Capri Bar, the combined Sinfonia Lounge & Library (~120 seats), the dedicated Ambassador Cigar Room, the wellness Vitamin Bar, and the outdoor Pool Bar. Dining highlights are the Il Galeone and Il Covo main dining rooms, La Terrazza Buffet, Caffè del Mare pool-side café, and the Gelateria Italiana Italian ice-cream counter. Family and activity spaces include the Doremi Spray Park interactive water-play area (added in the 2015 Renaissance Programme lengthening), LEGO and Chicco kids' club branding, the Pinocchio Playroom, the Teens Area on Deck 12 Bizet, a Virtual Arcade, two saltwater swimming pools with whirlpools, the 943 m² MSC Aurea Spa with thalassotherapy and a Balinese massage programme, an MSC Gym powered by Technogym, an outdoor jogging track, and a climbing wall. The Top 13 Exclusive Solarium serves Aurea Experience guests. Note: MSC Sinfonia does NOT carry the MSC Yacht Club (not installed on the Lirica class) and does NOT carry Cirque du Soleil at Sea (Meraviglia-class only).

MSC Sinfonia Deck Plans

MSC Sinfonia's 13-deck layout (nine guest-accessible) is organised around Italian-themed deck names. Deck 5 holds the Il Galeone main dining room; Deck 6 adds the Il Covo alternative dining room, the Sanremo Casino, and the Café le Baroque. The Deck 7-8 public-room axis carries the Manhattan Bar, Buddha Bar, Sinfonia Lounge & Library, Shelagh's House Irish pub, Ambassador Cigar Room, Capri Bar, and Vitamin Bar. Deck 11 is the pool and casual-dining level with La Terrazza Buffet, Caffè del Mare, the two saltwater pools, the Doremi Spray Park, and the 943 m² MSC Aurea Spa. Deck 12 Bizet has the Pinocchio Playroom and Teens Area. The topmost outdoor deck holds the Top 13 Exclusive Solarium, the jogging track, and the climbing wall.

Frequently Asked Questions About MSC Sinfonia

What dining options are on MSC Sinfonia?

MSC Sinfonia has two complimentary main dining rooms — Il Galeone Restaurant (Deck 5, approximately 1,039 seats, three meals a day) and Il Covo Restaurant (Deck 6, approximately 353 seats, dinner only) — plus La Terrazza Buffet on Deck 11 (24-hour casual dining) and the Caffè del Mare pool-side café for pizza, burgers, and pasta. A Gelateria Italiana serves Italian gelato and there is a Vitamin Bar for juices and smoothies. MSC Sinfonia does not carry the MSC Yacht Club, so there is no private Yacht Club Restaurant.

Which ships are in the same class as MSC Sinfonia?

MSC Sinfonia is the fourth ship in MSC Cruises' Lirica Class, together with MSC Lirica (2003), MSC Opera (2004), and MSC Armonia (originally MS European Vision 2001, joined MSC 2004). All four ships were built by Chantiers de l'Atlantique at Saint-Nazaire and all four received 24-metre lengthening midsections at Fincantieri Palermo between 2014 and 2015 under MSC's Renaissance Programme. Each ship uses different Italian-themed venue names — Sinfonia's signature is the Teatro San Carlo theatre (named after the historic Naples opera house), distinct from Lirica's Broadway Theatre, Opera's Teatro dell'Opera, and Armonia's Teatro La Fenice.

Does MSC Sinfonia have the MSC Yacht Club?

No. The MSC Yacht Club ship-within-a-ship concept debuted on MSC Fantasia in 2008 and has never been installed on the Lirica Class. Sinfonia, Lirica, Opera, and Armonia are the four older Lirica-class ships, all delivered 2001-2004, and none carry a Yacht Club enclave. MSC's Yacht Club retrofit programme has focused on the Musica Class (Magnifica late 2025, Poesia early 2026, Musica late 2026, Orchestra Q1 2027) — the Lirica Class has not been announced for a Yacht Club retrofit.

Where does MSC Sinfonia sail in 2026?

MSC Sinfonia wrapped up her 2025-26 winter South America season out of Santos, Brazil, departing on 17 March 2026 for a transatlantic repositioning voyage. Her 2026 summer season is based in Bari, Italy, operating 7-night Greek Isles round-trips calling at Santorini, Piraeus (Athens), Katakolon, Kefalonia, and Corfu. As of 11 April 2026 VesselFinder shows her in the Adriatic Sea approaching Bari. She returns to South America (Brazil) for winter 2026-27 as part of MSC's five-ship South America deployment.

Was MSC Sinfonia originally built for MSC?

No. MSC Sinfonia was originally built by Chantiers de l'Atlantique at Saint-Nazaire and delivered in April 2002 as MS European Stars for Festival Cruises — not to be confused with today's MSC Armonia, which was originally MS European Vision from the same Festival Cruises fleet. Following Festival Cruises' 2004 bankruptcy, MSC acquired both ships in July 2004 for approximately EUR 220 million and renamed them. European Stars entered MSC service in March 2005 as MSC Sinfonia after a rebranding drydock. In January-March 2015 she underwent a 24-metre lengthening at Fincantieri Palermo as Phase 2 of MSC's Renaissance Programme, adding 193 passenger cabins and 59 crew cabins and bringing her gross tonnage to 65,542 GT. She is registered in Panama (Colón) and carries IMO number 9210153.
