The Pearl

Royal Caribbean — Ship Neighborhood

Quick Answer: The Pearl is a stunning glass-enclosed multi-story structure on Icon Class ships. This architectural centerpiece features the Pearl Café (24/7 dining), lounges, and serves as a central gathering area. The dramatic glass design offers incredible views and natural light throughout the day.

Best For: Anyone wanting to experience Icon Class's stunning architecture. Late-night snackers (Pearl Café is 24/7). Those seeking a central meeting spot. Architecture and design enthusiasts.

Key Facts

  • Location: Central ship location, Icon Class
  • Style: Multi-story glass-enclosed structure
  • Dining: Pearl Café (24/7, complimentary)
  • Ships: Icon of the Seas, Star of the Seas
  • Access: Open to all guests

An architectural jewel at sea.

The Pearl is one of Icon Class's most distinctive features—a multi-story glass-enclosed space that serves as both a functional hub and an architectural statement. Natural light floods through the glass panels during the day, while the space transforms into a sophisticated gathering spot at night. The Pearl Café provides 24/7 complimentary snacks and light bites, making it perfect for any-hour cravings. Return to the Restaurants hub →

Frequently Asked Questions

Is The Pearl free to visit?

Yes, The Pearl is an open area accessible to all guests at no charge.

What is Pearl Café?

Pearl Café is a complimentary 24/7 dining spot within The Pearl offering snacks, light bites, and beverages.

Is Pearl Café always open?

Yes, Pearl Café operates 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.

Which ships have The Pearl?

The Pearl is exclusive to Icon Class ships: Icon of the Seas and Star of the Seas.

Is it good for photos?

Absolutely! The glass architecture provides stunning backdrops, especially during golden hour.

Can I sit and relax there?

Yes, lounge seating is available throughout The Pearl area.
