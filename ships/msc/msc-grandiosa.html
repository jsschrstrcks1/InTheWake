MSC Grandiosa — Deck Plans, Live Tracker, Dining & Videos

MSC Grandiosa is the first ship of MSC Cruises' Meraviglia-Plus sub-class, delivered on 31 October 2019 by Chantiers de l'Atlantique at Saint-Nazaire and christened in Hamburg on 9 November 2019 by Sophia Loren. She measures 181,541 gross tons and 331 metres in length, carries 4,888 guests at double occupancy (6,334 maximum) across 2,421 cabins with a crew of 1,704, and flies the Maltese flag from Valletta. She carries Cirque du Soleil at Sea in the purpose-built Carousel Lounge and was the first major cruise ship to resume sailing after the 2020 industry shutdown. From winter 2026-27 onwards she transitions to year-round service from Port Canaveral, Florida — MSC's first year-round commitment in Central Florida.

Quick Answer: MSC Grandiosa (181,541 GT, 4,888 double / 6,334 max, 1,704 crew, delivered 2019) is the first Meraviglia-Plus Class ship — sisters with MSC Virtuosa and MSC Euribia. She carries four main dining rooms (Il Campo, La Loggia, La Perla Grigia, Le Perle Grise), the Galleria Grandiosa LED-sky promenade, Cirque du Soleil at Sea in the Carousel Lounge, the Himalayan Bridge on Deck 19, and the MSC Yacht Club. Western Mediterranean summer 2026, then year-round Port Canaveral from winter 2026-27 onwards.

Who She's For: Families and entertainment lovers drawn to MSC's Cirque du Soleil at Sea programme (in the Carousel Lounge), travellers wanting a large Meraviglia-Plus ship with the full Galleria Grandiosa LED-dome experience, and American cruisers eyeing a year-round Port Canaveral alternative to the main Florida ports from winter 2026-27 onwards. The MSC Yacht Club enclave on Decks 15-19 makes her compelling for guests wanting a premium cocoon inside a large ship. Not for guests looking for the newest hardware (see the Meraviglia-Plus LNG sister MSC Euribia or the World class) or for travellers seeking a smaller, quieter ship.

MSC Grandiosa is the first of three Meraviglia-Plus Class ships, alongside MSC Virtuosa (2021) and MSC Euribia (2023). All three were built by Chantiers de l'Atlantique at Saint-Nazaire. The related base Meraviglia class is MSC Meraviglia (2017) and MSC Bellissima (2019). The Meraviglia-Plus ships are slightly larger than the base Meraviglia pair and share the signature two-deck Galleria indoor promenade with the LED sky ceiling — on Grandiosa it is called Galleria Grandiosa. Dining rotates across four MDRs (Il Campo, La Loggia, La Perla Grigia, and Le Perle Grise on Deck 6), with specialty venues on Deck 7 (Butcher's Cut, the Kaito sushi/teppanyaki pair, Hola! Tapas Bar) and a dedicated French L'Atelier Bistrot on Deck 6. She carries Cirque du Soleil at Sea in the Carousel Lounge on Deck 7 (two productions debuted with Grandiosa in 2019), plus the Théâtre La Comédie for MSC's production shows, the Himalayan Bridge ropes course on Deck 19, and the full MSC Yacht Club enclave. A three-week drydock at Palumbo Shipyard Malta in October-November 2024 refreshed public rooms and staterooms before her 2024-25 South America season.

Entertainment & Activities on MSC Grandiosa

Entertainment on MSC Grandiosa is anchored by Cirque du Soleil at Sea in the purpose-built Carousel Lounge on Deck 7, where two new Cirque productions debuted with the ship in 2019. The Théâtre La Comédie (Decks 5-6) runs MSC's in-house production shows and guest acts. The signature Galleria Grandiosa (Decks 6-7) is a two-deck indoor promenade crowned by an LED sky ceiling that cycles through atmospheric scenes throughout the day. Late-night life runs through the Attic Club on Deck 18, the Sky Lounge piano lounge, and the Horizon Bar on the upper deck. Deck 19 carries the Himalayan Bridge ropes course and the Wild Forest Bar. The Le Grand Casino on Deck 6 handles gaming. Wellness lives at the MSC Aurea Spa with its thermal area, treatment rooms, sauna, and steam rooms, and the MSC Gym. Families have MSC's multilingual youth programme for toddlers through teens. Shopping runs along Piazza del Lusso (Decks 5-6) with the Iris Galerie and The Art Wall on Deck 6.

Key Facts About MSC Grandiosa

MSC Grandiosa Deck Plans

MSC Grandiosa occupies 19 decks with 2,421 cabins. Deck 5 holds the Infinity Atrium, Infinity Bar, Purple Crab specialty, and the lower Théâtre La Comédie. Deck 6 is the main dining and Galleria axis with all four MDRs (Il Campo, La Loggia, La Perla Grigia, Le Perle Grise), L'Atelier Bistrot, Jean-Philippe Chocolat & Café, Jean-Philippe Crêpes & Gelato, Le Grand Casino, the Grandiosa Bar & Lounge, Edge Cocktail Bar, and the Piazza del Lusso shopping piazza. Deck 7 is the specialty and entertainment axis with Butcher's Cut, the Kaito venues, Hola! Tapas Bar, Carousel Lounge (Cirque du Soleil at Sea), Champagne Bar, Masters of the Sea pub, and TV Studio & Bar. Decks 15-16 hold the pool deck, Marketplace Buffet, Safari Bar, Sports Bar, and the MSC Aurea Spa/Gym. The MSC Yacht Club enclave occupies Decks 15-19 with Top Sail Lounge (Deck 16), Yacht Club Restaurant (Deck 16), Yacht Club Grill & Bar (Deck 19), Yacht Club Pool, and private sundeck. Deck 19 also carries the Himalayan Bridge ropes course and the Wild Forest Bar.

Frequently Asked Questions About MSC Grandiosa

What dining options are on MSC Grandiosa?

MSC Grandiosa has four complimentary main dining rooms on Deck 6 — Il Campo Restaurant, La Loggia, La Perla Grigia, and Le Perle Grise — plus the Marketplace Buffet on Deck 15. Specialty (extra-charge) venues include Butcher's Cut steakhouse, Kaito Sushi Bar, Kaito Teppanyaki, Hola! Tapas Bar, the French L'Atelier Bistrot, and Purple Crab. Sweets come from Jean-Philippe Chocolat & Café, Jean-Philippe Crêpes & Gelato, and the Atmosphere Ice Cream Bar. MSC Yacht Club guests have their own Yacht Club Restaurant, Grill & Bar, and pool bar on Decks 16-19. Note: MSC Grandiosa uses the original "Hola! Tapas Bar" branding, not the "Tacos & Cantina" name used on some other MSC ships.

Which ships are in the same class as MSC Grandiosa?

MSC Grandiosa is the first ship of the Meraviglia-Plus sub-class, together with MSC Virtuosa (2021) and MSC Euribia (2023). All three were built by Chantiers de l'Atlantique at Saint-Nazaire. The related base Meraviglia class is MSC Meraviglia (2017) and MSC Bellissima (2019). The Meraviglia-Plus ships are slightly larger than the base pair and share the signature two-deck Galleria indoor promenade with the LED sky ceiling — on Grandiosa it is called Galleria Grandiosa.

Does MSC Grandiosa have Cirque du Soleil at Sea?

Yes. MSC Grandiosa hosts Cirque du Soleil at Sea in the purpose-built Carousel Lounge on Deck 7, with exclusive shows developed for MSC. Two new Cirque productions debuted with Grandiosa at her 2019 launch. Cirque du Soleil at Sea is carried on MSC Meraviglia, MSC Bellissima, MSC Grandiosa, and MSC Virtuosa — but not on MSC Euribia, which has a Carousel Lounge but is not Cirque-equipped.

Where does MSC Grandiosa sail in 2026-2027?

In summer 2026 MSC Grandiosa sails 7-night Western Mediterranean round-trips calling at Genoa, Civitavecchia (Rome), Palermo, Valletta, Marseille or Cannes, Barcelona, and Palma de Mallorca, with guests embarking from multiple homeports. From winter 2026-27 onwards she transitions to year-round service from Port Canaveral, Florida — MSC's first year-round commitment to Central Florida — running 7-night Eastern and Western Caribbean/Bahamas itineraries to Nassau, Ocean Cay MSC Marine Reserve, Puerto Plata, San Juan, Cozumel, Costa Maya, Grand Cayman, and Falmouth. She arrived at Port Canaveral in December 2025 as MSC's largest ship ever based in Central Florida up to that point.

Who christened MSC Grandiosa?

MSC Grandiosa was christened on 9 November 2019 in Hamburg, Germany by Sophia Loren — her 15th MSC ship naming. The ceremony took place on the River Elbe with a coordinated light show on the Elbphilharmonie concert hall, hosted by Michelle Hunziker and Jochen Schropp. Her maiden voyage departed Hamburg on 10 November 2019 for a 10-night inaugural to Genoa via Southampton, Lisbon, Barcelona, and Marseille. MSC Grandiosa is registered in Valletta, Malta and carries IMO number 9803613.
