Vancouver

Vancouver: Where Mountains Meet the Sea

Vancouver's Canada Place terminal is one of the most spectacular cruise embarkation points in the world — five white sails reaching skyward, surrounded by mountains, water, and one of North America's most beautiful cities. This is the northern gateway for Alaska cruises, offering one-way itineraries to/from Seattle.

The city itself deserves serious exploration: Stanley Park's seawall, Granville Island's markets, Gastown's steam clock, and some of the best Asian cuisine outside of Asia. Plan extra days — you'll be glad you did.

Vancouver Magic: Rent bikes and ride the Stanley Park seawall at sunset. When the mountains turn pink and the cruise ships light up in the harbor, you'll understand why people fall in love with this city.

Getting to the Port

  • Terminal: Canada Place, 999 Canada Place
  • From YVR Airport: 25-35 minutes via Canada Line SkyTrain
  • SkyTrain: Direct to Waterfront Station, short walk to terminal
  • Taxi/Uber: $35-45 CAD from airport
  • From Seattle: 3-4 hours via I-5 North (bring passport)

Parking Options

  • Cruise parking: Limited and expensive in downtown Vancouver
  • Recommendation: Park at airport and take SkyTrain, or fly in
  • If driving from US: Several off-site lots offer cruise parking
  • Note: Most Alaska cruisers fly into Vancouver rather than drive

Nearby Hotels

  • Fairmont Waterfront: Steps from Canada Place, harbor views
  • Pan Pacific Vancouver: Inside Canada Place itself
  • Fairmont Hotel Vancouver: Historic "Castle in the City"
  • JW Marriott Parq: Modern luxury near waterfront
  • Victorian Hotel: Budget-friendly in Gastown

Pre-Cruise Activities

  • Stanley Park: 1,000-acre urban park, seawall bike ride, totem poles
  • Granville Island: Public market, artisan shops, fresh seafood
  • Gastown: Historic district, steam clock, restaurants and bars
  • Capilano Suspension Bridge: Swaying bridge 230 feet above the forest floor
  • Grouse Mountain: Gondola with city and mountain views
  • Chinatown: One of North America's largest, great dim sum

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Do I need a passport for Vancouver?
A: Yes — US citizens need a valid passport to enter Canada and for the Alaska cruise.

Q: Vancouver or Seattle for Alaska cruises?
A: Both are great. One-way cruises between the two see more of the Inside Passage. Vancouver is worth extra time to explore.

Q: Best way from airport?
A: The Canada Line SkyTrain is efficient, affordable, and drops you right at the waterfront.

Q: Currency?
A: Canadian dollars. US dollars often accepted but at poor exchange rates. Use credit cards or withdraw CAD.

At a Glance
