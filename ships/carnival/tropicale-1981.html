The Ship That Defined Carnival's Look

Historic Significance: TSS Tropicale was Carnival's first purpose-built ship and introduced the iconic winged funnel design by Joe Farcus. This distinctive whale-tail shape became Carnival's signature and remains on every ship today.

Built in Denmark, Tropicale pioneered Carnival's expansion to Alaska, Mexico, San Juan, New Orleans, and Tampa — proving the Fun Ship concept could work anywhere.

Timeline

1982: Built by Aalborg Verft, Denmark. Debuts the iconic winged funnel.
1982-2000: Serves Carnival for 18 years. First ship in San Juan, New Orleans, Tampa.
2000: Transferred to Costa Cruises as Costa Tropicale.
2005: Becomes Pacific Star for P&O Australia.
2008: Sold to Pullmantur as Ocean Dream.
2012: Chartered to Peace Boat for humanitarian voyages.
January 2021: Scrapped at Alang, India after 38 years.
