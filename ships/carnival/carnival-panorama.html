Overview

Carnival Panorama is the 3rd and largest Vista-class cruise ship, delivered in 2019. As Carnival's first new ship on the West Coast in 20 years, she brought the full Fun Ship experience to Long Beach with innovative features including SkyRide, the first IMAX Theatre at sea, and the Cuban-inspired Havana area.

Built at Fincantieri's Marghera shipyard in Italy, Carnival Panorama sails week-long Mexican Riviera itineraries to popular destinations like Cabo San Lucas, Mazatlán, and Puerto Vallarta. Her 133,868 gross tonnage makes her the largest Vista-class vessel.

Frequently Asked Questions

What class is Carnival Panorama?

Carnival Panorama is the 3rd and largest Vista-class ship at 133,868 GT, built in 2019. Sister ships include Carnival Vista (2016) and Carnival Horizon (2018).

Where does Carnival Panorama sail from?

Carnival Panorama is homeported at the Port of Long Beach, California, offering week-long Mexican Riviera itineraries to destinations like Cabo San Lucas, Mazatlán, and Puerto Vallarta.

What is the Havana area on Carnival Panorama?

The Havana area is an exclusive Cuban-themed retreat with Havana Cabana staterooms, a private outdoor pool, and the Havana Bar. Access is reserved for Havana stateroom guests during the day.

Does Carnival Panorama have an IMAX theater?

Yes, Carnival Panorama features the Carnival Multiplex with the first IMAX Theatre at sea, showing first-run Hollywood films and IMAX documentaries.

What is SkyRide on Carnival Panorama?

SkyRide is a suspended aerial cycling experience where guests pedal hanging bikes along an 800-foot track high above the ship's top deck, offering panoramic ocean views.
