Costa Favolosa — Deck Plans, Live Tracker, Dining & Videos

Costa Favolosa is a Concordia Class cruise ship operated by Costa Cruises. She entered service in 2011, measures 114,500 gross tons, and carries approximately 3,012 guests at double occupancy with a crew of 1,100.

Key Facts

  • Class: Concordia Class
  • Year: 2011
  • Tonnage: 114,500 GT
  • Guests: 3,012
  • Crew: 1,100
  • IMO: 9479852

Quick Answer: Costa Favolosa is the middle of three Concordia Class sisters — 114,500 GT, 3,012 guests, launched in 2011. Themed around fairy tales and fantasy, she offers Italian-heritage cruising at a mid-range scale. This page covers deck plans, live tracking, dining venues, and videos.

Who She's For: Families and couples who want a mid-sized Costa ship with a whimsical design theme. Favolosa suits travelers who enjoy Italian cuisine, multilingual entertainment, and competitive pricing on Mediterranean or South American routes.

Costa Favolosa is one of three Concordia Class sisters (Pacifica 2009, Favolosa 2011, Fascinosa 2012), themed around "fabulous" stories and fairy tales. At 114,500 GT she shares the same hull as her sisters. The Concordia Class sits in the middle of Costa's fleet — smaller than the Vista, Diadema, and Excellence Class flagships but larger than the older Luminosa Class (Deliziosa). Frequently Asked Questions About Costa Favolosa

What dining options are on Costa Favolosa?

Two main dining rooms (Ristorante Duca d'Orleans and Ristorante Duca di Borgogna, decks 3-4) and the Ca' d'Oro buffet are complimentary. Specialty venues with cover charges include Club Favolosa (Deck 11), Samsara Restaurant, Archipelago (seafood), Sushino, Teppanyaki, and Pummid'Oro Pizzeria. See the Dining section above.

How big is Costa Favolosa?

114,500 GT, 3,012 guests at double occupancy, 1,100 crew. Concordia Class, entered service 2011. Sister to Costa Pacifica and Costa Fascinosa.

Where does Costa Favolosa sail?

Mediterranean and South American itineraries. Check the live tracker above for current position.
