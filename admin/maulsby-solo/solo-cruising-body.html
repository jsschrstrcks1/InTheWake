Good questions

Solo cruising, answered

Will I actually be alone on the ship?

Only as alone as you want to be. You get your own cabin and the freedom to do your own thing — but with our hosted sailings and community, there are friendly faces for dinner, shore days, or a drink whenever you'd like company.

Do I have to be single to travel solo?

Not at all. Solo isn't single. Plenty of our solo cruisers are married — they just love traveling on their own terms. This is a solo community, not a dating or singles group.

What about the single supplement — won't sailing alone cost double?

Sometimes solo travel carries a supplement, but it varies a lot by cruise line and sailing. Part of my job is finding the lines, ships, and dates that treat solo travelers fairly — including dedicated solo cabins where they exist.

Is solo cruising safe?

Cruising is one of the most comfortable ways to travel on your own — a secure ship, staff who know you, and a built-in community if you want it. I'll walk you through what to expect so you board with confidence.

Does booking with you cost more than booking direct?

No. You get a specialist and a real person to call, at no extra cost over booking the cruise yourself.

What if it's my very first solo trip?

That's my favorite kind of client. I'll help you pick a first sailing that's easy to love, and you'll have both me and the community in your corner the whole way.
