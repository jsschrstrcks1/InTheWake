## Doctrine note

This adversarial review applies the lesson learned from the original Anthem orchestra pass: external LLMs hallucinate specific quotes, line numbers, and patterns. Every concrete claim made by these five reviewers is grep-verified against the actual pack body before being accepted as wheat. Claims that don't survive grep are chaff regardless of how confident the source LLM sounded.

**Wheat criteria:**
1. The specific quoted phrase exists in the pack body (grep confirms).
2. The pattern claimed is actually present at the frequency/density alleged.
3. The fix proposed would materially improve voice authenticity OR mechanical surface tells.

**Chaff criteria:**
1. The specific quoted phrase does NOT exist in the pack body (hallucination).
2. The pattern is real but within v2.3.0 voice-audit doctrine allowances (e.g., single instance, attributed, brand-signature).
3. The "fix" would harm voice without improving authenticity (e.g., stripping all hedging, removing all structure).
4. The claim is generic AI-detection FUD with no specific anchor in this pack.

### Reviewer 1 — Unlabeled (likely ChatGPT/GPT-style)

> Here's a hard, adversarial read of AI-writing signals in your document. I'm not assuming it is AI—but I'm flagging patterns that are statistically common in LLM-generated or heavily AI-assisted writing.

**High-Confidence AI Signals claimed:**

1. **Over-structured, systematized formatting** — Repeated rigid hierarchy: "Section X — …", bullet clusters with identical rhythm, consistent "Honest read…" / "For first-timers…" / "Pack for…" pattern. Claim: LLMs default to modular knowledge packaging; humans drift stylistically over 40+ pages.

2. **Exhaustive completeness (borderline over-coverage)** — Every port includes free options, paid options, accessibility, packing notes. Every system includes "honest read", "for first-timers", "for accessibility". Claim: AI tends toward coverage maximization; humans forget categories, emphasize favorites, show uneven depth.

3. **Repetitive rhetorical templates** — Reused phrasing: "Honest read on ___", "For first-timers:", "The realistic timeline:", "What to pack for ___:", "Decision rule:", "If you remember nothing else:". Claim: classic prompt-conditioned output behavior.

4. **Hyper-balanced tone (no real bias drift)** — Voice avoids exaggeration, avoids strong personal preference, gives "both sides", qualifies advice. Example cited: "The iconic excursion is genuinely worth it… but…"

5. **Density of "practical heuristics"** — "Decision rule: for Alaska…", "Pick ONE activity…", "Don't over-plan…", "The balcony pays back…". Claim: reads like compressed advisory heuristics typical of AI summarization.

6. **Unnaturally even sentence quality** — No real drop in clarity, no rambling sections, no rough transitions across 40+ pages.

7. **"Calm by design" language pattern** — "It is calm by design. There is no urgency, no upsell, no countdown." Claim: minimalist marketing tone with paired negation is a common LLM rhetorical pattern.

8. **Redundant reassurance statements** — "Verify your specific sailing…", "Your booking confirmation is the authoritative reference", "Confirm in the app". Claim: AI over-includes safety disclaimers.

9. **Information symmetry across sections** — Every section feels like it was generated with the same internal checklist: logistics, cost, accessibility, timing, alternatives.

10. **Slightly "too clean" expertise distribution** — Document demonstrates maritime knowledge, travel logistics, pricing norms, accessibility awareness, packing science, behavioral advice — all at a consistent level.

**Counter-signals acknowledged by reviewer:**
- Specific, non-generic insights: Sitka shuttle pricing nuance, White Pass booking difference vs cruise line, Port Valet explanation, Pier 91 Uber correction note.
- Personality leakage: "This is not a joke. The most underrated activity of the week."
- Brand voice consistency: deliberate, restrained, anti-sales.

**Verdict:** "Most likely scenario: This is AI-assisted, not purely AI-generated."
