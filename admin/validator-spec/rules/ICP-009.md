## Rule
Pages must NOT include a `<meta name="keywords" content="..."/>` tag. The keywords meta has been ignored by every major search engine since 2009. Its presence is a tell that someone's SEO advice is 15 years old.

## Why (rationale)
ICP-2 v2.1 explicitly rejects "SEO theater" — practices that look like SEO but add no value. `meta keywords` is the canonical example. Its presence on modern pages is noise at best, and at worst signals that the page author is running a stale SEO checklist that may include other dead practices (keyword stuffing, hidden text, etc.).

Standards docs don't currently ban it; backfill required in Phase 6.

## Pass example
```html
<!-- no meta keywords; clean -->
<meta name="description" content="..."/>
<meta name="ai-summary" content="..."/>
```

## Fail example
```html
<meta name="keywords" content="nassau, bahamas cruise, cruise port, caribbean cruise, cruise ship"/>
```
Validator emits: `meta keywords tag is SEO theater — remove it (dead since 2009)`.

## Fix guidance
Delete the tag. Do not replace it with anything. Keywords stuffed elsewhere (title, description, hidden divs) are also forbidden — see VOI-001 for content-level rules.
