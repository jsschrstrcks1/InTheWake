Quick Answer: St. Kitts has Cockleshell Beach with Killer Bee rum punch, UNESCO Brimstone Hill Fortress with insane views, and the last scenic railway in the West Indies with unlimited rum punch and choir singing.

St. Kitts: My Fortress & Beach Paradise

Sailing into Basseterre with Brimstone Hill Fortress looming on the hill feels like entering a pirate movie. My perfect day: shared taxi straight to Cockleshell Beach (great value) – two miles of golden sand, Carambola Beach Club, and the best grilled mahi tacos with Killer Bee rum punch at Reggae Beach Bar.

After lunch we do the scenic train ride around the island (the "last railway in the West Indies") with unlimited rum punch and choir singing. Then Brimstone Hill Fortress at golden hour – UNESCO site with insane views. Sunset back at the Port Zante bars with Carib beers watching the ship lights come on.

The Moment That Stays With Me: Standing on top of Brimstone Hill as the sun set over Nevis with a handful of other sunset chasers, feeling like I owned the entire Caribbean.

Getting Around St. Kitts

Port Zante – literally walk off into town. Shared taxis everywhere to beaches (great value).

  • Cockleshell Beach: Shared taxi (won't break the bank)
  • Brimstone Hill Fortress: Taxi or tour (worth every penny)
  • Scenic Railway: Book through cruise line or independently

Positively Worded Word of Warning

St. Kitts' hilly roads and rum punches are the island's way of giving you a free scenic roller-coaster with a buzz!

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Best beach?
A: Cockleshell or South Friars – both stunning.

Q: Killer Bee danger level?
A: Two and you're buzzing, four and you're dancing.

Q: Train worth it?
A: 100% – best money you'll spend on the island.
