Overview

Anthem of the Seas, the second vessel in Royal Caribbean International's groundbreaking Quantum class, redefined modern cruising when she debuted in 2015. At 168,666 gross tons, she blends high-tech thrills with luxurious comfort, accommodating up to 4,905 guests across 16 decks. From the soaring North Star observation pod offering 360-degree vistas to the adrenaline-pumping RipCord by iFLY skydiving simulator, Anthem delivers unforgettable experiences for families, adventurers, and relaxation seekers alike.

Currently sailing pristine Alaskan waters, Anthem's sleek design and innovative spaces—like the transforming SeaPlex with bumper cars and roller skating—make her a standout in RCI's fleet of 26 active ships. Whether you're tracing her path through fjords or envisioning her under starry skies, Anthem embodies the spirit of exploration on the high seas.

History

Ordered in 2012 as part of Royal Caribbean's "Project Sunshine," Anthem of the Seas was delivered by Meyer Werft in Papenburg, Germany, on April 10, 2015. She arrived in Southampton, England, on April 15 for her maiden European season, featuring Mediterranean and Northern Europe itineraries. Christened by British singer Emma Wilby (Katy B), the ceremony included a dramatic champagne zipline from the North Star pod.

Repositioned to New York for her North American debut, Anthem sailed to Bermuda, the Caribbean, Canada, and New England. In 2021, she pioneered UK "cruises to nowhere" post-COVID, departing Southampton for British Isles voyages. By 2025, Anthem marked her 10th anniversary with Alaskan summers from Seattle and a repositioning to Australia for South Pacific adventures, including Sydney departures to Bora Bora and Hawaii. Despite weathering storms like Hurricane Hermine in 2016, she remains a resilient icon of innovation.

Signature Features

  • North Star®: Engineering marvel rising 300+ feet for panoramic views—the tallest observation deck at sea.
  • RipCord® by iFLY®: Indoor skydiving with neon-lit sessions for freefall thrills.
  • SeaPlex®: World's largest indoor activity space with bumper cars, roller skating, basketball, and circus school.
  • Two70°: Futuristic lounge with roboshows, Vistarama® projections, and immersive virtual vistas.
  • Bionic Bar®: Robotic bartenders crafting cocktails at 2 per minute from endless combinations.

Dining

Anthem's culinary scene rivals urban hotspots, with 25 options (14 complimentary). Savor global flavors from Tuscan-inspired dishes to Japanese sushi and whimsical Wonderland creations. Highlights include Chops Grille for steaks, Izumi for hibachi, and La Patisserie for decadent macarons. Casual bites at the SeaPlex Dog House food truck offer hot dogs amid the action. For specialty indulgence, Johnny Rockets delivers classic American diner fare.

Sample Itineraries (2025-2026)

  • Alaska Experience: 7 nights roundtrip Seattle (Juneau, Skagway, Ketchikan; glaciers, wildlife spotting). Departs weekly summer 2025.
  • South Pacific: 9-12 nights from Sydney (Nouméa, Bora Bora, Maui; holiday specials Dec 2025).
  • Europe (Past): British Isles and Mediterranean from Southampton (2021 revival).
  • Transpacific: Repositioning voyages to Asia/Australia (e.g., Singapore arrival Nov 2024).

Bookings open for 2026 Alaska and Australia seasons, with festive Christmas/New Year's sailings.
