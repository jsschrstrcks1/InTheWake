Carnival Fascination

Retired 2022 Hollywood Theme

Mary Tyler Moore's Floating Cinema

Carnival Fascination is a Fantasy Class cruise ship operated by Carnival Cruise Line. She entered service in 1994, measures 70,367 gross tons, and carries approximately 2,056 guests at double occupancy.

Quick Answer: Carnival Fascination (70,367 GT | 2,056 guests | 1994) was a Fantasy Class ship with a Hollywood movie theme — one of eight Fantasy-class sisters that served Carnival for decades. This page covers historical deck plans, dining venues, and ship details.

Best For: Cruise history researchers or travelers comparing Carnival's fleet evolution — Fascination represented the height of Fantasy-class deployment.

Hollywood on Water

Carnival Fascination was the Fantasy Class's tribute to cinema. The fourth ship in the series, launched in 1994, featured Hollywood décor throughout — movie memorabilia, classic film references, the glamour of golden-age entertainment brought to Caribbean waters.

Mary Tyler Moore christened her, bringing genuine star power to the ceremony. America's sweetheart blessing a ship designed to capture Hollywood's magic felt perfectly appropriate.

Fascination served San Juan for years before moving to Mobile, bringing her Hollywood theme to different audiences while maintaining the Fantasy Class standards her sisters established.

Frequently Asked Questions About Carnival Fascination

What was Carnival Fascination's theme?

Carnival Fascination featured a Hollywood cinema theme with movie memorabilia, film-inspired decor, and references to classic entertainment throughout the ship.

Who was Carnival Fascination's godmother?

Mary Tyler Moore christened Carnival Fascination in 1994, bringing genuine Hollywood star power to the ship's debut ceremony.

When was Carnival Fascination retired?

Carnival Fascination was retired in February 2022 after 28 years of service. She was scrapped in Pakistan.

How many ships were in the Fantasy Class?

Eight ships comprised the Fantasy Class: Fantasy (lead ship), Ecstasy, Sensation, Fascination, Imagination, Inspiration, Elation, and Paradise. Fascination was the fourth ship in the class.

What were Carnival Fascination's homeports?

Carnival Fascination served San Juan, Puerto Rico from 1994 to 2014, then relocated to Mobile, Alabama where she operated until the COVID-19 pandemic ended her service.
